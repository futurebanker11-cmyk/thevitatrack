  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why this sheet matters</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="lead">In an emergency, seconds count. A single page with medicines, allergies, diagnoses, and contacts prevents delays and errors.</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Helps paramedics give the <strong>right care fast</strong>.</li>
          <li>Reduces medicine mix-ups and repeated tests.</li>
          <li>Makes hospital registration smoother.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok"><strong>Goal today:</strong> Fill the templates below, print one for the fridge, and save a photo on your phone.</div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- What to include -->
  <section id="what" class="section">
    <h2>What to include</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Identification</strong>: full name, DOB, address, preferred language.</li>
          <li><strong>Contacts</strong>: primary caregiver, emergency contacts (with phone numbers).</li>
          <li><strong>Diagnoses</strong>: major conditions (e.g., CHF, CKD, diabetes, COPD).</li>
          <li><strong>Allergies</strong>: drugs/foods + reaction.</li>
          <li><strong>Medicines</strong>: name, dose, times, purpose.</li>
          <li><strong>Devices</strong>: pacemaker/ICD, stents, valves, joint replacements, catheters.</li>
          <li><strong>Baseline data</strong>: usual BP/HR, weight (if CHF/CKD), typical O₂ if on oxygen.</li>
          <li><strong>Preferences</strong>: hospital/doctor, blood thinners, special precautions.</li>
          <li><strong>Documents</strong>: advance directive/medical power-of-attorney (where kept).</li>
          <li><strong>Insurance</strong>: plan name/number (optional on fridge, carry a copy in wallet).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Keep information <strong>short and legible</strong>. Use a black pen or type and print. Update after any medicine change or hospital visit.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Organize & update -->
  <section id="organize" class="section">
    <h2>How to organize & update</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Where to keep it</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fridge door (top-right) or inside main door — magnet/clip.</li>
          <li>Binder front pocket + photo in phone gallery.</li>
          <li>Copy in caregiver’s phone and a backup relative.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When to update</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Any medicine change or new diagnosis.</li>
          <li>After hospital/ER visit or new device implant.</li>
          <li>At least every <strong>3 months</strong> for accuracy.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Special notes -->
  <section id="special" class="section">
    <h2>Special notes (devices & directives)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Devices & precautions</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pacemaker/ICD: carry device card; note model and implant date.</li>
          <li>Joint replacements/stents/valves: note year and side/location.</li>
          <li>Catheters/ports/feeding tubes: note type and care instructions.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        If you have an <strong>advance directive</strong> or medical power-of-attorney, write where it’s kept and who to contact. Keep a copy in the binder.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="redflags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Chest pain/pressure, severe shortness of breath, fainting.</li>
        <li>One-sided weakness, facial droop, slurred speech (possible stroke).</li>
        <li>Severe bleeding, black/bloody stool, vomiting blood.</li>
        <li>Confusion with high fever or dehydration (very sleepy, no urine).</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Call your local emergency number. Bring this sheet and the pill box.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is one page really enough?</p>
        <p>Yes. Keep it short for speed. Add extra details in your binder if needed.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Paper or phone?</p>
        <p>Both. Paper on the fridge is easy for responders; a phone photo travels with you.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if I can’t list every medicine?</p>
        <p>Include the most important ones (blood thinners, heart, diabetes, seizure, inhalers) and bring the pill box or photos of labels.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I keep versions straight?</p>
        <p>Write the update date on top. When you change medicines, cross out the old page and print a fresh one.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
