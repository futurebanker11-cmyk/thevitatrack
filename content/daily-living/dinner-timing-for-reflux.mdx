  <!-- Why -->
  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why timing matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="kicker">In short:</span> Lying down with a full stomach makes reflux more likely. Finishing dinner early, keeping portions moderate, and elevating the upper body at night reduce acid backflow and nighttime cough.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Key mechanisms</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stomach pressure and delayed emptying after heavy or late meals</li>
          <li>Gravity loss when lying flat</li>
          <li>Fatty/spicy foods and alcohol can relax the lower esophageal sphincter</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Small changes help: moving dinner earlier by even 60–90 minutes can noticeably reduce nighttime symptoms.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Rules -->
  <section id="rules" class="section">
    <h2>Timing rules that work</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Finish dinner 3–4 hours before bed.</strong> Earlier (4–5 hours) if meals are heavier or you have severe symptoms.</li>
          <li><strong>No lying flat</strong> within 3 hours of eating. Sit or walk gently after meals.</li>
          <li>If hungry late, have a <strong>small, low-fat snack</strong> 60–90 minutes before bed (see swaps).</li>
          <li>Keep evening portions at about <strong>½ your lunch size</strong>.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Left-side vs right-side:</strong> If you must lie down, resting on the <em>left side</em> reduces reflux compared with the right.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Plate -->
  <section id="plate" class="section">
    <h2>Evening plate & portions</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Reflux-friendly evening plate">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Choose more</th><th>Choose less (evening)</th><th>Portion cues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Lean proteins (dal, lentils, tofu, fish, skinless chicken), soft-cooked veg, oats/khichdi, rice or chapati, bananas, melon, yogurt (if tolerated)</td>
          <td>Very fatty/fried foods, large tomatoes/onion/garlic-heavy curries late, mint/chocolate, chilies, citrus, pickles</td>
          <td>Protein = palm size; grains = ½–1 cup; veg = 1–2 cups</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Warm, mildly seasoned dishes</td>
          <td>Over-spiced, acidic sauces close to bedtime</td>
          <td>Stop at <strong>comfortable fullness</strong> (6–7/10)</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Drinks -->
  <section id="drinks" class="section">
    <h2>Smart beverages</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Better choices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Water in <strong>small sips</strong> the last 2–3 hours</li>
          <li>Herbal teas (chamomile, ginger—not peppermint)</li>
          <li>Warm milk (if tolerated)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Limit late:</strong> alcohol, coffee, strong tea, cola, citrus juices, and large volumes of any drink close to bedtime.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Sleep -->
  <section id="sleep" class="section">
    <h2>After-dinner & sleep setup</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Gentle walk</strong> 10–15 minutes after dinner; avoid vigorous exercise late.</li>
          <li><strong>Torso elevation:</strong> sleep on a <strong>wedge pillow (30–45°)</strong> or adjustable bed; add a thin head pillow on top.</li>
          <li>Avoid tight waistbands; pick breathable sleepwear.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Stacking soft pillows often kinks the neck. A solid wedge lifts the whole upper body and works better.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine timing</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common schedules (confirm your plan)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>PPI</strong> (omeprazole, pantoprazole): typically <strong>30–60 min before breakfast</strong>; if twice daily, second dose <strong>30–60 min before dinner</strong>.</li>
          <li><strong>H2 blocker</strong> (famotidine): often at <strong>bedtime</strong> for nocturnal symptoms or as your clinician directs.</li>
          <li><strong>Antacids/alginates</strong>: for breakthrough symptoms after meals/at bedtime—keep 2–3 hours apart from some meds (e.g., certain antibiotics/thyroid pills).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Medication check:</strong> Ask about reflux-worsening drugs (some pain pills, certain blood pressure meds) or pills that irritate the esophagus—take those with water and stay upright 30 minutes.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Special -->
  <section id="special" class="section">
    <h2>Special situations</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Special situations">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Situation</th><th>Adjustments</th><th>Watch-outs</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Diabetes (risk of lows)</strong></td>
          <td>Don’t skip dinner. If moving dinner earlier, discuss dosing; keep a <strong>planned small snack</strong> if needed.</td>
          <td>Treat hypoglycemia promptly; avoid large sugary snacks late.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Gastroparesis</strong></td>
          <td>Smaller, more frequent meals; low-fat, softer textures; finish even earlier.</td>
          <td>Ask about pro-motility meds and individualized plan.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Osteoporosis meds (bisphosphonates)</strong></td>
          <td>Take on an empty stomach with water in the morning; stay upright 30 min.</td>
          <td>Reduces esophageal irritation.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Obesity/OSA</strong></td>
          <td>Prefer side-sleep; use wedge; avoid late heavy meals.</td>
          <td>Use CPAP if prescribed; avoid alcohol late.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If bedtime is 10:30 pm</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>6:15–6:45 pm: Dinner (light–moderate, low-fat)</li>
          <li>7:00 pm: 10–15 min easy walk</li>
          <li>9:15–9:30 pm: Optional small snack if hungry (e.g., oatmeal or yogurt)</li>
          <li>10:00 pm: Set wedge, left-side sleep readiness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If bedtime is 12:00 am</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>7:30–8:00 pm: Dinner</li>
          <li>8:15 pm: Gentle walk; avoid lying down until 11:00 pm</li>
          <li>10:30–11:00 pm: Herbal tea in small sips; light snack only if needed</li>
          <li>11:45 pm: Set wedge; lights down</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Swaps -->
  <section id="swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Quick swaps (late-evening)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Reflux-friendly swaps">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Instead of</th><th>Try</th><th>Reason</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Greasy fried snacks</td>
          <td>Plain crackers with peanut butter, small bowl of oats</td>
          <td>Lower fat keeps the valve tighter; easier digestion</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Chocolate or mint ice-cream</td>
          <td>Banana with yogurt (if tolerated)</td>
          <td>Chocolate/mint can trigger reflux</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Large citrus juice</td>
          <td>Small water + chamomile/ginger tea</td>
          <td>Lower acidity/volume near bedtime</td>
        </tr>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long before bed should I stop eating?</p>
        <p>Most people do best finishing dinner <strong>3–4 hours</strong> before bed. Heavier meals: stop 4–5 hours before.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is it okay to have a bedtime snack?</p>
        <p>Yes—if you’re hungry, choose a <strong>small, low-fat, non-acidic</strong> option 60–90 minutes before bed.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does sleeping position matter?</p>
        <p>Yes. <strong>Left-side</strong> and <strong>torso elevation</strong> reduce reflux compared with right-side or flat on the back.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I change my medicines?</p>
        <p>Don’t change doses on your own. Ask about the best timing for PPIs, H2 blockers, and antacids—and whether any current meds aggravate reflux.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
