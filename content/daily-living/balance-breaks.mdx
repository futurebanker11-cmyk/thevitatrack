    <h2>Why balance breaks matter</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="lead"><span class="kicker">Little and often:</span> short balance practice throughout the day trains ankle reactions, hip control, and posture. It also counters long sitting, stiffness, and “deconditioning.”</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Do them near a <strong>counter or sturdy chair</strong> for support.</li>
          <li>Go barefoot or wear <strong>supportive, non-slip shoes</strong>.</li>
          <li>Stop if you feel unwell; resume when steady.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Even 60–120 seconds, 3–5 times daily, can improve steadiness in a few weeks.
      </div>
    </div>

  <section id="routine" class="section">
    <h2>Your 5-part balance break (about 90 seconds)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Posture set (10s)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stand tall, soften knees, lengthen through the crown of the head.</li>
          <li>Lightly touch the counter with fingertips if needed.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Ankle rocks (20s)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Shift weight forward and back from ankles (not hips), slow and smooth.</li>
          <li>Exhale during the forward shift; inhale on the return.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Side shifts (20s)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Shift body over the left foot, then right, keeping torso upright.</li>
          <li>Feel the big toe and heel stay grounded; avoid leaning from the waist.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">4) Tandem stance (20s)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>One foot in front of the other (heel to toe). Breathe slowly.</li>
          <li>Switch feet after 10 seconds. Hold light support as needed.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">5) Heel raises (20s)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rise onto toes, pause, lower with control. 8–10 gentle reps.</li>
          <li>Think tall; avoid wobbling by engaging lower tummy muscles.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="progressions" class="section">
    <h2>Simple progressions</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Start</th><th scope="col">Make it easier</th><th scope="col">Make it harder</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Fingertips on counter</td>
          <td>Full hand on counter; widen stance</td>
          <td>One finger touch → hands by sides</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Feet hip-width</td>
          <td>Wider than hips</td>
          <td>Tandem stance → semi-tandem → eyes closed (only if safe)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Heel raises double-leg</td>
          <td>Smaller range</td>
          <td>Single-leg heel raises with light support</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Side shifts</td>
          <td>Shorter shifts</td>
          <td>Add slow head turns while shifting</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Advance one change at a time. If unsteady, step back to the previous level.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Strength -->
  <section id="strength" class="section">
    <h2>Mini-strength: ankles, hips, and core</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Ankles: toe taps & alphabet</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hold counter; tap one foot forward-side-back (10 taps each side).</li>
          <li>Seated or standing: draw the alphabet with your foot to mobilize the ankle.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Hips: side steps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Step right then left along the counter, 6–10 steps each way.</li>
          <li>Keep knees soft; avoid crossing feet.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Core: tall stance breathing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hands on ribs; inhale to expand sideways, exhale to gently brace low tummy.</li>
          <li>5 calm breaths while standing tall improves postural control.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Pair a balance break with daily habits: after brushing teeth, boiling water, or during TV adverts.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Home setup & footwear -->
  <section id="home" class="section">
    <h2>Home setup & footwear</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Clear walkways; secure or remove loose rugs; tidy cords.</li>
          <li>Add night lights on the path to the bathroom and kitchen.</li>
          <li>Install grab bars where needed; use a sturdy chair for practice.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Choose shoes with a firm heel counter and non-slip sole.</li>
          <li>Replace worn slippers; avoid backless, floppy styles.</li>
          <li>Check fit yearly; consider orthotics if advised.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Dizziness -->
  <section id="dizzy" class="section">
    <h2>If you get dizzy on standing</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Before standing: ankle pumps, clench calves, take 3 slow breaths.</li>
          <li>Stand → pause 10–15 seconds holding the counter, then walk.</li>
          <li>Rise more slowly in the night; keep a torch nearby.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Frequent dizziness may relate to dehydration, blood pressure, or medicines — discuss with your clinician.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="redflags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek care</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>New fainting, chest pain, or sudden breathlessness.</li>
        <li>Sudden weakness, numbness, vision or speech changes.</li>
        <li>Falls with head strike, confusion, or severe pain.</li>
        <li>Worsening dizziness despite slowing down and hydrating.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies. Call your local emergency number right away.</p>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How often should I do balance breaks?</p>
        <p>Briefly, 3–5 times per day. Link them to routines like meals or tea time.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I hold the counter?</p>
        <p>Yes. Start with support and reduce it as you steady. Safety first.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if my knees hurt?</p>
        <p>Keep knees soft, move within comfort, and shorten the range. If pain persists, ask your clinician.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will this stop all falls?</p>
        <p>No single step prevents all falls. Balance breaks help alongside home safety, vision and footwear checks, and reviewing medicines.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
