  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why timing with food matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Absorption & comfort</span> Food can change how medicines absorb and can protect the stomach. Some drugs need food to work best; others work best away from meals.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Food can</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Reduce nausea/heartburn from irritating meds</li>
          <li>Increase or decrease absorption</li>
          <li>Interact with specific nutrients (calcium, vitamin K)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Always follow your prescription label. If advice here conflicts with your clinician’s instructions, follow <strong>your</strong> plan.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- General rules -->
  <section id="rules" class="section">
    <h2>General rules (fast)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>“With food”</strong> = during a meal or within ~15 minutes after.</li>
          <li><strong>“Empty stomach”</strong> = 30–60 minutes before food or 2–4 hours after.</li>
          <li>Use a <strong>standard glass of water</strong> (150–250 ml) unless told otherwise.</li>
          <li>Do not crush/chew <strong>extended-release</strong> or <strong>enteric-coated</strong> tablets.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Keep a simple <strong>med + meal card</strong> on the fridge or with your pillbox for quick reference.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="with-food" class="section">
    <h2>Take <em>with food</em> (common examples)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Medicines recommended with food">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Medicine</th><th scope="col">Why with food</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Metformin</strong></td>
          <td>Less stomach upset</td>
          <td>Take with main meals (breakfast/dinner for ER)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>NSAIDs</strong> (ibuprofen, naproxen)</td>
          <td>Protect stomach lining</td>
          <td>Avoid if ulcer/bleed risk; ask before use if on blood thinners</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Steroids</strong> (prednisolone)</td>
          <td>Reduce indigestion</td>
          <td>Usually morning to protect sleep</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Antibiotics (selected)</strong></td>
          <td>Reduce nausea</td>
          <td>Amoxicillin OK with food; some have special rules—see label</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Opioids</strong> (codeine, tramadol)</td>
          <td>Reduce nausea</td>
          <td>Avoid alcohol; watch constipation (see fiber/fluids)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Rivaroxaban ≥15 mg</strong></td>
          <td>Needs food for absorption</td>
          <td>Take with the largest meal</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Iron</strong> (if stomach upset)</td>
          <td>Improves tolerance</td>
          <td>Food can reduce absorption—see <a href="#avoid">“avoid/space”</a></td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>

  <section id="empty" class="section">
    <h2>Take on an <em>empty stomach</em> (common examples)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Medicines recommended on empty stomach">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Medicine</th><th scope="col">Timing</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Levothyroxine</strong></td>
          <td>Morning: 30–60 min before breakfast</td>
          <td>Keep 4 hrs apart from calcium/iron/multivitamins</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bisphosphonates</strong> (alendronate, risedronate)</td>
          <td>First thing, with plain water only</td>
          <td>Stay upright 30 min; no food/coffee/juice in that window</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>PPIs</strong> (omeprazole, pantoprazole)</td>
          <td>~30 min before breakfast</td>
          <td>Helps activation to reduce acid</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Some antibiotics</strong> (e.g., flucloxacillin)</td>
          <td>1 hour before or 2 hours after food</td>
          <td>Follow label—varies by drug</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- Avoid / space -->
  <section id="avoid" class="section">
    <h2>Foods & drinks to avoid or space from medicines</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Food–medicine interactions">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Food / drink</th><th scope="col">Interacts with</th><th scope="col">What to do</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Grapefruit / Seville orange</strong></td>
          <td>Some statins (simvastatin), calcium-channel blockers, certain psych meds</td>
          <td>Avoid unless clinician says it’s safe with your specific drug</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>High-calcium & iron</strong> (milk, yogurt, antacids; iron tabs)</td>
          <td>Thyroid pills, some antibiotics (tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones), iron itself</td>
          <td>Separate by 2–4 hours; Vitamin C may improve iron absorption</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Vitamin K-rich greens</strong> (spinach, kale, methi)</td>
          <td>Warfarin</td>
          <td>Keep intake <em>consistent</em> week to week; don’t binge or avoid</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Alcohol</strong></td>
          <td>Sleep pills, opioids, some diabetes meds, metronidazole</td>
          <td>Often unsafe—ask; avoid with metronidazole and sedatives</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Very high-fiber meals</strong></td>
          <td>Can slow absorption of several meds</td>
          <td>Keep fiber consistent day to day; space from “empty stomach” meds</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Coffee/tea right with pills</strong></td>
          <td>Iron, thyroid</td>
          <td>Use water for pills; have tea/coffee later</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Not a full list—check your labels and pharmacist guidance.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Nausea -->
  <section id="nausea" class="section">
    <h2>If a medicine causes nausea</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Take with a <strong>small snack</strong> (cracker, yogurt) unless labeled “empty stomach”.</li>
          <li>Use <strong>room-temperature</strong> water; avoid big gulps.</li>
          <li>Avoid greasy or spicy meals near dosing; try ginger tea.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Persistent vomiting, black stools, severe stomach pain, or blood in vomit/stool → <strong>seek care</strong>.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="redflags" class="section">
    <h2>Red flags (call your clinician promptly)</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Severe or worsening chest/abdominal pain after a new medicine</li>
        <li>Fainting, severe dizziness, new confusion</li>
        <li>Rash, swelling of lips/tongue, trouble breathing (possible allergy)</li>
        <li>Black/tarry stools, vomiting blood, or bright red blood in stool</li>
        <li>Very low sugars or repeated lows if on insulin/sulfonylureas</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Bring your full <strong>medicine + supplements</strong> list to appointments.</p>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What counts as “with food”?</p>
        <p>Take during a meal or within ~15 minutes after the first bites. Even a small snack can help with stomach-irritating meds.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I take pills with tea or coffee?</p>
        <p>Use water. Tea/coffee can interfere with iron and thyroid pills—drink them later.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do soups and milk count as “food” with medicines?</p>
        <p>Yes, but dairy calcium can block some meds (thyroid, certain antibiotics). If so, choose non-dairy snacks or separate by 2–4 hours.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Warfarin and greens?</p>
        <p>Keep leafy greens consistent—not zero. Sudden increases or cuts change your INR. Tell your clinic about diet changes.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Rivaroxaban with meals?</p>
        <p>Doses ≥15 mg should be taken <strong>with food</strong> for proper absorption. Lower doses may not require it—follow your label.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I split extended-release tablets?</p>
        <p>No. Don’t crush or split extended-release or enteric-coated pills unless your pharmacist confirms it’s safe.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
