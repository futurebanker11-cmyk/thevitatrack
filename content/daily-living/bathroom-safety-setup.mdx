  <!-- Why -->
  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why bathroom safety matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Slips happen fast</span> Wet floors, low toilets, poor lighting, and rush-to-bathroom urgency raise fall risk. Small changes (grab bars, non-slip surfaces, lighting) prevent injuries, hospital stays, and loss of independence.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common hazards</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Loose rugs/mats and soapy floors</li>
          <li>Low toilet height and no handholds</li>
          <li>Dim night lighting and glare</li>
          <li>Dizziness from meds or standing up too quickly</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Goal: <strong>1 safe path</strong> from bed → bathroom with steady light, clear floor, and sturdy supports.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Quick risk check -->
  <section id="check" class="section">
    <h2>Quick risk check</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If you answer “yes”, prioritize setup now</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Have you felt <strong>unsteady</strong> or grabbed furniture in the bathroom?</li>
          <li>Any <strong>near-falls</strong> or actual falls in the last year?</li>
          <li>Do you rush for the toilet (urgency/leakage) or wake 2+ times/night?</li>
          <li>New <strong>dizziness</strong> when you stand up?</li>
          <li>Do you take sleep pills, anxiety meds, strong pain meds, or blood pressure meds at night?</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        If you use a <strong>cane/walker</strong>, make sure it fits through the doorway and can stand safely by the sink and toilet.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Your 6-step setup (about 60–90 minutes)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Light the path</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Install <strong>motion night lights</strong> from bed → bathroom.</li>
          <li>Add a <strong>bright, non-glare</strong> light near sink and shower.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Make floors non-slip</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Place <strong>rubber-backed, low-profile</strong> bath mats (no loose rugs).</li>
          <li>Use <strong>non-slip decals</strong> or a full mat inside the tub/shower.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Add sturdy handholds</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Install <strong>screw-mounted grab bars</strong>: beside toilet; vertical at shower entry; horizontal inside.</li>
          <li>Do <strong>not</strong> rely on towel bars or suction-only cups as primary support.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">4) Sit to shower</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use a <strong>shower chair/bench</strong> with rubber feet; add a <strong>handheld shower</strong>.</li>
          <li>Keep shampoo/soap <strong>within reach</strong> to avoid bending.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">5) Right-height toilet</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Add a <strong>raised seat</strong> or replace with a comfort-height toilet (43–46 cm / 17–18 in).</li>
          <li>Consider <strong>toilet safety frame</strong> or armrests for push-off support.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <p class="kicker">6) Declutter & organize</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Remove extra rugs, bins, and low stools; route cords away.</li>
          <li>Store daily items at <strong>waist height</strong>; keep a dry towel within easy reach.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Grab bar placement">

        <tr><th scope="col">Location</th><th scope="col">Placement idea</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>

      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Toilet</strong></td>
          <td>One bar <strong>beside</strong> the toilet (wall-mounted) OR a freestanding <strong>safety frame</strong></td>
          <td>Align at standing wrist height for push support</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Shower entry</strong></td>
          <td>Vertical bar near the entrance</td>
          <td>Helps step in/out; combine with non-slip threshold</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Inside shower/tub</strong></td>
          <td>Horizontal bar on long wall; optional angled bar</td>
          <td>Use <strong>studs or anchors</strong> rated for grab bars; stainless steel preferred</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sink area</strong></td>
          <td>Small vertical “assist” bar</td>
          <td>For steadying during grooming</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">If renting, ask for permission; many bars can mount into studs with minimal repair later.</p>

  <!-- Lighting & Flooring -->
  <section id="light-floor" class="section">
    <h2>Lighting, flooring & clutter</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lighting</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>warm, even</strong> LEDs to reduce glare and shadows.</li>
          <li>Install <strong>motion sensors</strong> or night lights with battery backup.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Flooring</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Prefer <strong>textured tiles</strong> or anti-slip coating; dry floors after each use.</li>
          <li>Secure mats by the tub and sink; avoid thick, loose rugs.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Clutter & storage</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Shower caddy at chest height; pump bottles to avoid fiddly caps.</li>
          <li>Hamper outside the bathroom to keep floor clear.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Keep a <strong>spare pair</strong> of non-slip bathroom slippers with good grip; avoid smooth, worn soles.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines & conditions -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines & conditions that affect bathroom safety</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Meds and conditions">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Factor</th><th scope="col">Effect</th><th scope="col">What to do</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sleep pills, sedatives, strong pain meds</strong></td>
          <td>Drowsy, unsteady at night</td>
          <td>Use night lights; move slowly; discuss dose/timing with clinician</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Blood pressure meds / diuretics</strong></td>
          <td>Dizziness on standing; frequent urination</td>
          <td>Rise in stages (sit → stand pause); ask about earlier dosing</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Diabetes meds (insulin/sulfonylureas)</strong></td>
          <td>Low glucose → shakiness, confusion</td>
          <td>Keep glucose tabs nearby; follow low-sugar plan</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Arthritis, neuropathy, stroke</strong></td>
          <td>Limited balance/strength; foot sensation issues</td>
          <td>Use seating, bars, and assistive devices; footwear with grip</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>BPH/overactive bladder</strong></td>
          <td>Urgency → rushing</td>
          <td>Clear path; consider bedside commode; limit late fluids</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Emergency plan -->
  <section id="emergency" class="section">
    <h2>Emergency plan (simple & ready)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prepare</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep a <strong>water-resistant phone</strong> or emergency button within reach.</li>
          <li>Place a <strong>non-slip bath bench</strong> for safe seated showering.</li>
          <li>Stick a small card with <strong>ICE contacts</strong> on the inside of the cabinet door.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <p class="kicker">If a fall happens</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stay calm; call for help or use your device.</li>
          <li>Roll onto hands and knees to a stable surface; use bars/chair to rise if uninjured.</li>
          <li>If dizzy, <strong>crawl</strong> to a dry area first—wet floors increase risk of a second fall.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small">Head injury, severe pain, bleeding, or you take blood thinners → seek urgent care.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are suction grab bars safe?</p>
        <p>They can help <em>temporarily</em>, but for primary support use <strong>screw-mounted</strong> bars into studs or proper anchors.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What height should a grab bar be?</p>
        <p>Place bars where your hand naturally lands when standing by the toilet or stepping in/out (often 80–90 cm / 32–36 in from floor). Comfort and reach trump strict numbers.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is a raised toilet seat worth it?</p>
        <p>Yes for knee/hip pain or low strength—less strain and easier stand-up. Add armrests or a safety frame if you need push support.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What about small bathrooms?</p>
        <p>Use a corner shower stool, vertical bars, low-profile mats, and wall caddies; ensure the door opens outward or keep a slide-bolt you can unlock from outside.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
