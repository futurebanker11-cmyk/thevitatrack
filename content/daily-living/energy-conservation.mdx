  <!-- Why -->
  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why conserve energy</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="kicker">Goal:</span> Do what matters most with less breathlessness, pain, or fatigue. Energy conservation is not “doing less”—it’s doing <em>smarter</em> so you can do more of what you love.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common trouble spots</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Morning rush (bathing/dressing) leaves you wiped out</li>
          <li>Standing chores (cooking, dishes) cause back/leg fatigue</li>
          <li>Errands stacked together → afternoon crash</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        If fatigue or shortness of breath is <strong>new or rapidly worse</strong>, see the warning signs section and speak with a clinician.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- 4P -->
  <section id="fourp" class="section">
    <h2>The 4 P’s method</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">1</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Prioritize</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Circle today’s <strong>top 2–3</strong> activities; defer or delegate the rest.</li>
          <li>Batch low-value tasks (emails, folding) on strong-energy days.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">2</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Plan</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Alternate <strong>heavy ↔ light</strong> tasks; avoid stacking two hard jobs.</li>
          <li>Schedule a 10–15 minute sit-break every 45–60 minutes.</li>
          <li>Prep the night before (clothes laid out, breakfast ready).</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">3</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Pace</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use a calm, steady rhythm; breathe out on effort (see below).</li>
          <li>Stop at <strong>7/10 effort</strong>—avoid the crash that costs tomorrow.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">4</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Position</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sit for tasks when possible (shower chair, seated food prep).</li>
          <li>Keep items at <strong>mid-chest to hip height</strong> to reduce bending/reaching.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Breath & body mechanics -->
  <section id="breath" class="section">
    <h2>Breathing & body mechanics</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Breathe to save energy</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Pursed-lip:</strong> Inhale through nose 2 counts → exhale gently through pursed lips 4 counts.</li>
          <li><strong>Exhale on exertion:</strong> Breathe out as you stand, lift, or climb.</li>
          <li>Pause to breathe every few minutes during chores.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Move smarter</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Slide rather than lift; use both hands close to the body.</li>
          <li>Split big tasks (e.g., shower at night, wash hair next day).</li>
          <li>Use long-handled tools to avoid deep bends and high reaches.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Rooms -->
  <section id="rooms" class="section">
    <h2>Room-by-room tweaks</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Room-by-room energy conservation">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Area</th><th>Do this</th><th>Why it helps</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bathroom</strong></td>
          <td>Shower chair; hand-held shower; sit to dry; pump bottles; organize within reach.</td>
          <td>Reduces standing time and overhead reaching.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bedroom</strong></td>
          <td>Lay out clothes the night before; sit on a chair to dress; use slip-on supportive shoes.</td>
          <td>Fewer bends and balance challenges.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Kitchen</strong></td>
          <td>Keep daily items at waist–chest height; meal prep seated; use light cookware; soak dishes instead of heavy scrubbing.</td>
          <td>Minimizes lifting and reaching.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Living area</strong></td>
          <td>Keep a small caddy (water, meds, phone) by your seat; use a rolling cart for transfers.</td>
          <td>Prevents repeated trips across rooms.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Laundry</strong></td>
          <td>Do smaller loads; use wheeled baskets; place hampers at hip height.</td>
          <td>Less carrying and bending.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Outdoors & errands</strong></td>
          <td>Park close; combine trips; use delivery for heavy items; plan outings in cooler hours.</td>
          <td>Conserves energy for the activity itself.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Tools -->
  <section id="tools" class="section">
    <h2>Helpful tools & swaps</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Shower chair, non-slip mat, long-handled sponge</li>
          <li>Reacher/grabber, sock aid, long-handled shoehorn</li>
          <li>Lightweight cordless vacuum; rolling kitchen cart</li>
          <li>Electric can opener; lightweight cookware; kettle instead of lifting pots</li>
          <li>Pill organizer + water bottle at stations you use</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        If you use oxygen, a walker, or a cane—set up <strong>stations</strong> on each floor/area to avoid extra trips.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Schedule -->
  <section id="schedule" class="section">
    <h2>Sample day schedules</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">“Two peaks” day (common pattern)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Morning (peak):</strong> personal care + one priority task</li>
          <li><strong>Late morning:</strong> snack + sit-break, light chore seated</li>
          <li><strong>Afternoon (dip):</strong> rest, quiet tasks (calls, paperwork)</li>
          <li><strong>Early evening (mini-peak):</strong> simple meal, short walk</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Errand day</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Prep breakfast & clothes night before; pack water/meds</li>
          <li>Schedule errands in a single area; park once, do 1–2 stops</li>
          <li>Sit for 10–15 min between stops; use delivery for heavy items</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Fuel -->
  <section id="fuel" class="section">
    <h2>Hydration & fuel</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Drink regularly through the day; small sips before tasks.</li>
          <li>Favor steady-energy snacks: yogurt, nuts, fruit, oats, khichdi.</li>
          <li>Don’t skip protein—include a palm-sized portion at meals.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        If you have fluid or salt limits (heart/kidney conditions), follow your clinician’s plan while still scheduling sips to avoid dehydration fatigue.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="signals" class="section">
    <h2>Warning signs & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card danger">
        <strong>Stop & seek care now</strong> if you have chest pain/pressure, severe shortness of breath at rest, fainting, new confusion, or one-sided weakness.
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When to call your clinician soon</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Energy keeps dropping for >1–2 weeks despite pacing</li>
          <li>New swelling, fast heartbeat, or night breathlessness</li>
          <li>Weight loss, poor appetite, or persistent low mood</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Help set the day’s top 2–3 tasks; schedule sit-breaks.</li>
          <li>Pre-stage items (towels, clothes, cooking tools) within reach.</li>
          <li>Offer choices: “Now or after a 10-minute rest?”</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Celebrate small wins. Consistency—not perfection—builds stamina over time.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is resting the same as conserving energy?</p>
        <p>No. Energy conservation is proactive—arranging tasks, tools, and posture to <em>spend less</em> energy while staying active.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I avoid “boom and bust” days?</p>
        <p>Stop tasks at about 70% effort, schedule sit-breaks, and rotate heavy with light activities.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does breathing technique really help?</p>
        <p>Yes—pursed-lip breathing and exhaling on exertion reduce breathlessness and slow heart rate during effort.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What’s the best first change?</p>
        <p>Sit for repetitive tasks (shower, meal prep) and keep essentials at mid-height to reduce bending and reaching.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
