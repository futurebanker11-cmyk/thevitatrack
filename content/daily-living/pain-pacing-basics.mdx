  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why pacing helps</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="lead">Pacing smooths activity so pain and fatigue don’t spike. You keep moving most days, but you stop <em>before</em> you flare.</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Prevents the <strong>boom-and-bust cycle</strong> (overdo on good days → crash after).</li>
          <li>Builds <strong>capacity slowly</strong> with fewer setbacks.</li>
          <li>Supports sleep, mood, and confidence.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">Aim for “just enough, often.” Small, regular sessions beat rare big efforts.</div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Baseline -->
  <section id="baseline" class="section">
    <h2>Find your safe baseline</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Step 1 — Pick one activity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Choose walking, dishes, sweeping, or gentle exercises.</li>
          <li>Note the longest time you can do it today <em>without worsening pain</em> in the next 24 hours.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="kicker" style="margin-top:10px">Step 2 — Set baseline</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Baseline = <strong>60–70%</strong> of that time. If 10 minutes is okay, start at 6–7 minutes.</li>
          <li>Use a timer. Stop on time even if you “feel fine.”</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        If you flare after the baseline, drop by 20–30% for 3–4 days, then retry.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Time-based -->
  <section id="time-based" class="section">
    <h2>Time-based activity (not pain-based)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use a <strong>fixed time</strong> (e.g., 6 minutes walk), not “until it hurts.”</li>
          <li>Alternate: activity block → <strong>micro-break</strong> (1–2 minutes sit/lean, relax shoulders, slow breaths).</li>
          <li>Plan 2–4 small blocks through the day rather than one long session.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        A kitchen timer, phone alarm, or smartwatch gentle vibrate works well and is easy to hear.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Spread -->
  <section id="spread" class="section">
    <h2>Spread tasks & micro-breaks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Split big chores</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Do half the sink now, half later. Fold 5 items, pause, then 5 more.</li>
          <li>Use <strong>stools</strong> for tasks (cooking, brushing teeth) to reduce back/leg load.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Micro-break recipe (1–2 min)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>3 slow nasal breaths, relax jaw/shoulders.</li>
          <li>Gentle shoulder rolls × 5, ankle pumps × 10.</li>
          <li>Rate pain/tiredness 0–10; if rising >2 points, stop the block.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Flare rules -->
  <section id="flare" class="section">
    <h2>Flare rules (plan B)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Reduce all blocks by <strong>30–50%</strong> for 2–4 days.</li>
          <li>Use heat/ice as advised; prioritize sleep and hydration.</li>
          <li>Keep gentle movement (e.g., 2–3 minute strolls) to avoid stiffness.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card danger">
        Red flags: new severe pain, fever, chest pain, weakness/numbness, or bladder/bowel changes. Seek medical care.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Upgrade -->
  <section id="upgrade" class="section">
    <h2>Upgrade schedule: the 10% rule</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">After 4–7 steady days without flare</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Increase each block by <strong>~10%</strong>. Example: 6 → 7 minutes.</li>
          <li>Or add <strong>one extra block</strong> of the same time.</li>
          <li>Only change one thing per week (time <em>or</em> total blocks).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">Slow and steady wins. If a flare occurs, step back and stabilize before the next increase.</div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Mistakes -->
  <section id="mistakes" class="section">
    <h2>Common mistakes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Going by pain</strong> instead of the timer.</li>
          <li><strong>Jumping too fast</strong> after a good day.</li>
          <li>Skipping breaks, then crashing later.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">Use a written plan. “If I feel great, I still stop at the timer.”</div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="redflags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>New severe pain, fever, or swelling/redness at a joint.</li>
        <li>Chest pain, breathlessness, or fainting.</li>
        <li>Numbness/weakness or bladder/bowel changes.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Contact your clinician to review your plan and rule out new causes.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long should a block be?</p>
        <p>Start where you can finish without worsening pain the next day, then use ~60–70% of that as your baseline.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if I miss a day?</p>
        <p>Resume at the last stable level. If a flare happened, reduce 20–30% for a few days, then rebuild.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I pace different tasks?</p>
        <p>Yes. Use separate timers for walking, chores, and exercises. Keep total effort steady across the week.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When will I notice progress?</p>
        <p>Often within 2–4 weeks of steady pacing. Capacity rises with fewer flare-ups.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
