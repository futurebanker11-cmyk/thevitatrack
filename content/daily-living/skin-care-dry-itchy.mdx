  <!-- Why -->
  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why skin gets dry/itchy with age</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="lead">Oil and water content in skin drops with age; soaps, hot water, and some medicines pull out moisture. Scratching breaks the barrier and leads to more itching.</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Thinner outer layer → water escapes faster.</li>
          <li>Fewer oil glands → weaker barrier.</li>
          <li>Heat, low humidity, and fragrances can irritate.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">Good news: consistent gentle care (cleanse → seal with moisturizer) usually calms itch in 1–2 weeks.</div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily routine -->
  <section id="daily" class="section">
    <h2>Daily routine (AM / PM)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Morning</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Quick lukewarm rinse or face/underarms/groin cleanse with <strong>fragrance-free gentle cleanser</strong>.</li>
          <li>Pat dry (don’t rub). Within 3 minutes, apply <strong>cream or ointment</strong> head-to-toe.</li>
          <li>Hands: re-apply moisturizer after washing, every time.</li>
          <li>Sun exposed areas: add SPF 30+ (fragrance-free) after moisturizer.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Evening</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Short shower (≤10 minutes), lukewarm.</li>
          <li>Use cleanser on sweaty/soiled areas only; water alone elsewhere.</li>
          <li>Pat dry; apply <strong>thicker layer</strong> of moisturizer. Itchy spots: consider <strong>colloidal oatmeal</strong> cream or <strong>pramoxine</strong> lotion.</li>
          <li>Very dry heels/shins: seal with a thin <strong>petrolatum</strong> layer.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Bathing -->
  <section id="bath" class="section">
    <h2>Bathing & hand-washing</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Temperature: <strong>lukewarm</strong>, not hot.</li>
          <li>Time: ≤10 minutes; avoid long soaks.</li>
          <li>Cleansers: fragrance-free, dye-free; avoid antibacterial bars unless prescribed.</li>
          <li>Hands: switch to <strong>gentle liquid</strong> wash; rinse well; moisturize immediately.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">“3-minute rule”: moisturize within 3 minutes after bathing to trap water in the skin.</div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Moisturizers -->
  <section id="moisturizers" class="section">
    <h2>Moisturizers: ointment, cream, lotion — which & when</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Moisturizer guide">
        <thead>
          <tr><th>Type</th><th>When to use</th><th>Pros</th><th>Watch for</th></tr>
        </thead>
        <tbody>
          <tr>
            <td><strong>Ointment</strong> (petrolatum, dimethicone blends)</td>
            <td>Night; very dry heels/shins, eczema patches</td>
            <td>Best seal, strong itch relief</td>
            <td>Greasy feel — use thin layer</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td><strong>Cream</strong> (ceramides, glycerin, shea)</td>
            <td>Daily AM/PM for most body areas</td>
            <td>Balanced feel, long-lasting</td>
            <td>Pick fragrance-free</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td><strong>Lotion</strong> (lighter)</td>
            <td>Humid weather, hairy areas</td>
            <td>Spreads easily</td>
            <td>Less sealing — may need 2–3 coats/day</td>
          </tr>
        </tbody>
      </table>
      <p class="small" style="margin-top:6px"><strong>Helpful add-ons:</strong> urea 5–10% or lactic acid 5–12% for rough heels (avoid open skin); ceramides for barrier repair; colloidal oatmeal for itch.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Itch control -->
  <section id="itch" class="section">
    <h2>Itch control & scratch safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cold, not scratch: apply a <strong>cool pack</strong> for 5–10 minutes.</li>
          <li>Short nails; wear soft cotton at night to reduce skin damage.</li>
          <li>Spot treatment: 1% <strong>hydrocortisone</strong> thin layer, 1–2×/day for ≤7 days on small itchy patches (avoid face/groin unless advised).</li>
          <li><strong>Pramoxine</strong> or <strong>menthol</strong> lotions can calm itch without steroids.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Many oral antihistamines cause drowsiness/confusion in older adults. Ask your clinician before use.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Clothing & home -->
  <section id="clothes" class="section">
    <h2>Clothing, laundry & home humidity</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Clothing & laundry</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Choose <strong>soft cotton</strong> layers; avoid scratchy wool on skin.</li>
          <li>Fragrance-free detergent; skip fabric softener sheets on itchy days.</li>
          <li>Rinse cycle on “extra rinse” if sensitive.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Home humidity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Target indoor humidity: <strong>40–50%</strong>.</li>
          <li>Use a small room humidifier in bedroom; clean weekly.</li>
          <li>Avoid very hot rooms and direct heater air.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Labels -->
  <section id="labels" class="section">
    <h2>Reading labels: what to prefer / what to avoid</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prefer</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>“<strong>Fragrance-free</strong>”, “dye-free”, “hypoallergenic”.</li>
          <li>Barrier helpers: <strong>ceramides, glycerin, petrolatum, dimethicone</strong>.</li>
          <li>Soothers: <strong>colloidal oatmeal, allantoin, panthenol</strong>.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Avoid (for sensitive skin)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Perfumes, essential oils on broken skin.</li>
          <li>Harsh exfoliants or peels on thin senior skin.</li>
          <li>Strong alpha/beta-hydroxy acids on large areas without guidance.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Special cases -->
  <section id="special" class="section">
    <h2>Special cases (when to tailor the plan)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes / CKD</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Check feet daily; moisturize tops/soles (not between toes).</li>
          <li>See clinician promptly for cracks, ulcers, or infection signs.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Eczema / dermatitis history</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use steroid-sparing moisturizers daily; short steroid courses for flares as prescribed.</li>
          <li>Patch-test new products on a small area for 48 hours.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Troubleshooting -->
  <section id="troubleshoot" class="section">
    <h2>Troubleshooting</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Troubleshooting dry/itchy skin">
        <thead>
          <tr><th>Problem</th><th>Likely cause</th><th>Try this</th></tr>
        </thead>
        <tbody>
          <tr>
            <td>Still itchy after 2 weeks</td>
            <td>Not sealing moisture; hot showers; fragrance exposure</td>
            <td>Switch to thicker cream/ointment; shorter lukewarm baths; strict fragrance-free routine</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Stings with moisturizer</td>
            <td>Acids/menthol on broken skin</td>
            <td>Use bland barrier (petrolatum/ceramide cream) until healed; avoid actives on cracks</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Hands crack despite cream</td>
            <td>Frequent washing, sanitizer alcohol</td>
            <td>Cream after each wash + cotton gloves at night with ointment layer</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Itch worse at night</td>
            <td>Dry air, warm bedding, delayed moisturizer</td>
            <td>Humidify 40–50%; moisturizer 1–2 hours before bed; cool pack on hot spots</td>
          </tr>
        </tbody>
      </table>
    </div>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="redflags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek care</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Spreading <strong>redness, warmth, pus</strong>, or fever.</li>
        <li><strong>Yellow skin or eyes</strong>, severe new rash, or widespread hives.</li>
        <li>Severe itch with <strong>weight loss</strong> or night sweats.</li>
        <li>New <strong>blisters</strong> or sudden painful skin.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may need urgent assessment or prescription treatment.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How often should I moisturize?</p>
        <p>At least <strong>twice daily</strong> and after every wash. Very dry areas may need 3–4×/day in winter.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Which is better: cream or oil?</p>
        <p>Creams with ceramides + glycerin are best for daytime; a thin petrolatum layer locks in moisture overnight.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I use coconut or mustard oil?</p>
        <p>Light oils can soften but don’t seal as well as ointments. If used, apply on damp skin and <em>then</em> add a cream to seal.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is hydrocortisone safe?</p>
        <p>Short courses (≤7 days) on small itchy patches can help. Avoid face/groin unless advised. If not better, see a clinician.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
