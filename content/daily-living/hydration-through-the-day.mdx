  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why steady hydration matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Benefits</span> Better energy, fewer falls (stable blood pressure), clearer thinking, easier bowel movements, healthier kidneys, and safer medicine effects.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common signs you need more fluids</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dry mouth, headache, dizziness on standing</li>
          <li>Dark yellow urine or going &lt;4 times/day</li>
          <li>Constipation, fatigue, muscle cramps</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Important:</strong> If you have a clinician-set fluid limit (heart/kidney/liver conditions), <em>follow that limit</em>. This page gives general guidance.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- How much -->
  <section id="how-much" class="section">
    <h2>How much is right for me?</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Simple starting target (no fluid restriction)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>1.8–2.4 liters/day</strong> total fluids (about <strong>7–10 cups</strong>), spread from morning to early evening.</li>
          <li>Hot weather, exercise, fever, diarrhea, or vomiting → you’ll likely need more.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Personalize</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Urine light-yellow = usually enough. Dark = add fluids; clear + frequent night trips = too much late.</li>
          <li>Include water, tea/coffee, milk, soups, ORS, and high-water foods (fruit/veg) in your total.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Schedule -->
  <section id="schedule" class="section">
    <h2>Your day-by-day schedule</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Hydration schedule">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Time</th><th scope="col">Goal</th><th scope="col">Ideas</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>On waking</strong></td>
          <td>200–300 ml</td>
          <td>Room-temp water; warm lemon water; tea/coffee if you like</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Breakfast</strong></td>
          <td>200–300 ml</td>
          <td>Water, milk, or herbal tea; fruit or yogurt adds fluids</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Mid-morning</strong></td>
          <td>200–250 ml</td>
          <td>Water bottle check-in; light snack (fruit, nuts)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Lunch</strong></td>
          <td>250–300 ml</td>
          <td>Water or buttermilk; soup counts toward total</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Mid-afternoon</strong></td>
          <td>200–300 ml</td>
          <td>Water or diluted juice; brief walk to stimulate thirst</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Early evening</strong></td>
          <td>200–250 ml</td>
          <td>Finish most fluids now; avoid big drinks after this</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>With dinner</strong></td>
          <td>150–200 ml</td>
          <td>Small sips; avoid large, fizzy drinks if reflux</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>2–3 h before bed</strong></td>
          <td>Small sips only</td>
          <td>Helps reduce night bathroom trips (nocturia/BPH)</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Adjust amounts to match your daily total and medical advice.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Choices -->
  <section id="choices" class="section">
    <h2>Smart drink choices</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Good everyday options</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Plain or filtered water; <strong>ORS</strong> if dehydrated/ill</li>
          <li>Unsweetened tea/coffee (limit late day if insomnia)</li>
          <li>Buttermilk, milk, diluted fruit juice, coconut water</li>
          <li>Soups/broths; high-water foods (cucumber, watermelon, oranges)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <p class="kicker">Limit/avoid</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Very sugary drinks (sodas, energy drinks) — spike glucose</li>
          <li>Excess alcohol — dehydrates and disrupts sleep</li>
          <li>Very salty sports drinks without need — can raise blood pressure</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Electrolytes -->
  <section id="electrolytes" class="section">
    <h2>Electrolytes & heat days</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When to use ORS</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Diarrhea, vomiting, fever, or heavy sweating</li>
          <li>Make-at-home: <strong>6 level tsp sugar + ½ tsp salt in 1 liter</strong> clean water. Stir well.</li>
          <li>Sip 100–200 ml every 15–20 minutes; slow down if bloated.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Potassium/salt cautions:</strong> If you take ACE inhibitors/ARBs, spironolactone, or have kidney disease, ask before using high-potassium drinks or salt substitutes.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines that change fluids</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Medicines affecting hydration">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Medicine</th><th scope="col">Effect</th><th scope="col">Tips</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Diuretics</strong> (furosemide, HCTZ)</td>
          <td>Increase urine, lower fluid</td>
          <td>Often taken in morning; avoid late-day doses if nocturia</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>ACE/ARB, Spironolactone</strong></td>
          <td>Raise potassium</td>
          <td>Be cautious with high-K drinks/salt substitutes</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>NSAIDs</strong></td>
          <td>Kidney strain</td>
          <td>Avoid dehydration; use only as advised</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>SGLT2 inhibitors</strong> (e.g., dapagliflozin)</td>
          <td>Mild diuresis</td>
          <td>Drink steadily; watch for dizziness on standing</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Laxatives</strong></td>
          <td>Can shift fluids</td>
          <td>Hydrate; don’t overuse stimulant types</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Adjustments -->
  <section id="adjust" class="section">
    <h2>Adjustments by condition</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Hydration adjustments">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Condition</th><th scope="col">Do</th><th scope="col">Watch-outs</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Heart failure / CKD</strong></td>
          <td>Follow clinician-set daily limit; spread sips; track weight daily</td>
          <td>Swelling, shortness of breath, sudden weight ↑ → call</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Diabetes</strong></td>
          <td>Prefer water/unsweetened drinks; ORS for illness per plan</td>
          <td>Very high sugars cause dehydration—monitor when unwell</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>BPH / nocturia</strong></td>
          <td>Front-load fluids; <strong>limit 2–3 h before bed</strong>; double-void at lights-out</td>
          <td>Dizziness on standing — rise slowly, night lights on</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Kidney stones history</strong></td>
          <td>Target clear to light-yellow urine; lemon water can help citrate</td>
          <td>Ask about specific fluid type if advised (e.g., low soda)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Diarrhea/vomiting</strong></td>
          <td>Use ORS; small frequent sips; seek care for red flags</td>
          <td>Black stools, blood, severe weakness → urgent care</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Urine -->
  <section id="urine" class="section">
    <h2>Urine color guide</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Pale straw/light yellow:</strong> well hydrated</li>
          <li><strong>Amber/dark yellow:</strong> add fluids steadily</li>
          <li><strong>Brown, red, or tea-colored:</strong> <em>seek medical advice</em></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Aim to urinate every <strong>3–4 hours</strong> while awake. Frequent clear urine + night trips? Shift more fluids earlier and reduce late-evening sips.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Daily total fluids (rough cups or ml)</li>
          <li>Urine color & frequency</li>
          <li>Weight (HF/CKD): same time each morning</li>
          <li>Symptoms: dizziness, swelling, cramps, constipation</li>
          <li>Hot days/illness episodes and ORS used</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Bring this log to appointments — it speeds adjustments to your plan.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does tea/coffee count?</p>
        <p>Yes. Moderate tea/coffee contribute to hydration; limit late-day if sleep or reflux issues.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I drink more without running to the bathroom all night?</p>
        <p>Front-load fluids: finish most by early evening; use only small sips 2–3 hours before bed; double-void at lights-out.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When should I use electrolytes?</p>
        <p>With illness (vomiting/diarrhea), heat, or heavy sweating. Otherwise water and meals usually provide enough salts.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When is dehydration dangerous?</p>
        <p>Very little dark urine, confusion, fainting, fast heartbeat, or inability to keep fluids down — seek urgent care.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
