  <!-- Why -->
  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why post-meal walking helps</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Benefits</span> A short, easy walk after meals can flatten the blood sugar spike, aid digestion, reduce gas/bloating, improve mood, and help maintain mobility and balance.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Glucose & digestion</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Muscles use glucose from your meal → steadier energy and fewer highs.</li>
          <li>Gentle upright movement helps stomach emptying and bowel motility.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Reflux note:</strong> Easy, upright walking often helps reflux, but <em>strenuous</em> activity or bending soon after a large meal can worsen it. Keep the pace conversational.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Routine -->
  <section id="routine" class="section">
    <h2>A simple 5-step routine (10–15 minutes)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Finish your meal mindfully (1–2 min)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stop at “comfortably full,” not stuffed. Sip a little water.</li>
          <li>Put on supportive shoes; grab a light layer if it’s cool.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Start gently (2–3 min)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stand, roll shoulders, take 3–4 slow breaths.</li>
          <li>Begin at an easy stroll — you should be able to chat.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Settle into “conversation pace” (6–8 min)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Short steps, upright chest, eyes forward.</li>
          <li>Use halls, balcony, courtyard, or safe block loop.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">4) Cool down (1–2 min)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Slow to an easy stroll; do gentle ankle circles and calf stretch.</li>
          <li>Finish standing a minute before sitting to avoid lightheadedness.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <p class="kicker">5) Log & prepare</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Mark it done; note distance/time or steps.</li>
          <li>Set shoes near table to cue the next walk.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Timing / Pace -->
  <section id="timing" class="section">
    <h2>Timing, pace & posture</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Timing and pace guidance">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">After which meals?</th><th scope="col">When to start</th><th scope="col">How fast?</th><th scope="col">Posture</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Breakfast & lunch</strong></td>
          <td>Within 10–20 minutes</td>
          <td>“Conversation pace” — breathing slightly faster but can talk</td>
          <td>Upright chest, relaxed shoulders, no hunching/bending</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dinner</strong></td>
          <td>10–30 minutes after finishing (closer to 30 if it was large)</td>
          <td>Gentle — avoid vigorous effort late if reflux or insomnia</td>
          <td>Stay upright; avoid tight belts/waistbands</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Snack-size meals</strong></td>
          <td>Right away is fine</td>
          <td>Easy stroll</td>
          <td>Short, relaxed steps</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety first</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Green-light tips</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Choose flat, well-lit routes; use a partner, cane, or walker if helpful.</li>
          <li>Carry your phone; wear shoes with good grip.</li>
          <li>Hot, humid, or polluted days → walk indoors (hallway, mall, corridor).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Stop and seek care</strong> for chest pain, severe shortness of breath, new jaw/arm pain, fainting, or sudden weakness. If you’re on insulin/sulfonylureas and feel shaky/sweaty/confused, check glucose and treat lows.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Adjustments -->
  <section id="adjust" class="section">
    <h2>Adjustments by condition</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Adjustments by health condition">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Condition</th><th scope="col">How to tailor</th><th scope="col">Watch-outs</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Type 2 diabetes</strong></td>
          <td>Walk 10–15 min after each meal or at least after the largest carb meal.</td>
          <td>If on insulin/sulfonylureas: carry glucose tabs; check if symptoms of lows.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>GERD / reflux</strong></td>
          <td>Prefer gentle upright walking; start 15–30 min after large meals.</td>
          <td>Avoid jogging, bending, or tight belts right after eating.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Arthritis / balance issues</strong></td>
          <td>Use cushioned, supportive shoes; pick smooth indoor routes; shorten to 5-minute bouts done twice.</td>
          <td>Pain that alters gait → slow down or pause; consider a cane/walker.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Heart failure / CKD</strong></td>
          <td>Gentle, steady pace; avoid heat; split into shorter bouts.</td>
          <td>New swelling, breathlessness, or sudden weight gain → call clinician.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>COPD</strong></td>
          <td>Pursed-lip breathing; keep rescue inhaler handy; indoor routes on poor-air days.</td>
          <td>Stop for severe breathlessness or chest pain.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Neuropathy/foot ulcers</strong></td>
          <td>Inspect feet daily; wear socks + cushioned shoes; consider stationary walk (march in place) if foot sore.</td>
          <td>Open sores or new foot pain → rest and seek advice.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Gear -->
  <section id="gear" class="section">
    <h2>Footwear & support gear</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Good picks</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Lightweight, closed-toe walking shoes with firm heel counter</li>
          <li>Non-slip rubber soles; cushioned insoles; moisture-wicking socks</li>
          <li>Foldable cane, walker, or trekking pole if balance is limited</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Replace worn shoes (flattened soles or uneven wear) — they increase fall risk.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meals -->
  <section id="meals" class="section">
    <h2>What to eat before/after</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Before the walk</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stop at “comfortably full.” Avoid heavy, very fatty meals if you plan to walk soon.</li>
          <li>Take small sips of water; avoid big gulping right before starting.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">After the walk</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>If diabetic and walking after dinner, re-check sugars as advised.</li>
          <li>Gentle stretches for calves and hips; then sit and relax.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Did you walk after each meal? (✓/✗)</li>
          <li>Duration or steps (e.g., 10–15 min or 600–1500 steps)</li>
          <li>Symptoms: breathlessness, chest pain, dizziness, foot pain</li>
          <li>For diabetes: pre- and 1–2 hr post-meal glucose (per plan)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Small is big: even <strong>3 × 10 minutes</strong> most days adds up to meaningful health gains.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long should I walk after a meal?</p>
        <p>Most people do well with <strong>10–15 minutes</strong> at a gentle, conversational pace. Even 5 minutes helps if you’re starting out.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is it bad to walk right after eating?</p>
        <p>Gentle walking is helpful. Wait closer to <strong>15–30 minutes</strong> after a large or heavy meal, and avoid strenuous effort or bending soon after eating.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will this help my blood sugar?</p>
        <p>Yes — a short post-meal walk can reduce glucose spikes. If you take insulin or sulfonylureas, carry glucose and follow your low-sugar plan.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if the weather is bad?</p>
        <p>Walk indoors: hallway laps, mall walking, or around the home with music. Safety and consistency matter most.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
