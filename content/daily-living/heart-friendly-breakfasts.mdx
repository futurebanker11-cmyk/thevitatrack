    <h2>Why breakfast matters for your heart</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="lead"><span class="kicker">Benefits:</span> better morning blood sugar, steady energy for activity, fewer salty/greasy choices later, and daily fiber that supports cholesterol and digestion.</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Include <strong>fiber</strong> (oats, whole grains, beans, fruit) + <strong>lean protein</strong> (egg, yogurt, dal, tofu) + <strong>healthy fats</strong> (nuts, seeds).</li>
          <li>Keep salt low; use herbs, lemon, and spices for flavor.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Small, consistent breakfasts beat big, occasional ones. Add a short walk after eating when possible.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Ideas -->
  <section id="ideas" class="section">
    <h2>10 quick breakfast ideas</h2>

    <div class="grid">
      <!-- 1 -->
      <article class="card recipe">
        <h3>1) Oats & Flax Bowl</h3>
        <p class="sub">Soluble fiber + omega-3s</p>
        <div class="cols">
          <div>
            <ul class="list">
              <li>½ cup rolled oats</li>
              <li>1 tbsp ground flaxseed</li>
              <li>½ cup low-fat milk/curd (or soy)</li>
              <li>Fruit: ½ banana or berries</li>
              <li>Cinnamon</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p>Cook oats or soak overnight. Stir in flax and cinnamon. Top with fruit. Optional: chopped nuts (unsalted).</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </article>

      <!-- 2 -->
      <article class="card recipe">
        <h3>2) Moong Dal Chilla + Curd</h3>
        <p class="sub">Protein-rich savory pancake</p>
        <div class="cols">
          <div>
            <ul class="list">
              <li>½ cup soaked moong dal</li>
              <li>Onion/tomato/coriander</li>
              <li>Spices: cumin, pepper</li>
              <li>¼ cup low-fat curd (side)</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p>Blend dal with water to a batter. Add veg/spices. Cook on non-stick; serve with curd and lemon.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </article>

      <!-- 3 -->
      <article class="card recipe">
        <h3>3) Veggie Omelet + Toast</h3>
        <p class="sub">Egg + greens, low salt</p>
        <div class="cols">
          <div>
            <ul class="list">
              <li>1–2 eggs (or tofu scramble)</li>
              <li>Spinach, onion, tomato</li>
              <li>Whole-wheat toast</li>
              <li>Pepper, herbs</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p>Saute veg, add beaten eggs (or crumbled tofu). Serve with toast. Use pepper/herbs instead of salt.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </article>

      <!-- 4 -->
      <article class="card recipe">
        <h3>4) Upma with Veg + Peanuts</h3>
        <p class="sub">Fiber + crunch, go easy on oil</p>
        <div class="cols">
          <div>
            <ul class="list">
              <li>Rava/semolina (small bowl)</li>
              <li>Carrot, peas, beans</li>
              <li>1 tbsp peanuts (unsalted)</li>
              <li>Curry leaves, mustard seeds</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p>Lightly roast rava; temper spices; add veg & water; finish with peanuts and lemon.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </article>

      <!-- 5 -->
      <article class="card recipe">
        <h3>5) Idli with Sambar</h3>
        <p class="sub">Steamed, low-fat, protein from dal</p>
        <div class="cols">
          <div>
            <ul class="list">
              <li>2–3 idlis</li>
              <li>Sambar with extra veg</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p>Prefer sambar over chutneys high in coconut/ salt. Add lemon and coriander for flavor.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </article>

      <!-- 6 -->
      <article class="card recipe">
        <h3>6) Curd–Chia Fruit Cup</h3>
        <p class="sub">Protein + fiber, no added sugar</p>
        <div class="cols">
          <div>
            <ul class="list">
              <li>¾ cup low-fat curd</li>
              <li>1 tbsp chia seeds</li>
              <li>Fruit: papaya/berries/apple</li>
              <li>Pinch cinnamon</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p>Stir chia into curd; rest 5–10 min. Add chopped fruit and cinnamon.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </article>

      <!-- 7 -->
      <article class="card recipe">
        <h3>7) Peanut Butter Banana Toast</h3>
        <p class="sub">Whole-grain + healthy fat</p>
        <div class="cols">
          <div>
            <ul class="list">
              <li>Whole-wheat toast</li>
              <li>1 tbsp peanut butter (unsweetened)</li>
              <li>Banana slices (few)</li>
              <li>Flax/ sesame (sprinkle)</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p>Spread nut butter thin; top with few banana slices; add seeds. Skip extra sugar.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </article>

      <!-- 8 -->
      <article class="card recipe">
        <h3>8) Poha with Veg & Lemon</h3>
        <p class="sub">Light, citrusy, low salt</p>
        <div class="cols">
          <div>
            <ul class="list">
              <li>Flattened rice (poha)</li>
              <li>Onion/peas/carrots</li>
              <li>Peanuts (1 tbsp)</li>
              <li>Turmeric, curry leaves, lemon</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p>Rinse poha; temper spices; add veg; fold in poha and lemon. Herbs for flavor.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </article>

      <!-- 9 -->
      <article class="card recipe">
        <h3>9) Khichdi (Small Bowl)</h3>
        <p class="sub">Comforting fiber + protein</p>
        <div class="cols">
          <div>
            <ul class="list">
              <li>Moong dal + rice (more dal for fiber)</li>
              <li>Veg: carrot/peas/spinach</li>
              <li>Cumin, turmeric, ginger</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p>Cook soft; finish with lemon & coriander. Keep ghee minimal; avoid pickle for salt control.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </article>

      <!-- 10 -->
      <article class="card recipe">
        <h3>10) Overnight Oats Jar</h3>
        <p class="sub">Make-ahead grab-and-go</p>
        <div class="cols">
          <div>
            <ul class="list">
              <li>½ cup oats + ¾ cup milk/soy</li>
              <li>1 tbsp chia or flax</li>
              <li>Fruit & nuts (unsalted)</li>
              <li>Vanilla/cinnamon</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p>Mix in a jar, chill overnight. Add toppings in the morning. No added sugar needed.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>

  <!-- Swaps & sodium -->
  <section id="swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Smart swaps & sodium control</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Swap this → for that</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>White bread → whole-wheat/multigrain.</li>
          <li>Butter → peanut/almond butter (thin layer) or avocado.</li>
          <li>Sugary cereal → plain oats + nuts/fruit.</li>
          <li>Processed meats → egg, paneer (low-fat), or beans/dal.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Keep salt low</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Flavor with lemon, herbs, ginger, garlic, pepper.</li>
          <li>Rinse canned beans/veg to remove extra salt.</li>
          <li>Limit pickles, papad, instant mixes, processed cheese.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Read labels: choose “low sodium” and “no added sugar” versions where possible.
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        A small handful (1 tbsp) of nuts/seeds adds crunch and heart-healthy fats — no need for extra salt.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Fiber -->
  <section id="fiber" class="section">
    <h2>Fiber & cholesterol</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Add soluble fiber</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Oats, barley, dal/beans, apple, berries, citrus, flax/chia.</li>
          <li>Target: add one fiber food at breakfast daily.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Increase fiber slowly and drink water through the day to avoid bloating.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Conditions -->
  <section id="conditions" class="section">
    <h2>Adjustments for common conditions</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Condition</th><th scope="col">Adjust breakfast</th><th scope="col">Watch-outs</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Type 2 diabetes</strong></td>
          <td>Balance carbs with protein & fiber. Example: oats + curd + nuts; egg + toast + fruit (small).</td>
          <td>Skip juices/sugary cereal; treat lows per plan.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>High blood pressure</strong></td>
          <td>Low-salt choices; use lemon/herbs. Add potassium-rich fruit if allowed.</td>
          <td>Processed meats/instant mixes raise sodium quickly.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>High cholesterol</strong></td>
          <td>Focus on oats/beans/nuts; limit butter/ghee; prefer olive/mustard oil (small).</td>
          <td>Skip trans-fat snacks; keep egg yolks moderate if advised.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>CKD (kidney)</strong></td>
          <td>Follow potassium/phosphorus guidance. Choose lower-potassium fruit portions; limit dairy if advised.</td>
          <td>Discuss bananas/dairy/beans portions with clinician/dietitian.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>GERD / reflux</strong></td>
          <td>Smaller, low-fat breakfast; avoid citrus/spicy if they trigger you. Sit upright after eating.</td>
          <td>Mint/chocolate/coffee can worsen reflux for some.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>
