  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why memory changes</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Normal vs concerning</span> With age, we may need more <strong>time</strong> to learn new things and names. That’s different from memory loss that disrupts daily life. The tips below strengthen attention and recall, and help set up smart supports.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Normal “senior moments”</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Tip-of-the-tongue names, but recall later</li>
          <li>Misplacing items but finding them with a system</li>
          <li>Needing written reminders for complex tasks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Medical checks that help:</strong> vision/hearing, sleep quality, mood, thyroid/B12, and a medicine review — these all affect memory and attention.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Toolkit -->
  <section id="kit" class="section">
    <h2>Your memory toolkit</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">External supports (make it hard to forget)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>One calendar</strong> for everything (paper or phone) + daily check time</li>
          <li><strong>Pill organizer</strong> + alarms tied to meals</li>
          <li><strong>Landing zones</strong> by the door: keys–wallet–phone bowl</li>
          <li><strong>Checklists</strong> on the fridge (morning, shopping, travel)</li>
          <li><strong>Labels</strong> on drawers/shelves; large fonts for essentials</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Mental strategies (remember better)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>WARM</strong>: <em>Write</em> it, <em>Associate</em> it, <em>Repeat</em> it, <em>Make a story</em></li>
          <li><strong>Chunking</strong>: group numbers (e.g., 984–60–321)</li>
          <li><strong>Spaced practice</strong>: review after 10 min, 1 hr, next day</li>
          <li><strong>Say it out loud</strong> when you put something down: “Glasses on desk”</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- 10-minute practice -->
  <section id="ten" class="section">
    <h2>10-minute daily practice (build the habit)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Morning focus (3 min)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Check calendar + pillbox; star the top <strong>3</strong> tasks</li>
          <li>Place needed items in a visible staging area</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Name–face drill (2 min)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Review yesterday’s new names/people; repeat the name + one fact</li>
          <li>Use a contact photo in your phone with their name large</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Mid-day capture (2 min)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Empty pockets/notes into the calendar or a single list</li>
          <li>Set alarms for anything due later today</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <p class="kicker">4) Evening review (3 min)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Mark done items; move 1–2 to tomorrow</li>
          <li>Lay out clothes/meds; prep your “landing zone”</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Names -->
  <section id="names" class="section">
    <h2>Remembering names (fast methods)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Name recall strategies">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">How to do it</th><th scope="col">Example</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hear</strong></td>
          <td>Listen fully; ask to repeat/spell if unclear</td>
          <td>“Is it <em>Sonal</em> with an S?”</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Say</strong></td>
          <td>Use the name right away and at goodbye</td>
          <td>“Nice to meet you, Sonal.”</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Link</strong></td>
          <td>Attach a visual or fact</td>
          <td>Sonal → “sun” badge on her tote</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Store</strong></td>
          <td>Save contact with photo + note where you met</td>
          <td>“Neighbor in blue house; loves gardening”</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">If you forget, be honest: “Please remind me of your name.” Most people appreciate it.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Home cues -->
  <section id="home" class="section">
    <h2>Home cues & organization</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make the environment do the work</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Big <strong>whiteboard or calendar</strong> in a high-traffic spot</li>
          <li><strong>Color code</strong> documents and chargers by room</li>
          <li><strong>Tray by the door</strong> for outgoing items (mail, returns)</li>
          <li><strong>Voice notes</strong> to yourself (phone or smart speaker)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Reduce “memory friction”: fewer hiding places, clear labels, good lighting (see <a href="#">Lighting for Aging Eyes</a>).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Sleep, meds & health</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Health factors that affect memory">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Factor</th><th scope="col">Effect</th><th scope="col">What helps</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Poor sleep / sleep apnea</strong></td>
          <td>Worse attention and recall</td>
          <td>Consistent sleep window; evaluate snoring/apneas; see <a href="#">Evening Wind-Down</a></td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hearing/vision issues</strong></td>
          <td>Missed info → “memory” problems</td>
          <td>Regular checks; use hearing aids, brighter light, large print</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Mood (depression/anxiety)</strong></td>
          <td>Low concentration, slowed thinking</td>
          <td>Talk therapy, activity, clinician review</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medicines</strong> (sleep pills, some allergy meds, strong pain meds)</td>
          <td>Drowsiness, confusion, memory blocks</td>
          <td>Ask for a <strong>medication review</strong>; consider safer alternatives</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hydration/nutrition</strong></td>
          <td>Low energy, dizziness</td>
          <td>See <a href="#">Hydration Through the Day</a>; balanced meals</td>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="redflags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek help (red flags)</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Getting <strong>lost</strong> in familiar places</li>
        <li>Repeat questions many times on the same day</li>
        <li>Big changes in <strong>money management</strong> or paying bills</li>
        <li>New problems with <strong>cooking, medicines, or safety</strong></li>
        <li>Personality or behavior changes; suspiciousness</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Bring a family member and a list of examples to your clinician. Ask about reversible causes (sleep, mood, meds, B12/thyroid) and whether memory clinic testing is appropriate.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Missed meds/appointments (date & situation)</li>
          <li>New names learned this week (✓ when you recall next day)</li>
          <li>Sleep hours, mood notes, exercise/walks</li>
          <li>Changes after med adjustments or new glasses/hearing aids</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Review your notes every <strong>2–4 weeks</strong> to see what’s helping — then do more of that.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are crosswords enough?</p>
        <p>They’re good, but pair them with <strong>movement + social</strong> activity (short walks, phone a friend) for better brain benefits.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Paper or phone reminders?</p>
        <p>Either, but keep <strong>one primary system</strong> and check it at the same time daily.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I stop losing keys?</p>
        <p>Create a <strong>landing zone</strong> and say it out loud when you drop them: “Keys in bowl.” Add a bright key tag or tracker.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do naps help memory?</p>
        <p>Short naps (15–30 min) can refresh attention. Avoid long, late naps that disrupt night sleep.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
