    <h2>Why this matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="kicker">Big wins:</span> fewer injuries, more independence, and confidence moving around at home—especially at night or when tired.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Top hazards (often easy fixes)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Poor lighting and dark hallways</li>
          <li>Loose rugs, cords, and clutter</li>
          <li>Slippery bathrooms and stairways without rails</li>
          <li>Unsupportive footwear or poorly fitted walking aids</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Re-check the home after any <strong>new medication, surgery, illness, or fall</strong>—risk changes quickly.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Whole home -->
  <section id="whole-home" class="section">
    <h2>Whole-home quick fixes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Lighting:</strong> add motion night lights from bed to bathroom; lamps at entrances; bright bulbs (warm 2700–3000K).</li>
          <li><strong>Clear paths:</strong> remove cords/clutter; tape rug edges or remove loose rugs entirely.</li>
          <li><strong>Contrast:</strong> add dark tape to stair edges and light switch plates you can see easily.</li>
          <li><strong>Handholds:</strong> install railings both sides of stairs; grab bars by toilet and in shower.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Do a 10-minute “test walk” at night with only night lights on—fix any dim or shadowed areas you notice.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Rooms -->
  <section id="rooms" class="section">
    <h2>Room-by-room checklist</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Room-by-room fall-risk checklist">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Area</th><th>Do this</th><th>Watch-outs</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Entry & stairs</strong></td>
          <td>Rails both sides; nonslip treads; bright light with switches at top/bottom; keep shoes and cane by the door.</td>
          <td>Wet leaves or mats; carry items in small loads or use a bag with strap.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Living room</strong></td>
          <td>Route cords along walls; stable furniture you can push on; clear low tables from walkways.</td>
          <td>Wheeled chairs that move when you sit; low, floppy couches.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Kitchen</strong></td>
          <td>Frequently used items at waist to shoulder height; step stool with handrail (no chairs); nonslip mat near sink.</td>
          <td>Water on floor; reaching overhead without support.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bathroom</strong></td>
          <td>Grab bars (toilet & shower), raised toilet seat if needed, <strong>nonslip</strong> strips in shower, shower chair, hand-held shower.</td>
          <td>Loose bathmats; smooth tile when wet; rushing to the toilet at night.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bedroom</strong></td>
          <td>Bed height at knee level; nightstand with lamp/phone/glasses; clear path to bathroom; motion lights.</td>
          <td>Throw rugs and piles of clothes; getting up too fast—sit first, count to 10.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hallways</strong></td>
          <td>Night lights; handrail if long corridor; visual contrast at thresholds.</td>
          <td>Threshold lips and curled mats—tape down or remove.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Outdoors</strong></td>
          <td>Good lighting; level paths; nonslip paint/treads on steps; sturdy handrail.</td>
          <td>Uneven paving, moss, loose gravel; pets underfoot.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Footwear & devices -->
  <section id="footwear" class="section">
    <h2>Shoes & walking aids</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Everyday shoes</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Grippy outsole, low heel (≤2.5 cm), firm heel counter, <strong>no floppy slippers</strong>.</li>
          <li>Replace worn tread; use indoor “house shoes” rather than socks on tile.</li>
          <li>See: <a href="#">Shoe & Fit Check</a> for toe room, width, and lacing fixes.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Canes & walkers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Set height: wrist crease aligns with handle when standing tall with arms at sides.</li>
          <li>Rubber tips intact; tennis-ball sliders replaced with proper glides for the floor type.</li>
          <li>Use the device <em>every time</em> you do stairs/uneven ground until your clinician says otherwise.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Vision & meds -->
  <section id="vision" class="section">
    <h2>Vision, hearing & meds</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Glasses:</strong> keep a current pair at bedside; clean lenses; update prescription yearly.</li>
          <li><strong>Hearing:</strong> wear aids consistently; replace batteries/charge nightly—hearing steps and alerts improves safety.</li>
          <li><strong>Medication review:</strong> ask about drugs that may cause dizziness or sleepiness (some BP pills, sleep aids, pain meds). Never stop on your own—request safer timing or alternatives.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        After any medication change, do a <strong>7-day extra-caution period</strong>: slower position changes, night lights on, cane/walker for bathroom trips.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Exercise -->
  <section id="exercise" class="section">
    <h2>Strength & balance (5-minute starters)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily micro-routine</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Sit-to-stand x10</strong> from a sturdy chair (use arms as needed).</li>
          <li><strong>Heel raises x10</strong> holding a counter.</li>
          <li><strong>March in place x20</strong> with light support.</li>
          <li><strong>Tandem stand</strong> (one foot in front) 10–20 sec each side.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Add a balance class (Tai Chi, OT/PT programs). Progress slowly—quality over speed. Stop with chest pain, severe breathlessness, or dizziness.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Plan -->
  <section id="plan" class="section">
    <h2>If a fall happens</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make a plan</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Carry a phone or wear an alert device at home.</li>
          <li>Post emergency numbers by each phone; share a spare key with a neighbor/family.</li>
          <li>Practice getting up: roll to side → crawl to a sturdy chair → one knee up → push to stand.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card danger">
        <strong>Call for help immediately</strong> if you hit your head, can’t stand, have severe pain, new weakness/confusion, or are on blood thinners.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What’s the most important change to start with?</p>
        <p>Night lighting and bathroom safety: motion lights, grab bars, and nonslip shower strips reduce common nighttime falls.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are throw rugs always bad?</p>
        <p>Loose rugs are a frequent trip hazard. If you keep them, use <strong>nonslip backing + edge tape</strong> and ensure they’re flat and secure.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need a walker?</p>
        <p>If you feel unsteady, have near-falls, or walk while holding furniture, ask for a PT/OT assessment to choose and fit the right device.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How often should I re-check the home?</p>
        <p>Seasonally and after any health or medication change—or any slip, trip, or fall.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
