    <h2>Why this works</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="kicker">Small inputs → real gains:</span> Light, movement, connection, and achievement signals nudge brain chemistry (serotonin, dopamine, circadian rhythm). Consistency beats intensity.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What you’ll need</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>A bright spot (window/balcony) or lamp</li>
          <li>Comfortable chair and shoes</li>
          <li>Phone (1 call/text) and a sticky note or notebook</li>
          <li>Glass of water or warm tea</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Low mood can come from sleep issues, pain, medicines, thyroid, B12, grief, or loneliness—this plan helps, but medical review may still be useful.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Your 5-step mini-plan (10–20 minutes total)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <!-- Step 1 -->
      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">1</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Light + sip (2 min)</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Open curtains or sit by a bright window; face light.</li>
          <li>Drink a few sips of water/tea to cue “start.”</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <!-- Step 2 -->
      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">2</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Move gently (3–6 min)</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Seated or standing: march in place x30, shoulder rolls x10, ankle circles x10/side, slow neck look L/R x5.</li>
          <li>Optional: short walk to the mailbox/balcony.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <!-- Step 3 -->
      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">3</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Connect (2–5 min)</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Send a <strong>one-line text/voice note</strong>: “Thinking of you—how’s today?”</li>
          <li>No phone? Wave to a neighbor, greet a caregiver, or pet an animal.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <!-- Step 4 -->
      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">4</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Tiny win (2–4 min)</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Choose a <strong>2-minute task</strong>: make the bed, water one plant, sort today’s pills, put 5 items away.</li>
          <li>Say out loud: “Done.” Write a ✓ on a sticky note.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <!-- Step 5 -->
      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">5</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Calm the system (1–3 min)</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Pursed-lip breathing:</strong> inhale nose 2, exhale lips 4 (x5–8 breaths).</li>
          <li>Option: 10-second gratitude—name one good thing from the last 24 hours.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Options -->
  <section id="options" class="section">
    <h2>Pick-one options (swap as you like)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If you’re low on energy</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sunlight at window + 2-minute ankle/hand moves</li>
          <li>Call a friend while seated</li>
          <li>Hold a warm mug; 5 slow breaths</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If you’re anxious or keyed up</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Feet on floor, hands on belly—slow exhale focus</li>
          <li>Light music or nature sounds (low volume)</li>
          <li>Note & park worries on paper: “I’ll revisit at 4 pm.”</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If you’re lonely</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Send “photo + one line” to family group</li>
          <li>Schedule a 10-minute call this week (put on calendar)</li>
          <li>Join one recurring activity (library talk, walking group)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Repeat the mini-plan <strong>once in the morning</strong> and again mid-afternoon for best effect.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="signals" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek help</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card danger">
        <strong>Urgent now:</strong> thoughts of self-harm, not wanting to live, severe confusion, or inability to care for yourself (not eating/drinking). Call your local emergency number or crisis line.
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Call your clinician soon if</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Low mood lasts most days &gt;2 weeks, or loss of interest/pleasure</li>
          <li>Major sleep/appetite changes, weight loss, or new memory issues</li>
          <li>Med changes preceded mood drop, or alcohol is creeping up</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does this replace treatment for depression?</p>
        <p>No—this is a supportive routine. Persistent or severe symptoms need clinical care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">I don’t feel like doing anything—where do I start?</p>
        <p>Start at step 1 only (light + sip). Often step 2 follows naturally. Set a 3-minute timer.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if I have pain or limited mobility?</p>
        <p>Do seated versions of movements and keep steps tiny. Even ankle/hand motions plus breath work help.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best time of day?</p>
        <p>Morning and mid-afternoon. Consistency at the same times improves results.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
