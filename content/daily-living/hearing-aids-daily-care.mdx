    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Soft cloth or alcohol-free wipes</li>
          <li>Small brush & vent tool (often included)</li>
          <li>Pack of <strong>wax guards</strong> and spare <strong>domes</strong></li>
          <li>Drying jar/dehumidifier or electronic dryer</li>
          <li>Recharge case or spare <strong>batteries (size 10/13/312)</strong></li>
          <li>Label “L” and “R” markers; a small mirror</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Keep the box where you charge at night — less hunting, fewer missed cleans.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Daily -->
  <section id="daily" class="section">
    <h2>Daily routine: clean • dry • charge (2 minutes)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Wipe</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Remove aids. Wipe shell, microphone ports, and earpiece with a dry cloth.</li>
          <li>Use the small <strong>brush</strong> to lightly sweep microphone openings.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="kicker" style="margin-top:10px">2) Check</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Inspect dome/tube or earmold. If wax is visible at the tip, clean or replace the guard.</li>
          <li>Confirm left/right labels before storing.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Dry</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Place in a <strong>drying jar or electronic dryer</strong> overnight (best in humid climates).</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="kicker" style="margin-top:10px">4) Charge or store</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rechargeables: seat fully in the charger; confirm lights.</li>
          <li>Disposable batteries: open battery doors overnight to air-dry if not using a dryer.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Moisture -->
  <section id="moisture" class="section">
    <h2>Moisture & rain/sweat protection</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Avoid showers or swimming with aids. Remove before hair spray or powder.</li>
          <li>Use a <strong>sweatband/retainer</strong> for exercise or hot weather.</li>
          <li>If wet: power off, remove battery (if disposable), dry exterior, and place in dryer (no heat gun).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Routine nightly drying prevents most “weak/muffled” issues, especially during monsoon or coastal humidity.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Wax/Domes -->
  <section id="wax" class="section">
    <h2>Wax guards, domes & tubes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When to change</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sound is softer or dead on one side.</li>
          <li>Wax seen in the guard or dome tip.</li>
          <li>Every 4–8 weeks as routine, sooner if waxy ears.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How to change a guard (typical system)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Remove dome gently to expose the guard.</li>
          <li>Use the new guard stick: pull old guard out, flip stick, press new guard in, discard stick.</li>
          <li>Re-fit dome firmly (match size). Tug lightly to ensure it’s secure.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">If unsure of your system (CeruShield, HF3, etc.), check your brand’s leaflet or clinic video.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Fit -->
  <section id="fit" class="section">
    <h2>Fit & comfort checks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dome sits flush; no sharp edges or soreness after 2–3 hours.</li>
          <li>For earmolds: no rubbing or whistling when you cup your hand lightly over the ear.</li>
          <li>Persistent soreness → contact clinic for refit or different dome size.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        A good seal reduces feedback and improves clarity, especially in noisy places.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Connectivity -->
  <section id="connect" class="section">
    <h2>Phone calls & Bluetooth tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep your phone’s Bluetooth on and the brand app updated.</li>
          <li>If calls sound choppy: toggle Bluetooth off/on, then open the app; or reboot phone.</li>
          <li>For TV streamers: power cycle the streamer, then reconnect in the app.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Many apps offer <strong>programs</strong> (restaurant, outdoor, TV). Save your favorites and label them clearly.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Battery -->
  <section id="battery" class="section">
    <h2>Rechargeable & battery safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Rechargeables</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Seat fully; green/solid light means charging. Keep charger on a surge-protected outlet.</li>
          <li>For power cuts: use a small power bank if your charger supports USB.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Disposable zinc-air</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Remove tab and <strong>wait 1–2 minutes</strong> before inserting to activate.</li>
          <li>Store cool and dry; never in bathroom. Recycle used batteries safely.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Weekly -->
  <section id="weekly" class="section">
    <h2>Weekly deep clean (10 minutes)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Brush microphone ports and receiver tip thoroughly.</li>
          <li>Change wax guard <em>if</em> buildup noted.</li>
          <li>Wash earmolds/tubes with mild soapy water (if your model allows); dry completely before use.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Never use alcohol or solvents on the aid body unless your brand explicitly says it’s safe.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Troubleshooting -->
  <section id="troubleshoot" class="section">
    <h2>Troubleshooting quick fixes</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <table class="table" role="table">
        <thead>
          <tr><th>Problem</th><th>Likely cause</th><th>Try this</th></tr>
        </thead>
        <tbody>
          <tr>
            <td>No sound</td>
            <td>Dead battery, clogged wax guard, power off</td>
            <td>Recharge/replace battery; change wax guard; confirm power switch or case lid</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Muffled / weak</td>
            <td>Moisture, partial wax blockage</td>
            <td>Nightly dryer; brush tip; replace guard; ensure dome seal</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Whistling (feedback)</td>
            <td>Poor seal, dome too small, earmold leak</td>
            <td>Re-seat aid; try larger dome; check for earwax; clinic refit if persists</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Cuts in/out on calls</td>
            <td>Bluetooth glitch</td>
            <td>Toggle Bluetooth; reopen app; restart phone; re-pair if needed</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Ear soreness</td>
            <td>Dome size/shape, dry skin</td>
            <td>Switch dome size; short breaks; tiny amount of approved lubricant; clinic if not relieved</td>
          </tr>
        </tbody>
      </table>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="redflags" class="section">
    <h2>When to get help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Sudden hearing drop, severe ear pain, discharge, or fever.</li>
        <li>Bleeding or suspected dome stuck in ear canal.</li>
        <li>Persistent dizziness or new ringing with hearing change.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Contact your audiologist/ENT urgently for these issues.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Travel -->
  <section id="travel" class="section">
    <h2>Travel & power cuts</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pack a <strong>travel pouch</strong>: charger or spare batteries, domes, guards, brush, dryer sachet.</li>
          <li>Use a <strong>power bank</strong> if your charger supports USB; carry a plug adaptor.</li>
          <li>Airport security: keep aids on; show staff the charger if asked.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
