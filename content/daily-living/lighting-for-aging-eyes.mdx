  <!-- Why -->
  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why lighting matters with age</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Needs change</span> Aging lenses yellow and pupils shrink, so we may need <strong>2–3× more light</strong> to see detail. More light helps, but only if it’s <strong>even and low-glare</strong>.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Benefits of a lighting tune-up</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fewer trips and missteps on stairs and thresholds</li>
          <li>Easier reading, sewing, pill sorting, and cooking</li>
          <li>Reduced eye strain and headaches</li>
          <li>Better mood and day–night rhythm</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        If you have cataracts, glaucoma, macular degeneration, or diabetes eye disease, small lighting changes can make a big difference in daily safety.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Basics -->
  <section id="basics" class="section">
    <h2>Lighting basics (quick guide)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Pick bulbs</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Brightness:</strong> 800–1100 lumens for table lamps; 1100–1600 lumens for task lamps.</li>
          <li><strong>Color temperature (CCT):</strong> 3000–3500K warm-neutral for evenings; 4000K neutral for kitchens/work areas.</li>
          <li><strong>CRI:</strong> 90+ shows true colors (helps with meds/food).</li>
          <li><strong>Dimmable LEDs</strong> give control; avoid flicker (look for “flicker-free”).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Layer light: <strong>ambient</strong> (room fill) + <strong>task</strong> (focused) + <strong>accent</strong> (glare-free highlights). Aim for even coverage—no dark corners.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Rooms -->
  <section id="rooms" class="section">
    <h2>Room-by-room checklist</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Room by room lighting checklist">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Area</th><th scope="col">Do this</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Entry / Hallways</strong></td>
          <td>Bright, even ceiling lights every 1.5–2 m; add motion night lights.</td>
          <td>Mark level changes with contrasting tape.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Living room</strong></td>
          <td>Two lamps at 800–1100 lm each; reading lamp over shoulder.</td>
          <td>Shade the bulb (fabric/opal) to cut glare on TV and glasses.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Kitchen</strong></td>
          <td>Under-cabinet task lights along counters; 4000K ceiling light.</td>
          <td>Light the sink and stove; avoid shadows from your body.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bedroom</strong></td>
          <td>Bedside lamps with large switches; motion night lights to bathroom.</td>
          <td>Keep contrast: light walls, darker floor edge strip near bed.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bathroom</strong></td>
          <td>Even vanity lighting (two sconces or wide bar) + ceiling light.</td>
          <td>Non-glare bulbs; night light on sensor; see <a href="#">Bathroom Safety Setup</a>.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Stairs</strong></td>
          <td>Light both top and bottom landings; step lights or wall washers.</td>
          <td>High-contrast nosing tape on the first/last step.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Reading / Hobbies</strong></td>
          <td>Adjustable arm lamp, 1100–1600 lm, 3500–4000K, CRI 90+.</td>
          <td>Place light over the shoulder of your dominant hand.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Glare & Contrast -->
  <section id="glare" class="section">
    <h2>Glare & contrast fixes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reduce glare</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>diffusers/shades</strong> (frosted/opal) instead of bare bulbs.</li>
          <li>Turn glossy surfaces matte or reposition lamps to avoid reflections.</li>
          <li>Close sheer curtains to soften harsh daylight.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Boost contrast</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dark edge strip on light floors to outline pathways.</li>
          <li>High-contrast cutting board (white on black, black on white) in kitchen.</li>
          <li>Large-print, high-contrast labels for meds and switches.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Night path -->
  <section id="night" class="section">
    <h2>Night path & bathroom lights</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Install <strong>motion-sensor night lights</strong> from bed → bathroom.</li>
          <li>Keep level, <strong>warm</strong> light (2700–3000K) to protect sleep.</li>
          <li>Place a small lamp or torch within arm’s reach of the bed.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Pair night lights with <strong>clutter-free paths</strong>, non-slip mats, and grab bars where needed.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Low-vision -->
  <section id="lowvision" class="section">
    <h2>Low-vision adaptations</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Task lamp + magnifier</strong> (gooseneck) for reading/meds.</li>
          <li><strong>Voice assistants</strong> or large-button switches/dimmers.</li>
          <li><strong>Pathway lighting strips</strong> along skirting boards.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Sudden vision change, halos, eye pain, or flashing lights → seek eye care promptly.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Meds & conditions that affect vision</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Meds and conditions">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Factor</th><th scope="col">Effect</th><th scope="col">Tips</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Glaucoma, cataracts, AMD, diabetic eye disease</strong></td>
          <td>Reduced contrast, glare sensitivity, blind spots</td>
          <td>Boost ambient light; add task lighting; reduce glare; regular checkups</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sleep pills, sedatives, antihistamines</strong></td>
          <td>Drowsy, blurred vision at night</td>
          <td>Use night lights; avoid sudden brightness; review timing with clinician</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Blood pressure meds</strong></td>
          <td>Dizziness on standing → missteps in dim light</td>
          <td>Stage your rise (sit→stand); ensure night path lighting</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Power -->
  <section id="power" class="section">
    <h2>Power cuts: backup lighting</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep <strong>rechargeable torches</strong> at bed, kitchen, and entry.</li>
          <li>Use <strong>auto-on emergency bulbs</strong> (stay lit during outages).</li>
          <li>Solar or battery motion lights for hallways/stairs.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Test backup lights monthly; charge devices on a routine (e.g., first of each month).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dark spots or glare sources found and fixed (✓/✗)</li>
          <li>Night path coverage (bed→bathroom) with motion lights (✓/✗)</li>
          <li>Bulb list by room (lumens, CCT, CRI)</li>
          <li>Falls/near-falls in low light (date/time and cause)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Re-check lighting every <strong>6–12 months</strong> or after any vision/medicine change.

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Warm or cool light?</p>
        <p><strong>Evening:</strong> 2700–3000K (warmer). <strong>Tasks:</strong> 3500–4000K (neutral). Avoid harsh 6500K indoors.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How bright should reading light be?</p>
        <p>About <strong>1100–1600 lumens</strong> at the lamp head with a diffuser, placed over your shoulder.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What’s CRI?</p>
        <p>Color Rendering Index. Choose <strong>CRI 90+</strong> so labels, pills, and food look true to color.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are motion night lights worth it?</p>
        <p>Yes — they reduce fumbles for switches and prevent harsh light shocks that can cause dizziness at night.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
