  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why position matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="kicker">Benefits:</span> less neck/shoulder/back pain, fewer reflux episodes, steadier breathing/snoring control, better morning balance, and reduced skin pressure.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Quick alignment rule</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep <strong>ears, shoulders, and hips</strong> in a straight line.</li>
          <li>Fill the <strong>gaps</strong> (neck, waist, knees) with pillows—support, don’t shove.</li>
          <li>Neutral joints: no end-range twist or tilt for many hours.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        If you wake with numb hands, headache, jaw tightness, or back stiffness—your pillow height or position likely needs a small adjustment.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="positions" class="section">
    <h2>Best positions (by sleeper type)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Sleep positions">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Position</th><th>How to set up</th><th>Good for</th><th>Watch-outs</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Side (left or right)</strong></td>
          <td>Medium-high pillow to fill neck space; thin pillow between knees; small towel roll at waist if there’s a gap.</td>
          <td>Snoring/sleep apnea (usually <em>side</em> better than back), back pain, hip pain (with knee pillow), pregnancy & reflux (left).</td>
          <td>Shoulder soreness—try hugging a pillow and slightly rolling toward stomach to unload the bottom shoulder.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Back (supine)</strong></td>
          <td>Low–medium pillow to keep chin neutral; small pillow under knees or a wedge under thighs to relax low back.</td>
          <td>Neck/shoulder pain relief (with proper height), spinal stenosis (knees up), swelling control.</td>
          <td>Snoring/apnea may worsen; elevate head/chest if needed.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Partial prone “recovery” (3/4 turn)</strong></td>
          <td>Side-lying with top leg forward on a thick pillow; hug a pillow; head on medium pillow.</td>
          <td>Rotator cuff discomfort, some hip pain patterns.</td>
          <td>Full stomach-sleeping strains neck—avoid long hours fully prone.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Reclined (wedge/adjustable bed)</strong></td>
          <td>Wedge (20–30 cm lift at head/upper chest) or adjustable base; support knees slightly.</td>
          <td>Reflux/GERD, heart failure orthopnea, COPD (easier breathing), back pain when flat.</td>
          <td>Don’t stack many soft pillows—they fold the neck. Prefer a wedge or adjustable base.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Pillows -->
  <section id="pillows" class="section">
    <h2>Pillows: types & sizing</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common types</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Memory foam (contour)</strong>: holds shape; good neck support for back/side.</li>
          <li><strong>Latex</strong>: springy/cooler; durable; good if you change positions.</li>
          <li><strong>Feather/down</strong>: moldable; may flatten—add a thin foam underlayer.</li>
          <li><strong>Adjustable fill</strong> (shredded foam/beads): remove/add fill to dial height.</li>
          <li><strong>Body pillow/U-pillow</strong>: excellent for side-sleeping pressure relief.</li>
          <li><strong>Wedge pillow</strong>: head/upper-chest elevation (reflux, snoring) or leg elevation (swelling, back comfort).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Pick the right height (“loft”)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Side:</strong> medium–high (≈10–13 cm) to keep nose in line with sternum.</li>
          <li><strong>Back:</strong> low–medium (≈7–10 cm); avoid chin-to-chest curl.</li>
          <li><strong>Mixed:</strong> adjustable fill or latex, so it adapts when you roll.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Stacks -->
  <section id="stacks" class="section">
    <h2>Smart pillow stacks (by condition)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Pillow stacks by condition">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Need</th><th>Setup</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Reflux / GERD</strong></td>
          <td>Use a <strong>30–45° wedge</strong> under shoulders to mid-back <em>plus</em> a thin head pillow.</td>
          <td>Stacking soft pillows bends the neck; a wedge keeps the whole torso elevated.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Snoring / OSA (with CPAP)</strong></td>
          <td>Side-sleep with a medium pillow; if supine, elevate head/torso on wedge. Consider a <strong>CPAP-friendly pillow</strong> with mask cutouts.</td>
          <td>Keep mask tubing slack; avoid chin-tucked posture that narrows the airway.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Shoulder pain</strong></td>
          <td>Sleep on the <em>opposite</em> side; hug a pillow to open chest; place a thin pillow behind back to prevent rolling.</td>
          <td>If you must lie on the sore shoulder, add a small pillow under that upper arm to unload the joint.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Low-back pain</strong></td>
          <td>Back-sleepers: thin pillow under knees. Side-sleepers: pillow between knees & small towel at waist gap.</td>
          <td>Try a short <em>knee-to-chest</em> stretch before bed if safe for you.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hip/knee arthritis</strong></td>
          <td>Thicker <strong>knee pillow</strong> to keep hips level; for knee pain, place a <em>second</em> thin pillow between ankles.</td>
          <td>Keep feet aligned—don’t let top leg drop forward without support.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Heart failure / COPD</strong></td>
          <td>Reclined position with wedge or adjustable bed; extra pillow under arms for comfort.</td>
          <td>Report new need for many pillows (“2 → 4 pillows”)—could signal fluid changes.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Swelling / varicose veins</strong></td>
          <td>Leg elevation: wedge or 2 pillows under <em>calves</em> (not knees) so heels float.</td>
          <td>Prevents heel pressure sores; don’t hyperflex the knees.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Neck pain</strong></td>
          <td>Use a <strong>contour pillow</strong> (cervical roll under neck) with neutral chin.</td>
          <td>If shoulders are broad, add loft; if petite, reduce loft.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Pressure -->
  <section id="pressure" class="section">
    <h2>Pressure relief & skin safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Prevent sore spots: <strong>float heels</strong> (pillow under calves), place a thin pillow between knees/ankles, and change sides.</li>
          <li>Check bony areas (heels, hips, tailbone, shoulders) for redness that <em>doesn’t</em> fade in 20–30 minutes.</li>
          <li>Use smooth sheets and breathable fabrics; keep skin clean and dry.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        If you’re on bed rest or have diabetes/vascular disease, ask about a <strong>pressure-redistribution mattress pad</strong> or schedule for assisted turns.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Bed moves -->
  <section id="bed-moves" class="section">
    <h2>Getting in/out of bed</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Log-roll technique</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sit to the bed, <strong>lie onto your side</strong>, knees slightly bent.</li>
          <li>Roll as one unit onto your back while bringing legs up.</li>
          <li>To get out: roll to side, drop lower legs off the bed, push up with arms.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Dizziness? Sit at the edge for 10–20 seconds before standing. Add a <strong>bed rail</strong> or sturdy nightstand for support if needed.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Care -->
  <section id="care" class="section">
    <h2>Care & replacement</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Wash pillowcases weekly; wash pillows per label (many foam pillows: <em>spot clean</em> only, air out).</li>
          <li><strong>Replace pillows</strong> every 18–36 months (or when permanently flat/lumpy).</li>
          <li>For allergies, use <strong>dust-mite covers</strong> and wash bedding hot (≥60°C) if tolerated.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Travel & naps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pack a small inflatable or compressible pillow to keep neck neutral in cars/planes.</li>
          <li>For recliner naps: support the <strong>neck</strong> and <strong>lower back</strong>; avoid chin-to-chest curl.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
