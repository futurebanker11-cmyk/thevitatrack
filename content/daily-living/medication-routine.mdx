  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why a routine matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="kicker">Benefits:</span> better control of blood pressure/sugar, fewer side effects, fewer hospital visits, and less stress for you and caregivers.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make it <em>automatic</em></p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Attach doses to daily anchors: breakfast, lunch, dinner, bedtime.</li>
          <li>Keep meds in one safe, well-lit spot (not the steamy bathroom).</li>
          <li>Use the same organizer + reminder times every day.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Bring an <strong>updated medicine list</strong> (with doses and reasons) to every appointment. Include vitamins and over-the-counter (OTC) items.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Your 5-step daily flow</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">1</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Morning check</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Take morning pills after a quick sip of water and a small bite if “with food.”</li>
          <li>Check vitals you track (BP, glucose, weight) before medicines if advised.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">2</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Midday touchpoint</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Set a lunch alarm for any noon doses.</li>
          <li>Refill your water bottle; light snack if required with meds.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">3</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Evening setup</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Place bedtime meds on a tray by the toothbrush/bedside (out of children’s reach).</li>
          <li>Check organizer for the next day; pre-pour if you use a smart dispenser.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">4</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Bedtime dose</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Take nighttime pills; avoid lying flat for 30 min after pills that can irritate the esophagus (ask your clinician/pharmacist).</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
      <article class="card step">
        <div class="num">5</div>
        <h3 class="kicker">Weekly reset</h3>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Once weekly, clean organizers, refill for 7 days, and review the med list for changes.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Organizer -->
  <section id="organizer" class="section">
    <h2>Pill organizer setup</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Choose the right box</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>1×/day</strong> or <strong>AM/PM</strong> for simple plans; <strong>4-times/day</strong> for complex regimens.</li>
          <li>Flip-top lids with large labels; high-contrast printing; weekly + monthly refills if stable.</li>
          <li>For tremor/vision issues: consider a <strong>locking</strong> or <strong>smart</strong> dispenser with alerts.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Filling tips:</strong> good light, no distractions, one medicine at a time, double-check against your written schedule. Keep original bottles for reference.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Reminders -->
  <section id="reminders" class="section">
    <h2>Reminders that actually work</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Reminder systems">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Tool</th><th>How to use</th><th>Best for</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Phone alarms / smart speaker</td>
          <td>Label alarms “BP pill” “insulin” etc.; set recurring times; voice confirm after taking.</td>
          <td>Daily reliability</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Fridge or bedside chart</td>
          <td>Tick boxes after each dose; replace weekly.</td>
          <td>Visual confirmation</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Care-partner check-ins</td>
          <td>Brief call or text at key times; share photo of the organizer if needed.</td>
          <td>Extra accountability</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Smart caps / blister packs</td>
          <td>Packaging or caps that beep/log openings.</td>
          <td>Complex regimens</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Timing -->
  <section id="timing" class="section">
    <h2>Timing with food & sleep</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>With food:</strong> take during or right after a meal/snack to reduce stomach upset.</li>
          <li><strong>Empty stomach:</strong> typically 30–60 min before breakfast or 2–3 hours after eating (examples: some thyroid pills, certain PPIs).</li>
          <li><strong>Bedtime meds:</strong> avoid lying flat for 30 min if pills can irritate the esophagus; use water, not juice or hot drinks.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Interactions to ask about:</strong> alcohol, grapefruit/Seville orange, antacids & supplements (iron, calcium, magnesium) which can block some antibiotics/thyroid pills—separate by several hours as advised.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Refills -->
  <section id="refills" class="section">
    <h2>Refills & syncing</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make it one trip</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Ask pharmacy to <strong>sync refills</strong> to one pick-up date.</li>
          <li>Enable auto-refill and delivery if available.</li>
          <li>Set a <strong>7-day remaining</strong> alarm to reorder (or when organizer is half-empty).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Keep a <strong>buffer</strong> for essentials (per prescriber rules). For controlled meds, know earliest refill date.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety checks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>One pharmacy</strong> when possible—they catch interactions.</li>
          <li><strong>Brown-bag review</strong> every 6–12 months: bring all bottles (Rx + OTC + supplements) to a visit to see what can be simplified.</li>
          <li>Store away from heat/humidity; keep child-safe and pet-safe.</li>
          <li>Never share medicines. Dispose of expired drugs at take-back sites.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card danger">
        <strong>Urgent call signs:</strong> new severe rash, swelling of lips/tongue, breathing trouble, fainting, black/tarry stools, bloody vomit, or confusion after med changes.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Travel -->
  <section id="travel" class="section">
    <h2>Travel & storage</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pack meds in carry-on, in original labeled bottles or clearly labeled organizer.</li>
          <li>Bring an up-to-date <strong>medication list</strong> and prescriber contacts.</li>
          <li>Time-zone change: keep doses on <em>home time</em> for short trips; for longer trips, shift by 30–60 min per day until aligned.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Heat/cold can damage meds; avoid leaving in a hot car. Refrigerated meds: use an insulated pouch with a cold pack (not touching the vial directly).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Missed -->
  <section id="missed" class="section">
    <h2>Missed dose: what now?</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Missed dose guidance (general)">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Situation</th><th>General approach</th><th>Examples/notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Just realized and it’s <em>close</em> to the usual time</td>
          <td>Take it now (if within a few hours) unless told otherwise.</td>
          <td>Common with blood pressure or thyroid meds—ask for specifics.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>It’s <em>near</em> the next dose time</td>
          <td>Skip the missed dose; resume regular schedule. <strong>Do not double up</strong> unless your clinician said so.</td>
          <td>Typical for many daily pills.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Time-critical meds</td>
          <td>Follow personalized plan from your prescriber.</td>
          <td>Insulin, blood thinners, seizure meds may need special steps.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">When in doubt, call your pharmacist/clinician—have the exact medicine name and strength ready.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What’s the simplest routine?</p>
        <p>AM/PM organizers anchored to breakfast and bedtime, with phone alarms and a weekly refill on the same day.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I split pills to save money?</p>
        <p>Only if your clinician says the pill is safe to split (not all are). Never split extended-release or enteric-coated tablets.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are OTCs and supplements important to list?</p>
        <p>Yes—many interact with prescriptions (e.g., some antacids, St. John’s wort). Always include them on your med list.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What’s a “deprescribing” review?</p>
        <p>A planned check to stop or simplify medicines that are no longer needed or causing side effects—ask yearly or after hospital stays.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
