  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why daily checks matter</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Small issues → big problems</span> Tiny cuts, pressure spots, or nail problems can turn into serious infections before they hurt — especially if you have <strong>diabetes</strong> or <strong>reduced sensation</strong>. A quick daily check finds trouble early.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common risks</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dry, cracked heels; fungal nails; athlete’s foot</li>
          <li>Pressure points from tight shoes or bunions</li>
          <li>Blisters under calluses you can’t feel</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        If bending is hard or vision is limited, use a <strong>hand mirror</strong> or ask a family member/caregiver to check with you.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Kit -->
  <section id="kit" class="section">
    <h2>What you’ll need</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Good light and a <strong>hand mirror</strong></li>
          <li>Soft towel; mild soap</li>
          <li>Unscented moisturizer (avoid between toes)</li>
          <li>Nail clippers/emery board</li>
          <li>Clean cotton or moisture-wicking socks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Keep the kit together near your chair or bedside to make the routine easy and consistent.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Your 6-step check (5–7 minutes)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Look top & bottom</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use the mirror to see heels, soles, and between toes.</li>
          <li>Check for redness, cuts, cracks, blisters, or color changes.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Feel for trouble</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Press gently along the sole and toes for tender or warm spots.</li>
          <li>Compare both feet — swelling or warmth on one side can signal infection.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Dry well — especially between toes</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Moisture between toes leads to fungal infections and skin splits.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">4) Moisturize skin</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Apply cream to tops/soles/heels; <strong>skip between toes</strong>.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">5) Check nails</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Trim straight across; smooth edges. Don’t dig into corners.</li>
          <li>Yellow, thick, or curved nails? Consider podiatry review.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <p class="kicker">6) Socks & shoes</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Shake out shoes; inspect insoles for wear or stones.</li>
          <li>Wear clean, dry socks; choose shoes with a roomy toe box.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Clean / moisturize -->
  <section id="clean" class="section">
    <h2>Clean, dry & moisturize (quick guide)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Cleaning and moisturizing">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Do</th><th scope="col">Avoid</th><th scope="col">Why</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Short, lukewarm wash (not hot)</td>
          <td>Soaking &gt;10 minutes</td>
          <td>Long soaks soften skin and increase splits</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Pat dry, especially between toes</td>
          <td>Rubbing hard</td>
          <td>Prevents skin tears</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Moisturize heels/soles daily</td>
          <td>Cream between toes</td>
          <td>Moisture between toes → fungal/maceration</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Use pumice gently on thick heels</td>
          <td>Sharp blades or callus shavers</td>
          <td>Risk of cuts and infection</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Nails -->
  <section id="nails" class="section">
    <h2>Nail care & callus</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Trim nails when they’re dry and firm; cut <strong>straight across</strong>.</li>
          <li>Round sharp edges with a file; avoid digging into corners.</li>
          <li>Thick or curved nails (ingrown risk) → book a podiatry visit.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Diabetes/anticoagulants:</strong> Consider professional nail care to avoid cuts and bleeding.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Shoes -->
  <section id="shoes" class="section">
    <h2>Shoes & socks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fit checklist</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Roomy toe box; thumb’s width in front of the longest toe.</li>
          <li>Firm heel counter; non-slip sole; cushioned insole.</li>
          <li>No inside seams rubbing bunions or hammer toes.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Socks: cotton or moisture-wicking; seamless if sensitive skin; change if damp.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="redflags" class="section">
    <h2>Red flags (call your clinician promptly)</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Any open sore, ulcer, or skin breakdown</li>
        <li>Spreading redness, warmth, pus, or fever</li>
        <li>Black/blue skin patches, sudden severe pain, or numb area that’s getting bigger</li>
        <li>Ingrown nail with swelling/pain, especially if diabetic</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Mark the edge of redness with a pen to see if it’s spreading while you arrange care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best time to check?</p>
        <p>After your bath or before bed when feet are clean and you can sit with good light.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I go barefoot at home?</p>
        <p>It’s safer to wear supportive slippers/shoes with a closed toe to avoid cuts and stubs.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if I can’t reach my feet?</p>
        <p>Use a long-handled mirror or ask a helper to check. Consider periodic podiatry visits.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lotion between toes — yes or no?</p>
        <p>No. Keep that area dry to prevent fungal skin splits. Moisturize the rest of the foot.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
