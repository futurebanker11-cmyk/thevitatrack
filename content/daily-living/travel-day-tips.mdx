    <h2>Why this matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="kicker">Big wins:</span> less rushing, fewer aches, better hydration and sleep, and a smoother security/boarding experience.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common travel day snags</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rushed boarding or long security lines</li>
          <li>Missed meds or dehydration</li>
          <li>Ankle swelling, cramps, or back stiffness</li>
          <li>Confusing gate changes and heavy bags</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Book <strong>wheelchair/assistance</strong> during ticket purchase if walking long distances is hard. It speeds security and boarding.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Packing -->
  <section id="pack" class="section">
    <h2>Smart packing (carry-on first)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">In your personal item (under-seat bag)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Medications for the entire trip + 2–3 day buffer (in original/labeled containers)</li>
          <li>Medication list, IDs, tickets, insurance cards, emergency contacts</li>
          <li>Water bottle (empty for security), snacks: nuts, granola bar, crackers</li>
          <li>Light jacket or shawl, compression socks if advised, neck pillow</li>
          <li>Hand sanitizer, wipes, lip balm, small lotion</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Keep heavy items <strong>off your shoulders</strong>: use a rolling bag or backpack with chest/waist strap to protect back and balance.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds & health -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Meds, health & documents</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Set travel-day alarms for <strong>all doses</strong>. Time-critical meds: carry spares in a separate pocket.</li>
          <li>Keep a <strong>one-page health summary</strong>: conditions, allergies, meds/doses, clinician contacts.</li>
          <li>Diabetes: pack glucose meter/strips, quick sugar (glucose tabs), and snacks.</li>
          <li>CPAP/BiPAP: most airlines allow as a free medical device—carry on.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Sip water regularly; aim to use the restroom before boarding and once mid-flight or at a rest stop.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Day-of plan -->
  <section id="dayof" class="section">
    <h2>Day-of game plan (step-by-step)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Day-of travel plan">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>When</th><th>Do this</th><th>Why it helps</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Night before</strong></td>
          <td>Check-in online; pack meds, documents, snacks; lay out clothes/shoes; set alarms.</td>
          <td>Reduces morning rush and errors.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Leaving home</strong></td>
          <td>Hydrate; light meal; confirm wallet/phone/ID/tickets/med bag; book a ride with extra buffer.</td>
          <td>Prevents dehydration and forgotten essentials.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>At security</strong></td>
          <td>Use assistance lanes if available; declare medical devices/CPAP; keep meds in a clear pouch.</td>
          <td>Smoother screening, less hassle.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>At gate</strong></td>
          <td>Pre-board if eligible; stretch calves/ankles; confirm arrival transport.</td>
          <td>Better seat setup, circulation, and post-landing plan.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>In seat</strong></td>
          <td>Wipe surfaces; set phone to airplane mode + alarms; stow meds and water within reach.</td>
          <td>Hygiene and easy access.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>During trip</strong></td>
          <td>Every hour: ankle pumps x20, calf squeezes, short aisle walk when allowed; sip water; light snack.</td>
          <td>Helps swelling, cramps, and energy.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Arrival</strong></td>
          <td>Stand slowly; do 1–2 minutes of gentle stretches; confirm local time and next dose schedule.</td>
          <td>Prevents dizziness; keeps meds on track.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Security -->
  <section id="security" class="section">
    <h2>Airport & security tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Ask for <strong>wheelchair or buggy</strong> at check-in; keep boarding pass handy.</li>
          <li>Liquids rule: meds, gel packs for insulin, and formula usually permitted—declare them.</li>
          <li>Wear easy-on shoes; empty pockets into a small zip pouch before the line.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Mobility devices, pacemakers, and joint implants can affect screening—tell the officer. You can request a pat-down instead of certain scanners.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Mobility & swelling -->
  <section id="move" class="section">
    <h2>Mobility, swelling & comfort</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">In-seat moves (repeat hourly)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Ankle pumps x20, heel-toe taps x20</li>
          <li>Calf squeeze 10 sec x3 each side</li>
          <li>Shoulder rolls x10, gentle neck look left/right x5</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Comfort setup</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Lumbar support: roll a jacket at the small of your back</li>
          <li>Compression socks if your clinician advised</li>
          <li>Layer clothing; cabin temps vary</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Jet lag -->
  <section id="jetlag" class="section">
    <h2>Jet lag & time zones</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>For short trips (&lt;3–4 days): keep meds and sleep on <strong>home time</strong>.</li>
          <li>For longer trips: shift schedule by 30–60 minutes/day toward destination for 2–3 days before travel.</li>
          <li>Morning light on arrival for eastward travel; afternoon light for westward.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Ask your clinician about timing-sensitive meds (insulin, blood thinners, seizure meds) when crossing time zones.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & scams</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep valuables in a <strong>money belt/neck pouch</strong> under clothing.</li>
          <li>Use official taxi stands or verified ride apps; confirm license plate.</li>
          <li>Beware distraction scams (spills, “free bracelets”). Keep bags zipped and in front.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card danger">
        <strong>Seek urgent care</strong> for chest pain, severe breathlessness, one-sided leg swelling/redness, fainting, or confusion.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How early should I arrive?</p>
        <p>Domestic flights: ~2 hours; international: ~3 hours (more if mobility assistance, holidays, or large airports).</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What should I eat on travel day?</p>
        <p>Light, familiar foods: yogurt, fruit, nuts, sandwiches; avoid heavy, greasy, or very salty foods that worsen swelling or reflux.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I take medicines through security?</p>
        <p>Yes. Keep them in carry-on, preferably in original/labeled containers. Declare liquids/needles as medical supplies if applicable.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I prevent ankle swelling?</p>
        <p>Wear compression socks if advised, do ankle pumps hourly, walk when allowed, and stay hydrated. See a clinician for persistent one-sided swelling.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
