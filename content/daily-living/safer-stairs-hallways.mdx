    <h2>Quick fixes today</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Install <strong>motion night lights</strong> bed → hallway → bathroom.</li>
          <li>Clear clutter; <strong>remove loose rugs</strong> from landings and corridors.</li>
          <li>Place a <strong>non-slip mat</strong> at the stair base and top landing.</li>
          <li>Keep <strong>one free hand</strong> for the rail; use a small cross-body bag instead of carrying items.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">Small upgrades reduce risk immediately. Start with light, rails, contrast, and clutter.</div>
    </div>

  <!-- Lighting -->
  <section id="lighting" class="section">
    <h2>Lighting & night path</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make the path obvious</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Two-way switches</strong> top and bottom of stairs; hallway switches at each end.</li>
          <li><strong>Motion lights</strong> or plug-in night lights at skirting height; aim for even light without glare.</li>
          <li>Use <strong>bulbs 800–1100 lumens</strong> warm-white (2700–3000K) in halls; brighter (3000–4000K) for stair areas if vision is reduced.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Glare hides step edges. Choose matte finishes and shaded fixtures; clean lenses regularly.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Rails -->
  <section id="rails" class="section">
    <h2>Handrails & step edges</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Handrails</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Rails on both sides</strong>, continuous, 34–38 in (86–97 cm) height, easy-grip (round/oval).</li>
          <li>Extend rails slightly beyond the first and last step; avoid gaps at turns.</li>
          <li><strong>Secure fixings</strong> into studs or solid masonry; check wobbles monthly.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">High-contrast edges</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Apply <strong>contrasting nosing tape</strong> or paint on the <em>leading edge</em> of each step.</li>
          <li>Use <strong>tread depth ≥ 10 in (25 cm)</strong> and consistent riser height; avoid overhang lips that catch toes.</li>
          <li>Mark top and bottom steps with an extra contrast strip.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treads & flooring -->
  <section id="treads" class="section">
    <h2>Non-slip treads & flooring</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>anti-slip adhesive treads</strong> or stair carpet with <strong>firm underlay</strong> and secure nosing bars.</li>
          <li>Avoid thick, loose, or frayed carpets; fix curled edges at thresholds.</li>
          <li>Choose <strong>low-gloss</strong> finishes; shiny floors increase glare and slips.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        If stairs are narrow or steep, add extra contrast and brighter lighting; consider a second landing light.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Declutter & carry -->
  <section id="declutter" class="section">
    <h2>Declutter & carry strategy</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Keep hands free</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use a <strong>cross-body pouch</strong> or waist pack for phone, glasses, keys.</li>
          <li>Move laundry in <strong>small bags</strong> while keeping one hand on the rail.</li>
          <li>Park shoes and parcels <strong>away from landings</strong>; never store items on steps.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Weekly “5-minute hallway reset”: pick up items, coil cables, check mats, test lights.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Vision & footwear -->
  <section id="vision-footwear" class="section">
    <h2>Vision & footwear</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Update <strong>prescription</strong>; clean lenses; use single-vision distance glasses on stairs if multifocals cause missteps.</li>
          <li><strong>Footwear:</strong> firm heel counter, non-slip sole, no backless slippers; replace worn treads.</li>
          <li>Keep a spare pair by the bedroom for night trips.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Better contrast + right glasses + grippy shoes = fewer near-misses on steps.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Equipment -->
  <section id="equipment" class="section">
    <h2>Canes, walkers, & stair lifts</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Canes & walkers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Do not use wheeled walkers on stairs.</strong></li>
          <li>With a <strong>single-point cane</strong>: up = “good” leg first, down = “bad” leg first. Cane moves with the affected leg.</li>
          <li>Check cane height (wrist crease at handle) and tip wear.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Stair lifts & alternatives</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Consider if stairs limit daily life despite upgrades.</li>
          <li>Ensure <strong>backup power</strong>, clear landings, and regular servicing.</li>
          <li>Short-term option: <strong>bedroom relocation</strong> to ground floor during recovery.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Practice -->
  <section id="practice" class="section">
    <h2>Practice: stair skills & balance</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Stair pattern (with rail)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Up: <strong>stronger leg</strong> first, then the other + cane.</li>
          <li>Down: <strong>weaker leg</strong> first with cane, then the other.</li>
          <li>Step to the next step fully; avoid half-foot placements.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Balance boosters (near a counter)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Heel raises × 10; side weight shifts × 10 each; tandem stance 10–20 s each side.</li>
          <li>Rise-slow rule: sit → pause 3 breaths → stand, steady → walk.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card small">
        Related: <a href="#">Balance Breaks</a> · <a href="#">Falls (Injury Prevention)</a> · <a href="#">Night Bathroom Plan</a>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="redflags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>New falls on stairs, near-misses, or frequent tripping.</li>
        <li>Sudden vision changes, dizziness, or fainting on standing.</li>
        <li>Pain, weakness, or numbness making stairs unsafe.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Ask your clinician for a falls assessment and a home safety review.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What’s the single best upgrade?</p>
        <p>Rails on both sides plus high-contrast step edges. Lighting is a close third.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are stair carpets safe?</p>
        <p>Yes if tightly fitted with firm underlay and secure nosings. Loose or thick shag carpets increase risk.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Multifocal glasses on stairs?</p>
        <p>They can distort step depth. Use single-vision distance lenses on stairs if advised.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can exercises help quickly?</p>
        <p>Balance and calf-strength work 3–5 times daily improves control within weeks. Practice near support.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
