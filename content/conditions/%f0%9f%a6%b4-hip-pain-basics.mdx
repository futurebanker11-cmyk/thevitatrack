      <details class="section warn" open>
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">!</span> Urgent signs — act now <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p><strong>Seek urgent care</strong> for a very painful, red, hot joint with fever or chills; sudden inability to walk after a fall; new numbness/weakness; or if the skin looks infected. Hip pain with shortness of breath or chest pain is also an emergency.</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Severe pain after a twist or fall, leg looks shortened or turned out (possible fracture)</li>
            <li>Open skin, pus, or streaking redness over the hip</li>
            <li>Night fevers with deep joint pain (possible septic arthritis)</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 2 Patterns -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">1</span> What is hip pain? Common patterns & clues <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div>
              <p><strong>Hip pain</strong> can come from the joint itself or nearby tissues like bursae, tendons, and the lower back. Where you feel pain gives clues: <em>groin/front of hip</em> often points to joint arthritis; <em>outer hip</em> suggests bursitis or tendon irritation; pain that runs <em>down the leg</em> can be from the back (sciatica).</p>
              <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Common causes and clues">
                <table class="responsive">
                  <thead><tr><th>Condition</th><th>Clues</th></tr></thead>
                  <tbody>
                    <tr>
                      <td data-col="Condition">Osteoarthritis</td>
                      <td data-col="Clues">Deep ache in groin/front of thigh, stiffness after sitting, better with gentle movement</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr>
                      <td data-col="Condition">Trochanteric bursitis</td>
                      <td data-col="Clues">Tender outer hip, worse lying on that side, pain with stairs</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr>
                      <td data-col="Condition">Tendinopathy</td>
                      <td data-col="Clues">Pinpoint tendon pain, worse with certain steps or getting up</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr>
                      <td data-col="Condition">Sciatica (back)</td>
                      <td data-col="Clues">Back/buttock pain that shoots down leg, tingling or numbness</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr>
                      <td data-col="Condition">Hip fracture</td>
                      <td data-col="Clues">After fall/twist; severe pain, can’t bear weight</td>
                    </tr>
                  </tbody>
                </table>
              </div>
            </div>
            <div class="note tip">
              <strong>Balance & falls:</strong> Hip pain raises fall risk. Use a cane or walker during flares, keep pathways clear, and wear supportive shoes.
            </div>

      <!-- 3 Fast relief -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> Fast relief in first 24–48 hours <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Rest & support:</strong> Short rest periods; avoid painful steps. Use pillow between knees when lying on side.</li>
            <li><strong>Ice or heat:</strong> Ice helps sharp, hot, or after-activity pain; heat helps morning stiffness. Try 10–15 minutes at a time with a cloth barrier.</li>
            <li><strong>Medicines:</strong> Many people use anti-inflammatories, acetaminophen, or topical gels—<em>only</em> as prescribed, especially if you have kidney, heart, stomach issues, or take blood thinners.</li>
            <li><strong>Gentle motion:</strong> Pain-free range-of-motion 2–3 times a day to prevent stiffness.</li>
          </ol>

      <!-- 5 Triggers -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Common triggers & daily fixes <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Overuse or sudden increase</strong> in stairs, long walks, or hills</li>
            <li><strong>Weak glute muscles</strong> or tight hip flexors</li>
            <li><strong>Hard chairs or poor mattress</strong> that tilt the hip</li>
            <li><strong>Carrying heavy loads</strong> on one side</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 6 Exercises -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Gentle exercises (senior-safe) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Range of motion</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Seated knee lifts (march in chair), 10–15 smooth reps</li>
                <li>Hip rotations: slow circles while seated</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Strength</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Glute squeezes (buttock sets) 5–10 seconds × 10</li>
                <li>Side-lying leg lifts (light), 8–12 each side</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Balance & walk</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Short daily walks on flat ground</li>
                <li>Heel-to-toe standing at the counter for support</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
          <p class="muted">Stop any exercise that causes sharp pain, numbness, or new weakness. A physical therapist can tailor a plan for you.</p>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 7 Medicines -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Medicines (talk with your clinician) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Pain relief</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Acetaminophen may help soreness (mind total daily dose)</li>
                <li>Anti-inflammatories or topical gels—only if safe for your kidneys, stomach, heart, and medicines</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Injections & other care</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Bursa or joint steroid injections (selected cases)</li>
                <li>Physical therapy and gait training reduce future flares</li>
              </ul>
            </div>

      <!-- 8 When to see doctor -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> When to see a doctor & common tests <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Pain lasts >2–3 weeks, or keeps returning</li>
            <li>Can’t bear weight, leg gives way, or night pain wakes you</li>
            <li>Numbness, tingling, or weakness in the leg</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Typical tests">

                  <td data-col="What it shows">Soft tissue, cartilage, stress fracture</td>

      <!-- 9 Surgery -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Hip surgery basics (very short overview) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>For severe arthritis or fracture, surgery may be needed. Options include <strong>hip replacement</strong> or repair procedures. Most people benefit from early walking with a therapist, home safety checks, and fall-prevention. Your surgeon will explain risks, benefits, and recovery steps customized to you.</p>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 10 Caregiver -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">9</span> Caregiver & home safety plan <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Use a cane or walker during flares; add night lights and remove loose rugs</li>
            <li>Raised toilet seat and firm chair with armrests to reduce hip strain</li>
            <li>Keep a written plan: medicines, ice/heat instructions, who to call</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 11 Myths -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">10</span> Myths to ignore <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>“Pain means stop all movement.”</strong> Gentle motion often helps hips feel better.</li>
            <li><strong>“Heat is always best.”</strong> Ice can be better for hot, sharp flares.</li>
            <li><strong>“A cane makes me weaker.”</strong> Using support prevents falls and speeds healing.</li>

      <!-- 12 FAQs -->
      <details class="section" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/FAQPage">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">11</span> FAQs <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">When should I worry that it’s a fracture?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">After a fall with severe pain, inability to bear weight, or the leg looks shortened/turned out—treat as urgent and seek care.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Is walking good or bad for hip arthritis?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Short, regular walks on flat ground usually help stiffness and strength. Stop if pain becomes sharp or the leg feels weak.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Do I need an MRI?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Not always. Many hip issues are diagnosed with history, exam, and X-rays. MRI is used if symptoms persist or other problems are suspected.</p>
            </div>
