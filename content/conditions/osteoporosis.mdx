  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What it is</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Osteoporosis means bones are less dense and more fragile, raising the chance of hip, spine, and wrist fractures. Many people have no symptoms until a fracture happens—so screening and prevention matter.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good news:</strong> Daily habits, fall-proofing, and when appropriate, medicines can lower fracture risk substantially.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Severe new back pain with sudden height loss (possible vertebral fracture)</li>
        <li>Hip pain after a fall; inability to bear weight</li>
        <li>Wrist fracture or other fracture after a minor fall</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies. Seek immediate care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="risk" class="section">
    <h2>Risk factors</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Osteoporosis risk factors">
      <thead><tr><th>Category</th><th>Examples</th><th>Notes</th></tr></thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Age & sex</td>
          <td>Older age; postmenopausal females</td>
          <td>Bone loss accelerates after menopause</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Low body weight</td>
          <td>BMI &lt; 20; unintentional weight loss</td>
          <td>Lower reserves → higher fracture risk</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Family & prior fractures</td>
          <td>Parent hip fracture; any adult low-trauma fracture</td>
          <td>Strong predictors of future fracture</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Medicines</td>
          <td>Long-term steroids, aromatase inhibitors, some prostate/breast cancer therapy</td>
          <td>Ask about bone protection plan</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Conditions</td>
          <td>Hyperthyroidism, hyperparathyroidism, celiac, CKD, COPD, rheumatoid arthritis</td>
          <td>Some increase bone loss or falls</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Lifestyle</td>
          <td>Smoking; heavy alcohol; inactivity; low calcium/vitamin D</td>
          <td>Changeable risk—small steps help</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- Testing -->
  <section id="testing" class="section">
    <h2>Testing (DEXA, T-score, FRAX)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">DEXA scan (bone density)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Measures bone density at hip and spine</li>
          <li><strong>T-score</strong> compares to healthy young adults</li>
        </ul>
        <table class="table small" aria-label="T-score interpretation" style="margin-top:8px">
          <thead><tr><th>T-score</th><th>Interpretation</th></tr></thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr><td>≥ −1.0</td><td>Normal</td></tr>
            <tr><td>−1.0 to &gt; −2.5</td><td>Osteopenia (low bone mass)</td></tr>
            <tr><td>≤ −2.5</td><td>Osteoporosis</td></tr>
            <tr><td>≤ −2.5 + fragility fracture</td><td>Severe osteoporosis</td></tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">FRAX fracture risk</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Estimates 10-year hip & major osteoporotic fracture risk</li>
          <li>Uses age, sex, BMI, prior fracture, smoking, steroids, and optional femoral neck BMD</li>
          <li>Helps decide who benefits from medicine</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment choices</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Foundations for everyone</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Calcium & vitamin D targets (see below)</li>
          <li>Weight-bearing, resistance, and balance exercises</li>
          <li>Quit smoking; limit alcohol; review fall risks & medicines</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Antiresorptives</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Bisphosphonates:</strong> alendronate, risedronate, ibandronate, zoledronic acid</li>
          <li><strong>Denosumab</strong> (injection every 6 months)</li>
          <li>Discuss rare risks (jaw osteonecrosis, atypical femur fracture), dental check before therapy</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Anabolic/dual-action options</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Teriparatide</strong> / <strong>Abaloparatide</strong> (bone-building)</li>
          <li><strong>Romosozumab</strong> (dual action)</li>
          <li>Often for very high risk or multiple fractures</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How taken matters</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Oral bisphosphonate: morning, empty stomach, full glass of water; remain upright 30–60 min</li>
          <li>Missed denosumab doses can rebound—plan timely follow-up</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">Medicine choices are individualized. Your clinician balances benefits, risks, and convenience.</div>
  </section>

  <!-- Exercise -->
  <section id="exercise" class="section">
    <h2>Exercise & balance plan</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Weight-bearing & strength</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Walking most days; gentle stair use as tolerated</li>
          <li>2–3 sessions/week of resistance (bands, light weights, sit-to-stands)</li>
          <li>Back-extensor strengthening supports posture</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Balance & safety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Tai chi, heel-to-toe walk, single-leg stands near a counter</li>
          <li>Avoid deep forward spine flexion & twisting with heavy loads</li>
          <li>Supportive, non-slip footwear; vision and hearing checks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Calcium, vitamin D, and foods</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Targets (typical)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Calcium:</strong> ~1,000–1,200 mg/day (diet + supplements)</li>
          <li><strong>Vitamin D:</strong> commonly 800–1,000 IU/day</li>
          <li>Adjust if kidney stones, CKD, or other conditions—ask your clinician</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Food ideas</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dairy or fortified alternatives; tofu set with calcium, leafy greens</li>
          <li>Fish with soft bones (sardines/salmon), almonds, sesame (til), soy</li>
          <li>Take calcium supplements away from high-iron meals if advised</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Related: Senior-Friendly Recipes & Drinks</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Falls -->
  <section id="falls" class="section">
    <h2>Fall-proofing home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Clear clutter, cords, and loose rugs; add night lights</li>
          <li>Grab bars in bathroom; non-slip bath mats</li>
          <li>Keep commonly used items at waist height; use a reacher tool</li>
          <li>Consider hip protectors if high fall risk</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Medication review:</strong> Ask which medicines raise fall risk (sleep aids, some anxiety pills, blood pressure meds) and whether timing or doses can be adjusted.
      </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If my T-score is −2.3, do I need medicine?</p>
        <p>Maybe. In osteopenia, FRAX (10-year risk) helps decide. Higher risk or prior fracture often favors treatment—review with your clinician.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long do I take osteoporosis medicine?</p>
        <p>Varies. Some take 3–5 years then reassess (“drug holiday”) if risk is lower; others need longer or different therapy. Follow a personalized plan.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I just take calcium and vitamin D?</p>
        <p>They support bone, but by themselves don’t prevent many fractures in higher-risk adults. Exercise, fall-proofing, and medicines (when indicated) add protection.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is yoga safe?</p>
        <p>Many poses are, but avoid deep forward bends and loaded twists if you have spine osteoporosis. Choose neutral-spine, balance-focused sessions.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
