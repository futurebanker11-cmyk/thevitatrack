      <details class="section" open>
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">1</span> What is BPH? <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p><strong>Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH)</strong> is a non-cancer enlargement of the prostate that can squeeze the urethra and slow urine flow. It’s common with age and can range from mild annoyance to significant blockage.</p>
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Typical symptoms</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Slow or weak stream; starting and stopping</li>
                <li>Urgency or frequent urination (especially at night)</li>
                <li>Feeling of incomplete emptying</li>
                <li>Leaking or dribbling after finishing</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Why it matters</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Better bladder emptying improves comfort and sleep</li>
                <li>Prevents bladder strain and infections</li>
                <li>Reduces risk of sudden retention (unable to pass urine)</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 2 Red flags -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> When to seek urgent care <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="note warn">
            <ul>
              <li>Unable to urinate (acute retention)</li>
              <li>Fever, chills, or severe pelvic pain (possible infection)</li>
              <li>Blood in urine with clots or dizziness</li>
              <li>Severe back/side pain with fever (possible kidney involvement)</li>
            </ul>
            <p><strong>Emergency:</strong> If you cannot pass urine or feel very unwell, seek urgent care immediately.</p>
          </div>
        </div>

      <!-- 3 First steps -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Simple daily steps that often help <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Bathroom habits</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Double voiding: finish, relax for a moment, try again</li>
                <li>Sit to urinate if balance or flow is poor</li>
                <li>Plan regular bathroom trips; don’t hold for long periods</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Fluids & timing</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Steady fluids during the day; slow down 2–3 hours before bed</li>
                <li>Limit evening caffeine and alcohol (they can irritate the bladder)</li>
                <li>Keep bowels regular—constipation worsens symptoms</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Pelvic floor & posture</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Try relaxed breathing and gentle pelvic floor training</li>
                <li>Warmth and calm help the sphincter relax during voiding</li>
                <li>Elevate legs in late afternoon if swelling worsens night trips</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 4 How doctors evaluate -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> How clinicians evaluate BPH <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Typical office checks</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Symptom questionnaire (e.g., IPSS) and history</li>
                <li>Physical exam; sometimes a digital rectal exam (DRE)</li>
                <li>Urinalysis to rule out infection or blood</li>
                <li>PSA blood test when appropriate</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Further testing (if needed)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Bladder scan for post-void residual (how much urine is left)</li>
                <li>Ultrasound of kidneys/prostate in select cases</li>
                <li>Uroflow or cystoscopy when symptoms are severe/unclear</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 5 Medications -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Medication options (plain guidance) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Medicine choice depends on symptoms, blood pressure, other conditions, and side-effect risks. Always follow your prescriber’s advice.</p>

          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="BPH medications summary">
            <table>
              <thead>
                <tr><th>Type</th><th>How it helps</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
              </thead>
              <tbody>
                <tr>
                  <td>Alpha-blockers</td>
                  <td>Relax the prostate/urethra to improve flow</td>
                  <td>May cause dizziness, fatigue; take at night if advised</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td>5-alpha-reductase inhibitors</td>
                  <td>Shrink the prostate over months</td>
                  <td>Best for larger glands; may affect libido; monitor PSA strategy</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td>Combo therapy</td>
                  <td>Faster symptom relief + long-term shrinkage</td>
                  <td>Discuss side-effect balance and blood pressure monitoring</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td>Antimuscarinics / β3 agonists</td>
                  <td>Calm urgency/overactive bladder symptoms</td>
                  <td>Used when frequency/urgency dominate; watch for dry mouth/constipation</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td>PDE-5 inhibitor (daily)</td>
                  <td>Can improve symptoms; useful if ED is also present</td>
                  <td>Check drug interactions (e.g., nitrates)</td>
                </tr>
              </tbody>
            </table>

      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Procedures (when meds aren’t enough) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Minimally invasive options can improve flow with shorter recovery; surgery offers the most durable relief in some cases.</p>
          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="BPH procedure overview">
            <table>
              <thead>
                <tr><th>Option</th><th>What it does</th><th>Recovery notes</th></tr>
              </thead>
              <tbody>
                <tr>
                  <td>Urethral lift (e.g., implants)</td>
                  <td>Opens the channel by pulling tissue aside</td>
                  <td>Quick recovery; may have temporary burning/urgency</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td>Water vapor / thermal therapies</td>
                  <td>Shrink tissue with controlled energy</td>
                  <td>Symptom relief builds over weeks</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td>Laser/TURP</td>
                  <td>Removes obstructing tissue</td>
                  <td>Strong relief; hospital stay varies; short-term bleeding possible</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td>Prostate artery embolization</td>
                  <td>Reduces blood flow to shrink the prostate</td>
                  <td>Interventional radiology option; relief over weeks</td>
                </tr>

      <!-- 7 Catheter basics -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> Catheter basics (temporary or long-term) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Key points</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Keep drainage bag below bladder; avoid kinks</li>
                <li>Secure tubing to leg to reduce pulling</li>
                <li>Empty bag with clean technique; wash hands before/after</li>
                <li>Watch for fever, chills, pain, or cloudy/foul urine—report promptly</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Intermittent (in-and-out) catheterization</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>May be used to prevent bladder over-stretch</li>
                <li>Training ensures cleanliness and comfort</li>
                <li>Schedule individualized by your clinician</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 8 Sleep & nocturia -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Better nights (nocturia tips) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Finish most fluids earlier in the day; lighter sips after dinner</li>
            <li>Limit evening caffeine and alcohol</li>
            <li>Elevate legs 1–2 hours before bed if ankles swell</li>
            <li>Keep a safe path to the bathroom (night light, clear floor)</li>
          </ul>

        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">9</span> FAQs & next steps <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Does BPH turn into prostate cancer?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">No—BPH is not cancer and does not become cancer. Your clinician may still use PSA and exam to screen when appropriate.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Which treatment works fastest?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Alpha-blockers often improve flow within days. Medicines that shrink the prostate take months. Procedures can offer stronger relief when blockage is significant.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Can I manage BPH without medicine?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Some people do well with timed voiding, evening fluid/caffeine limits, and constipation control. If symptoms bother sleep, daily life, or bladder emptying, discuss medicine or procedures.</p>
            </div>

    closeAll && closeAll.addEventListener('click', ()=>{
      sections.forEach((d,i)=> d.open=(i===0));
      sections[0].scrollIntoView({behavior:'smooth', block:'start'});
    });
  })();
