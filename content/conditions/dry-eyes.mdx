  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is dry eye?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> The surface of the eye isn’t well-lubricated because tears are <strong>too few</strong>, <strong>evaporate too fast</strong>, or are <strong>poor quality</strong>. Symptoms include stinging, burning, grittiness, fluctuating vision, light sensitivity, and tearing that comes and goes.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Why it matters:</strong> Dry eye can blur vision, increase fall risk outdoors (glare/wind), and raise the chance of corneal infection if severe.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>Urgent warning signs</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Sudden <strong>vision loss</strong>, severe eye pain, or halos around lights</li>
        <li>Marked light sensitivity with a very red eye</li>
        <li>Eye injury, chemical exposure, or contact lens pain</li>
        <li>Yellow/green discharge or eyelid swelling with fever</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies — seek care immediately.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & triggers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Age & eyelid oil glands</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Meibomian gland dysfunction</strong> (oily layer problems → fast evaporation)</li>
          <li>Eyelid inflammation (blepharitis), rosacea</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Environment & habits</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Air-conditioning, heaters, wind, smoke</li>
          <li>Screen time with low blinking; long reading sessions</li>
          <li>CPAP air leak toward eyes</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Body conditions</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Autoimmune (e.g., Sjögren’s), thyroid disease</li>
          <li>Diabetes, vitamin A deficiency (rare), low omega-3 intake</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines that dry eyes</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Antihistamines, some antidepressants/antipsychotics</li>
          <li>Diuretics, beta-blockers, isotretinoin</li>
          <li>Glaucoma drops with preservatives (check with eye doctor)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Home care -->
  <section id="home" class="section">
    <h2>At-home care that helps</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Blink & break routine</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>20-20-20</strong>: every 20 minutes, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds and blink fully 5–10 times</li>
          <li>“<strong>Close-squeeze-open</strong>” blinks (x10) to spread tears</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Warm compress & lid hygiene</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Warm compress 5–10 min, then gently massage lids toward lash line</li>
          <li>Lid cleansing foam or diluted baby shampoo on lash margins (if advised)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Protect the surface</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Humidifier, avoid fans blowing at face, wraparound glasses outdoors</li>
          <li>Artificial tears during day; gel/ointment at night if lids don’t close fully</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Many notice improvement after <strong>2–4 weeks</strong> of daily compress + hygiene + regular drops.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Drops -->
  <section id="drops" class="section">
    <h2>Drops, gels & ointments — how to choose</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Type</th><th scope="col">When to use</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Artificial tears</strong> (low–mid viscosity)</td>
          <td>Daytime, mild–moderate symptoms</td>
          <td>Use 3–6×/day. If frequent, choose <strong>preservative-free</strong> vials.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Gel drops</strong></td>
          <td>More dryness or windy days</td>
          <td>Last longer; may blur for a minute after instilling.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Night ointment</strong></td>
          <td>Overnight dryness, incomplete lid closure</td>
          <td>Thicker; expect blur until morning.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Redness-relief vasoconstrictors</strong></td>
          <td>Generally <strong>avoid</strong></td>
          <td>Can cause rebound redness and irritation.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Tip: Chill preservative-free tears for comfort. Discard single-use vials after opening.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines that dry -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines that may worsen dryness</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Antihistamines</strong> (diphenhydramine, cetirizine), <strong>decongestants</strong></li>
        <li><strong>Antidepressants</strong> (tricyclics, some SSRIs/SNRIs), <strong>antipsychotics</strong></li>
        <li><strong>Diuretics, beta-blockers</strong>, isotretinoin</li>
        <li>Some <strong>glaucoma drops</strong> with preservatives — ask about preservative-free options</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Never change prescriptions without a clinician’s advice. A review can often improve symptoms.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>How doctors check</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Surface & tear tests</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>TBUT</strong> (tear breakup time): how quickly tears evaporate</li>
          <li><strong>Staining</strong> with special dyes to see dry spots</li>
          <li><strong>Schirmer</strong> test to measure tear production</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Oil gland & lids</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Eyelid margin exam for blepharitis</li>
          <li><strong>Meibography</strong> (imaging of oil glands) in some clinics</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Clinic treatments & prescriptions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prescription drops</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Cyclosporine</strong> or <strong>lifitegrast</strong> to reduce inflammation</li>
          <li>Short courses of <strong>steroid drops</strong> for flares (monitored)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Conserving tears</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Punctal plugs</strong> to slow tear drainage</li>
          <li>Moisture chamber glasses; taping lids at night if they don’t close</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Oil gland therapies</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>In-office <strong>thermal pulsation</strong>, <strong>IPL</strong> (intense pulsed light)</li>
          <li>Lid debridement of thickened oils</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Advanced options</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Scleral lenses</strong> to protect the cornea in severe cases</li>
          <li><strong>Autologous serum tears</strong> under specialist care</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Most plans combine daily home routine + the right drops; clinic options are added if symptoms persist.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- When to call -->
  <section id="when-to-call" class="section">
    <h2>When to contact your eye doctor</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Pain, light sensitivity, or vision changes</li>
        <li>No improvement after 2–4 weeks of routine and drops</li>
        <li>Frequent tearing, burning, or mucus despite care</li>
        <li>History of autoimmune disease, eyelid problems, or prior corneal issues</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are preservative-free drops worth it?</p>
        <p>Yes if you use drops more than 4 times daily or feel stinging — they’re gentler on the surface.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do omega-3s help?</p>
        <p>Some people report benefit, but results vary. Discuss supplements and medicine interactions first.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best way to use ointment?</p>
        <p>At bedtime. A rice-grain amount inside the lower lid; expect blur until morning.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can CPAP worsen dry eye?</p>
        <p>Yes if air leaks toward the eyes. Ask about mask fit or eye shields; add bedtime gel.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
