  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What counts as insomnia?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Sleep difficulty at least 3 nights per week for 3 months or more, with daytime impact (fatigue, concentration, mood, balance). Short-term insomnia can follow stress or illness; chronic insomnia often needs structured therapy.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Why it matters:</strong> Poor sleep worsens memory, blood pressure, blood sugar, and fall risk. Good sleep protects mood, immunity, and heart health.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent care</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>New chest pain, severe shortness of breath at night</li>
        <li>Confusion, new weakness, fainting, or falls</li>
        <li>Severe depression, suicidal thoughts, or hallucinations</li>
        <li>Dangerous sleep behaviors (e.g., leaving home, cooking while asleep)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may signal medical emergencies or unsafe sleep disorders.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes in older adults</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medical & mental health</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pain (arthritis, neuropathy), breathlessness (COPD/CHF), reflux</li>
          <li>Depression, anxiety, grief, loneliness</li>
          <li>Thyroid issues, nocturia (BPH/overactive bladder)</li>
          <li>Sleep apnea, restless legs, periodic limb movements</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lifestyle & environment</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Late caffeine, alcohol at night, heavy evening meals</li>
          <li>Irregular sleep schedule, long daytime naps</li>
          <li>Noise, light, room temperature, uncomfortable bedding</li>
          <li>Screen time in the hour before bed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Sleep hygiene -->
  <section id="sleep-hygiene" class="section">
    <h2>Sleep hygiene that actually works</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Timing rules</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fixed wake-up time daily (set an alarm—even on weekends)</li>
          <li>Bedtime only when sleepy; if awake >20 minutes, get up to a dim-light calm activity, then return to bed when sleepy</li>
          <li>Naps: keep to ≤20–30 minutes and before 2 pm</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Evening habits</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stop caffeine after mid-afternoon; limit alcohol (disrupts sleep later)</li>
          <li>Light snack if hungry; avoid large or spicy meals within 3 hours of bed</li>
          <li>Screen “sunset”: dim lights and screens 60 minutes before bed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Bedroom environment</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cool, dark, quiet room; consider eye mask/earplugs/white-noise</li>
          <li>Comfortable mattress/pillows; clear paths and nightlights for safety</li>
          <li>Use the bed only for sleep and intimacy (no TV or phone scrolling)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Build a short pre-sleep routine: warm shower, gentle stretches, slow breathing (inhale 4, exhale 6), then lights out.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Daytime -->
  <section id="daytime" class="section">
    <h2>Daytime habits that improve sleep</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Light & movement</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Morning daylight for 15–30 minutes anchors the body clock</li>
          <li>Daily activity: a walk after breakfast or lunch helps nighttime sleep</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fluids & bathroom</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hydrate earlier; taper fluids 2–3 hours before bed</li>
          <li>Limit evening caffeine and diuretics; review bladder meds with your clinician</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- CBT-I -->
  <section id="cbti" class="section">
    <h2>CBT-I (gold standard)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What it is</p>
        <p>Structured program (usually 4–8 weeks) that uses stimulus control, sleep restriction, relaxation, and cognitive skills to retrain sleep.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Why it’s first-line</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Works as well as or better than medicines—benefits last</li>
          <li>Safe for older adults; no morning grogginess or falls from sedation</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Ask for a CBT-I referral or a reputable digital CBT-I program if a local therapist isn’t available.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines: safer choices & cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Often preferred (discuss with your clinician)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Doxepin (low-dose)</strong> for sleep maintenance</li>
          <li><strong>Melatonin</strong> 1–3 mg 1–2 hours before bed for timing issues</li>
          <li><strong>Ramelteon</strong> (melatonin-receptor agonist)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Use with caution or avoid</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Benzodiazepines and “Z-drugs” (falls, confusion, dependence)</li>
          <li>Diphenhydramine/“PM” products (anticholinergic side effects)</li>
          <li>Alcohol to sleep—fragments sleep and raises fall risk</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Medicines are most helpful short-term while CBT-I and habits take hold. Review interactions (e.g., other sedatives, opioids, alcohol).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Screen -->
  <section id="screen" class="section">
    <h2>What to screen for</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sleep disorders</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Obstructive sleep apnea (snoring, pauses, waking gasping)</li>
          <li>Restless legs/limb movements (urge to move, evening worse)</li>
          <li>REM behavior disorder (acting out dreams)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medical triggers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Thyroid imbalance, pain, reflux, COPD/CHF</li>
          <li>Nocturia from BPH/overactive bladder; diuretic timing</li>
          <li>Medicines: steroids, stimulants, late-day caffeine, decongestants</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Routines -->
  <section id="routines" class="section">
    <h2>Wind-down & bedroom setup</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">30–60 minutes before bed</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dim lights → warm shower → gentle stretches → breathing 4-6</li>
          <li>Write tomorrow’s to-dos to quiet mental chatter</li>
          <li>Set phone on Do Not Disturb; charge outside the bedroom if possible</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Bed safety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Nightlights along the path to the bathroom</li>
          <li>Clear floors; supportive shoes if you rise at night</li>
          <li>Keep water and glasses within reach to avoid searching in the dark</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How much sleep do older adults need?</p>
        <p>Most do well with 7–8 hours. Quality and consistency matter more than exact number.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is melatonin safe?</p>
        <p>Often well-tolerated at low doses (1–3 mg). Higher doses can cause grogginess—start low and review with your clinician.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Why do I wake at 3 am?</p>
        <p>Common with early bedtimes, stress, pain, reflux, or sleep apnea. Use the 20-minute rule: quiet activity in dim light until sleepy again.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When to see a sleep specialist?</p>
        <p>Snoring with pauses, acting out dreams, persistent insomnia despite CBT-I, or if medicines are needed beyond short-term.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
