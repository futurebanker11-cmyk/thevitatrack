  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is IBS?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> IBS is a long-term pattern of <strong>recurrent abdominal pain</strong> related to bowel movements plus changes in stool frequency or form. It’s a disorder of <strong>gut–brain interaction</strong>, not damage—symptoms are real and treatable.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good to know:</strong> Many older adults labeled with “IBS for years” benefit from a fresh review for anemia, celiac disease, inflammatory bowel disease, medication side effects, and bile acid problems—especially if symptoms changed recently.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Subtypes -->
  <section id="subtypes" class="section">
    <h2>IBS subtypes</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="IBS subtypes">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Subtype</th><th>Stool pattern (Rome-style)</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>IBS-C</strong> (constipation-predominant)</td>
          <td>Hard/lumpy stools ≥25% of BMs, loose <25%</td>
          <td>Ensure fiber and fluids; avoid constipating meds; consider osmotic laxatives</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>IBS-D</strong> (diarrhea-predominant)</td>
          <td>Loose/watery stools ≥25% of BMs, hard <25%</td>
          <td>Rule out bile acid diarrhea, lactose/fructose malabsorption; consider loperamide or rifaximin</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>IBS-M</strong> (mixed)</td>
          <td>Both hard and loose stools ≥25%</td>
          <td>Target pain + regulate rhythm; gentle fiber, peppermint oil, mind–gut tools</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>IBS-U</strong></td>
          <td>Doesn’t neatly fit above</td>
          <td>Track diary to define pattern; personalize plan</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent care</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Rectal bleeding or black stools</li>
        <li>Unintentional weight loss, fever, or night sweats</li>
        <li>New anemia, vomiting, or persistent severe pain</li>
        <li>Sudden change in bowel habit after age 60</li>
        <li>Family history of colon cancer, IBD, or celiac disease</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These signs are not typical for IBS and need urgent evaluation.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms & patterns</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cramping belly pain that eases after a bowel movement</li>
          <li>Bloating, gas, sense of incomplete emptying</li>
          <li>Alternating constipation and diarrhea (IBS-M)</li>
          <li>Mucus in stool without blood</li>
          <li>Worse with stress, certain foods, or poor sleep</li>
        </ul>

  <!-- Triggers -->
  <section id="triggers" class="section">
    <h2>Typical triggers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Food & drink</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>High-FODMAP foods: onions/garlic, beans, apples, pears, wheat, certain sweeteners (sorbitol)</li>
          <li>Fatty or spicy meals; very large portions</li>
          <li>Caffeine, alcohol, and fizzy drinks</li>
          <li>Lactose (if intolerant); excess fructose</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Other</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stress, anxiety, poor sleep</li>
          <li>Recent gut infection or antibiotics</li>
          <li>Medications: iron pills, opioids, anticholinergics (constipation); metformin, magnesium (diarrhea)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Rule out -->
  <section id="ruleout" class="section">
    <h2>Ruling out other conditions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Basic evaluation</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>History, exam, and medication review</li>
          <li>Blood tests: CBC (anemia), CRP, thyroid, celiac screen if diarrhea</li>
          <li>Stool tests in diarrhea (calprotectin for inflammation, infectious panel as needed)</li>
          <li>Colonoscopy based on age/screening schedule or alarm features</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>IBS vs IBD:</strong> IBS causes symptoms without gut damage; IBD (Crohn’s/UC) causes inflammation, bleeding, fever, and weight loss. Tests help tell them apart.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet & fiber -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Diet & fiber (how to do it safely)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Low-FODMAP—simple start</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Try a <strong>4–6 week</strong> trial with a dietitian or reliable guide</li>
          <li>Limit high-FODMAP foods; choose low-FODMAP swaps (e.g., oats, rice, potatoes, carrots, berries, lactose-free dairy)</li>
          <li>Re-introduce foods slowly to find your personal triggers</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fiber tips (senior-safe)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Psyllium husk</strong> (soluble) can help pain and stool form—start small (½–1 tsp/day) and increase weekly with fluids</li>
          <li>Avoid large sudden increases—bloating can worsen</li>
          <li>For IBS-D, soluble fiber may thicken stools; avoid wheat bran (can irritate)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Hydration & meals</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular small meals; don’t skip breakfast (gastro-colic reflex helps rhythm)</li>
          <li>Water first; limit fizzy drinks and late caffeine</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Peppermint oil</strong> enteric-coated capsules may ease cramping for some; check interactions (e.g., with antacids).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines: options & cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">For pain & cramps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Antispasmodics (e.g., dicyclomine) for short-term cramps—watch for dry mouth, constipation, confusion in seniors</li>
          <li>Peppermint oil (enteric-coated)</li>
          <li>Low-dose <strong>TCAs</strong> (e.g., amitriptyline) for pain-predominant IBS—monitor anticholinergic effects and falls</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">For IBS-C</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Osmotic laxatives (PEG) first-line; titrate slowly</li>
          <li>Secretagogues (linaclotide, plecanatide) if needed—watch dehydration</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">For IBS-D</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Loperamide for urgency/loose stools (symptom control)</li>
          <li><strong>Rifaximin</strong> course for some with bloating/diarrhea</li>
          <li>Bile acid binders if bile acid diarrhea suspected (discuss timing with other meds)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Avoid routine opioids for IBS pain—they worsen constipation, tolerance, and sensitivity. Review all medicines with your clinician for side effects on bowel habits.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Mind–gut -->
  <section id="mind-gut" class="section">
    <h2>Mind–gut tools</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Stress & sleep</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>10–15 minutes daily of paced breathing, gentle yoga, or tai chi</li>
          <li>Set a regular sleep–wake time; reduce late caffeine</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Therapies</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Gut-directed hypnotherapy or CBT can reduce pain and urgency</li>
          <li>Brief programs (6–8 weeks) help build durable skills</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Pelvic floor -->
  <section id="pelvic" class="section">
    <h2>Pelvic floor & constipation</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Clues</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Straining, prolonged time on toilet, feeling of blockage</li>
          <li>Need for manual support to pass stool</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Help</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Referral for pelvic floor physical therapy/biofeedback</li>
          <li>Toilet posture: feet on a small stool, lean forward, relax belly</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Flare plan -->
  <section id="flare" class="section">
    <h2>Your flare plan (stepwise)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Step 1 — settle the gut</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Switch to <strong>low-FODMAP bland</strong> choices for 24–48 hours (rice, oats, eggs, banana, chicken, carrots)</li>
          <li>Small sips of water or oral rehydration; avoid fizzy drinks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Step 2 — symptom relief</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>IBS-D: loperamide for urgency (per label)</li>
          <li>IBS-C: gentle PEG as discussed with clinician</li>
          <li>Cramps: peppermint oil or prescribed antispasmodic</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Step 3 — reset & track</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Note food, stress, sleep, and medicines in a 1-week diary</li>
          <li>Re-introduce usual meals gradually; keep effective habits</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card small">
        If severe pain, fever, bleeding, black stools, persistent vomiting, or dehydration → seek urgent care.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is IBS dangerous?</p>
        <p>IBS affects quality of life but doesn’t damage the bowel. New red-flag symptoms need medical review.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do probiotics help?</p>
        <p>Some people improve with specific strains; results vary. Try one product for 4–6 weeks and stop if no benefit.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can IBS start later in life?</p>
        <p>Yes, but late onset requires careful evaluation to rule out other causes before confirming IBS.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does exercise help?</p>
        <p>Regular gentle activity supports bowel rhythm and stress control—aim for most days of the week.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
