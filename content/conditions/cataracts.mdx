  <!-- What are cataracts -->
  <section id="what-are-cataracts" class="section">
    <h2>What are cataracts?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> A cataract is a <strong>clouding of the eye’s natural lens</strong>, usually from age-related changes. Vision becomes blurry or dim, colors fade, and lights may glare — especially at night. The fix, when vision interferes with daily life, is a brief surgery replacing the cloudy lens with a clear artificial lens.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good to know:</strong> Cataracts are very common and highly treatable. Glasses, brighter lighting, and anti-glare steps can help until surgery is appropriate.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red-flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Sudden severe eye pain, <strong>halo with red eye</strong>, or vomiting (possible acute glaucoma)</li>
        <li><strong>Sudden vision loss</strong>, curtain or shadow over vision (possible retinal detachment)</li>
        <li>Eye injury with vision change</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These are different from typical cataract symptoms and need urgent eye care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Blurred, hazy, or “film” over vision</li>
          <li>Glare and halos around lights; worse night driving</li>
          <li>Faded colors; frequent glasses prescription changes</li>
          <li>Needing brighter light to read; difficulty with contrast</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Risk factors -->
  <section id="risks" class="section">
    <h2>Risk factors & prevention</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Older age (most common), family history</li>
          <li>Diabetes, smoking, long-term steroid use</li>
          <li>Excess UV light exposure without eye protection</li>
          <li>Eye injury, past eye surgery</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prevention basics</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Wear UV-blocking sunglasses and a brimmed hat outdoors</li>
          <li>Don’t smoke; manage blood sugar and blood pressure</li>
          <li>Use protective eyewear during yard work and DIY projects</li>
          <li>Keep regular eye exams to track changes</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diagnosis -->
  <section id="diagnosis" class="section">
    <h2>How cataracts are diagnosed</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Vision testing (visual acuity, glare, contrast)</li>
        <li>Slit-lamp exam to see lens clouding and type (nuclear, cortical, posterior subcapsular)</li>
        <li>Refraction check (glasses prescription)</li>
        <li>If surgery planned: eye measurements for lens power (biometry), discussion of other eye conditions (macula, glaucoma)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Diagnosis is clinical — no blood tests are needed for cataracts.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Non-surgical now</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Brighter task lighting, high-contrast materials, large print</li>
          <li>Anti-glare glasses; up-to-date prescription</li>
          <li>Night driving limits if glare is severe</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When to consider surgery</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Vision interferes with reading, driving, or daily tasks</li>
          <li>Glare/halos limit night driving or outdoor activities</li>
          <li>Eye exam shows cataract consistent with symptoms</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small note" style="margin-top:12px">Surgery removes the cloudy lens and places a clear intraocular lens (IOL). It’s usually outpatient with quick recovery.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Lens options -->
  <section id="lenses" class="section">
    <h2>Lens options (IOLs)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Lens type</th><th scope="col">What it offers</th><th scope="col">Considerations</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Monofocal</strong></td>
          <td>Sharp focus at one distance (usually distance)</td>
          <td>Most common and often clearest contrast; reading glasses typically needed</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Toric</strong></td>
          <td>Corrects astigmatism</td>
          <td>Good for those with significant corneal astigmatism</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Extended depth of focus (EDOF)</strong></td>
          <td>Improved range (distance → intermediate)</td>
          <td>May still need readers; fewer halos than multifocal for many</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Multifocal</strong></td>
          <td>Distance and near without glasses for some</td>
          <td>Possible halos/glare; contrast can be lower; careful selection needed</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Monovision (strategy)</strong></td>
          <td>One eye distance, other near</td>
          <td>Try with contact lens trial first if possible to see tolerance</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Recovery -->
  <section id="recovery" class="section">
    <h2>Recovery & timeline</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">First 24–72 hours</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Mild scratchy sensation or light sensitivity is common</li>
          <li>Use prescribed drops (antibiotic/anti-inflammatory) exactly as directed</li>
          <li>Shield at night; avoid rubbing the eye</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">First 1–4 weeks</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Vision usually sharpens over days; colors look brighter</li>
          <li>Light activity is fine; avoid heavy lifting and dirty water in the eye</li>
          <li>Final glasses prescription often at 3–4 weeks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small calm" style="margin-top:10px">Call your surgeon if you notice worsening pain, sudden drop in vision, new floaters/flashes, or increasing redness.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily vision & driving -->
  <section id="daily-vision" class="section">
    <h2>Daily vision tips & driving</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make things easier to see</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use bright, even lighting; add task lamps for reading</li>
          <li>Increase contrast: dark placemats under light dishes, bold markers</li>
          <li>Large-print phone settings and high-contrast mode</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Driving safety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Avoid night driving if glare is troublesome</li>
          <li>Clean windshields and lenses; consider anti-glare coatings</li>
          <li>Plan surgery earlier if driving becomes unsafe</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="small" style="margin-top:8px">

  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions for your eye surgeon</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>How much of my vision trouble is due to cataract vs. other eye issues?</li>
        <li>Do I meet the threshold where surgery will meaningfully improve daily tasks?</li>
        <li>Which lens type fits my eyes and hobbies? (monofocal, toric, EDOF, multifocal)</li>
        <li>What results should I expect with/without glasses after surgery?</li>
        <li>What are the drop schedule and activity limits after surgery?</li>
        <li>What are the risks and how often do complications happen here?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do cataract drops dissolve cataracts?</p>
        <p>No. There are no proven drops that reverse a cataract. Surgery is the definitive fix when needed.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can cataracts come back?</p>
        <p>The removed lens does not return. A cloudy <em>posterior capsule</em> can develop months–years later and is treated with a brief laser (YAG capsulotomy).</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will I still need glasses?</p>
        <p>Often yes for some tasks, depending on lens choice and your eyes. Monofocal lenses typically need readers; EDOF/multifocal reduce glasses for some.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long is the surgery?</p>
        <p>The procedure itself is usually brief. Most people go home the same day and notice clearer vision within days.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
