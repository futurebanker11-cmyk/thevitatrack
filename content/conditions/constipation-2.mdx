  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What constipation means</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Constipation is infrequent, difficult, or incomplete bowel movements. For seniors, it’s often due to <strong>low fiber/fluids, less activity, or medicines</strong>. New or worsening constipation deserves attention—especially with warning signs below.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Normal varies:</strong> anywhere from 3 stools/day to 3/week can be normal if easy to pass. Focus on <em>effort, comfort, and completeness</em> more than exact frequency.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Severe belly pain, swollen/firm abdomen, <strong>no gas or stool</strong> (possible obstruction)</li>
        <li>Vomiting with constipation, or fever/chills with severe pain</li>
        <li>Black/tarry stools or <strong>bright-red blood</strong> in stool</li>
        <li>Unintentional weight loss, persistent pencil-thin stools, or anemia</li>
        <li>New constipation after age 50 without clear cause</li>
        <li>Recent start of <strong>opioids</strong> with severe symptoms despite treatment</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can signal blockage, bleeding, infection, or other urgent problems.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Common causes of constipation">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Category</th><th scope="col">Examples</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Lifestyle</strong></td>
          <td>Low fiber, low fluids, less mobility/bedrest, ignoring urge, travel</td>
          <td>Very common in older adults; often multi-factor</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medicines</strong></td>
          <td>Opioids, iron, calcium supplements, anticholinergics, some antidepressants, calcium-channel blockers, antacids with aluminum</td>
          <td>Review list with clinician; adjust safely</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medical conditions</strong></td>
          <td>Diabetes, hypothyroidism, Parkinson’s, stroke, kidney disease</td>
          <td>May slow gut movement or reduce activity</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Structural/functional</strong></td>
          <td>Colon strictures, pelvic floor dyssynergia, rectal prolapse, hemorrhoids/fissures (pain → withholding)</td>
          <td>Pelvic floor therapy can help outlet problems</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Relief steps at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">First 24–48 hours (no red-flags)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Fluids:</strong> aim for 6–8 cups/day unless fluid-restricted</li>
          <li><strong>Fiber:</strong> add gradually (oats, fruits, veggies, beans) to target 20–30g/day</li>
          <li><strong>Movement:</strong> 10–15 min walks 2–3×/day; gentle core stretches</li>
          <li><strong>Toilet timing:</strong> sit after breakfast or warm drink; feet on a small <em>stool</em> to straighten the rectum</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">If still uncomfortable</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Osmotic laxative</strong> (PEG 3350) is often gentle and effective</li>
          <li>Consider <strong>stool softener</strong> (docusate) if stools are hard</li>
          <li>Avoid frequent use of strong <strong>stimulant</strong> laxatives unless advised</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small">This page is educational and not a diagnosis. Seek urgent care for warning signs.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Laxatives & medicine cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Laxative options overview">
          <thead>
            <tr><th scope="col">Type</th><th scope="col">What it does</th><th scope="col">Notes for seniors</th></tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Osmotic</strong> (PEG 3350, lactulose)</td>
              <td>Draws water into stool, softens</td>
              <td>Often first-line; start low, increase slowly</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Bulk fiber</strong> (psyllium, methylcellulose)</td>
              <td>Adds bulk, improves form</td>
              <td>Requires fluids; may bloat—titrate</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Stimulant</strong> (senna, bisacodyl)</td>
              <td>Speeds colon activity</td>
              <td>Useful short-term or for opioid-induced; avoid daily long-term without advice</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Stool softener</strong> (docusate)</td>
              <td>Softens stool</td>
              <td>Helps if hard/dry; combine with fiber/PEG</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Suppositories/enemas</strong></td>
              <td>Rectal stimulation/softening</td>
              <td>Glycerin or bisacodyl suppository for outlet delay; <strong>avoid sodium phosphate enemas</strong> in kidney/heart disease—ask first</td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Medicine check:</strong> Opioids, iron, some antacids, antidepressants, and blood-pressure meds can cause constipation. Do <em>not</em> stop prescriptions on your own—ask about safer alternatives or bowel regimens.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet & routine -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Fiber, fluids & routine</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Helpful daily habits</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Breakfast + warm drink → bathroom time (train the bowel)</li>
          <li>Fiber targets: oats, berries, prunes/kiwi, veggies, beans, chia/flax</li>
          <li>Stay active: walking, light stretching, pelvic floor relaxation</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Limit/avoid</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dehydration, heavy cheese & processed meats, excess alcohol</li>
          <li>Overuse of stimulant laxatives without guidance</li>
          <li>Ignoring the urge to go (tightens the outlet over time)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How often should I have a bowel movement?</p>
        <p>Anything from three times a day to three times a week can be normal if stools are soft and easy to pass.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What is the safest first step?</p>
        <p>Increase fluids and fiber gradually, add regular toilet timing and walking, and consider a gentle osmotic laxative like PEG if needed.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When should I get checked?</p>
        <p>Any red-flags, new constipation after age 50, or symptoms that persist despite home measures deserve evaluation.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do probiotics help?</p>
        <p>Some people find benefit, but results vary. Diet, fluids, activity, and a simple bowel regimen are usually more reliable.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
