  <!-- What is BPH -->
  <section id="what-is-bph" class="section">
    <h2>What is BPH?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> BPH is a non-cancerous growth of the prostate that can narrow the urethra and affect bladder outflow. It can cause slow stream, frequent trips (especially at night), urgency, and a feeling of incomplete emptying.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Key idea:</strong> Many men improve with simple habits or medicines; others benefit from minimally invasive procedures or surgery if symptoms remain bothersome or complications develop.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Inability to urinate (acute urinary retention)</li>
        <li>Fever, chills, burning urine with back/side pain (possible infection)</li>
        <li>Blood in urine with clots or severe pain</li>
        <li>Severe lower abdominal pain with a full bladder</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies—seek urgent care or local emergency services.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Weak or interrupted urine stream; straining to start</li>
          <li>Frequent urination; waking at night (nocturia)</li>
          <li>Urgency or leakage if you can’t get to a toilet in time</li>
          <li>Dribbling at the end of urination</li>
          <li>Feeling of incomplete emptying</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Causes & risks -->
  <section id="causes-risks" class="section">
    <h2>Causes & risk factors</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Likely contributors</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Age-related hormonal changes leading to prostate growth</li>
          <li>Family history of prostate enlargement</li>
          <li>Metabolic factors (diabetes, obesity), inactivity</li>
          <li>Medications that tighten the prostate/bladder neck (some decongestants) or increase urine (diuretics)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Possible complications</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Urinary retention or recurrent urinary infections</li>
          <li>Bladder stones or bleeding</li>
          <li>Back-pressure on kidneys if severe and prolonged</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Assessment -->
  <section id="assessment" class="section">
    <h2>How clinicians assess BPH</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Symptom score (e.g., IPSS) to rate severity and track progress</li>
        <li>Focused exam; sometimes a digital rectal exam (DRE)</li>
        <li>Urinalysis to check for infection or blood</li>
        <li>Blood tests when indicated (e.g., kidney function; PSA based on shared decision-making)</li>
        <li>Ultrasound or bladder scan for post-void residual (PVR) if emptying is a concern</li>
        <li>Cystoscopy or imaging only in selected cases (e.g., persistent blood in urine, recurrent infections, poor response)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">The plan is matched to your goals, symptom score, and any complications.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>

    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Daily strategies (often first step)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Limit fluids 2–3 hours before bed; avoid late caffeine and alcohol</li>
          <li>“Double void” before outings and bedtime (urinate, relax, try again)</li>
          <li>Take time; don’t strain. Sit to void if dizzy or unsteady</li>
          <li>Review medicines that may worsen symptoms (with your clinician)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Medicines</p>
        <table class="table" role="table">
          <thead>
            <tr><th scope="col">Class (example)</th><th scope="col">What it does</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Alpha-blockers</strong> (tamsulosin, alfuzosin)</td>
              <td>Relax prostate/bladder neck to improve flow</td>
              <td>Can cause dizziness/low BP or ejaculatory changes; often work quickly</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>5-alpha-reductase inhibitors</strong> (finasteride, dutasteride)</td>
              <td>Shrink prostate over months</td>
              <td>Best for larger prostates; possible sexual side effects</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Combo therapy</strong></td>
              <td>Flow + shrinkage</td>
              <td>Used when symptoms are moderate–severe with large prostate</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>PDE5 inhibitor</strong> (tadalafil)</td>
              <td>May help urinary symptoms and erectile function</td>
              <td>Not for some heart medicines—review interactions</td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Office procedures</p>
        <table class="table" role="table">
          <thead>
            <tr><th scope="col">Option</th><th scope="col">Idea</th><th scope="col">Typical recovery</th></tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Prostatic urethral lift</strong> (e.g., UroLift)</td>
              <td>Small implants hold tissue away from urethra</td>
              <td>Short recovery; minimal sexual side effects</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Water-vapor therapy</strong> (e.g., Rezūm)</td>
              <td>Steam ablates prostate tissue</td>
              <td>Symptom relief builds over weeks</td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">4) Surgery</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>TURP</strong> (transurethral resection), <strong>HoLEP</strong> (laser enucleation), or other approaches for severe BPH</li>
          <li>Considered for retention, recurrent infections, bladder stones, bleeding, or kidney impact</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <p class="small note" style="margin-top:12px">Treatment is individualized. Review benefits, side effects, and procedure risks with your clinician.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily care -->
  <section id="daily-care" class="section">
    <h2>Daily care & bladder habits</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Better nights</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Finish most fluids by late afternoon; sip lightly in the evening</li>
          <li>Limit evening caffeine, alcohol, and very salty foods</li>
          <li>Empty before bedtime; keep a night-light and clear path to the bathroom</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Comfort & safety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stand slowly to reduce dizziness (some medicines can lower BP)</li>
          <li>Plan bathroom breaks on trips; locate facilities ahead of time</li>
          <li>Talk about pelvic-floor strategies if urgency is a problem</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Track & review</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Note daytime and nighttime frequency for a week before visits</li>
          <li>Track triggers (drinks, timing, medicines)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Small changes often reduce symptoms. If things worsen or you can’t pass urine, seek care quickly.
      </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>BPH — quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does BPH lead to prostate cancer?</p>
        <p>No. BPH is non-cancerous. Both can occur in the same person, so screening discussions may still be relevant.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Why do symptoms worsen at night?</p>
        <p>Fluid shifts when lying down increase urine production; evening drinks, caffeine, and alcohol also add to nighttime trips.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How fast do medicines help?</p>
        <p>Alpha-blockers often help within days; 5-alpha-reductase inhibitors can take months and work best with larger prostates.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I stop meds after a procedure?</p>
        <p>Often yes if symptoms are controlled; confirm with your clinician after recovery.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
