  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is seasonal influenza?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> A contagious viral infection that attacks the nose, throat, and lungs. In older adults it can progress quickly to pneumonia, worsen heart failure or COPD, and increase stroke or heart attack risk in the weeks after infection.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Key window:</strong> Antiviral medicines work best if started within the first <strong>48 hours</strong> of symptoms—earlier is better.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Typical symptoms in older adults</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sudden fever or feeling feverish/chills (fever may be mild/absent in seniors)</li>
          <li>Dry cough, sore throat, runny/stuffy nose</li>
          <li>Headache, muscle or body aches, extreme tiredness</li>
          <li>Shortness of breath, chest tightness, wheeze (especially with COPD/asthma)</li>
          <li>Confusion, dizziness, poor appetite, or falls (common atypical signs)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Related topics</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><a href="/symptoms/shortness-of-breath">Shortness of Breath</a> · <a href="/conditions/chronic-obstructive-pulmonary-disease-copd">COPD</a> · <a href="/conditions/congestive-heart-failure-chf">Heart Failure (CHF)</a></li>
          <li>Dehydration · <a href="/conditions/falls-injury-prevention">Falls (Injury Prevention)</a></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent care</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Blue/gray lips, severe shortness of breath, chest pain, or new confusion</li>
        <li>High fever that won’t settle, repeated vomiting, inability to drink/pee</li>
        <li>Symptoms improving then suddenly worse (secondary pneumonia)</li>
        <li>Worsening COPD/asthma or heart failure signs (swelling, breathlessness)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Call your local emergency number if these occur.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Risks -->
  <section id="risks" class="section">
    <h2>Who is at higher risk</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Age ≥65, frailty, nursing-home residence</li>
          <li>Heart or lung disease (CHF, CAD, COPD, asthma)</li>
          <li>Diabetes, kidney disease, neurological conditions, obesity</li>
          <li>Weakened immunity or cancer treatment</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        If you’re high risk and develop flu-like illness, contact your clinician as soon as possible—don’t wait for severe symptoms.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Testing -->
  <section id="testing" class="section">
    <h2>Testing: when and how</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When to test</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Within the first 1–2 days of symptoms (or anytime for those very ill/hospitalized)</li>
          <li>During local outbreaks or flu season with typical symptoms</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How testing is done</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rapid antigen or PCR swab from nose/throat</li>
          <li>Some clinics test for both <strong>flu & COVID</strong> at once</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment (antivirals)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Start early</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Antivirals work best if started <strong>within 48 hours</strong> of symptoms—may still help later in severe or high-risk cases</li>
          <li>Common options: oseltamivir (pill), baloxavir (single dose), or inhaled zanamivir (not for severe lung disease)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Safety & adjustments</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Kidney disease often requires dose changes—review with your clinician</li>
          <li>Watch for nausea or neuropsychiatric effects; report unusual symptoms</li>
          <li>Continue usual heart, lung, and diabetes medicines unless told otherwise</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">Antibiotics don’t treat flu, but may be needed if bacterial pneumonia is suspected.</div>
  </section>

  <!-- Home care -->
  <section id="homecare" class="section">
    <h2>Home care & hydration</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fluids & fever</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Small frequent sips; oral rehydration or broths if appetite is low</li>
          <li>Acetaminophen for fever/aches if approved for you; avoid duplicate products</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Breathing & rest</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Head-of-bed elevation, paced breathing; use prescribed inhalers correctly</li>
          <li>Light activity between rests to reduce deconditioning and clots risk</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reduce spread</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stay home until fever-free for 24 hours without fever-reducers</li>
          <li>Mask around others, open windows, clean high-touch surfaces</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Vaccines & prevention</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best timing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Get vaccinated each season—ideally before local circulation increases</li>
          <li>Protection builds over ~2 weeks after the shot</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Vaccine types for 65+</p>
        <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Vaccine options for older adults">
          <thead>
            <tr><th>Type</th><th>What’s different</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>High-dose inactivated</strong></td>
              <td>More antigen</td>
              <td>Designed to produce a stronger immune response in seniors</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Adjuvanted inactivated</strong></td>
              <td>Immune booster (adjuvant)</td>
              <td>Another good option for ≥65</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td>Standard-dose</td>
              <td>Regular antigen amount</td>
              <td>Use if high-dose/adjuvanted not available—don’t delay vaccination</td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Around the home</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Caregivers and family members should be vaccinated</li>
          <li>Wash hands, cover coughs, and ventilate rooms</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Flu vs others -->
  <section id="covid-cold" class="section">
    <h2>Flu vs. cold vs. COVID</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Feature</th><th>Flu</th><th>Common cold</th><th>COVID-19</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr><td>Onset</td><td>Sudden</td><td>Gradual</td><td>Varies</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Fever/aches</td><td>Common, prominent</td><td>Mild</td><td>Common</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Cough</td><td>Dry, bothersome</td><td>Mild–moderate</td><td>Common</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Taste/smell loss</td><td>Uncommon</td><td>Uncommon</td><td>More common</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Testing</td><td>Flu swab</td><td>Usually none</td><td>COVID test</td></tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Because symptoms overlap, combined testing may be recommended.</p>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I still need the shot if I had flu last year?</p>
        <p>Yes—viruses change and immunity fades. Get vaccinated every season.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I take antivirals if I also have COVID?</p>
        <p>Yes—your clinician may treat both, depending on timing and risk. Bring a full medication list to check interactions.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long am I contagious?</p>
        <p>From about a day before symptoms until at least 5–7 days after they start—longer if very ill or immunocompromised.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Egg allergy and the vaccine?</p>
        <p>Most people with egg allergy can get any age-appropriate flu vaccine; ensure vaccination is in a setting that can manage rare reactions.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
