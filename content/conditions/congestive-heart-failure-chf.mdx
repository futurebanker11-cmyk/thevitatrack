  <!-- What is CHF -->
  <section id="what-is-chf" class="section">
    <h2>What is CHF?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> The heart doesn’t pump or fill well enough, so fluid backs up into lungs and tissues. That can cause breathlessness, swelling, and fatigue. With the right plan, many people live well for years.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Goals:</strong> fewer symptoms and hospital visits, steady daily activity, and protection of heart and kidneys.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Severe shortness of breath at rest, pink frothy sputum, or cannot lie flat</li>
        <li>Fainting, chest pain, or new confusion</li>
        <li>Fast/irregular heartbeat with dizziness or near-faint</li>
        <li>Oxygen level very low if you monitor at home</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies. Seek care immediately.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Shortness of breath with activity or when lying flat</li>
          <li>Waking at night breathless or needing extra pillows</li>
          <li>Swelling of ankles/legs, rapid weight gain (fluid)</li>
          <li>Fatigue, low exercise tolerance</li>
          <li>Cough or wheeze, poor appetite or early fullness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Types -->
  <section id="types" class="section">
    <h2>Types (HFrEF vs HFpEF)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Type</th><th scope="col">What it means</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>HFrEF</strong> (reduced EF)</td>
          <td>Heart’s squeeze is weak (low ejection fraction)</td>
          <td>Often benefits from ACEi/ARB/ARNI, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2, diuretics</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>HFpEF</strong> (preserved EF)</td>
          <td>Heart is stiff and doesn’t relax/fill well</td>
          <td>Focus on BP control, diuretics for congestion, SGLT2; treat triggers (AFib, obesity, sleep apnea)</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Coronary artery disease or prior heart attack</li>
          <li>Long-standing high blood pressure</li>
          <li>Valve problems (aortic/mitral)</li>
          <li>Heart rhythm issues (AFib), cardiomyopathies</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Diabetes, kidney disease, sleep apnea, obesity</li>
          <li>Viral myocarditis, alcohol/toxins, cancer therapies (some)</li>
          <li>Thyroid disorders, anemia, infections</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>How CHF is diagnosed</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>History & exam</strong> (swelling, weight change, breathlessness, risk factors)</li>
        <li><strong>BNP/NT-proBNP</strong> blood tests to support diagnosis</li>
        <li><strong>Echocardiogram</strong> (heart structure, ejection fraction, valves)</li>
        <li>ECG, chest X-ray; labs (kidney, electrolytes, thyroid, iron)</li>
        <li>Further tests if needed (stress imaging, coronary eval, sleep study)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">The plan is tailored to the specific type, cause, and other conditions.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment overview</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card heart">
        <p class="kicker">Fluid relief</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Diuretics</strong> (e.g., furosemide) for swelling/breathlessness; dosing may change based on weight and symptoms</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart protection (HFrEF)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>ACEi/ARB or <strong>ARNI</strong>, plus a <strong>beta-blocker</strong>, an <strong>MRA</strong> (spironolactone/eplerenone), and an <strong>SGLT2 inhibitor</strong></li>
          <li>Careful monitoring of BP, kidney function, and potassium</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">HFpEF focus</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Blood pressure control, diuretics for fluid, <strong>SGLT2 inhibitors</strong></li>
          <li>Treat triggers: AFib, obesity, sleep apnea, kidney disease</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Plans are individualized. Medicine names and doses depend on your health, blood pressure, kidney function, and other medicines.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily care -->
  <section id="daily-care" class="section">
    <h2>Daily care: salt, fluids, weight & BP</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Salt & fluids</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep sodium low (follow your clinician’s target; common goal ~1500–2000 mg/day)</li>
          <li>Fluid limits only if advised (often 1.5–2 L/day; follow your plan)</li>
          <li>Choose fresh foods; avoid salty packaged and restaurant items</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Weight & BP log</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Weigh every morning after bathroom, before breakfast; record it</li>
          <li>Call plan: e.g., weight ↑ by 1–2 kg (2–4 lb) in 1–3 days → follow your action steps</li>
          <li>Track blood pressure and heart rate; bring logs to visits</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Activity & vaccines</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Walk most days at an easy pace; cardiac rehab if offered</li>
          <li>Stay current with recommended vaccines</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Keep a simple checklist: pills taken, weight, symptoms, salt goal, and a daily walk.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Devices -->
  <section id="devices" class="section">
    <h2>Devices & procedures</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>ICD</strong> (defibrillator) for selected HFrEF to prevent dangerous rhythms</li>
          <li><strong>CRT</strong> (biventricular pacing) for specific conduction patterns with HFrEF</li>
          <li>Valve repair/replacement when valve disease is a major cause</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card small">
        <p class="kicker">Rhythm care</p>
        <p>Manage AFib (rate/rhythm control, anticoagulation if indicated) to reduce symptoms and risks.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Travel / flare prevention -->
  <section id="travel" class="section">
    <h2>Flare prevention & travel</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">At home</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep a low-salt grocery list and simple meals</li>
          <li>Set reminders for medicines and refills</li>
          <li>Have a written action plan for weight gain or swelling</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">On the road</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Carry a <em>medicine list</em> and an extra day or two of pills</li>
          <li>Choose low-salt options; watch fluids if restricted</li>
          <li>Walk the aisle or stretch on long trips; elevate legs when resting</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- When to see -->
  <section id="when-to-see" class="section">
    <h2>When to call your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Weight up by 1–2 kg (2–4 lb) in a few days or swelling increases</li>
        <li>More shortness of breath, new cough at night, or less activity tolerance</li>
        <li>Dizziness, very low BP, or side effects from medicines</li>
        <li>Any red flags from above</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions to ask</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Is my CHF HFrEF or HFpEF, and what’s the likely cause?</li>
        <li>Which medicines should I be on now, and how will we build the plan?</li>
        <li>What is my daily sodium and (if needed) fluid target?</li>
        <li>What’s my action plan for sudden weight gain or swelling?</li>
        <li>Do I qualify for cardiac rehab, ICD, or CRT?</li>
        <li>How do my kidney function and blood pressure affect the plan?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can CHF get better?</p>
        <p>Many people improve with guideline-based medicines, low-salt habits, and activity. Some causes are reversible or modifiable.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Why weigh daily?</p>
        <p>Rapid weight gain often means fluid build-up. Early action can prevent hospital visits.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need to stop drinking water?</p>
        <p>Not unless you’re on a fluid restriction. Most plans focus on sodium limits; follow your personalized advice.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is exercise safe?</p>
        <p>Yes—gentle, regular activity is encouraged. Cardiac rehab is ideal when available.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
