      <!-- 3 60-second resets -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> 60-second calm resets (anywhere) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Box breathing (4-4-4-4):</strong> Inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4. Repeat 4 times.</li>
            <li><strong>5-4-3-2-1 grounding:</strong> Name 5 things you see, 4 feel, 3 hear, 2 smell, 1 taste.</li>
            <li><strong>Temperature reset:</strong> Cool water on wrists/face or hold a cold glass to interrupt the stress spike.</li>
            <li><strong>Muscle release:</strong> Clench fists for 5 seconds, then relax. Work up arms, shoulders, jaw.</li>
          </ol>

      <!-- 4 Daily routine -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Daily routine that calms the system <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Move gently daily:</strong> 10–20 minutes of walking, light stretches, or chair exercises.</li>
            <li><strong>Steady fluids & meals:</strong> Protein + fiber; limit caffeine and alcohol which can spike anxiety.</li>
            <li><strong>Media diet:</strong> Turn off upsetting news in the evening; choose soothing music or nature shows.</li>
            <li><strong>Connection:</strong> Brief chat with a friend/family daily—voice or in person beats texting for calm.</li>
            <li><strong>Simple notes:</strong> One notebook or phone note for to-dos and questions; cross off wins.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Common triggers</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Too much caffeine, missed meals, dehydration</li>
                <li>Pain, constipation, infections, medicine changes</li>
                <li>Overbooking the day; long unstructured time</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Early warning signs</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Racing thoughts, jaw clench, shoulder tension</li>
                <li>Fast breathing, chest flutter, sweaty palms</li>
                <li>Snappy or restless; trouble sitting still</li>
              </ul>

      <!-- 6 Breathing & relaxation -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Breathing & relaxation (how-to) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Paced breathing</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Inhale through nose for <strong>4</strong>, exhale through pursed lips for <strong>6</strong>.</li>
                <li>One hand on belly—feel it rise on inhale, fall on exhale.</li>
                <li>Try 2–5 minutes, 2–3 times per day.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Progressive muscle relax</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Tense a body area 5 seconds, then fully relax 10–15 seconds.</li>
                <li>Feet → calves → thighs → belly → hands → arms → shoulders → face.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>

      <!-- 7 Sleep -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Sleep & “worry time” routine <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Same sleep & wake:</strong> Keep a regular schedule; short nap (20–30 min) if needed, not late.</li>
            <li><strong>Wind-down:</strong> Dim lights, light stretch, slow breaths, relaxing audio or reading.</li>
            <li><strong>Worry window:</strong> 10 minutes in the afternoon to write worries + one small action. Close the notebook afterward.</li>
            <li><strong>Bedroom:</strong> Cool, dark, quiet; night-lights for safe path to bathroom.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 8 Panic plan -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> Panic attack? Use this quick plan <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Pause & plant feet.</strong> Tell yourself: “This is uncomfortable and temporary.”</li>
            <li><strong>Breathe:</strong> 4-in / 6-out for 1–2 minutes. Count out loud if helpful.</li>
            <li><strong>Ground:</strong> 5-4-3-2-1 senses. Name what’s real around you.</li>
            <li><strong>Move:</strong> Slow walk or shoulder rolls. Shake out hands.</li>
            <li><strong>Aftercare:</strong> Water, light snack; jot the trigger and what helped.</li>
          </ol>

      <!-- 9 Medicines -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Medicines & safety (talk with your clinician) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Many people benefit from therapy, skills practice, and sometimes medication. Your plan is personal—<strong>never start/stop a medicine without clinician guidance.</strong></p>
          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Medicines overview">
            <table class="responsive">
              <thead><tr><th>Topic</th><th>Notes</th></tr></thead>
              <tbody>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Topic">Antidepressants (SSRIs/SNRIs)</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Often used for persistent anxiety; benefits build over weeks; interactions reviewed by your clinician.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Topic">Benzodiazepines</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">May cause drowsiness, confusion, and falls in older adults; short-term/limited use only if prescribed.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Topic">Other factors</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Caffeine, decongestants, some stimulants can worsen anxiety. Bring a full med/supplement list to visits.</td>
                </tr>
              </tbody>
            </table>
          </div>
          <p class="muted">Ask about counseling options (CBT), group programs, and local resources.</p>
        </div>

      <!-- 10 Caregivers -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">9</span> Caregivers & family: what helps most <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Use a <strong>calm tone</strong>, speak slowly, give one step at a time.</li>
            <li>Validate (“I see this feels scary”) then guide to a <strong>short, doable action</strong> (sip water, 4-6 breathing).</li>
            <li>Reduce background noise and clutter; plan simple routines.</li>
            <li>Celebrate small wins; keep a visible list of what helped last time.</li>
          </ul>

      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">10</span> Myths to ignore <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>“Anxiety means I’m weak.”</strong> It’s a common health condition, not a character flaw.</li>
            <li><strong>“Nothing helps.”</strong> Skills + routine + support usually bring real relief.</li>
