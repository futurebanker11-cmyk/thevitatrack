      <!-- 2 What it is -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">1</span> What constipation is (and isn’t) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div>
              <p>Constipation means stools are <strong>hard, dry, infrequent, or difficult to pass</strong>. “Normal” frequency varies (from 3 times/day to 3 times/week). Focus on <em>comfort, ease, and pattern</em>, not a perfect number.</p>
              <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Common causes">
                <table class="responsive">
                  <thead><tr><th>Common causes</th><th>Examples</th></tr></thead>
                  <tbody>
                    <tr><td data-col="Common causes">Not enough fiber/fluids</td><td data-col="Examples">Low intake, poor appetite, hot weather</td></tr>
                    <tr><td data-col="Common causes">Less movement</td><td data-col="Examples">Illness, hospitalization, recovery</td></tr>
                    <tr><td data-col="Common causes">Medications</td><td data-col="Examples">Opioids, iron, some antacids, some BP or bladder meds</td></tr>
                    <tr><td data-col="Common causes">Pelvic floor issues</td><td data-col="Examples">Difficulty coordinating “relax & push”</td></tr>
                  </tbody>
                </table>
              </div>
            </div>
            <div class="note tip"><strong>Good news:</strong> A simple daily routine plus the right fiber/fluids and movement often restores regularity in days to weeks.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 3 Quick relief -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> Quick relief steps (today) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Warm drink</strong> after waking (water or caffeine if allowed).</li>
            <li><strong>Breakfast fiber</strong>: oatmeal with fruit or whole-grain toast with peanut butter.</li>
            <li><strong>Gentle walk</strong> 5–15 minutes (hall laps count).</li>
            <li><strong>Toilet posture</strong>: feet on a <em>footstool</em>, lean forward, relax belly; don’t hold breath.</li>
            <li><strong>Don’t strain long</strong>: if no urge in ~10 minutes, try later.</li>
          </ol>

      <!-- 4 Daily routine -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Daily bowel routine (simple, repeatable) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Same time daily:</strong> sit 20–30 minutes after breakfast (gastro-colic reflex).</li>
            <li><strong>Fiber target (food first):</strong> gradually toward ~20–30 g/day if tolerated.</li>
            <li><strong>Fluids:</strong> steady sips through the day (unless on restriction).</li>
            <li><strong>Move:</strong> short walks, light chair exercises, ankle pumps.</li>
            <li><strong>Respond to urges:</strong> don’t delay; make bathroom easy and unhurried.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 5 Fiber & fluids -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Fiber & fluids: practical picks <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Food ideas</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Oatmeal, bran or high-fiber cereal</li>
                <li>Beans/lentils; whole-grain bread/rice/pasta</li>
                <li>Prunes, pears, kiwi, berries; cooked veggies</li>
                <li>Nuts/seeds (chew well), ground flax or chia</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Fluids (examples)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Water, herbal tea, warm lemon water</li>
                <li>Prune or pear juice (small glasses)</li>
                <li>Broths/soups add fluid + salt (ask if OK)</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
          <p class="muted"><strong>Go slow with fiber.</strong> Add gradually with fluids to avoid gas/bloating. Some conditions need special guidance—ask your clinician.</p>

      <!-- 6 Medicines -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Medicines (overview & safety) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Laxative options (general info)">
            <table class="responsive">
              <thead><tr><th>Type</th><th>How it works</th><th>Notes</th></tr></thead>
              <tbody>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Type">Osmotic (e.g., PEG)</td>
                  <td data-col="How it works">Draws water into stool, softens</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Often first choice; start low and adjust. Check kidney guidance if using other osmotics.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Type">Bulk-forming (psyllium)</td>
                  <td data-col="How it works">Adds stool bulk + water</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Needs adequate fluid. May bloat if increased too fast.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Type">Stimulant (senna, bisacodyl)</td>
                  <td data-col="How it works">Stimulates bowel movement</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Useful short-term or PRN; discuss regular use with clinician.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Type">Stool softener (docusate)</td>
                  <td data-col="How it works">Helps water mix with stool</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">May help with hard stools; often combined with others.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Type">Suppository/enema</td>
                  <td data-col="How it works">Local stimulation/softening</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Short-term rescue for impaction; choose gentle options. Avoid frequent sodium phosphate enemas unless instructed.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Type">Prescription options</td>
                  <td data-col="How it works">Increase intestinal fluid/motility</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Discuss if chronic constipation or IBS-C persists after basics.</td>
                </tr>
              </tbody>
            </table>

      <!-- 7 Pelvic floor & posture -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Pelvic floor & toileting posture <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Footstool:</strong> knees slightly above hips; lean forward, elbows on thighs.</li>
            <li><strong>Relax first:</strong> breathe in through nose, out through pursed lips; let belly soften.</li>
            <li><strong>“Ssss” or “shhhh” exhale</strong> can cue gentle bearing down (no breath-holding).</li>
            <li>If pushing is always hard, ask about <strong>pelvic floor therapy</strong>.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 8 Mobility & conditions -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> If you have Parkinson’s, stroke, or limited mobility <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Short, frequent movement (chair marches, ankle pumps, hallway laps).</li>
            <li>Schedule bowel time when your <em>movement meds</em> work best (if applicable).</li>
            <li>Consider a <strong>regular osmotic + rescue plan</strong> with your clinician.</li>
            <li>Ensure safe transfers; raised toilet seat and grab bars help.</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 9 When to see the doctor -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> When to get evaluated; tests <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Symptoms persist ≥3 weeks despite routine + OTC plan</li>
            <li>New constipation after age 50, or family history of colon cancer</li>
            <li>Red flags: blood in stool, weight loss, anemia, severe pain</li>
            <li>Recurrent impactions or need for frequent enemas/suppositories</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Possible tests</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Labs (thyroid, electrolytes), medication review</li>
                <li>Stool testing as indicated</li>
                <li>Colonoscopy or imaging based on risk and symptoms</li>
                <li>Pelvic floor evaluation if coordination issues suspected</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Bring to the visit</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>2-week bowel log (times, stool type, meds, diet, fluids)</li>
                <li>Full med/supplement list; note any recent changes</li>
              </ul>

            <li>After surgery or new meds (iron, opioids), start a <strong>prevention plan</strong> early per clinician.</li>
