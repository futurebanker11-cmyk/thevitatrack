  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What they are & why they matter</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> <strong>Common respiratory infections</strong> include colds, influenza (flu), COVID-19, and RSV. Most are viral and improve with time. In older adults, they can lead to dehydration, pneumonia, or worsening of heart/lung disease—so early testing and supportive care prevent complications.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good to know:</strong> Antibiotics don’t treat viruses. Antivirals (for flu/COVID) work best when started early after symptom onset.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Breathing trouble, blue/gray lips, or oxygen readings consistently low</li>
        <li>Chest pain, confusion, fainting, or severe weakness</li>
        <li>High fever with shaking chills or very low temperature</li>
        <li>Cannot keep fluids down, minimal urine, dizziness on standing</li>
        <li>Symptoms worsening after day 3–4 or not improving by day 5–7</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Call emergency services or go to urgent care. Bring your medicine list and recent test results if available.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Signs -->
  <section id="signs" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Runny/stuffy nose, sore throat, cough (dry or wet)</li>
          <li>Fever or chills, body aches, headache</li>
          <li>Shortness of breath or wheeze (especially in lung disease)</li>
          <li>Fatigue, low appetite; in seniors: new confusion or falls</li>
          <li>RSV often causes heavy mucus and wheeze; COVID-19 can vary widely</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>When to test</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Consider home/clinic tests if:</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>You are 60+ or have heart/lung/kidney disease, diabetes, or are immunocompromised</li>
          <li>Symptoms are moderate–severe, or you live with vulnerable people</li>
          <li>You might qualify for <strong>antivirals</strong> (best started within days)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Common tests: rapid antigen or PCR for <strong>COVID-19</strong> and <strong>flu</strong>; some clinics can test for <strong>RSV</strong>.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Retest tip:</strong> If first home COVID test is negative but symptoms/significant exposure continue, repeat in 24–48 hours or seek a PCR/clinic assessment.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="otc" class="section">
    <h2>OTC medicines & safety</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="OTC guidance">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Symptom</th><th>Options</th><th>Safety notes (older adults)</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Fever/aches</td>
          <td>Acetaminophen; NSAIDs (clinician-guided)</td>
          <td>Avoid duplicate acetaminophen in combo products; NSAIDs may affect stomach, kidneys, heart</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Nasal congestion</td>
          <td>Saline spray/rinse; short-term decongestant</td>
          <td>Avoid oral decongestants if heart/BP issues or glaucoma; check interactions</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Cough</td>
          <td>Honey/lozenges; guaifenesin; dextromethorphan</td>
          <td>Avoid multi-ingredient sedating syrups; monitor for confusion or drowsiness</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Runny nose</td>
          <td>Non-drowsy antihistamines</td>
          <td>Avoid strong anticholinergics (e.g., diphenhydramine) due to confusion/fall risk</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Wheeze</td>
          <td>Inhalers as prescribed</td>
          <td>Do <em>not</em> start new inhalers without guidance; check technique and spacer use</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>

  <!-- Home care -->
  <section id="home" class="section">
    <h2>Home care & recovery</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily basics</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Hydrate:</strong> Water, broths, oral rehydration as allowed</li>
          <li><strong>Rest & pace:</strong> Short activities with breaks; avoid overexertion</li>
          <li><strong>Humidify:</strong> Steam showers or humidifier to loosen mucus</li>
          <li><strong>Nutrition:</strong> Small, frequent meals; protein with each</li>
          <li><strong>Track:</strong> Temperature, symptoms, and home oxygen (if used)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Timeline:</strong> Colds 5–10 days; flu/COVID-19 often 1–2+ weeks of fatigue. Call if not clearly improving by day 5–7 or if any red flag appears.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Breathing -->
  <section id="breathing" class="section">
    <h2>Breathing & comfort</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Pursed-lip breathing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Inhale through nose for <strong>2</strong> counts</li>
          <li>Exhale slowly through puckered lips for <strong>4</strong> counts</li>
          <li>Repeat 5–10 cycles; helps ease breathlessness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Sleep & cough:</strong> Elevate the head of the bed; side-lying can reduce cough. Honey tea before bed (unless contraindicated) may soothe the throat.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Prevention & vaccines</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Prevention">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Why it helps</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Vaccines</td>
          <td>Reduces severe illness and hospitalization</td>
          <td><strong>Flu</strong> yearly; <strong>COVID-19</strong> per guidance; ask about <strong>RSV</strong> for eligible seniors; keep <strong>pneumococcal</strong> up to date</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Masks & ventilation</td>
          <td>Lower exposure in crowds/indoors</td>
          <td>Use well-fitting masks during surges; improve airflow or meet outdoors</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Hand & respiratory hygiene</td>
          <td>Cuts spread of viruses</td>
          <td>Wash hands, cover coughs/sneezes, avoid sharing utensils when sick</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Stay home when ill</td>
          <td>Protects others, especially high-risk adults</td>
          <td>Return to activities gradually once fever-free and improving</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need antibiotics?</p>
        <p>Usually <strong>no</strong> for viral infections (cold, most flu/COVID/RSV). Antibiotics are for bacterial infections—your clinician will advise if needed.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When should I start antivirals?</p>
        <p>As soon as possible if eligible—typically within days of symptom onset for flu and COVID-19. Early testing helps decide.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long am I contagious?</p>
        <p>Often 1–2 days before symptoms and up to ~5–10 days after. Contagious period may be longer in severe illness or weak immunity.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What’s the fastest comfort win?</p>
        <p>Hydration + humidified air + scheduled acetaminophen (if safe) for fever/aches, plus rest and gentle movement to keep lungs open.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
