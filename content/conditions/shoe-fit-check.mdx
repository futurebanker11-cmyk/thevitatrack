  <section id="why" class="section">
    <h2>Why fit matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="kicker">Big wins:</span> fewer calluses & blisters, steadier balance, less knee/back pain, and better protection for people with neuropathy or diabetes.</p>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Red flags (stop using that pair)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Toe numbness, new corns/blisters, or skin breakdown</li>
          <li>Frequent tripping, heel slippage, or ankle rolling</li>
          <li>Soles worn smooth or tilted (inside/outside edge)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Feet often change size with age and swelling—<strong>re-measure yearly</strong> and when switching brands.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>5-step fit check</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Right time of day</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Shop/try on in the <strong>late afternoon</strong> when feet are slightly swollen—it prevents buying too-tight shoes.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Length & toe room</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stand. You should have a <strong>thumb’s width (≈1–1.5 cm)</strong> between longest toe and the front.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Width & bunion room</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Upper should not bulge or dig. If snug across the forefoot, try a <strong>wider width</strong> or stretchy mesh upper.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">4) Heel hold</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Walk. Heel should stay put with <em>minimal</em> lift. Use heel-lock lacing if slightly loose.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">5) Insole test</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Remove the insole and stand on it. Your foot should not spill over at the sides or toes.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Sizing -->
  <section id="sizing" class="section">
    <h2>Sizing & width</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Sizing & width">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Tip</th><th>Why</th><th>How</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Measure both feet</td>
          <td>Feet can differ; fit the <strong>larger</strong> foot</td>
          <td>Use a Brannock gauge; note length & width</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Match socks</td>
          <td>Thickness changes fit</td>
          <td>Bring the socks you’ll wear most</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Width letters matter</td>
          <td>Standard isn’t one-size-fits-all</td>
          <td>Narrow (N), Medium (M), Wide (W), Extra-wide (XW)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Room for inserts</td>
          <td>Orthotics take space</td>
          <td>Choose “removable insole” styles; size up ½ if needed</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- Lacing -->
  <section id="lacing" class="section">
    <h2>Lacing & closure tricks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common fixes</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Heel lock (runner’s loop):</strong> adds heel hold to reduce slippage.</li>
          <li><strong>Skip-eyelet over bunion:</strong> skip the hole over the sore spot to reduce pressure.</li>
          <li><strong>Window lacing:</strong> relieve top-of-foot pressure by crossing laces around (not over) the tender area.</li>
          <li><strong>Elastic laces/Velcro:</strong> easier for arthritis or limited hand strength.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        If you use an <strong>ankle brace</strong> or compression sock, try shoes on with the device—many need a wider or higher-volume model.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Insoles -->
  <section id="insoles" class="section">
    <h2>Insoles & orthotics</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Cushion insoles</strong> add shock absorption for sensitive feet.</li>
          <li><strong>Support insoles</strong> add arch control—helpful for plantar fasciitis or over-pronation.</li>
          <li><strong>Custom orthotics</strong> per clinician/podiatrist for deformities or stubborn pain.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Remove the <em>original</em> insole first to avoid crowding. Check that heel sits deep and stable after any insert is added.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Conditions -->
  <section id="conditions" class="section">
    <h2>Special situations</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Special situations">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Condition</th><th>Shoe setup</th><th>Watch-outs</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Diabetes / neuropathy</strong></td>
          <td>Wide toe box, smooth interior, seam-free socks; consider extra-depth shoes</td>
          <td>Daily foot checks; <strong>no barefoot</strong>; treat any skin break promptly</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bunions / hammer toes</strong></td>
          <td>Stretchy knit upper, wide/extra-wide, high toe box</td>
          <td>Avoid hard overlays that rub the bunion area</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Edema / swelling</strong></td>
          <td>Adjustable straps or laces; consider expandable uppers; try on late day</td>
          <td>Room for swelling without cutting off circulation</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Balance issues / fall risk</strong></td>
          <td>Low heel (≤2.5 cm), firm heel counter, grippy outsole, rocker-sole only if advised</td>
          <td>Avoid floppy slip-ons and slick soles</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Heel pain (plantar fasciitis)</strong></td>
          <td>Moderate arch support, cushioned heel, slight heel-to-toe drop</td>
          <td>Replace worn shoes sooner; consider support insoles</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Toe deformities / sensitive nails</strong></td>
          <td>High toe box; seamless/smooth lining; roomy depth</td>
          <td>Trim nails straight; avoid pointy toe shapes</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Features -->
  <section id="features" class="section">
    <h2>Shoe features that help</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Upper:</strong> breathable mesh or soft leather; minimal seams over pressure spots</li>
          <li><strong>Midsole:</strong> cushioning that isn’t mushy—stable when you press</li>
          <li><strong>Outsole:</strong> non-slip tread, wide base; avoid heavy weight</li>
          <li><strong>Heel counter:</strong> firm cup that doesn’t fold when pinched</li>
          <li><strong>Closure:</strong> lace/Velcro/BOA for adjustability</li>
          <li><strong>Removable insole:</strong> makes room for inserts/orthotics</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Try <strong>house shoes</strong> (indoor-only, grippy) instead of slippers—more stable and still easy to slip on/off.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Replace -->
  <section id="replace" class="section">
    <h2>When to replace</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Outsole tread is smooth or unevenly worn</li>
          <li>Midsole feels flat/compressed; new aches by day’s end</li>
          <li>Upper tears or heel counter floppy</li>
          <li>Typical lifespan: walking shoes <strong>9–18 months</strong> depending on use</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Rotate two pairs to extend life and let cushioning rebound.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Care -->
  <section id="care" class="section">
    <h2>Care & daily checks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Air shoes out; remove insoles to dry if sweaty/rained on</li>
          <li>Clean with mild soap; avoid high heat dryers</li>
          <li>Use seamless socks; change daily</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card danger">
        <strong>Daily foot scan:</strong> look for redness, blisters, cuts, or nail problems—especially if you have diabetes or neuropathy. Seek care early for any wounds.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How much toe room do I need?</p>
        <p>About a thumb’s width (≈1–1.5 cm) beyond the longest toe when standing.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">My heels slip—what can I try?</p>
        <p>Use a heel-lock lacing pattern or a shoe with a firmer heel counter. If still loose, try a different last/brand.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are slip-on shoes okay?</p>
        <p>Choose ones with firm heel cups and good tread. Many older adults do better with adjustable closures for a snug, safe fit.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need wide shoes?</p>
        <p>If the upper bulges or rubs the forefoot/bunion, a wide or extra-wide size—or a stretch-mesh upper—often helps.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
