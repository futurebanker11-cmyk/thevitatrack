  <!-- What is CKD -->
  <section id="what-is-ckd" class="section">
    <h2>What is CKD?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Chronic Kidney Disease means the kidneys are not filtering blood as well as they should for <strong>3 months or longer</strong> — often shown by a lower <strong>eGFR</strong> or protein/leakage in urine (<strong>albumin</strong>). Goals: protect kidney function, control blood pressure & diabetes, and prevent heart problems.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good to know:</strong> Many people live well with early CKD by following steady routines: low-salt eating, movement, safe medicines, and regular checks.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Severe shortness of breath, chest pain, or confusion</li>
        <li>Very little urine, sudden leg/face swelling, or rapid weight gain</li>
        <li>High potassium symptoms: muscle weakness, palpitations</li>
        <li>Blood in urine, severe flank pain with fever (possible infection/stone)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may be emergencies. Seek care immediately.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Tiredness, low appetite, morning nausea</li>
          <li>Swelling in feet/ankles, puffiness around eyes</li>
          <li>Night urination, foamy urine (protein)</li>
          <li>Itching, muscle cramps, trouble concentrating (later stages)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Stages -->
  <section id="stages" class="section">
    <h2>CKD stages & eGFR</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Stage</th><th scope="col">eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²)</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr><td><strong>G1</strong></td><td>≥ 90</td><td>Normal eGFR with other kidney markers (e.g., albumin) abnormal</td></tr>
        <tr><td><strong>G2</strong></td><td>60–89</td><td>Mildly reduced; protect with BP, sugar, and salt control</td></tr>
        <tr><td><strong>G3a</strong></td><td>45–59</td><td>Moderate; check medicines, labs, and albumin regularly</td></tr>
        <tr><td><strong>G3b</strong></td><td>30–44</td><td>Moderate-to-severe; plan ahead and review diet/minerals</td></tr>
        <tr><td><strong>G4</strong></td><td>15–29</td><td>Severe; close follow-up, discuss advanced planning</td></tr>
        <tr><td><strong>G5</strong></td><td>&lt; 15</td><td>Kidney failure; dialysis or transplant planning if appropriate</td></tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Albumin in urine (A1/A2/A3) adds risk information; both G and A categories guide care.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Diabetes (high blood sugar damages filters)</li>
          <li>High blood pressure (long-term strain on kidneys)</li>
          <li>Glomerular diseases, polycystic kidney disease</li>
          <li>Repeated kidney infections, stones, obstruction (prostate)</li>
          <li>Long-term high-dose painkillers/NSAIDs or certain medicines</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Tip:</strong> Treating the cause slows decline. Glucose & blood pressure control are the biggest wins for many people.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests & monitoring -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>Key tests & monitoring</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Blood:</strong> eGFR/creatinine, electrolytes (especially potassium), bicarbonate, HbA1c if diabetic</li>
        <li><strong>Urine:</strong> albumin-creatinine ratio (ACR) to measure leak</li>
        <li><strong>BP checks:</strong> home BP log; share averages</li>
        <li><strong>Mineral & bone:</strong> calcium, phosphate, vitamin D, parathyroid hormone (later stages)</li>
        <li><strong>Medication review:</strong> avoid kidney-straining drugs when possible</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Frequency depends on stage and albumin level (often every 3–6 months, closer in advanced CKD).</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine safety & kidney-protective meds</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Often protective (ask your clinician)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>BP control (targets individualized); medicines may include ACE-inhibitor/ARB</li>
          <li>For diabetes with CKD: selected options that can protect kidneys (ask about eligibility)</li>
          <li>Statins for heart protection in many older adults with CKD</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Use carefully / avoid unless advised</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>NSAIDs (ibuprofen, diclofenac) — can reduce kidney blood flow</li>
          <li>Certain antibiotics and contrast dyes — dose/choice may change</li>
          <li>Herbal supplements of unknown content</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small note" style="margin-top:12px">Always check doses against kidney function and review all OTC/supplements with your clinician.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Kidney-smart diet (salt, protein, potassium, phosphorus)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Salt (sodium)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Aim for lower-salt choices to help BP and swelling</li>
          <li>Rinse canned foods; choose fresh or low-salt versions</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Protein</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Moderate portion sizes; lean proteins (fish, poultry, tofu)</li>
          <li>Adjustments may be needed in advanced CKD under dietitian guidance</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Potassium</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Some CKD stages run high potassium — ask if you need limits</li>
          <li>Favor lower-potassium choices if advised; learn smart swaps</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Phosphorus</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Limit cola drinks and processed foods with “phos-” additives</li>
          <li>Dairy portions may need review in later stages</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="small" style="margin-top:8px">
      Related: <a href="#">Senior-Friendly Recipes & Drinks</a> · <a href="#">Hydration & Drinks</a> · <a href="#">Type 2 Diabetes</a>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Fluids -->
  <section id="fluids" class="section">
    <h2>Hydration & sick-day rules</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Hydration</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Steady small sips through the day unless on a fluid limit</li>
          <li>Evening cut-down if night urination is troublesome</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sick-day rules (ask for a written plan)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>During vomiting/diarrhea, some medicines may be paused — confirm which</li>
          <li>Re-start when eating/drinking normally and approved by your clinician</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small calm" style="margin-top:10px">If you cannot keep fluids down or feel faint, seek medical advice promptly.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Specialist -->
  <section id="specialist" class="section">
    <h2>When to see a nephrologist</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>eGFR persistently &lt; 30 (G4–G5) or fast decline</li>
        <li>Heavy albumin in urine or difficult-to-control BP</li>
        <li>Frequent high potassium, acid-base issues, or anemia of CKD</li>
        <li>Planning for advanced CKD and education about options</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily tips -->
  <section id="daily" class="section">
    <h2>Daily tips & home setup</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Home BP & weight log</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Measure BP the same way each time; bring averages</li>
          <li>Daily weights can catch fluid changes early</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Movement & energy</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Gentle walks most days; pace activities</li>
          <li>Manage swelling with leg-up breaks if advised</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medication list</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep an updated list; include doses and reasons</li>
          <li>Check for drug interactions and kidney dosing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Kidney care is a marathon — small, steady habits protect kidneys and the heart.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions for your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>What is my current stage and urine albumin level?</li>
        <li>What BP and A1c targets should I aim for?</li>
        <li>Which medicines protect my kidneys and which should I avoid?</li>
        <li>Do I need diet changes for potassium or phosphorus?</li>
        <li>How often should I repeat blood/urine tests?</li>
        <li>When should I see a kidney specialist?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can CKD improve?</p>
        <p>Early CKD can stabilize or improve when the cause is treated and blood pressure, sugar, and salt are well controlled.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is high potassium dangerous?</p>
        <p>Yes — it can affect the heart. Your team will guide diet and medicines to keep levels safe.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need to stop all painkillers?</p>
        <p>Not always. Many avoid routine NSAIDs; safer options and doses depend on your kidney function — ask for a personalized plan.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How often should I check labs?</p>
        <p>Varies by stage and albumin. Commonly every 3–6 months in mild/moderate CKD and more often in advanced stages.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
