            <div><strong>BP has two numbers:</strong> top (systolic) when the heart squeezes; bottom (diastolic) when it relaxes.</div>
            <div><strong>Trends beat single checks:</strong> measure the same way, calm, and compare averages.</div>
            <div><strong>Small habits help:</strong> after-meal walks, salt awareness, steady sleep, meds as prescribed.</div>
            <div><strong>Targets are personal:</strong> dizziness, kidney/heart health, and age guide your safe range.</div>

      <!-- Measure -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Measure at home (simple routine) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div>
              <ol>
                <li><strong>Rest 5 minutes:</strong> back supported, feet flat, legs uncrossed, bladder empty.</li>
                <li><strong>Arm & cuff fit:</strong> upper-arm cuff; forearm supported at heart level.</li>
                <li><strong>Avoid for 30 minutes:</strong> caffeine, smoking, hard exercise.</li>
                <li><strong>Take 2–3 readings 1 minute apart</strong> and write the average.</li>
                <li><strong>Same times:</strong> morning (before pills/food) & evening; repeat for several days when adjusting your plan.</li>
              </ol>
              <p class="muted">Bring your home cuff to clinic yearly to compare with their device.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="note tip"><strong>Why it matters:</strong> Calm posture and correct cuff height can change a reading by 5–10 points. Consistency makes your trend trustworthy.</div>

      <!-- Daily Plan -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Daily plan (small steps that add up) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Move gently</h3>
              <ul>
                <li><strong>5–10 min walk after meals</strong> supports BP, sugar, digestion.</li>
                <li><strong>Balance & strength:</strong> heel-to-toe near a counter, light hand squeezes, chair stands.</li>
                <li><strong>Rise-slow routine:</strong> sit → feet dangle → stand slowly to avoid dizziness.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Salt & fluids</h3>
              <ul>
                <li><strong>Taste first:</strong> swap some salt for lemon, herbs, pepper.</li>
                <li><strong>Read labels:</strong> soups, sauces, processed meats hide sodium.</li>
                <li><strong>Hydrate earlier:</strong> small sips daytime; reduce big drinks 2–3 hrs before bed if nocturia.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Sleep & stress</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Consistent bedtime; cool, dark room; safe night light.</li>
                <li><strong>Breathing break:</strong> 5 minutes nose-in / pursed-lip out.</li>
                <li>Gentle stretch in the evening to unwind.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>

      <!-- Meds -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Medication basics (plain language) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Many adults need medicine plus daily habits. Your prescriber considers other conditions and side-effect risks.</p>
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Diuretics</strong> (“water pills”): remove extra salt/fluid. You may urinate more—timing matters.</li>
            <li><strong>ACE inhibitors / ARBs:</strong> relax vessels; often used if kidney disease or diabetes. Report cough, swelling, dizziness.</li>
            <li><strong>Calcium channel blockers:</strong> relax vessel muscle; can cause ankle swelling for some.</li>
            <li><strong>Beta-blockers:</strong> steady/slow the heart; often used when heart disease is present.</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="note"><strong>Don’t stop meds suddenly.</strong> Call your clinician if you feel dizzy, overly tired, or notice swelling/cough/new symptoms.</div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- Monitor / Log -->
      <details class="section" id="monitor-log">

        <div class="content">
          <p>Log date/time, top & bottom numbers, pulse, arm used, and a short note (salty meal, poor sleep, missed dose). A morning/evening average for 3–7 days gives a clear snapshot for your clinician.</p>

      <!-- Special -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Special notes (65+, diabetes, kidney, HF) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Age 65+:</strong> Avoid dizziness and falls. If you feel light-headed on standing, tell your clinician. Targets or dosing time may change.</li>
            <li><strong>Diabetes:</strong> Good BP control protects eyes, kidneys, and nerves. Pair with A1C goals and foot care.</li>
            <li><strong>Chronic kidney disease:</strong> Ask about ACEi/ARB benefits, safe pain relievers (limit NSAIDs), and potassium checks.</li>
            <li><strong>Heart failure:</strong> Daily weight, watch swelling/breathlessness, follow fluid/salt limits if prescribed.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- Myths -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">9</span> Common myths <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>“I feel fine, so my BP is fine.”</strong> High BP is often silent. Home checks tell the truth.</li>
            <li><strong>“One high reading means danger.”</strong> Single checks can be off; trends over days matter.</li>
            <li><strong>“Salt only means the shaker.”</strong> Most sodium comes from packaged and restaurant foods.</li>
            <li><strong>“If I need medicine, I failed.”</strong> Medicine plus daily steps protects heart and brain.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- Caregivers -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">10</span> Caregivers & family <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Support calm, repeatable routines: pill boxes labeled by time, short walks after meals, low-salt cooking, and a quiet corner for BP checks. Keep a short question list for appointments. Celebrate small wins—consistency reduces risk.</p>
        </div>

<script>
  // Font size toggles
  (function(){
    const html=document.documentElement;
    const normal=document.getElementById('fontNormal');
    const large=document.getElementById('fontLarge');
    function set(size){ html.style.fontSize=size; document.body.style.fontSize=size; }
    normal && normal.addEventListener('click', ()=>set('var(--fs)'));
    large && large.addEventListener('click', ()=>set('var(--fs-lg)'));
  })();

  // Expand/Collapse all (keep first section open when collapsing)
  (function(){
    const openAll=document.getElementById('openAll');
    const closeAll=document.getElementById('closeAll');
    const sections=[...document.querySelectorAll('details.section')];
    openAll && openAll.addEventListener('click', ()=> sections.forEach(d=> d.open=true));
    closeAll && closeAll.addEventListener('click', ()=>{
      sections.forEach((d,i)=> d.open = (i===0));
      sections[0].scrollIntoView({behavior:'smooth', block:'start'});
    });
  })();

  // If any table overflows, add scroll hint (desktop/tablet only)
  (function(){
    const wrap = document.getElementById('rangesTableWrap');
    if(!wrap) return;
    const check = ()=> {
      const tbl = wrap.querySelector('table');
      if(tbl && tbl.scrollWidth > wrap.clientWidth){
        wrap.classList.add('is-scrollable');
      } else {
        wrap.classList.remove('is-scrollable');
      }
    };
    check();
    window.addEventListener('resize', check, {passive:true});
  })();
</script>
