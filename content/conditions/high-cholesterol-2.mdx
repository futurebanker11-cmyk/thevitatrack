  <!-- Basics -->
  <section id="basics" class="section">
    <h2>Cholesterol basics (LDL/HDL/TG)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Part of panel</th><th scope="col">What it does</th><th scope="col">Why it matters</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>LDL-C</strong> (“bad”)</td>
          <td>Carries cholesterol into artery walls</td>
          <td>Lower is better for heart/brain/kidney protection</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>HDL-C</strong> (“good”)</td>
          <td>Helps carry cholesterol away for disposal</td>
          <td>Higher can be helpful, but raising HDL with pills hasn’t shown benefit</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Triglycerides (TG)</strong></td>
          <td>Blood fats used for energy</td>
          <td>Very high TG raises pancreatitis risk; moderate elevations link to heart risk</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Non-HDL-C</strong></td>
          <td>Total cholesterol minus HDL</td>
          <td>Captures all “atherogenic” particles; useful when TG are high</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <div class="card small" style="margin-top:12px">
      <p><span class="badge">Key idea</span> Focus on <strong>LDL-C</strong> lowering and overall risk. For many older adults with heart, stroke, diabetes, or kidney disease, LDL goals are tighter.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- When to worry -->
  <section id="when-worry" class="section">
    <h2>When to worry & red flags</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Chest pain/pressure, breathlessness, jaw/arm pain, sudden weakness, or speech trouble</strong> → urgent care</li>
        <li><strong>TG &gt; 500–1000 mg/dL</strong>: discuss same-week plan to lower pancreatitis risk</li>
        <li>Yellow skin bumps (<em>xanthomas</em>) or early heart disease in family → ask about inherited lipid disorders</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Emergency symptoms may signal heart attack or stroke.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Labs -->
  <section id="labs" class="section">
    <h2>Your lipid panel: fasting vs non-fasting</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Non-fasting is often fine</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Total cholesterol, LDL (direct/calc), and HDL change little after a normal meal</li>
          <li>Useful for routine screening and follow-up</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When fasting helps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Triglycerides are high or variable</li>
          <li>Initial workup of severe elevations</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small" style="margin-top:8px">Ask how your lab calculates LDL and whether direct LDL is used when TG are high.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Targets -->
  <section id="targets" class="section">
    <h2>Targets depend on risk</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Higher risk → lower LDL goal</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>History of heart attack, stroke, TIA, angina, stent, or PAD</li>
          <li>Diabetes, chronic kidney disease, or very high calculated risk</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Your clinician sets a personal target (often LDL &lt; 70 mg/dL for very high risk; individualized in older adults).</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">Numbers are part of the picture</p>
        <p>Age, blood pressure, smoking, weight, family history, and inflammation also guide intensity of treatment.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Lifestyle -->
  <section id="lifestyle" class="section">
    <h2>Lifestyle that works</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Plate pattern</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>More vegetables, fruits, beans/lentils, whole grains, nuts/seeds</li>
          <li>Choose fish (esp. oily fish) twice weekly; lean poultry or tofu</li>
          <li>Use olive/canola oils; limit butter, ghee, palm oil</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fiber & sterols</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Soluble fiber</strong> (oats, barley, psyllium, beans) can lower LDL</li>
          <li>Plant sterol/stanol-fortified foods may give an extra small drop</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Movement & weight</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular walking + light strength/balance supports HDL and triglycerides</li>
          <li>Even modest weight loss (if advised) helps LDL/TG</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Match changes to your abilities and health conditions; build gradually.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Food swaps -->
  <section id="food-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Healthy food swaps (easy wins)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Swap this</th><th scope="col">For this</th><th scope="col">Why</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Butter/ghee on toast</td>
          <td>Olive oil + herbs or nut butter</td>
          <td>Less saturated fat, adds heart-healthy fats</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Red meat most nights</td>
          <td>Fish/legumes 3–4 nights</td>
          <td>Lowers saturated fat, adds fiber or omega-3s</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Creamy sauces</td>
          <td>Tomato-based, yogurt-based, or spice rubs</td>
          <td>Reduces saturated fat</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Refined snacks</td>
          <td>Nuts, roasted chickpeas, fruit + yogurt</td>
          <td>Fiber and healthy fats improve satiety</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>White bread/rice</td>
          <td>Whole-grain bread, brown rice, barley, oats</td>
          <td>More soluble fiber to help LDL</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Common medicines</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Group</th><th scope="col">Main effect</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Statins</strong> (e.g., atorvastatin, rosuvastatin)</td>
          <td>Lower LDL substantially; proven to reduce heart attack/stroke</td>
          <td>Muscle aches can occur; labs and dose adjust if needed</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Ezetimibe</strong></td>
          <td>Modest extra LDL drop; pairs with statin</td>
          <td>Well-tolerated; useful if statin isn’t enough</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>PCSK9 inhibitors</strong> (injections)</td>
          <td>Large LDL reduction</td>
          <td>For very high risk or inherited high cholesterol</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bempedoic acid</strong></td>
          <td>Additional LDL lowering (oral)</td>
          <td>Option if statin intolerance or add-on needed</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Fibrates</strong></td>
          <td>Lower triglycerides</td>
          <td>Consider with very high TG; monitor kidneys</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)</strong></td>
          <td>Lower TG; specific purified EPA has outcome data in select patients</td>
          <td>Discuss formulation and dose; may affect platelets</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">Medicines are individualized based on risk, other conditions, and preferences.</div>
  </section>

  <!-- Side effects -->
  <section id="side-effects" class="section">
    <h2>Side-effect tips & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Muscle symptoms on statins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Report new widespread aches, weakness, or cramps</li>
          <li>Sometimes a dose reduction, switch, or alternate-day dosing helps</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Interactions</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Some antibiotics, antifungals, and grapefruit interact with certain statins</li>
          <li>Bring a complete meds/supplements list to visits</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes note</p>
        <p>Statins are still recommended in most with diabetes because benefits outweigh small glucose effects.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Kidney & liver checks</p>
        <p>Periodic labs track safety and response, especially with dose changes or combination therapy.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Follow-up -->
  <section id="followup" class="section">
    <h2>Follow-up timing</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">After changes</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Re-check lipids typically in ~6–12 weeks after starting or adjusting therapy</li>
          <li>Once stable, monitor periodically as advised</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Beyond numbers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep up with blood pressure, A1C (if diabetes), kidney function</li>
          <li>Review diet, activity, weight trend, and smoking status</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do eggs raise cholesterol?</p>
        <p>Dietary cholesterol has a smaller effect than saturated fat. Overall pattern matters more; ask how eggs fit your plan.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I raise HDL with supplements?</p>
        <p>Chasing HDL increases hasn’t shown outcome benefit. Focus on LDL reduction and lifestyle.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need to fast for every test?</p>
        <p>Not usually. Fasting helps when triglycerides are high or the first time results are unclear.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if I can’t tolerate a statin?</p>
        <p>Options include dose changes, different statins, alternate-day dosing, or adding non-statins like ezetimibe or PCSK9 inhibitors.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
