      <!-- 3 CBT-I basics -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> Insomnia help: CBT-I basics (gentle version) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Same wake time daily.</strong> This anchors your body clock (even after a rough night).</li>
            <li><strong>Bed = sleep & intimacy only.</strong> If awake &gt; ~20 minutes, get up to a quiet chair with dim light; return sleepy.</li>
            <li><strong>Short sleep window (temporarily).</strong> Start with your average total sleep time (never below ~6 h without clinician guidance). Adjust earlier by 15–30 minutes once sleep becomes solid.</li>
            <li><strong>Wind-down routine (30–60 min).</strong> Dim lights, gentle stretch, slow breathing, calming audio—same order each night.</li>
            <li><strong>Daytime pillars.</strong> Light outdoor time, movement, and regular meals support better sleep.</li>
          </ol>

      <!-- 4 Light, movement, naps -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Light, movement, and naps (circadian helpers) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Morning light:</strong> 20–30 minutes outdoors or by a bright window soon after waking.</li>
            <li><strong>Move most days:</strong> Short walks after meals, simple strength/balance; finish vigorous exercise 3–4 h before bed.</li>
            <li><strong>Naps:</strong> If needed, keep to 20–30 minutes before mid-afternoon.</li>
            <li><strong>Evening dim:</strong> Lower lights and screens 1–2 h before bed; use “night mode.”</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 5 Comfort: pain, GERD, temperature, noise -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Comfort fixes: pain, GERD, temperature, noise <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Pain & stiffness</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Evening stretch/heat or brief walk; schedule pain meds as directed.</li>
                <li>Support pillows for back/knees/shoulders; try side-sleeping if snoring.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Reflux (GERD)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Finish supper 3+ hours before bed; avoid heavy/fatty meals late.</li>
                <li>Elevate head of bed 6–8 inches; discuss reflux plan with clinician.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Bathroom trips</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Limit late fluids; elevate legs for 30–60 min in evening to shift fluid earlier.</li>
                <li>Night-lights and clear path for safety.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Room setup</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Cool, dark, and quiet; fan or white-noise; comfortable, breathable bedding.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 6 Sleep apnea & snoring -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Snoring & sleep apnea (when to test) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Consider evaluation if you snore loudly, stop breathing during sleep, wake choking, or feel very sleepy by day. Treatment (like CPAP) can boost energy, mood, and heart-brain health.</p>
          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Simple STOP questions (screening prompt)">
            <table class="responsive">
              <thead><tr><th>Question</th><th>Yes/No</th></tr></thead>
              <tbody>
                <tr><td data-col="Question">Do you Snore loudly?</td><td data-col="Yes/No">If yes, discuss with clinician</td></tr>
                <tr><td data-col="Question">Do you feel Tired or sleepy in daytime?</td><td data-col="Yes/No">Common with apnea</td></tr>
                <tr><td data-col="Question">Has anyone Observed you stop breathing?</td><td data-col="Yes/No">Key sign</td></tr>
                <tr><td data-col="Question">Do you have high blood Pressure?</td><td data-col="Yes/No">Raises risk</td></tr>
              </tbody>
            </table>
          </div>
          <p class="muted">Side-sleeping, avoiding alcohol/sedatives near bedtime, and weight management (if advised) can help while awaiting testing.</p>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 7 RLS / REM behavior / leg cramps -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Restless legs, REM behavior, leg cramps <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Restless legs (RLS)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Check ferritin/iron; treat deficiency if present.</li>
                <li>Evening leg stretches, massage, warm bath; review meds (some worsen RLS).</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>REM behavior disorder</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Make the sleep area safe (pad corners, move sharp objects, low bed).</li>
                <li>Clinician may suggest specific treatments; avoid sleep deprivation & alcohol.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Leg cramps</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Hydration through day; calf stretches before bed; steady electrolytes via food.</li>
                <li>Discuss persistent cramps before trying supplements.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 8 Medicines & supplements -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> Medicines & supplements: cautions for seniors <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Medicines & supplements">
            <table class="responsive">
              <thead><tr><th>Item</th><th>Notes</th></tr></thead>
              <tbody>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Item">Antihistamines (diphenhydramine, doxylamine)</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Can cause confusion, dry mouth, constipation, falls—<strong>generally avoid</strong> in older adults unless clinician advises.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Item">Alcohol</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Disrupts sleep cycles and worsens snoring/apnea. Best avoided near bedtime.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Item">Caffeine & decongestants</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Stop caffeine by early afternoon; some cold meds act like stimulants.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Item">Melatonin</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Low dose (e.g., 0.5–1 mg) earlier in evening sometimes helps timing issues; discuss with clinician and pharmacist.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Item">Prescription sleep meds</td>
                  <td data-col="Notes">Use cautiously and short-term when needed; CBT-I is first-line for chronic insomnia.</td>
                </tr>

            <li>Sit at bed edge before standing; use cane/walker as taught.</li>
            <li>Keep water and a charged phone within reach; avoid rushing.</li>

      <!-- 10 Caregivers -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">9</span> Caregivers & family: how to help sleep <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Keep a <strong>simple, steady routine</strong> (mealtimes, light walk, wind-down).</li>
            <li>Reduce evening noise and stimulation; soft lighting; avoid arguments at bedtime.</li>
            <li>For dementia: keep daytime activity and daylight; avoid long naps; reassure, don’t quiz.</li>
            <li>Track snoring, movements, and wake times for the next visit.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>

      <!-- 11 Myths -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">10</span> Myths to ignore <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>“I must sleep 8 hours every night.”</strong> Needs vary. Aim for refreshed days, not a number.</li>
            <li><strong>“Staying in bed longer makes up for poor sleep.”</strong> It often backfires—keep a steady wake time.</li>
            <li><strong>“A nightcap helps sleep.”</strong> Alcohol fragments sleep and worsens snoring/apnea.</li>
          </ul>
