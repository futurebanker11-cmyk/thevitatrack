  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients (base + sauce)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>250 g (about 9 oz) <strong>boneless skinless chicken</strong>, thinly sliced (breast or thigh)</li>
          <li>2 tsp <strong>oil</strong> (canola/peanut/olive)</li>
          <li>3 cups mixed <strong>veggies</strong>, bite-size: broccoli, bell pepper, carrots (thin), mushrooms, zucchini, snap peas, onions</li>
          <li>1 tsp <strong>cornstarch</strong> (optional, for light thickening)</li>
          <li>1 tsp <strong>fresh ginger</strong> and/or 1 clove garlic, minced (optional, mild)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Light sauce (low-sodium friendly)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>2 tbsp <strong>reduced-sodium soy sauce</strong> (or coconut aminos)</li>
          <li>2 tbsp <strong>water</strong> or low-sodium broth</li>
          <li>1 tsp <strong>rice vinegar</strong> or lemon juice</li>
          <li>½–1 tsp <strong>honey</strong> (optional)</li>
          <li>Pinch of pepper; chili flakes optional</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Tenderness tip: slice chicken across the grain; for extra softness, marinate in ½ tsp oil + ½ tsp cornstarch + 1 tsp water for 10 minutes.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prep (5 min)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Whisk <strong>sauce</strong> ingredients. If using cornstarch, stir it into the sauce now.</li>
          <li>Pat chicken dry; season lightly with pepper.</li>
          <li>Arrange veggies by <em>cook time</em>: firm (carrot/broccoli) → medium (peppers/mushrooms) → tender (zucchini/snap peas).</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Stir-fry (8–10 min)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Heat pan/wok over medium-high; add 1 tsp oil.</li>
          <li>Cook chicken 3–4 min, stirring until just cooked through (no pink; juices clear). Remove to a plate.</li>
          <li>Add 1 tsp oil; stir-fry firm veggies 2–3 min, add medium veggies 1–2 min, then tender veggies 1 min. Add ginger/garlic last 30 sec if using.</li>
          <li>Return chicken; pour in sauce; toss 30–60 sec until glossy and gently thickened.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">Food safety: cook chicken to <strong>74 °C / 165 °F</strong>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Go <strong>veg-heavy</strong> (3 cups) and keep starch small: ½ cup cooked brown rice or 1 small roti.</li>
          <li>Avoid sugary sauces; keep honey ≤ ½ tsp or skip.</li>
          <li>Add healthy fat (1 tsp olive oil) to improve satiety.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>reduced-sodium</strong> soy or coconut aminos; skip added salt.</li>
          <li>Cook in canola/olive oil; avoid deep-frying.</li>
          <li>Flavor with ginger, pepper, lemon, and herbs.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Favor lower-potassium veg (peppers, zucchini, onions, mushrooms).</li>
        </ul>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep protein portion moderate (85–100 g cooked chicken per serving) per your plan.</li>
          <li>Choose low-sodium sauces; discuss soy/coconut aminos with your clinician.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>mild seasonings</strong>; limit garlic/chili if triggering.</li>
          <li>Smaller, earlier meals; avoid eating within 3 hours of bed.</li>
          <li>Skip large onion amounts; add ginger and herbs instead.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Chew-soft tip:</strong> Slice chicken thinly across the grain; cook veggies until tender-crisp or softer if needed.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Make-ahead & storage</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prep once</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pre-slice chicken (same day) and veg (2–3 days in fridge).</li>
          <li>Mix dry sauce ingredients in a jar; add water/lemon just before cooking.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Leftovers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Refrigerate up to 2 days. Reheat gently 1–2 min; add a splash of water to prevent dryness.</li>
          <li>Avoid repeated reheats; portion out before storing.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Tips -->
  <section id="tips" class="section">
    <h2>Cook tips & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use a <strong>large pan</strong> to avoid steaming; cook in batches if needed.</li>
          <li>Keep heat medium-high but controlled; stir often to prevent sticking.</li>
          <li>For extra gloss without salt: finish with lemon zest and a drizzle of sesame or olive oil.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Food safety: separate raw chicken from veg; wash hands/boards/knives. Cook to 74 °C/165 °F. Refrigerate within 2 hours.

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Breast or thigh?</p>
        <p>Thigh stays juicier; breast is leaner. Both work when sliced thin and not overcooked.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">No soy?</p>
        <p>Use coconut aminos + lemon + pepper; add herbs for flavor depth.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Thick sauce without excess sodium?</p>
        <p>Use 1 tsp cornstarch in the sauce and reduce briefly; avoid extra soy.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Side ideas?</p>
        <p>Small brown rice portion, cauliflower “rice,” or simple cucumber salad.</p>
      </div>
