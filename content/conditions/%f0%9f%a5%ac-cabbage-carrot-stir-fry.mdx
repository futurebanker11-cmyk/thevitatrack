  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients & portions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2–3 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Cabbage:</strong> 3 cups (200–250 g) finely shredded</li>
          <li><strong>Carrot:</strong> 1 medium (90–100 g) grated or julienned</li>
          <li><strong>Oil:</strong> 2 tsp olive or neutral oil</li>
          <li><strong>Aromatics (mild):</strong> ½ tsp grated ginger; ¼ tsp turmeric (optional)</li>
          <li><strong>Seasoning:</strong> black pepper to taste; lemon juice (½ lemon)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Keep spices gentle; avoid chili powders for reflux-friendly results.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Protein:</strong> 2 tbsp roasted chana dal (pounded), or scrambled egg stirred in at the end</li>
          <li><strong>Fiber:</strong> 1 tbsp oats sprinkled and steamed in for 1 min (makes it softer)</li>
          <li><strong>Herbs:</strong> coriander/dill for aroma; a pinch of sesame for nuttiness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Stir-fry steps (soft texture)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ol class="list">
          <li><strong>Warm pan:</strong> Heat a wide non-stick pan on medium-low. Add oil.</li>
          <li><strong>Aromatics:</strong> Sauté ginger 20–30 sec (and turmeric if using).</li>
          <li><strong>Veg in:</strong> Add cabbage and carrot. Toss gently for 2–3 min.</li>
          <li><strong>Steam-soften:</strong> Sprinkle 2–3 tbsp water; cover 2–4 min until tender but not mushy. Stir once.</li>
          <li><strong>Finish:</strong> Lemon and pepper. No added salt needed.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Extra-soft option:</strong> Cook covered 1–2 minutes longer with a spoon of water, then mash lightly with a spatula.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use as a large non-starchy side. Pair with protein (dal/egg/fish) and a small whole grain portion.</li>
          <li>Skip sugary sauces; lemon and pepper add flavor without carbs.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>No added salt or soy sauce.</strong> Use herbs and lemon for brightness.</li>
          <li>Keep oil ~2 tsp total for the pan.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cabbage is kidney-friendly; keep carrot moderate. Avoid salty condiments.</li>
          <li>Portion based on your potassium plan; season with lemon, not salt.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skip chilies/onion if sensitive; stick to ginger and turmeric.</li>
          <li>Serve warm, not hot; avoid large late dinners.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Chewing/swallowing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Shred finely and cook covered a bit longer; moisten with a spoon of water or curd.</li>
          <li>Test tenderness with a fork before serving.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & storage</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Cut size:</strong> Fine shreds cook evenly and are easier to chew.</li>
          <li><strong>Temperature:</strong> Serve warm; let steam settle to prevent mouth burns.</li>
          <li><strong>Storage:</strong> Refrigerate within 2 hours; use in 2 days. Reheat gently with a spoon of water.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card danger">
        <strong>High-sodium sauces:</strong> Avoid soy/stock cubes unless sodium fits your plan. Choose lemon, pepper, and herbs instead.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Serve -->
  <section id="serve" class="section">
    <h2>How to serve</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>With dal and a small bowl of brown rice or millet</li>
          <li>Alongside grilled fish or paneer for protein</li>
          <li>As a soft filling in a whole-wheat chapati roll (lightly smeared with curd)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Portion cue:</strong> ~<strong>¾–1 cup</strong> per person as a side; increase for a veg-forward plate.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I avoid a raw smell?</p>
        <p>Start with a brief sauté of ginger and a pinch of turmeric, then steam the veg covered 2–4 minutes.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I add onion?</p>
        <p>Yes, if tolerated. Use a small amount, cook until soft, and skip if reflux flares.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if it dries out?</p>
        <p>Sprinkle 1–2 tbsp water, cover for 30–60 seconds, and turn off the heat to trap moisture.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make-ahead tips?</p>
        <p>Shred and store veg in the fridge for a day. Stir-fry fresh for best texture; reheat gently with a splash of water.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
