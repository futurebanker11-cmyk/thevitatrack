  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What it is</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> <strong>Memory loss</strong> has many causes. <strong>Dementia</strong> is a group of conditions with ongoing decline in memory and thinking that affects daily life. Early recognition helps plan supports, reduce risk, and maintain independence longer.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Normal aging vs. dementia:</strong> Occasional forgetfulness (misplacing items, names coming later) is common. Dementia affects daily function (repeating questions, getting lost, trouble with bills/meds).</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Sudden weakness on one side, face droop, slurred speech, new confusion (possible stroke)</li>
        <li>Fever with new confusion, severe dehydration, or uncontrolled blood sugars</li>
        <li>Falls or safety threats (leaving stove on, wandering, driving danger)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Sudden changes are emergencies. Call your local emergency number immediately.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">

          <li>Word-finding trouble; getting lost in familiar places</li>
          <li>Personality or mood changes, apathy, anxiety, irritability</li>
          <li>Sleep changes; visual hallucinations (some types)</li>

  <!-- Types -->
  <section id="types" class="section">
    <h2>Common types of memory disorders</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Types of dementia">
      <thead><tr><th>Type</th><th>Key features</th><th>Clues</th></tr></thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI)</strong></td>
          <td>Noticeable memory/thinking change but daily function mostly intact</td>
          <td>Higher risk of dementia; monitor, support habits</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Alzheimer’s disease</strong></td>
          <td>Memory first; later language, orientation, daily tasks</td>
          <td>Slow gradual decline; repetitive questions, misplacing items</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Vascular dementia</strong></td>
          <td>Thinking speed/attention problems; stepwise decline</td>
          <td>Past stroke, high BP, diabetes, cholesterol</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dementia with Lewy bodies</strong></td>
          <td>Fluctuating attention, visual hallucinations, dream enactment sleep</td>
          <td>Parkinsonism (slowness, stiffness), sensitivity to antipsychotics</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Frontotemporal dementia</strong></td>
          <td>Behavior/impulse or language changes early</td>
          <td>Earlier onset than Alzheimer’s in many cases</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Mixed dementia</strong></td>
          <td>Alzheimer’s + vascular (common in older adults)</td>
          <td>Manage blood pressure, glucose, activity, sleep</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Reversible -->
  <section id="reversible" class="section">
    <h2>Reversible causes to check</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hearing/vision loss, depression/anxiety, poor sleep (sleep apnea), pain</li>
          <li>Medication side effects (sedatives, anticholinergics, some sleep pills)</li>
          <li>Thyroid problems, low vitamin B12/folate, infection, dehydration</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Tip:</strong> Bring all medicines (including over-the-counter, herbal) to appointments. Ask which may cloud thinking or increase falls.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Testing -->
  <section id="testing" class="section">
    <h2>How doctors evaluate memory loss</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">First steps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>History with a family member/caregiver for examples of change</li>
          <li>Brief cognitive tests; mood screening for depression/anxiety</li>
          <li>Blood tests: B12, thyroid (TSH), electrolytes, kidney/liver; others as needed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sometimes</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Brain imaging (MRI/CT) to look for stroke, bleeding, tumors, hydrocephalus</li>
          <li>Sleep evaluation (sleep apnea), hearing/vision assessment</li>
          <li>Medication review with deprescribing plan if appropriate</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment & support</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lifestyle foundations</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hearing aids/vision care; regular physical activity; daylight exposure</li>
          <li>Mediterranean-style eating, hydration, blood pressure & glucose control</li>
          <li>Sleep routine; treat pain, anxiety, depression</li>
          <li>Cognitive engagement: conversation, music, puzzles, learning</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines (may help some types)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cholinesterase inhibitors (e.g., donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine)</li>
          <li>Memantine for moderate–severe Alzheimer’s</li>
          <li>Discuss benefits/risks; monitor side effects (nausea, slow heart rate, sleep changes)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">Non-drug supports are essential for all types. Medicine decisions are individualized with your clinician.</div>
  </section>

    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">

        <p class="kicker">Wandering & agitation</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Daily walks and daylight; calm evening routine</li>
          <li>Door alarms; ID card/bracelet; share a comforting playlist</li>
          <li>Avoid arguments—redirect with a task, photo album, or beverage</li>
        </ul>

    <h2>Driving & safety decisions</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Warning signs: getting lost, scrapes on car, near-misses, family concern</li>
        <li>Ask for an on-road assessment; consider limiting to daylight/local routes</li>
        <li>Plan alternatives early: family schedule, ride shares, community transport</li>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can depression look like dementia?</p>
        <p>Yes. Low mood and poor sleep can slow thinking. Treating depression often improves memory and attention.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do puzzles prevent dementia?</p>
        <p>Puzzles help keep the brain active, but the strongest benefits come from a mix: movement, social connection, sleep, hearing/vision care, and control of BP/sugars.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are there cures?</p>
        <p>No cures yet for most dementias. Early supports and risk control can slow decline and improve quality of life.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I change medicines?</p>
        <p>Never stop or add medicines without medical advice. Ask your clinician which ones may worsen thinking or falls.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
