  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is hearing loss?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> A reduction in the ability to hear sounds clearly. In seniors, it’s often gradual (<strong>presbycusis</strong>) and affects speech understanding, especially in noise. Treating it supports communication, mood, cognition, and safety.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Identify the type and cause, then choose the right supports: medical treatment when possible, plus hearing technology and communication strategies.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>Urgent red flags</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Sudden hearing loss</strong> in one ear over hours–days (with or without ear fullness/ringing)</li>
        <li>Hearing loss with <strong>severe ear pain</strong>, fever, or <strong>pus/bloody discharge</strong></li>
        <li>New <strong>unequal hearing</strong>, facial weakness, or severe dizziness</li>
        <li>Head trauma with hearing change</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Sudden sensorineural loss is time-sensitive — contact urgent care/ENT promptly.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common signs</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Asking people to repeat; “muffled” speech, especially with background noise</li>
          <li>Turning the TV up; difficulty hearing high-pitched voices</li>
          <li>Ringing or buzzing (<strong>tinnitus</strong>)</li>
          <li>Withdrawing from conversations; fatigue after social events</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Impact areas</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Safety: missed alarms, traffic sounds</li>
          <li>Health: medication instructions, telehealth calls</li>
          <li>Mood & cognition: isolation and memory strain</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Types & causes -->
  <section id="types" class="section">
    <h2>Types & causes</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Type</th><th scope="col">Where the problem is</th><th scope="col">Common causes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sensorineural</strong></td>
          <td>Inner ear (cochlea) or nerve</td>
          <td>Age-related loss (presbycusis), noise exposure, ototoxic medicines, Meniere’s, sudden loss, tumors (rare)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Conductive</strong></td>
          <td>Ear canal, eardrum, or middle ear</td>
          <td>Wax blockage, ear infection, fluid, <strong>otosclerosis</strong>, eardrum perforation</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Mixed</strong></td>
          <td>Combination of both</td>
          <td>Sensorineural loss plus wax/fluid or other middle-ear issue</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">
      Some medicines can affect hearing (loop diuretics, aminoglycosides, platinum chemotherapy, high-dose salicylates). Always review new symptoms with your clinician.
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>How it’s tested</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Clinic checks</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Ear exam for wax, infection, or eardrum problems</li>
          <li><strong>Tuning fork</strong> (Weber/Rinne) to screen type</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Audiology testing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Audiogram</strong>: measures softest sounds you hear at different pitches</li>
          <li><strong>Speech testing</strong> (clarity and in noise)</li>
          <li><strong>Tympanometry</strong>: middle-ear pressure/fluid; <strong>acoustic reflexes</strong></li>
          <li>Imaging (e.g., MRI) only if specific concerns (asymmetry, sudden loss, neurological signs)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment & devices -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment & devices</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medical/surgical (cause-based)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Wax removal</strong>, treat infections or fluid</li>
          <li><strong>Sudden sensorineural loss</strong>: prompt ENT assessment; steroids may be considered</li>
          <li><strong>Otosclerosis</strong>: hearing aids or surgery in selected cases</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Hearing aids</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Behind-the-ear (BTE), receiver-in-canal (RIC), in-the-ear (ITE) styles</li>
          <li>Features: directional mics, noise reduction, rechargeable, Bluetooth</li>
          <li>Fit and real-ear measurements improve results; allow an <strong>adaptation period</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Implants & other tech</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Cochlear implant</strong> for severe-to-profound sensorineural loss with limited aid benefit</li>
          <li><strong>Bone conduction</strong> devices for conductive or single-sided loss</li>
          <li>Assistive listening: TV streamers, remote mics, captioned calls, alerting devices</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Address both ears when appropriate — <strong>binaural</strong> hearing helps localization and hearing in noise.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Communication -->
  <section id="communication" class="section">
    <h2>Communication tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">For the listener</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Face the speaker; ask for <strong>clear speech</strong> at a normal pace</li>
          <li>Reduce background noise; move closer; add light for lip cues</li>
          <li>Confirm key details (“So 3 pm on Thursday at the clinic?”)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">For family & friends</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Get attention first, then speak; rephrase rather than just repeat</li>
          <li>One person talks at a time; avoid calling from another room</li>
          <li>Use captions for TV and video calls</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily care & safety -->
  <section id="daily" class="section">
    <h2>Daily care & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Device care</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Charge nightly or store with batteries off; keep <strong>dry box</strong> or desiccant</li>
          <li>Clean microphones and domes; replace wax guards as instructed</li>
          <li>Schedule follow-up for adjustments; check fit if sore spots</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Add <strong>visual alerts</strong> (doorbell, smoke alarm), consider vibrating alarm clocks, and keep important numbers in large print near phones.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions for your audiologist/ENT</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>What type and degree of hearing loss do I have?</li>
        <li>Is there a medical cause to treat first (wax, infection, sudden loss)?</li>
        <li>Which hearing aid style/features match my needs and budget?</li>
        <li>Would I benefit from accessories (remote mic, TV streamer) or a cochlear implant evaluation?</li>
        <li>How often should my hearing and devices be rechecked?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Hearing loss — quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do hearing aids restore normal hearing?</p>
        <p>No, but they can significantly improve clarity and reduce listening effort, especially when fitted and tuned properly.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">I can hear sounds but can’t understand words — why?</p>
        <p>High-frequency loss affects consonants and clarity. Directional mics, remote mics, and communication strategies help in noise.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When to consider a cochlear implant?</p>
        <p>Severe-to-profound loss with limited benefit from well-fitted hearing aids; an implant center can test candidacy.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is tinnitus related?</p>
        <p>Often. Amplification can reduce tinnitus awareness; sound therapy and stress management also help.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
