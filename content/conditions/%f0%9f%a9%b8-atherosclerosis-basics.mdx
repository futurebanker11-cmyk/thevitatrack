      <!-- 1 Pathophysiology -->
      <details class="section" open>
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">1</span> Pathophysiology (sequence and biology) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Endothelial dysfunction:</strong> shear stress, hypertension, hyperglycemia, and tobacco toxins impair nitric oxide signaling and barrier integrity.</li>
            <li><strong>Subendothelial LDL retention:</strong> ApoB-containing particles infiltrate and bind intimal proteoglycans; susceptibility increases with small dense LDL and elevated non-HDL-C.</li>
            <li><strong>Oxidative modification & innate immunity:</strong> oxidized LDL triggers macrophage recruitment, foam-cell formation, and cytokine release.</li>
            <li><strong>Plaque progression:</strong> necrotic core, neovascularization, fibrous cap remodeling; thin-cap fibroatheroma prone to rupture → thrombosis.</li>
            <li><strong>Clinical expression:</strong> CAD, cerebrovascular disease, and PAD via flow limitation and/or plaque rupture with thrombosis.</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 2 Risk stratification & targets -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> Risk stratification, lipid targets, adjunctive goals <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div>
              <h3>Risk tiers (conceptual)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li><strong>Very high risk:</strong> ASCVD or equivalents (e.g., prior MI, stroke/TIA, symptomatic PAD), diabetes with target-organ damage, or markedly elevated LDL-C.</li>
                <li><strong>High risk:</strong> multiple risk factors (age, hypertension, smoking, CKD, strong family history) or high coronary calcium.</li>
                <li><strong>Intermediate/primary prevention:</strong> elevated calculated 10-yr risk without established ASCVD.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div>
              <h3>Targets (typical frameworks)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li><strong>LDL-C:</strong> stepwise reduction with threshold-based intensification; non-HDL-C as secondary target when triglycerides are elevated.</li>
                <li><strong>Blood pressure:</strong> individualized control to reduce shear stress and cerebrovascular/coronary events.</li>
                <li><strong>Glycemia:</strong> A1C targets individualized; insulin resistance contributes to atherogenesis.</li>
                <li><strong>Weight/waist, smoking status, physical activity:</strong> direct impact on lipid phenotype and endothelial biology.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 3 Diagnostics -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Diagnostics & imaging <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Lipids:</strong> fasting or non-fasting lipid panel (LDL-C, HDL-C, TG, non-HDL-C); ApoB and Lp(a) in selected contexts (familial risk, premature ASCVD).</li>
            <li><strong>Coronary artery calcium (CAC):</strong> refines risk in borderline cases; higher CAC indicates higher event rates.</li>
            <li><strong>Functional testing:</strong> stress ECG, stress echo, or nuclear perfusion for symptomatic patients or ambiguity.</li>
            <li><strong>CT coronary angiography:</strong> plaque characterization; useful in selected diagnostic scenarios.</li>
            <li><strong>Vascular beds:</strong> carotid duplex (stenosis), ABI for PAD (with toe-brachial index when calcified vessels).</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 4 Therapeutics -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Therapeutics: lipid lowering & risk modification <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Lipids (evidence-based)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li><strong>Statins:</strong> first-line LDL-C lowering; robust outcome reduction across risk categories.</li>
                <li><strong>Ezetimibe:</strong> adjunct or alternative for further LDL-C reduction.</li>
                <li><strong>PCSK9 inhibitors:</strong> potent LDL-C lowering for very high risk or familial hypercholesterolemia.</li>
                <li><strong>Hypertriglyceridemia:</strong> address secondary causes; consider prescription omega-3s or fibrates when severe (pancreatitis risk mitigation).</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Global risk control</h3>
              <ul>
                <li><strong>Antiplatelet therapy:</strong> secondary prevention per indication; avoid routine primary prevention without clear net benefit.</li>
                <li><strong>Blood pressure control:</strong> reduce afterload and vascular injury dynamics; home BP logs improve titration.</li>
                <li><strong>Glycemic management:</strong> individualized A1C with attention to hypoglycemia and renal status.</li>
                <li><strong>Tobacco cessation, weight optimization, activity prescription:</strong> foundational for endothelial recovery.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 5 Nutrition & activity -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Nutrition & activity prescriptions <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div>
              <h3>Dietary pattern</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Mediterranean-style: vegetables, legumes, whole grains, nuts/seeds, olive oil; fish ≥2×/week.</li>
                <li>Reduce industrial trans fats; moderate saturated fat; prioritize unsaturated fats.</li>
                <li>Soluble fiber (oats, barley, pulses) for LDL-C reduction; sodium moderation supports BP control.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div>
              <h3>Activity</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Regular aerobic volume (e.g., brisk walking), plus resistance and balance work as appropriate.</li>
                <li>Progressive, individualized dosing with attention to orthopedic and cardiopulmonary status.</li>
              </ul>

      <!-- 6 Follow-up -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Monitoring & follow-up <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Lipids:</strong> recheck 6–12 weeks post-initiation/intensification, then 3–12 months per stability and risk.</li>
            <li><strong>BP/A1C/weight/waist:</strong> track per comorbid status and therapeutic changes.</li>
            <li><strong>Adherence & tolerance:</strong> review muscle symptoms, interactions, and lifestyle adherence; adjust therapy accordingly.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 7 Red flags -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> Red-flag presentations (urgent evaluation) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Acute chest pain/pressure</strong> (especially with radiation, dyspnea, diaphoresis, or nausea).</li>
            <li><strong>Focal neurologic deficit</strong> (sudden weakness, facial droop, speech/vision change).</li>
            <li><strong>Acute limb ischemia signs</strong> (pain, pallor, pulselessness, paresthesia, paralysis, poikilothermia).</li>
            <li><strong>Syncope with exertion</strong> or palpitations/new arrhythmia symptoms.</li>
            <li><strong>Rest pain</strong> or non-healing ulcers in peripheral artery disease.</li>
            <li><strong>Severe shortness of breath</strong> at rest or new orthopnea.</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="note warn"><strong>Emergency:</strong> When in doubt, call emergency services immediately.</div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 8 Practical orders & labs (optional use) -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Practical checklist (clinic workflow) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Baseline</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Lipids: LDL-C, HDL-C, TG, non-HDL-C; consider ApoB, Lp(a).</li>
                <li>BP, A1C (or fasting glucose), CMP (liver/renal), TSH if indicated.</li>
                <li>ASCVD risk estimate; CAC for risk refinement if borderline.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Initiate/adjust</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Start/intensify statin per risk; add ezetimibe or PCSK9i if needed.</li>
                <li>Antiplatelet for secondary prevention; avoid routine primary prevention.</li>
                <li>Lifestyle Rx: diet pattern, activity minutes/week, tobacco plan.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Follow-up</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Recheck lipids 6–12 weeks; document % LDL-C change and absolute level.</li>
                <li>Assess symptoms/side effects; review adherence and interactions.</li>
                <li>Adjust targets with age, comorbidity, and tolerance.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>

        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">9</span> FAQs <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Is atherosclerosis reversible?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Plaque stabilization and partial regression are possible with intensive LDL-C lowering and risk-factor control. Preventing progression and rupture is the core aim.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Do I need fasting labs?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Non-fasting panels are often acceptable; fasting may be preferred when triglycerides are high or for baseline comparability. Follow lab instructions.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">What if I can’t tolerate a statin?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Options include dose/agent change, alternate-day dosing, ezetimibe, or PCSK9 inhibitors. Discuss risks/benefits and monitoring with your clinician.</p>
