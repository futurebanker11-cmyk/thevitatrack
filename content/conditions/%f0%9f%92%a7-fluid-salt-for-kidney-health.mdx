      <!-- 1 Why it matters -->
      <details class="section" open>
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">1</span> Why fluids & salt matter for kidneys <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Kidneys balance water and minerals. Too little fluid can concentrate wastes and raise kidney stress; too much fluid (for some people) can cause swelling and shortness of breath. Excess sodium (salt) pulls water into the bloodstream, raising blood pressure and fluid load. Finding <em>your</em> safe middle helps blood pressure, swelling, and overall kidney comfort.</p>

      <!-- 2 How much to drink -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> How much should I drink? (personal targets) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>General idea</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Most adults feel best with steady sips across the day, aiming for <strong>pale-yellow urine</strong>.</li>
                <li>Targets vary with kidney function, medications (e.g., diuretics), weather, and heart or liver conditions.</li>
                <li>If your clinician gave a specific fluid limit or goal—<strong>follow that</strong>.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Signs of “too little” vs “too much”</h3>
              <ul>
                <li><strong>Too little:</strong> darker urine, dizziness, dry mouth, constipation</li>
                <li><strong>Too much (for some):</strong> ankle swelling, sudden weight gain, worsening shortness of breath</li>
                <li>Weigh daily if advised; report rapid changes (e.g., ≥2 lb / 1 kg overnight)</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

          <div class="note tip" style="margin-top:10px">
            <strong>Night urination?</strong> Shift more fluids earlier. Try a 2–3 hour evening cut-off unless told otherwise.
          </div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 3 Sodium basics -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Sodium basics: where it hides & simple limits <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Most sodium comes from packaged foods and restaurant meals—not the shaker. A lower-sodium pattern supports blood pressure and swelling control.</p>

          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Sodium guide and label decoding">
            <table>
              <thead>
                <tr><th>On the label</th><th>Plain meaning</th><th>Quick tip</th></tr>
              </thead>
              <tbody>
                <tr><td>“Sodium (mg)” per serving</td><td>Amount of sodium in <em>each</em> serving</td><td>Aim for lower numbers; watch portion size</td></tr>
                <tr><td>“% Daily Value”</td><td>Percent of a standard daily max</td><td>5% is low, 20%+ is high</td></tr>
                <tr><td>“Low sodium” claim</td><td>≤ 140 mg per serving</td><td>Good everyday choice</td></tr>
                <tr><td>“No added salt”</td><td>Salt not added during processing</td><td>Still check the number</td></tr>
              </tbody>
            </table>
          </div>

          <div class="two" style="margin-top:12px">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>High-sodium common foods</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Canned soups, instant noodles, boxed meals</li>
                <li>Processed meats (sausages, deli meats, bacon)</li>
                <li>Pickles, sauces (soy, teriyaki), salty snacks</li>
                <li>Restaurant/fast foods, pizza</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Better swaps</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>“No-salt-added” canned tomatoes/beans; rinse canned beans</li>
                <li>Fresh or frozen veggies; herb/lemon-based seasoning</li>
                <li>Plain yogurt, oats, unsalted nuts (check portions)</li>
                <li>Grilled/roasted lean protein with herbs, lemon, garlic</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

          <div class="note" style="margin-top:10px">
            <strong>Salt substitutes caution:</strong> Many use potassium chloride. If you have kidney disease or take medicines that raise potassium, ask your clinician before using them.
          </div>

      <!-- 4 Day plan -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> A simple day plan (timing that works) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Morning</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Start with a cup of water after waking</li>
                <li>Choose low-sodium breakfast (oats + fruit; eggs with herbs instead of salt)</li>
                <li>Take diuretics when prescribed <em>earlier</em> in the day (ask for your best time)</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Midday/Afternoon</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Steady sips; keep urine pale</li>
                <li>Lunch: “no-salt-added” base + herbs/lemon; watch sauces</li>
                <li>If you walk, try 5–10 mins after meals</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Evening</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Shift larger drinks earlier; small sips only within 2–3 hours of bed if nocturia</li>
                <li>Choose low-salt dinner sides; taste before salting</li>
                <li>Prepare next-day water bottle to avoid “catch-up” at night</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

          <div class="note tip" style="margin-top:10px">
            <strong>Restaurant trick:</strong> Ask for sauces on the side, choose grilled over breaded, and split salty items. Enjoy the company—bring leftover home.
          </div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 5 Beverages -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Best beverages & what to limit <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Beverage guide">
            <table>
              <thead>
                <tr><th>Beverage</th><th>General note</th><th>Tips</th></tr>
              </thead>
              <tbody>
                <tr><td>Water (plain or with lemon/lime)</td><td>Great base for most</td><td>Lemon/lime adds taste; may help some stone-formers</td></tr>
                <tr><td>Unsweetened tea</td><td>Okay in moderation</td><td>Watch caffeine near bedtime; herbal in evening</td></tr>
                <tr><td>Coffee</td><td>Usually fine in moderation</td><td>Can increase urination; avoid late-day if causing nocturia</td></tr>
                <tr><td>Sugary drinks</td><td>Not ideal</td><td>Add empty calories; choose water + fruit slice</td></tr>
                <tr><td>Sports/electrolyte drinks</td><td>Often unnecessary</td><td>Check sodium; many are salty or sugary</td></tr>
                <tr><td>Alcohol</td><td>Individualized</td><td>Ask your clinician; can affect BP, sleep, and meds</td></tr>
              </tbody>
            </table>

      <!-- 6 Meds & special notes -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Medications & special situations <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Common medications affecting fluids/salt</h3>
              <ul>
                <li><strong>Diuretics (“water pills”):</strong> planned urination; follow timing advice</li>
                <li><strong>ACE inhibitors / ARBs:</strong> may raise potassium; ask before using salt substitutes</li>
                <li><strong>NSAIDs (pain relievers):</strong> some can stress kidneys—ask about safer options</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>When to call</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>New/worse swelling of feet, sudden weight gain</li>
                <li>Rising blood pressure or worsening shortness of breath</li>
                <li>Very dark urine, dizziness, fainting, or confusion</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 7 Shopping list & swaps -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> Smart shopping: low-salt pantry & flavor swaps <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Pantry</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>No-salt-added canned tomatoes/beans; low-sodium broths</li>
                <li>Plain oats, brown rice, quinoa</li>
                <li>Unsalted nuts/seeds (portion aware)</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Flavor boosters</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Lemon/lime, vinegar splash</li>
                <li>Garlic, onion, pepper, paprika, herbs</li>
                <li>Fresh herbs: parsley, dill, cilantro, basil</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>What to limit</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Instant soups/noodles, boxed meal kits</li>
                <li>Pickles, soy/teriyaki sauce, BBQ sauce</li>
                <li>Processed meats and salty snacks</li>
              </ul>
            </div>

        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">9</span> FAQs <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Is there a single “right” amount of water for everyone?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">No. The safest target depends on kidney function, other conditions, medications, and your clinician’s advice. Use pale urine as a general guide unless you were given a strict limit.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Should I avoid salt substitutes?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Many salt substitutes contain potassium. If you have kidney disease or take medicines that raise potassium, ask before using them.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">How can I tell if a packaged food is “salty”?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Check “Sodium (mg)” and “% Daily Value.” As a quick rule, 5% DV is low and 20%+ is high. Compare brands—there’s often a lower-sodium choice.</p>
