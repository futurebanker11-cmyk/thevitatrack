  <!-- What -->
  <section id="what" class="section">
    <h2>What are skin tears?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> A skin tear is a traumatic wound where fragile skin separates from underlying tissue, often from minor bumps, tape removal, or shearing during transfers. Quick, gentle care helps the skin flap survive and speeds healing.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p class="kicker">ISTAP types</p>
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Type 1:</strong> no tissue loss (skin flap can be repositioned)</li>
        <li><strong>Type 2:</strong> partial flap loss</li>
        <li><strong>Type 3:</strong> complete flap loss</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Risk factors: thin/atrophic skin, steroids, anticoagulants/antiplatelets, edema, malnutrition, dehydration, cognitive or mobility limits, cluttered environment.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent care</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card danger">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Heavy bleeding that won’t stop after 10–15 minutes of firm pressure</li>
          <li>Deep wounds exposing fat, muscle, tendon, or bone</li>
          <li>Signs of infection with fever, spreading redness, or pus</li>
          <li>Loss of sensation, cold/blue fingers or toes, or severe pain out of proportion</li>
          <li>Dirty/contaminated wounds (soil, bites) or tetanus shots not up to date</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Head on blood thinners:</strong> any head injury, even minor, warrants medical assessment.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- First aid -->
  <section id="first-aid" class="section">
    <h2>First aid: step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ol class="list">
          <li><strong>Wash hands</strong> or use sanitizer; put on clean gloves if available.</li>
          <li><strong>Control bleeding:</strong> apply <em>direct pressure</em> with clean gauze for 5–10 minutes. Elevate if possible.</li>
          <li><strong>Gently clean</strong> with drinkable water or saline. Avoid scrubbing fragile tissue.</li>
          <li><strong>Save the skin flap:</strong> if present, moisten with saline and <em>gently lay it back</em> in place (do not cut off).</li>
          <li><strong>Pat dry</strong> surrounding skin; keep the wound bed slightly moist.</li>
          <li><strong>Approximate edges</strong> with <em>steri-strips</em> (see below) or cover with a <em>non-adherent silicone</em> dressing.</li>
          <li><strong>Cover & protect:</strong> add soft gauze/foam for padding; secure with <em>wraps</em> or netting—<strong>avoid strong adhesive tape</strong> on elderly skin.</li>
          <li><strong>Tetanus review:</strong> update if needed.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Do not</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use hydrogen peroxide, iodine, or alcohol inside the wound (damages healing tissue)</li>
          <li>Rip off the skin flap</li>
          <li>Place adhesive directly over the flap edge</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Closure -->
  <section id="closure" class="section">
    <h2>Closure choices (strips, glue, sutures)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Steri-strips</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Best for Type 1 tears with minimal tension</li>
          <li>Place <em>perpendicular</em> to the wound, starting at the center, edges lightly everted</li>
          <li>Use <strong>skin preps</strong> on surrounding intact skin to improve adherence</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Skin glue</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>For short, clean, low-tension edges; keep glue <em>off the wound bed</em></li>
          <li>Not for infected, actively bleeding, or high-tension areas</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sutures/staples</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>May be needed for deep, gaping, or high-tension wounds—done by clinicians</li>
          <li>Avoid staples over very delicate skin when possible</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Padding & offloading:</strong> protect from bumps; consider sleeves or soft protectors over shins/forearms.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Dressings -->
  <section id="dressings" class="section">
    <h2>Dressings that protect fragile skin</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Dressing</th><th scope="col">Use</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Silicone non-adherent contact layer</strong></td>
          <td>Directly over wound/flap</td>
          <td>Minimizes trauma on removal</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Foam pad</strong></td>
          <td>Moderate drainage, cushioning</td>
          <td>Choose bordered silicone foam to avoid harsh tape</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hydrogel</strong></td>
          <td>Dry wounds needing moisture</td>
          <td>Keep surrounding skin dry; cover with secondary dressing</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Calcium alginate</strong></td>
          <td>Oozing/bleeding control</td>
          <td>Requires secondary cover; avoid on dry wounds</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Gauze & soft roll</strong></td>
          <td>Securing layers without adhesive</td>
          <td>Use tubular netting for limbs to avoid tape</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <div class="card small" style="margin-top:12px">
      <p><strong>Change schedule:</strong> usually every 2–3 days or sooner if soaked/loose. Moisten non-adherent layers with saline before removing.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Infection -->
  <section id="infection" class="section">
    <h2>Infection signs & antibiotics</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Increasing pain, warmth, swelling, or <strong>spreading</strong> redness</li>
          <li>Pus, bad odor, wound not improving after several days</li>
          <li>Fever, chills, or feeling unwell</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Topical antibiotics are usually unnecessary; systemic antibiotics are used for clear infection per clinician advice.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Diabetes, vascular disease, or immunosuppression:</strong> lower threshold to seek care and monitor closely.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Prevention & home safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Skin care & nutrition</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Daily <strong>fragrance-free moisturizers</strong> (arms, shins)</li>
          <li>Adequate <strong>protein</strong>, vitamin C, zinc; hydrate well unless restricted</li>
          <li>Manage edema with elevation and clinician-guided compression</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Environment & clothing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Good lighting, remove clutter and sharp edges</li>
          <li>Long sleeves/soft shin guards for high-risk areas</li>
          <li>Use <strong>paper tape/silicone tape</strong>; peel low and slow, support skin</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Transfers & mobility</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use gait belts, avoid dragging/sliding along sheets</li>
          <li>Consider pressure-relieving cushions and heel protectors</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Review medicines:</strong> steroids, anticoagulants/antiplatelets, or sedatives increase risk—discuss if frequent tears occur.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Special -->
  <section id="special" class="section">
    <h2>Special situations</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">On blood thinners (warfarin, DOACs, antiplatelets)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Expect more bleeding; maintain <strong>firm pressure</strong> longer</li>
          <li>Seek care for persistent bleed or if wound is deep/large</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes / peripheral vascular disease</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Slower healing; tighter glucose and foot/leg checks</li>
          <li>Low threshold for wound clinic referral</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Tetanus update</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Booster every 10 years; sooner for dirty wounds if >5 years</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Kit -->
  <section id="kit" class="section">
    <h2>Home wound kit</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Saline wash or bottled water; clean gloves; hand sanitizer</li>
          <li>Non-adherent silicone contact layers; bordered silicone foam pads</li>
          <li>Steri-strips; paper/silicone tape; tubular netting; soft gauze</li>
          <li>Elastic wrap; small scissors; moisturizer</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Documentation:</strong> date, size, photo (with consent), product used, and when to change next.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I remove the skin flap?</p>
        <p>No—gently lay it back after cleaning. It can act like a natural dressing and improve healing.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best tape for elderly skin?</p>
        <p>Silicone or gentle paper tape. Support the skin and peel low and slow toward the wound.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How often to change dressings?</p>
        <p>Every 2–3 days, or sooner if wet/loose. Moisten non-adherent layers before removal to protect tissue.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When are antibiotics needed?</p>
        <p>Only with clear infection signs or clinician advice. Routine topical antibiotics are rarely required.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
