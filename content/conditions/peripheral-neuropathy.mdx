  <!-- What it is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What it is</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Peripheral neuropathy is damage to the nerves outside the brain and spinal cord. It often starts in the toes and feet, causing numbness, tingling, burning pain, or loss of balance. Treating the cause and protecting feet can prevent complications.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good news:</strong> Many causes are identifiable and manageable. Medicines, topical treatments, and lifestyle changes can reduce pain and improve function.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Sudden or rapidly worsening weakness, foot drop, or loss of bladder/bowel control</li>
        <li>New severe back pain with weakness or numbness in both legs</li>
        <li>Foot wounds, ulcers, or signs of infection (fever, spreading redness)</li>
        <li>Severe burning pain with fever or rash (possible shingles or infection)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies. Seek immediate care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sensory</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Numbness, tingling, “pins and needles,” electric shocks</li>
          <li>Burning or freezing pain; worse at night</li>
          <li>Reduced ability to feel temperature or pain (risk for unnoticed injuries)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Motor & autonomic</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Muscle weakness, cramps; difficulty with balance, falls</li>
          <li>Dry skin, reduced sweating; sometimes dizziness on standing</li>
          <li>In diabetes: slow wound healing, foot deformities over time</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Common causes</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Peripheral neuropathy causes">
      <thead><tr><th>Category</th><th>Examples</th><th>Notes</th></tr></thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Metabolic</td>
          <td>Diabetes (most common), prediabetes</td>
          <td>Blood sugar control helps pain and progression</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Deficiencies</td>
          <td>Vitamin B12, folate, B6 (high B6 can also cause neuropathy)</td>
          <td>Replace only after confirming labs</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Endocrine & renal</td>
          <td>Hypothyroidism, chronic kidney disease</td>
          <td>Treat underlying condition</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Toxic/medications</td>
          <td>Alcohol, chemotherapy agents, some antibiotics or HIV meds</td>
          <td>Review medicine list with clinician</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Immune/infectious</td>
          <td>Autoimmune, shingles, HIV, Lyme (region-dependent)</td>
          <td>Workup guided by history and exam</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Compression</td>
          <td>Carpal tunnel, tarsal tunnel, radiculopathy</td>
          <td>Often asymmetric; may need different management</td>
        </tr>

          <td>Unknown</td>
          <td>Idiopathic small-fiber neuropathy</td>
          <td>Symptom control and foot protection remain key</td>

  <!-- Diagnosis -->
  <section id="diagnosis" class="section">
    <h2>How it’s diagnosed</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">History & exam</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pattern: stocking–glove symptoms starting in feet</li>
          <li>Strength, reflexes, sensation (vibration/monofilament), balance</li>
          <li>Medicine, alcohol, and nutrition review</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Typical labs</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>A1C or fasting glucose; B12 ± methylmalonic acid; folate</li>
          <li>TSH; kidney and liver tests</li>
          <li>Sometimes SPEP/UPEP (protein disorders), autoimmune panels</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Tests when needed</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Nerve conduction studies/EMG (large-fiber involvement)</li>
          <li>Skin biopsy for small-fiber neuropathy in selected cases</li>
          <li>Imaging if focal deficits suggest compression or spine disease</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Monofilament check:</strong> Loss of protective sensation signals higher ulcer risk—prioritize daily foot checks and shoe safety.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Treat the cause</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Glucose control for diabetes; review A1C target</li>
          <li>B12 or folate replacement when deficient</li>
          <li>Address thyroid, renal, alcohol, or medicine triggers</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Pain management</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Duloxetine</strong> or certain <strong>TCAs</strong> (e.g., nortriptyline low dose at night)</li>
          <li><strong>Gabapentin</strong> / <strong>Pregabalin</strong> (dose cautiously; watch sedation)</li>
          <li><strong>Topicals:</strong> 5% lidocaine patches/gel; capsaicin cream/patch (with guidance)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Safety notes (older adults):</strong> Start low, go slow. Watch for dizziness, falls, confusion, and swelling. Avoid combining sedating drugs when possible.
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sleep & schedule</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Evening dosing for sedating meds; review interactions</li>
          <li>Non-drug pain strategies: warm foot soaks, gentle massage, mindfulness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Foot care -->
  <section id="footcare" class="section">
    <h2>Foot care & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Daily inspection: heels, toes, between toes; use a mirror or helper</li>
          <li>Moisturize dry skin (not between toes); trim nails carefully or seek podiatry</li>
          <li>Avoid barefoot walking; choose cushioned, well-fitting shoes with wide toe box</li>
          <li>Address corns/calluses with a clinician—skip sharp tools at home</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Wound plan:</strong> Any blister, cut, or sore that isn’t improving in 24–48 hours needs medical review—earlier if redness spreads or drainage appears.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Movement -->
  <section id="movement" class="section">
    <h2>Balance & movement</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Walk most days; add gentle strength (sit-to-stands, calf raises) 2–3×/week</li>
          <li>Balance practice: heel-to-toe walk, single-leg stands near a counter</li>
          <li>Physical therapy if falls, foot drop, or gait changes are present</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Home safety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Night lights, non-slip mats, remove loose rugs and cords</li>
          <li>Supportive footwear; consider a cane or walker if advised</li>
          <li>Check vision and hearing; review medicines that increase fall risk</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can neuropathy improve?</p>
        <p>Yes—when a reversible cause is treated (e.g., B12 deficiency, better glucose control). Pain often responds to a combination of medicine and lifestyle steps.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Which pain medicine is “best”?</p>
        <p>No single best choice. Duloxetine, gabapentin/pregabalin, or low-dose nortriptyline are common starts; side-effects, sleep, and other conditions guide the pick.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do supplements help?</p>
        <p>Only when a deficiency exists. Avoid high-dose B6—it can worsen neuropathy. Discuss any supplement with your clinician.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When to see a specialist?</p>
        <p>Rapid progression, significant weakness, atypical features, or unclear cause often warrant neurology referral and advanced testing.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
