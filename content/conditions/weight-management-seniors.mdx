  <!-- Why it matters -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>Why weight management is different for seniors</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> The goal isn’t just the scale—it’s <strong>muscle, strength, balance, and energy</strong>. Rapid weight loss can reduce muscle and bone. Slow, steady changes with adequate protein and strength training work best.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good to know:</strong> Even a modest change—like +2000 steps/day and protein at each meal—can improve blood sugar, mobility, and mood.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to get urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Unintentional weight loss</strong> ≥5% over 6–12 months</li>
        <li>Persistent vomiting, inability to keep fluids down, signs of dehydration</li>
        <li>Black/bloody stools, chest/abdominal pain, or swallowing problems</li>
        <li>New confusion, weakness, or repeated falls</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may signal infection, medication effects, depression, thyroid/kidney disease, cancer, or malnutrition. Call your clinician promptly.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Goals -->
  <section id="goals" class="section">
    <h2>Set your goals (safe targets)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Safe targets">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Goal</th><th>Typical target</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Weight <strong>loss</strong></td>
          <td>≈0.25–0.5 kg (½–1 lb) per week</td>
          <td>Pair with resistance training to protect muscle and bone</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Weight <strong>regain</strong> / prevent loss</td>
          <td>+0.1–0.25 kg (¼–½ lb) per week</td>
          <td>Prioritize protein, snacks, and strength exercises; check for medical causes</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Daily movement</td>
          <td>150+ min/week moderate activity <em>or</em> as tolerated</td>
          <td>Include <strong>2–3 strength</strong> sessions and <strong>balance</strong> work most days</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Plate method -->
  <section id="plate" class="section">
    <h2>Easy plate method</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Simple portions (most meals)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>½ plate</strong> non-starchy veg (greens, broccoli, peppers)</li>
          <li><strong>¼ plate</strong> protein (fish, eggs, tofu, chicken, beans)</li>
          <li><strong>¼ plate</strong> whole grains or starchy veg (oats, brown rice, sweet potato)</li>
          <li>Add healthy fats (olive oil, nuts) and fruit or dairy on the side</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Appetite low?</strong> Make it <em>smaller but protein-forward</em>: egg + Greek yogurt + fruit; lentil soup + cheese toast; cottage cheese + berries + nuts.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Protein & hydration -->
  <section id="protein" class="section">
    <h2>Protein & hydration guide</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Protein and hydration">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Item</th><th>Target</th><th>Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Protein (per day)</td>
          <td><strong>~1.0–1.2 g/kg</strong> body weight (higher if ill/rehabbing per clinician)</td>
          <td>Distribute over 2–3 meals (20–35 g each): eggs, fish, lean meats, dairy, tofu, beans, protein yogurt</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Hydration</td>
          <td>Aim for <strong>pale-yellow</strong> urine</td>
          <td>Water, milk, soups, herbal tea; adjust for heart/kidney advice</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Bone helpers</td>
          <td>Calcium & vitamin D per guidance</td>
          <td>Dairy/fortified alternatives, sunlight as appropriate, supplements if prescribed</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Activity -->
  <section id="activity" class="section">
    <h2>Activity: strength, balance, cardio</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Weekly mix</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Strength 2–3×/week:</strong> Chair stands, wall pushups, resistance bands, light weights</li>
          <li><strong>Balance most days:</strong> Heel-to-toe walk, single-leg stands (near support), Tai Chi</li>
          <li><strong>Cardio:</strong> Brisk walk, cycling, water aerobics; build up in 5–10 min chunks</li>
          <li><strong>Flexibility:</strong> Gentle stretches after warm-up or at day’s end</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Safety:</strong> Start low, progress slow. Stop for chest pain, severe breathlessness, or dizziness. Use supportive shoes and clear walking routes.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Plans -->
  <section id="plans" class="section">
    <h2>Two paths: lose vs regain</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If you want to <strong>lose weight</strong></p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep protein steady; trim calories from <em>refined snacks, sugary drinks, excess oils</em></li>
          <li>Swap: water/tea for soda; fruit for sweets; veggies + hummus for chips</li>
          <li>Use a <strong>smaller plate</strong>, eat slowly, and stop at “satisfied”</li>
          <li>Walk after meals (5–15 min) to help blood sugar and digestion</li>
          <li>Sleep 7–8 hours; poor sleep ↑ hunger and cravings</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If you need to <strong>regain/maintain</strong></p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Add <strong>protein snacks</strong> (yogurt, nuts, cheese, shake) between meals</li>
          <li>Enrich foods: olive oil, nut butters, powdered milk in soups/oatmeal</li>
          <li>Make meals <strong>easy</strong>: ready-to-eat soups, rotisserie chicken, frozen veg</li>
          <li>Address causes: mouth pain, dentures, loneliness at meals, depression, meds</li>
          <li>Try <strong>2–3 smaller meals + 2 snacks</strong> if appetite is low</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">Tiny steps count. Track what you try and how you feel—adjust each week.</div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medications & conditions that change weight</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Meds & conditions">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Category</th><th>May cause</th><th>What to do</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Diabetes meds, diuretics, thyroid meds</td>
          <td>Gain or loss, fluid changes</td>
          <td>Review doses; avoid rapid shifts; monitor sugars/weights</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Antidepressants, antipsychotics, steroids</td>
          <td>Weight gain, appetite changes</td>
          <td>Ask about alternatives or counter-strategies (diet/activity)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Weight-loss prescriptions</td>
          <td>Loss (sometimes too fast), nausea</td>
          <td>Need clinician oversight; prioritize protein and strength training</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Conditions</td>
          <td>Heart, kidney, thyroid disease; cancer; dental/oral issues; depression</td>
          <td>Treat causes; involve dietitian, dentist, or counselor as needed</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- Shopping & meals -->
  <section id="shopping" class="section">
    <h2>Grocery & meal ideas</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Smart staples</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Protein: eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, tofu/tempeh, beans/lentils, canned tuna/salmon, chicken</li>
          <li>Grains/starches: oats, whole-grain bread, brown rice, quinoa, potatoes</li>
          <li>Produce: frozen mixed veg, leafy greens, apples/bananas/berries</li>
          <li>Fats/flavor: olive oil, avocado, nuts/seeds, herbs/spices</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>5-minute meals:</strong> Scrambled eggs + spinach + toast • Tuna-bean salad with olive oil/lemon • Microwaved potato + cottage cheese + salsa • Lentil soup + whole-grain toast.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tools -->
  <section id="tools" class="section">
    <h2>Helpful tools & tech</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make tracking simple</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Step counter</strong> or phone pedometer; gradual weekly step goals</li>
          <li>Food diary (paper or app) for 1 week to spot patterns</li>
          <li>Kitchen scale/measure cups for a few days to learn portions</li>
          <li>Reminders for meals, meds, and short walks after eating</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Skin & muscle check:</strong> Note ring/watch looseness, rising fatigue, or harder-to-stand-from-chair—possible muscle loss → increase protein/strength work and notify clinician.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support without stress</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Make meals social; pair eating with a favorite show or phone call</li>
          <li>Prep snack boxes (protein + fruit/veg) at eye level in the fridge</li>
          <li>Set “movement dates”: short walks or simple band exercises together</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call the clinician for:</strong> Unplanned weight change, poor appetite >1–2 weeks, trouble swallowing, dental pain, persistent nausea/vomiting, or new weakness/falls.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is it safe to diet after 60?</p>
        <p>Yes—<strong>slowly</strong>, with enough protein and strength training. Avoid severe calorie cuts that reduce muscle and bone.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if the scale doesn’t move?</p>
        <p>Check protein at each meal, steps after meals, sleep, and hidden calories (drinks/snacks). Track waist or how clothes fit—muscle gain is a win.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are supplements necessary?</p>
        <p>Food first. Protein powders or shakes can help if appetite is low. Ask about vitamin D, calcium, and B12 based on your meds and labs.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I eat with diabetes?</p>
        <p>Spread carbs through the day, include protein/fiber each meal, and walk after eating. Monitor sugars; medication doses may need adjustment as weight changes.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
