  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is-dehydration" class="section">
    <h2>What is dehydration?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> The body doesn’t have enough water and electrolytes for normal function. In older adults this can appear quickly with heat, infections, medicines, or poor intake — and can worsen dizziness, kidney function, and blood pressure control.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Why it matters:</strong> Even mild dehydration can increase falls, confusion, constipation, and urinary infections. Early recognition and a simple drink plan usually helps; sometimes IV fluids are needed.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Very sleepy, confused, or fainting</li>
        <li>Unable to keep fluids down (vomiting) or severe diarrhea</li>
        <li>Very low urine output or dark, tea-colored urine with pain</li>
        <li>Fast heartbeat, breathing difficulty, or severe dizziness on standing</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies — seek care immediately.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common signs & symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Thirst or dry mouth, cracked lips, dry tongue</li>
          <li>Dizziness on standing, headache, fatigue</li>
          <li>Less urine, darker urine, constipation</li>
          <li>Muscle cramps; cool, dry skin or excessive heat with sweating</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Clues in daily life</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Not finishing drinks; avoiding fluids late due to night urination</li>
          <li>Hot weather, travel days, or fasting</li>
          <li>Recent illness with fever, vomiting, or diarrhea</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Why it happens in older adults</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common triggers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Heat & humidity</strong> or poor ventilation</li>
          <li><strong>Illness</strong> with fever, vomiting, or diarrhea</li>
          <li><strong>Low fluid intake</strong> (fear of night urination, poor appetite)</li>
          <li><strong>High-sugar drinks</strong> pulling water into the gut</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines to review</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Diuretics (“water pills”), some blood pressure medicines</li>
          <li>SGLT2 inhibitors (diabetes), laxatives</li>
          <li>Anticholinergics or sedatives that reduce awareness of thirst</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Never stop a medicine without medical advice — ask about dose timing during heat or illness.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>How dehydration is checked</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Vitals:</strong> pulse, blood pressure (drop on standing), temperature</li>
        <li><strong>Exam:</strong> dry mouth, low skin moisture, reduced urine</li>
        <li><strong>Labs:</strong> sodium, potassium, kidney tests (BUN/creatinine), sometimes urine tests</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Results guide whether oral fluids are enough or IV fluids are safer.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Rehydration -->
  <section id="rehydration" class="section">
    <h2>Home rehydration plan</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fluids</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Start with small, frequent sips every 5–10 minutes</li>
          <li>Use water plus an <strong>oral rehydration solution</strong> (ORS) or low-sugar electrolyte drink</li>
          <li>If nauseated: ice chips or a teaspoon every few minutes, then increase</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Targets (example)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>First 2–4 hours: ~250–500 mL, as tolerated</li>
          <li>Next 24 hours: spread sips to reach your daily goal from your clinician</li>
          <li>Avoid high-sugar sodas/juices and excessive caffeine</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Electrolytes & salt</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>ORS provides balanced sodium/potassium</li>
          <li>If on a salt-restricted diet (heart/kidney), ask for personalized advice</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        If you have <strong>heart failure or kidney disease</strong>, fluid goals may be lower and must be individualized. Use your care team’s plan.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Special situations -->
  <section id="special" class="section">
    <h2>Sick-day rules, heat & travel</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sick-day basics</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep ORS at home; take tiny, frequent sips during vomiting/diarrhea</li>
          <li>Ask if any medicines should be paused temporarily during severe illness (per your care plan)</li>
          <li>Monitor weight, urine output, and dizziness on standing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heat & travel</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Shade/AC, light clothing, cool showers</li>
          <li>Carry a water bottle and ORS packets; set phone reminders to sip hourly</li>
          <li>Avoid alcohol and very sweet drinks in hot weather</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Daily prevention checklist</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Drink regularly through the day; keep a bottle within reach</li>
          <li>Hydration routine: a glass at wake-up, with medicines (if allowed), and with meals</li>
          <li>Eat water-rich foods: soups, fruits, yogurt (as allowed by your diet)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Balance with night urination: shift more fluids earlier in the day; taper in the evening if instructed.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- When to call -->
  <section id="when-to-call" class="section">
    <h2>When to call your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Ongoing vomiting/diarrhea > 6–8 hours, or you can’t keep fluids down</li>
        <li>Worsening dizziness, very little urine, or new confusion</li>
        <li>Heart/kidney disease and unsure about fluid targets during illness</li>
        <li>Fever with worsening weakness despite fluids</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions to ask</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>How much should I drink on regular days vs sick days?</li>
        <li>Which electrolyte drink fits my heart/kidney/diabetes plan?</li>
        <li>Do any of my medicines raise dehydration risk during heat or illness?</li>
        <li>What signs mean I should go to urgent care or the ER?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I drink plain water or ORS?</p>
        <p>Both can help; ORS is best during vomiting/diarrhea or heavy sweating because it replaces electrolytes along with water.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I avoid nighttime bathroom trips?</p>
        <p>Front-load fluids earlier in the day, take steady sips at meals, and taper in the evening if advised.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can coffee or tea count?</p>
        <p>Yes in moderation, but avoid excess caffeine and very sweet drinks during rehydration.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When are IV fluids needed?</p>
        <p>Severe dehydration, inability to keep fluids down, or significant low blood pressure often require IV fluids in a clinic or hospital.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
