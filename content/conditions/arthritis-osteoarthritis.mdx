  <!-- What is OA -->
  <section id="what-is-oa" class="section">
    <h2>What is osteoarthritis?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Osteoarthritis is joint “wear and repair” imbalance leading to cartilage thinning, bony changes, pain, stiffness, and reduced function. It most often affects knees, hips, hands, and spine.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Key idea:</strong> OA management combines regular movement, weight and strength strategies, pain relief options, activity pacing, and joint protection. Plans are individualized to support mobility and daily life.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Hot, swollen, very painful joint with fever (possible infection)</li>
        <li>New severe joint pain after a fall or twist, inability to bear weight</li>
        <li>Rapidly worsening redness, swelling, or deformity</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies. Contact local emergency services or urgent care promptly.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Aching or sharp joint pain that worsens with activity</li>
          <li>Morning stiffness or stiffness after sitting (“start-up pain”)</li>
          <li>Reduced range of motion, joint clicking or grinding</li>
          <li>Swelling or a feeling of joint instability</li>
          <li>Hand OA: bony knuckles, thumb base pain, grip weakness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Causes & risks -->
  <section id="causes-risks" class="section">
    <h2>Causes & risk factors</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common contributors</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Age-related cartilage changes and prior joint injuries</li>
          <li>Excess body weight (knee/hip load), muscle weakness</li>
          <li>Repetitive joint stress from work or activities</li>
          <li>Genetics and bone alignment differences</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What can worsen flares</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Prolonged sitting or standing without movement breaks</li>
          <li>High-impact or sudden unaccustomed activity</li>
          <li>Poor sleep and stress, which heighten pain sensitivity</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Joints commonly affected -->

          <td>Neck or low-back ache, stiffness</td>

  <!-- Diagnosis -->
  <section id="diagnosis" class="section">
    <h2>How OA is diagnosed</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>History and exam: pain pattern, stiffness, function, previous injuries</li>
        <li>X-rays often sufficient (joint space narrowing, bone spurs)</li>
        <li>Advanced imaging only if symptoms are atypical or surgery considered</li>
        <li>Blood tests when other arthritis types need to be ruled out</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Findings are matched with symptoms to guide a practical plan.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Movement & strength</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Low-impact exercise: walking, cycling, water exercise, tai chi</li>
          <li>Targeted strengthening (quads, hips, core); supervised PT when possible</li>
          <li>Activity pacing: short bouts, frequent breaks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Weight & load</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Even modest weight loss reduces knee/hip load</li>
          <li>Supportive footwear; cushioned insoles; canes/walkers for stability</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Pain relief options</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Topical NSAIDs (first-line for many joints)</li>
          <li>Oral pain relievers as advised; review risks with your clinician</li>
          <li>Heat for stiffness; ice after activity flares</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">4) Procedures</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Joint injections (corticosteroid) for selected flares</li>
          <li>Viscosupplementation in some settings</li>
          <li>Joint replacement for severe, life-limiting OA after conservative care</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small note" style="margin-top:12px">Choices depend on symptoms, joint, health history, and goals. Review benefits and risks with your clinician.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily care -->
  <section id="daily-care" class="section">
    <h2>Daily care & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Home strategies</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Short warm-up before walking; gentle range-of-motion in the morning</li>
          <li>Use handrails, raised chairs, and bathroom safety aids</li>
          <li>Plan tasks to avoid long standing or heavy lifting</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Flare control</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Ice 10–15 minutes after overuse; heat before activity</li>
          <li>Break big tasks into smaller steps; rest between sets</li>
          <li>Track triggers to adjust pacing and supports</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Hands & tools</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Thumb/wrist splints for chores; jar openers, large-handled utensils</li>
          <li>Voice-activated devices to reduce repetitive strain</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Mind & sleep</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Relaxation/breathing practices to lower pain tension</li>
          <li>Regular sleep schedule; cushion painful joints with pillows</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>OA — quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is exercise safe?</p>
        <p>Yes. Low-impact, progressive exercise reduces pain and improves function. Start small and increase gradually.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heat or ice?</p>
        <p>Heat before activity to loosen; ice after flares to calm swelling or soreness.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do supplements help?</p>
        <p>Evidence is mixed. Discuss glucosamine/chondroitin or others with your clinician to avoid interactions and cost without benefit.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When think about surgery?</p>
        <p>When pain and disability remain high despite optimized non-surgical care and imaging matches symptoms.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
