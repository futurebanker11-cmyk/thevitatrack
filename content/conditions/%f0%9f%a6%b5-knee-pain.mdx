        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">!</span> Urgent signs — act now <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p><strong>Seek urgent care</strong> for a red, hot, very swollen knee with fever; a loud “pop” with inability to bear weight; major deformity after a fall; or deep calf pain with swelling (possible clot).</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Severe pain not relieved by rest</li>
            <li>Sudden locking or the knee giving way repeatedly</li>
            <li>Open wound, pus, or streaking redness</li>

      <!-- 2 Common causes -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">1</span> Common causes (simple overview) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div>
              <p>Knee pain often comes from <strong>osteoarthritis</strong> (cartilage wear), <strong>tendons</strong> and <strong>ligaments</strong> (overuse or sprain), <strong>meniscus</strong> tears, <strong>inflammation</strong> (bursitis), or <strong>crystal arthritis</strong> (gout/pseudogout). Weight, weak thigh muscles, flat feet, and past injuries raise risk.</p>
              <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Clues by cause">
                <table class="responsive">
                  <thead><tr><th>Cause</th><th>Clues</th></tr></thead>
                  <tbody>
                    <tr>
                      <td data-col="Cause">Osteoarthritis</td>
                      <td data-col="Clues">Stiff a.m. start-up pain, better with easy movement</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr>
                      <td data-col="Cause">Meniscus</td>
                      <td data-col="Clues">Sharp joint-line pain, catching/locking after twist</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr>
                      <td data-col="Cause">Patellofemoral</td>
                      <td data-col="Clues">Front-knee ache with stairs, sitting, or squats</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr>
                      <td data-col="Cause">Bursitis</td>
                      <td data-col="Clues">Local swelling/tenderness, kneeling irritation</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr>
                      <td data-col="Cause">Ligament sprain</td>
                      <td data-col="Clues">Pop, swelling, instability after misstep</td>
                    </tr>
                  </tbody>
                </table>
              </div>
            </div>
            <div class="note tip">
              <strong>Good news:</strong> Many knee problems improve with steady, gentle care: smart activity, targeted exercises, weight management, and proper footwear.

      <!-- 3 First aid -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> First aid: R.I.C.E. + pain relief (48–72 hours) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Rest & protect:</strong> Short breaks, avoid painful twists; use a cane in the opposite hand if needed.</li>
            <li><strong>Ice:</strong> 10–15 minutes, cloth barrier, 3–4×/day early on.</li>
            <li><strong>Compression:</strong> Light elastic wrap or sleeve if your clinician says it’s safe.</li>
            <li><strong>Elevation:</strong> Foot above heart when resting to reduce swelling.</li>
            <li><strong>Medicine:</strong> Use only as prescribed/approved by your clinician—especially if you have kidney, heart, stomach, or blood thinner concerns.</li>
          </ol>

      <!-- 4 Daily exercises -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Gentle daily exercises (strength • stretch • balance) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Strength (3–4×/wk)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li><strong>Quad sets:</strong> Press knee down into a towel roll, 5–10 sec × 10</li>
                <li><strong>Straight leg raise:</strong> Tighten thigh, lift foot 15–20 cm, 10 reps</li>
                <li><strong>Mini-squat:</strong> Hold counter, small bend, 8–10 reps</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Stretch (daily)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li><strong>Hamstrings:</strong> Seated strap stretch, 20–30 sec × 3</li>
                <li><strong>Calf:</strong> Wall stretch, 20–30 sec × 3</li>
                <li><strong>Hip flexor:</strong> Gentle lunge at counter</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Balance (daily)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Single-leg stand holding counter (10–20 sec each)</li>
                <li>Heel-to-toe line walk</li>
                <li>Chair sit-to-stand, slow and controlled</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
          <p class="muted">Pain should stay in a “mild” range and settle within 24 hours. If pain spikes or swelling rises, scale back and consult your clinician.</p>

      <!-- 5 Activity pacing & supports -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Activity pacing, braces & footwear <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Pace steps:</strong> Short, frequent walks; avoid long downhill or stairs at first.</li>
            <li><strong>Supports:</strong> Soft knee sleeve or patella strap may help some patterns; ask your clinician.</li>
            <li><strong>Shoes:</strong> Cushioned, supportive shoes with good grip; consider shock-absorbing insoles.</li>
            <li><strong>Home safety:</strong> Handrails, non-slip mats, well-lit stairs.</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 6 Weight & anti-inflammatory habits -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Weight & calming inflammation (simple wins) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Weight basics</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Even small loss reduces knee load with each step</li>
                <li>Protein each meal + veggies ½ plate supports joints</li>
                <li>Limit deep-fried foods and heavy late dinners</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Inflammation helpers</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Regular movement, good sleep, stress-breathing</li>
                <li>Spices like turmeric/ginger in meals (if tolerated)</li>
                <li>Quit smoking; moderate alcohol if you drink</li>
              </ul>
            </div>

      <!-- 7 When to see a clinician -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> When to see your clinician (and what to bring) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Pain >2–3 weeks, frequent swelling, night pain, or repeated giving way</li>
            <li>History of falls or balance worries</li>
            <li>Previous knee surgery with new symptoms</li>
          </ul>
          <p><strong>Bring:</strong> A short log of pain times, what worsens/helps, steps per day, and any swelling photos.</p>

      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> Treatments overview (meds • injections • surgery) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Medicines</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Pain relievers or anti-inflammatories as prescribed</li>

              </ul>
            </div>

      <!-- 9 OA vs rheumatoid vs gout -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Is it OA, rheumatoid, or gout? (quick differences) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Knee arthritis types">
            <table class="responsive">
              <thead><tr><th>Condition</th><th>Clues</th></tr></thead>
              <tbody>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Condition">Osteoarthritis (OA)</td>
                  <td data-col="Clues">Worse with use, brief morning stiffness, bony swelling</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Condition">Rheumatoid arthritis</td>
                  <td data-col="Clues">Long morning stiffness, multiple joints, systemic symptoms</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Condition">Gout / Pseudogout</td>
                  <td data-col="Clues">Sudden, red, hot swelling; may involve foot/ankle or knee</td>
                </tr>
              </tbody>
            </table>
          </div>
          <p class="muted">Only a clinician can confirm the cause—blood tests or imaging may help.</p>
        </div>
