  <!-- What it is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>Stroke vs TIA</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> A <strong>stroke</strong> happens when blood flow to part of the brain is blocked or a blood vessel bleeds, causing sudden <strong>neurologic deficits</strong>. A <strong>TIA</strong> (transient ischemic attack) is a <em>temporary</em> blockage with symptoms that resolve—often within minutes—but it’s a <strong>major warning</strong> that a stroke may follow.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Every minute counts:</strong> Brain cells die quickly without oxygen. Calling emergency services immediately enables time-critical treatments.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Warning signs -->
  <section id="warning" class="section">
    <h2>B-E-F-A-S-T warning signs</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>B — Balance:</strong> sudden trouble walking, dizziness, loss of balance/coordination</li>
          <li><strong>E — Eyes:</strong> sudden vision loss or double vision in one or both eyes</li>
          <li><strong>F — Face:</strong> facial droop or uneven smile</li>
          <li><strong>A — Arm:</strong> arm/leg weakness or numbness, especially on one side</li>
          <li><strong>S — Speech:</strong> slurred speech, difficulty speaking or understanding</li>
          <li><strong>T — Time:</strong> <em>call emergency now</em>; note the <strong>exact time</strong> symptoms started or last known well</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Other sudden symptoms:</strong> severe “worst” headache, confusion, trouble swallowing, new severe imbalance—treat as stroke until proven otherwise.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Do now -->
  <section id="do-now" class="section">
    <h2>Exactly what to do now</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Call emergency services immediately.</strong> Do <em>not</em> drive yourself.</li>
          <li>Note <strong>time of onset</strong> (or last known normal) and list of medicines (especially blood thinners).</li>
          <li>Do not eat/drink; avoid aspirin unless a clinician tells you (bleeding stroke risk).</li>
          <li>If the person becomes unresponsive, start <strong>CPR</strong> if trained.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>If symptoms resolve (possible TIA):</strong> still an emergency—risk of stroke is highest in the next 48 hours.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Hospital treatment & timelines</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Ischemic stroke (blood clot)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Clot-busting medicine</strong> (e.g., alteplase/tenecteplase) for eligible patients—best within <strong>4.5 hours</strong> of onset</li>
          <li><strong>Mechanical thrombectomy</strong> (catheter clot removal) for large artery blockages—select patients up to <strong>24 hours</strong> from last known well</li>
          <li>Blood pressure, glucose, oxygen optimization; early antiplatelet after bleeding is excluded (team-guided)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Hemorrhagic stroke (bleeding)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Blood pressure control, reversal of blood thinners when indicated</li>
          <li>Neurosurgical evaluation; manage swelling and complications</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Why the clock matters:</strong> more brain saved with earlier treatment—call first, don’t wait to “see if it gets better.”
      </div>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>How stroke/TIA is evaluated</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>CT head</strong> quickly rules out bleeding</li>
          <li><strong>MRI brain</strong> to confirm small/early infarcts (as available)</li>
          <li><strong>Vessel imaging</strong> (CTA/MRA/carotid ultrasound) to find blockages</li>
          <li><strong>Heart rhythm</strong> (ECG/telemetry) for atrial fibrillation; <strong>echocardiogram</strong> for clots/valves</li>
          <li>Blood tests (glucose, electrolytes, CBC, coagulation, lipids); swallowing screen before food/meds</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>TIA work-up is urgent:</strong> even if symptoms are gone, early imaging and prevention meds lower the chance of a full stroke.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Mimics -->
  <section id="mimics" class="section">
    <h2>Common mimics</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Low blood sugar, seizures/post-ictal weakness (Todd’s paralysis)</li>
          <li>Migraine aura, inner ear disorders, medication effects</li>
          <li>Peripheral neuropathy or nerve palsies</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Rule of thumb:</strong> sudden, focal, one-sided deficits → treat as stroke until clinicians confirm otherwise.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Recovery -->
  <section id="recovery" class="section">
    <h2>Recovery & rehab</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Team approach</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Early <strong>physical, occupational, speech therapy</strong> improves outcomes</li>
          <li>Swallow evaluation to prevent aspiration; nutrition plan</li>
          <li>Screen/treat mood changes (depression, anxiety), sleep disorders</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Home setup</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Remove trip hazards; grab bars; raised toilet/handrails</li>
          <li>Non-slip footwear; lighting for night paths</li>
          <li>Medication planner; BP/glucose tracking as advised</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevent" class="section">
    <h2>Prevention & medicines</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Key pillars</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Blood pressure</strong> control is most important</li>
          <li><strong>Statins</strong> for many ischemic strokes/TIAs</li>
          <li><strong>Diabetes</strong> control; healthy weight; exercise plan</li>
          <li>No smoking; low-salt, heart-healthy diet; limit alcohol</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Antiplatelet / anticoagulation</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Antiplatelets</strong> (e.g., aspirin, clopidogrel) for most non-cardioembolic TIAs/ischemic strokes</li>
          <li><strong>Anticoagulation</strong> (e.g., DOACs/warfarin) for <strong>atrial fibrillation-related</strong> stroke prevention—clinician determines timing after stroke</li>
          <li>Never combine medications without medical guidance</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety, swallowing & driving</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Do a <strong>swallow screen</strong> before food, drinks, or pills after stroke</li>
          <li>Use <strong>assistive devices</strong> (cane/walker) as trained; fall-prevention plan</li>
          <li>Review medicines for dizziness/sedation; simplify dosing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card small">
        <strong>Driving/work:</strong> return is individualized; follow local rules and clinician advice after formal assessment.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Symptoms stopped—am I okay?</p>
        <p>No. That could be a TIA. Stroke risk is highest soon after—seek urgent care the same day.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I take aspirin right away?</p>
        <p>Only after bleeding is ruled out by clinicians. Taking it during a bleed can be dangerous.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How soon should rehab start?</p>
        <p>As early as it’s safe—often within 24–48 hours. Early therapy improves recovery and independence.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do all strokes have severe headache?</p>
        <p>No. Many ischemic strokes are painless—focus on sudden weakness, speech or vision changes, or imbalance.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
