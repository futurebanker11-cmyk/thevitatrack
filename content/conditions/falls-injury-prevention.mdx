  <!-- Why it matters -->
  <section id="why-it-matters" class="section">
    <h2>Why it matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Falls are a leading cause of injury for older adults. Most falls have <strong>multiple causes</strong> (vision, balance, medicines, home hazards). Small changes at home and a simple movement plan can sharply reduce risk.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Stay steady, walk confidently, and prevent injuries that set back independence.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>Urgent red flags</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Head hit, <strong>loss of consciousness</strong>, confusion, or severe headache</li>
        <li>On blood thinners (e.g., warfarin, apixaban, rivaroxaban) with any head impact</li>
        <li>Severe pain, obvious deformity, inability to bear weight</li>
        <li>New weakness, numbness, or trouble speaking/seeing</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Call emergency services if any of these occur.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Home fixes -->
  <section id="home-fixes" class="section">
    <h2>Top home fixes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Clear paths</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Remove loose rugs and cords; tape edges or use rug grippers</li>
          <li>Keep floors dry; wipe spills right away</li>
          <li>Declutter walkways; store daily items at waist height</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Light the way</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Night lights from bed → bathroom; motion lights for hallways</li>
          <li>Bright bulbs at stairs and entrances</li>
          <li>Keep a flashlight by the bed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Stair safety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Handrails both sides if possible</li>
          <li>High-contrast tape at step edges</li>
          <li>Keep steps clear; no carrying heavy loads on stairs</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Simple upgrades reduce risk quickly — especially rugs, lighting, and handrails.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Bathroom -->
  <section id="bathroom" class="section">
    <h2>Bathroom & night safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Bathing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Non-slip mat inside and outside the tub/shower</li>
          <li><strong>Grab bars</strong> (not towel bars) near shower and toilet</li>
          <li>Shower chair or bench if unsteady; handheld shower head</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Night plan</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Path lights to bathroom; clear the route each evening</li>
          <li>Keep phone or alert button within reach</li>
          <li>Avoid rushing; sit a moment before standing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Footwear & vision -->
  <section id="footwear-vision" class="section">
    <h2>Footwear & vision</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Shoes</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Closed-back, non-slip soles; low heel</li>
          <li>Replace worn soles; avoid floppy slippers</li>
          <li>Compression socks if advised; check fit each year</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Vision & hearing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Eye exam yearly; clean glasses; avoid multifocals on stairs</li>
          <li>Hearing aids reduce missed cues; manage earwax buildup</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines to review</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Group</th><th scope="col">Examples</th><th scope="col">Why it matters</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Sleep & anxiety drugs</td>
          <td>Benzodiazepines, “Z-drugs,” sedating antihistamines</td>
          <td>Drowsy, slow reflexes, more falls — consider safer options</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Blood pressure meds</td>
          <td>Over-treatment or fast dose changes</td>
          <td>Dizziness on standing (orthostatic); ask about timing/target</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Pain medicines</td>
          <td>Opioids, high-dose gabapentin/pregabalin</td>
          <td>Drowsy, balance issues, confusion</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Others</td>
          <td>Antidepressants, antipsychotics, alcohol</td>
          <td>Can affect balance and alertness</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Bring a full list of medicines and supplements. <strong>Don’t stop on your own</strong> — ask for a step-down or safer alternative plan.</p>

  <!-- Strength & balance -->
  <section id="strength-balance" class="section">
    <h2>Strength & balance plan</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily moves (5–10 minutes)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Chair sit-to-stands</strong> (use arms as needed)</li>
          <li><strong>Heel raises</strong> & <strong>toe raises</strong> at the counter</li>
          <li><strong>Marching</strong> in place; gentle <strong>side steps</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Balance (near counter)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Tandem stand</strong> (heel-to-toe) 10–20 seconds</li>
          <li><strong>Single-leg stand</strong> 5–10 seconds each side</li>
          <li><strong>Head turns</strong> while standing, eyes open</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Aim for <strong>150 minutes/week</strong> of walking or light activity plus muscle & balance work 2–3×/week. A physical therapist can tailor a safe progression.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="aids" class="section">
    <h2>Canes, walkers & tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Choosing & using</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Device height: handgrip at wrist crease when standing</li>
          <li>Cane in the <strong>opposite</strong> hand of the weak/painful leg</li>
          <li>Four-point cane or <strong>rolling walker</strong> for more support</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Safety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep brakes engaged before sitting/standing (walkers)</li>
          <li>Remove tennis balls; use proper glides/tips</li>
          <li>Reassess fit after shoes or swelling changes</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- After a fall -->
  <section id="after-fall" class="section">
    <h2>After a fall: what to do</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If you can’t get up</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Call for help or use alert device</li>
          <li>Stay warm with a blanket; move joints gently while waiting</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If you can get up</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Roll to side → on hands/knees → crawl to a sturdy chair</li>
          <li>Place hands on seat, one foot forward, push up to sitting</li>
          <li>Rest, check for pain/dizziness before standing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Always tell your clinician after any fall — even if you feel fine — to review causes and adjust your plan.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is dizziness on standing a fall risk?</p>
        <p>Yes. A drop in blood pressure when standing increases risk. Hydration, slow rising, and medicine review help.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do walking sticks help?</p>
        <p>Yes if fitted correctly and used on the opposite side of the weaker leg. Many feel steadier with a rolling walker.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best first home change?</p>
        <p>Remove loose rugs and add night lights on the bed-to-bath path.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can new glasses increase risk?</p>
        <p>Multifocals can blur steps. Use single-vision distance glasses on stairs when possible.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
