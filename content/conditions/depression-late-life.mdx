  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is depression in older adults?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> A medical condition that affects mood, interest, energy, sleep, thinking, and daily function. It is not “just aging.” Effective treatments exist at every age.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Why it matters:</strong> Untreated depression increases fall risk, worsens heart and diabetes control, and reduces quality of life. Treatment improves energy, participation, and health outcomes.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>Urgent warning signs</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Thoughts of self-harm or suicide</li>
        <li>Talking about wanting to die, feeling hopeless, or being a burden</li>
        <li>Refusing food, water, or essential medicines</li>
        <li>New confusion with severe agitation</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">If these occur, seek immediate help from local emergency services or a crisis line.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Low mood or loss of interest most days</li>
          <li>Changes in sleep (too little or too much)</li>
          <li>Low energy, slowed movement, or restlessness</li>
          <li>Poor concentration or indecision</li>
          <li>Changes in appetite or weight</li>
          <li>Feelings of guilt, worthlessness, or hopelessness</li>
          <li>Unexplained aches, headaches, stomach upset</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Clues in late life</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stops hobbies or social visits</li>
          <li>Decline in self-care, mail or bills piling up</li>
          <li>Frequent urgent care visits for physical complaints without clear cause</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after symptoms) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Causes & risk factors</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medical & life factors</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Recent loss, isolation, caregiving stress</li>
          <li>Chronic illnesses (heart, stroke, diabetes, COPD, pain)</li>
          <li>Sleep disorders, low activity, alcohol use</li>
          <li>Low thyroid, vitamin B12/folate deficiency</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines to review</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Some sedatives, steroids, certain blood pressure or pain medicines</li>
          <li>Alcohol-drug interactions; multiple medicines adding grogginess</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Never stop a medicine without medical advice — ask about safer alternatives.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Screening -->
  <section id="screening" class="section">
    <h2>Screening & diagnosis</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Brief screens</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>PHQ-2:</strong> low mood and loss of interest</li>
          <li><strong>PHQ-9:</strong> symptom checklist with severity</li>
          <li>Evaluation also checks for anxiety, grief, substance use, and memory issues</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Rule out contributors</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Thyroid, B12/folate, anemia, electrolytes, kidney/liver tests as indicated</li>
          <li>Medication review and sleep assessment</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Talking therapies</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Behavioral activation (restarting meaningful activities)</li>
          <li>Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)</li>
          <li>Problem-solving therapy; grief-focused support</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>First-line often includes SSRIs/SNRIs; start low, go slow; monitor sodium</li>
          <li>Choice guided by sleep, pain, anxiety, prior response, and interactions</li>
          <li>Combine with therapy for stronger, longer-lasting benefit</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Other supports</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Exercise programs (walking or gentle strength), social groups</li>
          <li>Hearing/vision fixes to reduce isolation</li>
          <li>Treatment for co-existing anxiety, pain, or sleep disorders</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Antidepressants take time to work (often 2–4 weeks for first changes). Follow-up enables dose adjustments and side-effect checks.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after treatment) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Med cautions -->
  <section id="med-cautions" class="section">
    <h2>Medication cautions (older adults)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Topic</th><th scope="col">Why it matters</th><th scope="col">What to discuss</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Hyponatremia (low sodium)</td>
          <td>Can cause confusion, falls</td>
          <td>Check sodium after starting or dose changes</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Falls & sedation</td>
          <td>Dizziness with some medicines or combinations</td>
          <td>Review sedatives; nighttime bathroom safety</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Drug interactions</td>
          <td>Heart, blood thinners, pain meds may interact</td>
          <td>Share a complete medicine/supplement list</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Appetite/weight changes</td>
          <td>Unintended loss or gain affects health</td>
          <td>Weigh weekly; nutrition support if needed</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily routines -->
  <section id="daily" class="section">
    <h2>Daily routines that help</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Activity & structure</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Set a simple morning plan (wash, dress, fresh air, short walk)</li>
          <li>Small tasks: water plants, brief calls, light chores</li>
          <li>Track one rewarding activity per day</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sleep & body care</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular sleep/wake time; limit long daytime naps</li>
          <li>Sunlight in the morning; calming routine at night</li>
          <li>Balanced meals and hydration; limit alcohol</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Connection</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Schedule brief social contact most days (call, tea, short visit)</li>
          <li>Join a local group, class, or faith/community activity</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Recovery is step-by-step. Small, repeatable actions rebuild energy and interest over time.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers & family</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support without pressure</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Offer choices (“walk to mailbox or garden?”)</li>
          <li>Plan short, pleasant activities; avoid arguing about symptoms</li>
          <li>Notice changes in sleep, appetite, or medication use</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Safety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Ask directly about self-harm thoughts; remove obvious hazards if risk is present and seek help</li>
          <li>Share updates with the care team; attend visits if invited</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- When to call -->
  <section id="when-to-call" class="section">
    <h2>When to contact your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Symptoms most days for 2+ weeks affecting daily life</li>
        <li>Worse mood, new confusion, marked sleep or appetite change</li>
        <li>Side effects after starting or changing medicines</li>
        <li>Any thoughts of self-harm — seek immediate help</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is depression part of aging?</p>
        <p>No. Many older adults feel well. Depression is treatable at any age.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Therapy or medicine?</p>
        <p>Both work; together is often stronger. Choice depends on symptoms, preferences, and health conditions.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long does treatment last?</p>
        <p>Plans vary. Many continue treatment for several months after feeling better to prevent relapse.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if I can’t get to appointments?</p>
        <p>Ask about phone or video visits, home-based services, or community programs that offer transportation.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
