  <!-- What is anxiety -->
  <section id="what-is-anxiety" class="section">
    <h2>What is anxiety?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Anxiety involves ongoing worry, tension, or fear that can affect sleep, energy, focus, and daily activities. It can also cause physical symptoms like palpitations, chest tightness, stomach upset, or shortness of breath.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Key idea:</strong> Anxiety is common at older ages and often coexists with medical conditions (heart, lung, thyroid, pain) or medications that can worsen symptoms. Effective treatments and practical skills work at any age.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Thoughts of self-harm or suicide</li>
        <li>Chest pain, pressure, or trouble breathing that may be heart- or lung-related</li>
        <li>Severe agitation, confusion, or new sudden panic with fainting</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">If at immediate risk, contact local emergency services. If you have self-harm thoughts, reach out to your clinician or local crisis resources.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Excessive worry, feeling “on edge” or keyed up</li>
          <li>Restlessness, muscle tension, shakiness</li>
          <li>Poor sleep, fatigue, irritability</li>
          <li>Difficulty concentrating or mind going blank</li>
          <li>Physical symptoms: palpitations, shortness of breath, chest or stomach discomfort, nausea, sweating</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Related topics</p>

          <li><a href="/conditions/🌙-sleep-problems">Sleep</a></li>
        </ul>

  <!-- Types -->
  <section id="types" class="section">
    <h2>Types: GAD, panic, social anxiety, phobias</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Type</th><th scope="col">Typical features</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)</strong></td>
          <td>Chronic, excessive worry about multiple areas (health, family, finances), plus restlessness, fatigue, muscle tension, poor sleep.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Panic Disorder</strong></td>
          <td>Sudden panic attacks with intense fear, palpitations, shortness of breath, chest discomfort, trembling; worry about future attacks.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Social Anxiety</strong></td>
          <td>Marked fear of being judged or embarrassed in social situations; avoidance can limit daily activities.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Specific Phobias</strong></td>
          <td>Focused fears (elevators, flying, procedures, heights, animals) leading to avoidance.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes & risks -->
  <section id="causes-risks" class="section">
    <h2>Causes & risk factors</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common contributors</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stressful life events, loneliness, caregiving strain</li>
          <li>Medical conditions (thyroid issues, arrhythmias, lung disease, chronic pain)</li>
          <li>Medicines/substances (stimulants, decongestants, high caffeine, alcohol withdrawal)</li>
          <li>Sleep problems, inactivity</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Protective factors</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular movement and consistent sleep routine</li>
          <li>Social connection and meaningful activities</li>
          <li>Skills training (breathing, grounding, problem-solving)</li>
          <li>Timely treatment for medical and mental health conditions</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Assessment -->
  <section id="assessment" class="section">
    <h2>How clinicians assess</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Interview about symptoms, triggers, sleep, mood, and daily impact</li>
        <li>Review of medicines and substances (including caffeine, alcohol)</li>
        <li>Screening tools may be used to track severity</li>
        <li>Focused exam and selected tests if symptoms suggest a medical cause (e.g., thyroid, ECG when appropriate)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Assessment looks for anxiety disorders, medical contributors, and coexisting low mood or cognitive concerns.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Skills & therapies</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)</strong>: identifies worry patterns and builds coping skills</li>
          <li><strong>Exposure methods</strong> for panic/phobias</li>
          <li>Problem-solving therapy; brief structured approaches for older adults</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Medicines (when indicated)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>First-line: <strong>SSRIs/SNRIs</strong> (dose and choice individualized)</li>
          <li><strong>Buspirone</strong> for some with GAD</li>
          <li><strong>Benzodiazepines</strong>: generally avoided or used cautiously in older adults due to fall, memory, and dependence risks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Combined care</p>
        <p>Therapy plus medicine often provides the best long-term results, with periodic review and dose adjustments.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">4) Supportive steps</p>
        <p>Address sleep apnea, pain, thyroid disorders, and reduce substances that worsen symptoms (excess caffeine, alcohol, nicotine).</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small note" style="margin-top:12px">Plans are individualized. Discuss options, benefits, and risks with your clinician.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily coping skills -->
  <section id="skills" class="section">
    <h2>Daily coping skills</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Breathing & grounding</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>4-6 breathing:</strong> in 4 seconds, out 6 seconds; repeat for 1–2 minutes</li>
          <li><strong>5-4-3-2-1 grounding:</strong> notice 5 things you see, 4 feel, 3 hear, 2 smell, 1 taste</li>
          <li>Use cues (chair arms, wall) for safety during dizziness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sleep & routine</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular bedtime and wake time; wind-down routine with dim light</li>
          <li>Limit screens before bed; keep bedroom cool and quiet</li>
          <li>Cut caffeine after mid-afternoon; avoid late heavy meals</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Movement & body</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Light daily activity (walks, tai chi, stretching) to reduce tension</li>
          <li>Eat regular balanced meals; stay hydrated through the day</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Mind & connection</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Brief journaling to park worries; set a daily “worry window” if helpful</li>
          <li>Call a friend or join a group; schedule simple enjoyable activities</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="calm small" style="margin-top:8px">
      If anxiety limits daily life or you notice mood changes, speak with your clinician—effective help is available.
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions for your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Which type of anxiety fits my symptoms?</li>
        <li>Could any medical issues or medicines be contributing?</li>
        <li>Which therapy approach is a good first step for me?</li>
        <li>Would a medicine help, and what side effects should I watch for?</li>
        <li>How will we track progress and adjust the plan?</li>
        <li>What should prompt me to call sooner?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Anxiety — quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can anxiety feel like a heart problem?</p>
        <p>Yes. Palpitations, chest tightness, and shortness of breath can occur. Chest pain or concerning symptoms should be medically assessed.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do skills really help at older ages?</p>
        <p>Yes. Structured skills (CBT, breathing, grounding, problem-solving) are effective and can be combined with medicine when needed.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long until I notice improvement?</p>
        <p>Many notice early benefits from skills within days to weeks. Medicines may take several weeks; steady routines support results.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are naps helpful?</p>
        <p>Short daytime rests can help some people, but long or late naps may worsen nighttime sleep and next-day anxiety for others.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
