            <li>New trouble speaking or understanding</li>

Your plan often includes physical, occupational, and speech therapy, plus safer swallowing steps, mood care, and prevention of another stroke. Every recovery is different—small daily practice leads to steady gains.</p>

      <!-- 3 First 2–6 weeks -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> First 2–6 weeks: simple daily plan <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Therapy homework:</strong> Short sessions (5–10 min) spread through the day beat one long session.</li>
            <li><strong>Safe mobility:</strong> Use your cane/walker as taught. Sit at edge of bed for a moment before standing.</li>
            <li><strong>Energy pacing:</strong> Alternate activity and rest; keep a simple diary of wins and limits.</li>
            <li><strong>Skin & shoulder care:</strong> Support weak arm; avoid pulling on it; protect skin from pressure.</li>
            <li><strong>Prevent clots & pneumonia:</strong> Walk often as advised; practice deep breathing; stay hydrated unless restricted.</li>
          </ol>

      <!-- 4 Movement -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Movement & balance (home exercises) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Practice near a counter or sturdy chair. Stop for pain or dizziness. Ask your therapist to tailor these.</p>
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Sit-to-Stand (8–12x):</strong> Use armrests only as needed; slow and steady.</li>
            <li><strong>Weight shift:</strong> Standing, shift weight side-to-side, then forward-back (10–20 reps).</li>
            <li><strong>Step taps:</strong> Tap one foot on a low step; alternate (2×10 each).</li>
            <li><strong>Ankle pumps & circles:</strong> 2×10 each side to reduce swelling and improve control.</li>
            <li><strong>Heel-toe walking:</strong> Along a counter with light support.</li>
          </ol>
          <p class="muted">AFO/foot-drop braces, FES devices, and task-specific practice may be recommended by your therapist.</p>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 5 Arm & hand -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Arm & hand recovery (use it safely) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Positioning:</strong> Support shoulder and forearm with pillows when sitting/sleeping.</li>
            <li><strong>Task practice:</strong> Reach, grasp, and release light objects; stack towels; open/close clips.</li>
            <li><strong>Constraint practice:</strong> Some plans briefly limit strong hand use to train the weaker hand (therapist-guided).</li>
            <li><strong>Spasticity:</strong> Gentle stretches; ask about splints or botulinum therapy if needed.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 6 Speech & swallowing -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Speech & swallowing (SLP tips) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Speech & language</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Short sentences; pause between thoughts</li>
                <li>Pointing, writing, or phone text can help early on</li>
                <li>Daily “naming” and “repeat after me” drills from your SLP</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Swallow safety</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Upright posture for meals; stay upright 30 minutes after</li>
                <li>Small bites/sips; avoid talking while chewing</li>
                <li>Follow texture/thickener plan from your SLP</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 7 Thinking & memory -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Thinking, memory & vision <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Use a simple day planner or phone reminders for meds and appointments</li>
            <li>Do one task at a time; lower background noise</li>
            <li>For vision loss on one side, turn your head and scan the room/page</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 8 Mood, sleep, fatigue -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> Mood, sleep & fatigue <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Feeling down, worried, or easily frustrated is common after stroke. Tell your clinician—counseling, peer support, and medicines can help. Aim for regular sleep/wake times, short naps (20–30 minutes), gentle daylight walks, and relaxing routines before bed.</p>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 9 Diet & hydration -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Diet & hydration (heart-brain friendly) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Plate basics</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>½ veggies/fruit, ¼ lean protein, ¼ whole grains</li>
                <li>Healthy fats: olive oil, nuts, seeds, fish</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Fluids & fiber</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Water through the day unless on restriction</li>
                <li>Fiber for regular bowels (oats, beans, fruit)</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Swallow aware</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Follow SLP texture guidance; avoid dry, crumbly foods early</li>
                <li>Alcohol can worsen balance and blood pressure—limit/avoid</li>
              </ul>
            </div>

      <!-- 11 Prevent another stroke -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">10</span> Preventing another stroke (everyday steps) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Know your BP, sugar, and cholesterol targets and track them</li>
            <li>Move daily as advised; no tobacco; limit alcohol</li>
            <li>Take meds on time; keep a 7-day pill box; set phone reminders</li>
            <li>Follow-up visits: neurology, primary care, therapy</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 12 Home safety & caregiver -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">11</span> Home safety & caregiver plan <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Clear clutter, add grab bars and non-slip mats; install nightlights</li>
            <li>Teach family safe transfers; never pull on the affected arm</li>
            <li>Keep emergency numbers visible; review red flags monthly</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 13 Myths -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">12</span> Myths to ignore <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>“Recovery stops at 3 months.”</strong> Progress can continue with practice and therapy.</li>
            <li><strong>“Pain means harm.”</strong> Gentle muscle soreness can be normal; sharp pain is a stop sign.</li>
            <li><strong>“If words are hard, conversation should stop.”</strong> Short, patient talks help the brain relearn.</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 14 FAQs -->
      <details class="section" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/FAQPage">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">13</span> FAQs <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">How long does stroke recovery take?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">It varies widely. Many people improve most in the first 3–6 months, then more slowly after. Keep practicing the right tasks for you.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">When can I drive again?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Only after your clinician clears you. Vision, attention, reaction time, and seizure risk must be checked first.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
