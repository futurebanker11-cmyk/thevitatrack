  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is hyperthyroidism?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> The thyroid makes too much hormone, speeding up body systems: heart rate, metabolism, bowels, and nerves. In older adults, signs can be subtle (fatigue, weight loss, atrial fibrillation) rather than classic tremor and heat intolerance.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Why treatment matters:</strong> Persistent overactive thyroid can strain the heart, weaken bones, and increase fall risk.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Symptoms in older adults</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Unintentional weight loss, poor appetite tolerance, muscle weakness</li>
          <li>Rapid or irregular heartbeat (<strong>atrial fibrillation</strong>), palpitations</li>
          <li>Shortness of breath with activity, reduced exercise tolerance</li>
          <li>Nervousness, anxiety, sleep problems; sometimes apathy (“apathetic hyperthyroidism”)</li>
          <li>Frequent stools/diarrhea, heat intolerance, warm/moist skin</li>
          <li>Shaking hands (tremor), eye irritation or bulging with Graves’ disease</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent care</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Chest pain, fainting, or new severe shortness of breath</li>
        <li>Very fast or irregular pulse, dizziness, or confusion</li>
        <li>High fever, agitation, vomiting/diarrhea with weakness (possible <strong>thyroid storm</strong>)</li>
        <li>Eye pain, double vision, vision loss</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies. Call your local emergency number.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Cause</th><th scope="col">What’s happening</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Graves’ disease</strong></td>
          <td>Autoantibodies stimulate the thyroid</td>
          <td>May cause eye findings (gritty eyes, bulging); diffuse uptake on scan</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Toxic multinodular goiter</strong></td>
          <td>Multiple overactive nodules</td>
          <td>Common in older adults; patchy uptake on scan</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Toxic adenoma</strong></td>
          <td>Single overactive nodule</td>
          <td>“Hot” nodule on scan; surrounding tissue suppressed</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Thyroiditis</strong></td>
          <td>Leak of stored hormone</td>
          <td>Often transient; <em>low</em> uptake on scan; may follow infection or be painless</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medicines/iodine</strong></td>
          <td>Amiodarone, excessive iodine, thyroid hormone excess</td>
          <td>Review supplements and contrast exposures</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>How it’s diagnosed (TSH, Free T4/T3, scan)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Labs</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>TSH low</strong> with <strong>Free T4 and/or T3 high</strong> → hyperthyroidism</li>
          <li>“Subclinical”: TSH low, Free T4/T3 normal; decisions depend on age, heart/bone risk, and symptoms</li>
          <li>Thyroid antibodies (TRAb/TSI) support Graves’ diagnosis</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Imaging</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Radioactive iodine uptake (RAIU) and scan</strong> distinguishes overproduction (high/patchy uptake) vs thyroiditis (low uptake)</li>
          <li>Ultrasound helps define nodules and goiter anatomy</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Beta-blockers (symptom relief)</p>
        <p>Help slow heart rate and reduce tremor/palpitations while other treatments take effect. Choice depends on heart/lung status.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Antithyroid drugs</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Methimazole</strong> is first choice for most adults</li>
          <li><strong>Propylthiouracil (PTU)</strong> reserved for specific cases (e.g., early pregnancy or thyroid storm)</li>
          <li>Rare risks: low white count, liver injury—report fever/sore throat, dark urine, or jaundice</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Radioactive iodine (RAI)</p>
        <p>Common definitive therapy for Graves’ or toxic nodules. Many patients become hypothyroid afterward and need levothyroxine.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Surgery</p>
        <p>Consider for large goiter, compressive symptoms, suspicious nodules, or when RAI/drugs aren’t suitable. Requires experienced surgeon.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">Choice depends on cause, age, heart and bone health, eye disease, and personal preference.</div>
  </section>

  <!-- Interactions -->
  <section id="interactions" class="section">
    <h2>Interactions & what to avoid</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Iodine exposures</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Limit unnecessary high-iodine supplements</li>
          <li>Tell your team before <strong>iodinated contrast</strong> scans—can worsen or trigger hyperthyroidism</li>
          <li><strong>Amiodarone</strong> can cause over- or under-active thyroid; needs close monitoring</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Antithyroid drug cautions</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Seek care urgently for fever/sore throat (possible agranulocytosis)</li>
          <li>Report jaundice, severe abdominal pain, dark urine (possible liver injury)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Keep an updated medication/supplement list and share it at every visit.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Follow-up -->
  <section id="followup" class="section">
    <h2>Monitoring & follow-up</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lab checks</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Recheck TSH and Free T4 (± T3) about <strong>4–6 weeks</strong> after starting or changing therapy</li>
          <li>With antithyroid drugs, periodic labs continue until stable; then extend intervals</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & bone health</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pulse/blood pressure checks; rhythm monitoring if palpitations</li>
          <li>Bone protection after long-standing hyperthyroidism; discuss DEXA when appropriate</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily -->
  <section id="daily" class="section">
    <h2>Daily care & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Energy & sleep</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Limit late-day caffeine; use calming routines to improve sleep</li>
          <li>Balance gentle activity with rest while symptoms improve</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Eye comfort (Graves’)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Lubricating drops, sunglasses, and head-of-bed elevation for swelling</li>
          <li>Stop smoking; it worsens eye disease</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Safety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stand up slowly if light-headed; hydrate well</li>
          <li>Seek care for chest pain, racing heart, fever, or sudden weakness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can hyperthyroidism cause atrial fibrillation?</p>
        <p>Yes. Overactive thyroid increases heart irritability and can trigger AFib—especially in older adults. Treating the thyroid often helps control rhythm.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will I need treatment forever?</p>
        <p>It depends on the cause. Thyroiditis may resolve; Graves’ may remit on medicines, or be treated definitively with RAI or surgery.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What side effects should I watch for on methimazole?</p>
        <p>Fever/sore throat (possible low white count) and liver symptoms (jaundice, dark urine, severe abdominal pain). Seek care urgently.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I take iodine supplements?</p>
        <p>Avoid high-dose iodine unless your clinician prescribes it—excess iodine can worsen hyperthyroidism.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
