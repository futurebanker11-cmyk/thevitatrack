  <!-- What it is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What it is</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Parkinson’s disease is a brain condition where <strong>dopamine-producing</strong> cells gradually decline. This leads to slowed movement, stiffness, tremor, balance changes, and non-motor issues like constipation, sleep problems, and mood or thinking changes.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good news:</strong> Medication timing, purposeful exercise, fall-proofing, and targeted therapy can maintain function for years. Choices are individualized with your clinician.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Frequent falls with injury or head strike</li>
        <li>Severe confusion, agitation, or sudden hallucinations</li>
        <li>Inability to swallow or choking episodes</li>
        <li>Severe constipation with abdominal pain, no gas or stool</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may be emergencies. Seek immediate care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Symptoms (motor & non-motor)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Motor</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Bradykinesia:</strong> slowness (buttoning, handwriting smaller)</li>
          <li><strong>Rigidity:</strong> stiffness, reduced arm swing</li>
          <li><strong>Rest tremor:</strong> often one-sided “pill-rolling”</li>
          <li><strong>Postural instability:</strong> imbalance, freezing of gait</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Non-motor</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Constipation, decreased smell, sleep changes (REM behavior disorder)</li>
          <li>Depression, anxiety, apathy</li>
          <li>Low blood pressure on standing (light-headedness)</li>
          <li>Mild cognitive changes; later, possible dementia</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Parkinson’s staging (simplified)">

  <!-- Diagnosis -->
  <section id="diagnosis" class="section">
    <h2>How it’s diagnosed</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Neurologic exam for bradykinesia plus tremor/rigidity</li>
          <li>Response to <strong>levodopa</strong> supports the diagnosis</li>
          <li>Imaging is usually not required; sometimes used to rule out other causes</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Look-alikes:</strong> drug-induced parkinsonism, vascular parkinsonism, atypical parkinsonian syndromes. Your clinician checks clues that suggest these.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines & timing</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Levodopa (+ carbidopa)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Most effective for slowness, stiffness, tremor</li>
          <li>Best taken on schedule; protein can reduce absorption for some—ask about timing</li>
          <li>Later, <strong>wearing-off</strong> or <strong>dyskinesias</strong> may occur; dosing is adjusted</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Other options</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>MAO-B inhibitors:</strong> selegiline, rasagiline, safinamide</li>
          <li><strong>Dopamine agonists:</strong> pramipexole, ropinirole, rotigotine patch</li>
          <li><strong>COMT inhibitors:</strong> entacapone, opicapone (extend levodopa effect)</li>
          <li><strong>Amantadine:</strong> may reduce dyskinesias</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Side-effects to watch:</strong> low blood pressure, nausea, sleepiness, hallucinations, impulse-control issues (more with dopamine agonists). Report changes promptly.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Nonpharm -->
  <section id="nonpharm" class="section">
    <h2>Non-drug therapy</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Therapies</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Physiotherapy:</strong> gait, posture, cueing for freezing</li>
          <li><strong>Occupational therapy:</strong> safer daily tasks, home setup</li>
          <li><strong>Speech therapy:</strong> voice volume (LSVT LOUD), swallowing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Exercise that helps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Brisk walking or cycling; 150+ minutes/week as tolerated</li>
          <li>Strength & balance 2–3×/week (sit-to-stands, heel-to-toe)</li>
          <li>Big-amplitude moves (LSVT BIG-style), dance, tai chi, boxing programs</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Movement & falls -->
  <section id="movement" class="section">
    <h2>Daily movement & fall safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stand up slowly; pause before first step</li>
          <li>Use <strong>visual or auditory cues</strong> to overcome freezing (laser line, metronome, “1-2-3 step”)</li>
          <li>Sturdy shoes; remove loose rugs; add night lights and grab bars</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Blood pressure on standing:</strong> Add water and salt only if advised; rise slowly; compression stockings may help. Ask about medication timing.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Speech & swallow -->
  <section id="swallowing" class="section">
    <h2>Speech, swallowing & saliva</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Speak up: deliberate loudness practice improves clarity</li>
          <li>Swallow tips: upright posture, small bites/sips, double swallow</li>
          <li>Moisten foods; review for silent aspiration if coughing at meals</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Drooling:</strong> sugar-free gum/lozenges, posture, timing of medicines; discuss options if persistent.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Bowel & bladder -->
  <section id="bowel" class="section">
    <h2>Constipation & bladder</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fluids through the day; fiber from fruits, vegetables, oats</li>
          <li>Regular toilet timing after meals; gentle activity</li>
          <li>Osmotic laxatives or stool softeners if advised</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Bladder tips</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Limit evening fluids; avoid caffeine late</li>
          <li>Pelvic floor exercises; ask about bladder medicines if urgency/frequency wake you at night</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Sleep -->
  <section id="sleep" class="section">
    <h2>Sleep & daytime fatigue</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Set a wind-down routine; light, quiet, and cool bedroom</li>
          <li>Address nocturia and leg cramps; keep pathways lit</li>
          <li>Talk to your clinician about REM behavior disorder and restless legs</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Sudden sleep attacks:</strong> Some medicines can cause this—avoid driving until cleared.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Mood & thinking -->
  <section id="mood" class="section">
    <h2>Mood, thinking & hallucinations</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Depression/anxiety are common and treatable—therapy, activity, and medicines can help</li>
          <li>Mild thinking changes: use lists, one task at a time, routine</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Hallucinations</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Report visual hallucinations; review medicines first</li>
          <li>Specialized treatments may be considered if distressing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Devices -->
  <section id="devices" class="section">
    <h2>Devices & procedures (DBS, pumps)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Helps tremor, wearing-off, and dyskinesias in selected patients</li>
          <li>Requires evaluation of symptoms, response to levodopa, and thinking/mood status</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Levodopa infusions / pumps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Continuous delivery for advanced wearing-off</li>
          <li>Discuss benefits, device care, and eligibility with specialists</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Living well -->
  <section id="care" class="section">
    <h2>Living well checklist</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Take medicines on time; set alarms; carry a daily list</li>
          <li>Move daily (walk, stretch, balance); schedule therapy tune-ups</li>
          <li>Hydration and fiber for bowels; review sleep and mood regularly</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fall-proof the home; consider a cane/walker if advised</li>
          <li>Keep dental, vision, and hearing up to date</li>
          <li>Plan rides; avoid driving if sleepiness or reaction time concerns</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support daily routines</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Consistent medicine timing; simple calendars and pill boxes</li>
          <li>Encourage exercise classes and safe social time</li>
          <li>Track falls, sleep, bowel habits, and new symptoms for visits</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>When to call:</strong> frequent falls, new confusion or hallucinations, swallowing problems, or sudden change in walking or speech.

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is tremor required for Parkinson’s?</p>
        <p>No. Slowness and stiffness can be prominent without tremor, especially early.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When to start levodopa?</p>
        <p>When symptoms affect function or quality of life. Early use doesn’t “make it wear out”; dosing is individualized.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best exercise?</p>
        <p>The one you’ll do consistently: brisk walking or cycling plus strength, stretching, and balance. Big-amplitude training helps.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What about diet?</p>
        <p>Balanced meals; fiber and fluids for constipation. Some separate higher-protein meals from levodopa doses—ask your clinician.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
