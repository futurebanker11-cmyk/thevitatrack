        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div>
              <p>Falls usually come from a mix of <strong>home hazards</strong> (loose rugs, poor lighting), <strong>body changes</strong> (weak legs, poor balance), and <strong>medicines</strong> that cause dizziness or sleepiness. Vision, hearing, foot pain, low blood pressure on standing, and rushing to the bathroom at night all add risk.</p>

      <!-- 3 Home safety checklist -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> Home safety checklist (15-minute scan) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Floors & stairs</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Remove loose rugs or use non-slip backing</li>
                <li>Coil/tape cords along walls</li>
                <li>Non-slip treads on stairs; repair uneven steps</li>
                <li>Handrails on both sides, ends extend fully</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Lighting</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Nightlights in bedroom, hall, and bathroom</li>
                <li>Motion lights at entry; bright bulbs, no glare</li>
                <li>Keep a flashlight by the bed</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Bathroom</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Grab bars near toilet and inside shower</li>
                <li>Non-slip mat in shower/tub</li>
                <li>Raised toilet seat or toilet frame if needed</li>
                <li>Shower chair + hand-held shower</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 4 Strength & balance -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Daily strength & balance (5–10 minutes) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Do these near a counter or sturdy chair. Stop if you feel dizzy or pain.</p>
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Sit-to-Stand (10x):</strong> From a chair, stand up and sit down slowly.</li>
            <li><strong>Heel-to-Toe (2x 10 steps):</strong> Walk along a line, heel touching toe.</li>
            <li><strong>Single-Leg Hold (2x 10–20s each):</strong> Light fingertip support.</li>
            <li><strong>Calf Raises (2x 10–15):</strong> Rise on toes, lower with control.</li>
            <li><strong>Ankle ABCs:</strong> “Write” the alphabet with each foot to loosen ankles.</li>
          </ol>
          <p class="muted">Consider a local <em>falls prevention</em> or <em>tai chi</em> class for guided balance work.</p>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 5 Footwear & mobility aids -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Footwear & mobility aids (walk safer) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Shoes</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Low heel, firm heel counter, non-slip sole</li>
                <li>Avoid floppy slippers and long robes</li>
                <li>Use orthotics if advised for foot pain or neuropathy</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Canes & walkers</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Get fitted for the correct height</li>
                <li>Use indoors first, practice turning and doorways</li>
                <li>Replace worn cane tips; add night-reflective tape</li>
              </ul>
            </div>

      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Medicines to review with your clinician <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Common medicine categories">

                </tr>
                <tr>
                  <td data-col="Category">Blood pressure</td>
                  <td data-col="Examples">Strong BP drops, diuretics</td>
                  <td data-col="Possible issue">Dizziness on standing</td>

            </table>
          </div>
          <p class="muted">Never stop a prescription on your own. Ask if safer alternatives or timing changes can reduce falls.</p>
        </div>

      <!-- 7 Vision, hearing, BP -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Vision, hearing & standing BP checks <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Annual eye exam;</strong> use single-vision lenses for stairs if bifocals blur steps.</li>
            <li><strong>Hearing aids</strong> improve balance and awareness of surroundings.</li>
            <li><strong>Standing BP:</strong> Ask for an “orthostatic” check; slow position changes; hydrate unless restricted.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 8 Strong bones -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> Strong bones = fewer fractures <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Daily habits</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Protein with each meal; calcium-rich foods (dairy, fortified plant milk, tofu set with calcium)</li>
                <li>Vitamin D per clinician advice; safe sunlight as appropriate</li>
                <li>Weight-bearing walks; gentle strength work 2–3×/week</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Testing & treatment</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Bone density (DEXA) if over 65 or with risk factors</li>
                <li>Discuss bone-protective medicines if osteopenia/osteoporosis</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 9 Nighttime & bathroom strategy -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Nighttime & bathroom safety plan <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Keep a bedside lamp or motion light; clear path to bathroom</li>
            <li>Do not rush; <em>sit for a moment</em> before standing</li>
            <li>Limit large drinks right before bed unless advised otherwise</li>
            <li>Consider bedside commode if frequent nighttime urination</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 10 If a fall happens -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">9</span> If you fall: safe get-up steps & when to call <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Pause & check pain.</strong> If severe pain or head hit → call help.</li>
            <li><strong>Roll to your side,</strong> get on hands and knees.</li>
            <li><strong>Crawl</strong> to a sturdy chair or sofa.</li>
            <li><strong>Place hands on seat,</strong> bring one foot forward, push to stand.</li>
            <li><strong>Sit,</strong> breathe, call a family member, and note what caused the fall.</li>
          </ol>

      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">10</span> Caregiver & family plan <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Do a monthly home safety walk-through together</li>
            <li>Rehearse the “get-up” steps; keep emergency numbers visible</li>
            <li>Track falls: date, time, place, shoes, meds taken, symptoms</li>

      <!-- 12 Myths -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">11</span> Myths to ignore <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>“Staying still prevents falls.”</strong> Gentle strength and balance training actually lowers risk.</li>
            <li><strong>“Grab bars look odd.”</strong> Modern designs look clean and prevent injuries.</li>
            <li><strong>“All slippers are safe.”</strong> Many slip—choose non-slip soles with heel support.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
