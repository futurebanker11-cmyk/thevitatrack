  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is hypertension?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Blood pressure (BP) is the force of blood on artery walls. When it stays high over time, it strains the heart, brain, kidneys, and eyes. The good news: accurate home checks, simple daily steps, and the right medicines make a big difference.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Why care:</strong> Managing BP lowers the chance of heart attack, stroke, kidney problems, vision loss, and memory decline.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>Urgent red flags</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>BP readings consistently <strong>very high</strong> with <strong>severe headache, chest pain, breathlessness, vision change, weakness, or confusion</strong></li>
        <li>Neurologic symptoms (face droop, arm weakness, speech trouble)</li>
        <li>New severe kidney pain or reduced urine with very high BP</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies. Seek urgent care right away.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Measure -->
  <section id="measure" class="section">
    <h2>How to measure BP (at home)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Before you start</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use a validated <strong>upper-arm</strong> cuff that fits your arm</li>
          <li>No caffeine, smoking, or exercise for 30 minutes</li>
          <li>Empty bladder; sit quietly for 5 minutes</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Position & steps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sit with back supported, feet flat, legs uncrossed</li>
          <li>Arm on table at heart level; cuff on bare arm</li>
          <li>Take <strong>two readings</strong> 1 minute apart; record the average</li>
          <li>Measure at the <strong>same time</strong> each day (e.g., morning and evening)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">Bring your device and home log to appointments so settings and cuff fit can be checked.</div>
  </section>

  <!-- Targets & white coat -->
  <section id="targets" class="section">
    <h2>Understanding goals & white-coat effect</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Know your personal goal</p>
        <p>Targets vary by age, conditions (kidney, diabetes), and medicines. Your clinician will set a goal and adjust over time.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">White-coat vs masked</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>White-coat:</strong> High in clinic but okay at home — home logs help avoid overtreatment.</li>
          <li><strong>Masked:</strong> Normal in clinic but high at home — home logs catch hidden risk.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Lifestyle -->
  <section id="lifestyle" class="section">
    <h2>Daily steps that help</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Movement</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Walking most days; add light strength and balance work</li>
          <li>Break up long sitting with short movement breaks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Plate & drinks</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>More vegetables, fruits, beans, whole grains; choose fish and lean proteins</li>
          <li>Hydration plan; limit alcohol as advised</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sleep & stress</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular sleep routine; ask about snoring or apnea</li>
          <li>Relaxation: breathing, stretching, short walks, social time</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Any new plan should fit your abilities and other conditions. Build gradually.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Salt -->
  <section id="sodium" class="section">
    <h2>Salt & label tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Simple swaps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Taste before salting; use herbs, lemon, pepper, garlic</li>
          <li>Rinse canned beans/veg; choose lower-sodium versions</li>
          <li>Watch packaged soups, sauces, instant noodles, pickles</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reading labels</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Compare “<strong>mg sodium per serving</strong>” and serving size</li>
          <li>“% Daily Value” ≤5% is low, ≥20% is high</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Common medicines</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Group</th><th scope="col">How it helps</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Thiazide diuretics</strong> (e.g., chlorthalidone)</td>
          <td>Reduce fluid load, relax vessels over time</td>
          <td>Check electrolytes; watch for low sodium/potassium</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>ACE inhibitors</strong> (e.g., lisinopril)</td>
          <td>Relax vessels; kidney and heart benefits in many</td>
          <td>Cough in some; monitor kidney function & potassium</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>ARBs</strong> (e.g., losartan)</td>
          <td>Similar to ACEi without cough</td>
          <td>Monitor kidney function & potassium</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Calcium channel blockers</strong> (e.g., amlodipine)</td>
          <td>Relax vessel muscle</td>
          <td>Possible ankle swelling or flushing</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Beta blockers</strong> (e.g., metoprolol)</td>
          <td>Slow heart rate; helpful after heart attack, some arrhythmias</td>
          <td>Not first-line for simple BP alone; monitor for fatigue</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">
      Medicines are individualized, often combined. Bring an updated list to every visit and report side effects early.
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Home monitor -->
  <section id="monitor" class="section">
    <h2>Home log & follow-up</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Set up your log</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Record date, time, average of two readings, and notes (meds taken, symptoms)</li>
          <li>Track morning and evening for 1–2 weeks when adjusting treatment</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Share results</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Bring your device and log to visits</li>
          <li>Ask how often to re-check once stable</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Orthostatic -->
  <section id="orthostatic" class="section">
    <h2>Dizziness on standing</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Why it happens</p>
        <p>Some medicines and dehydration can drop BP when standing (<strong>orthostatic</strong>). This raises fall risk.</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rise slowly; pause 3 breaths before walking</li>
          <li>Flex ankles/calf pumps before standing</li>
          <li>Discuss symptoms with your clinician; do <strong>not</strong> stop medicines on your own</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Stay hydrated as advised for your heart/kidney status. Review any dizziness, fainting, or near-falls promptly.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">My home BP is higher in the morning — normal?</p>
        <p>Many people have a morning surge. Logs help your clinician tailor timing and dose of medicines.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need a wrist cuff?</p>
        <p>Upper-arm cuffs are preferred for accuracy. If wrist is used, follow exact positioning at heart level.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How fast should numbers improve?</p>
        <p>Adjustments often take days to weeks. The goal is steady control without dizziness or side effects.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I stop meds if BP looks good?</p>
        <p>Only with your clinician’s guidance. BP often rises again without treatment or lifestyle support.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
