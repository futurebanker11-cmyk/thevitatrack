  <!-- What is GERD -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is GERD?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Stomach contents and acid flow back into the esophagus because the valve (LES) doesn’t close well. This causes <strong>heartburn</strong>, sour taste, chest discomfort, cough, or throat irritation. Night symptoms are common.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Reduce reflux episodes, protect the esophagus, and improve sleep and daily comfort.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>Urgent red flags</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Chest pressure with <strong>shortness of breath, sweating, jaw/arm pain</strong> (consider heart causes)</li>
        <li><strong>Trouble swallowing</strong>, food sticking, painful swallowing</li>
        <li>Unintentional weight loss, <strong>black stools</strong> or vomiting blood</li>
        <li>Frequent vomiting, severe dehydration, persistent hoarseness</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Seek urgent assessment for these symptoms.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Typical symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Burning behind the breastbone; sour taste or regurgitation</li>
          <li>Worse after large or late meals; bending or lying down</li>
          <li>Throat clearing, cough, hoarseness; bad breath</li>
          <li>Night wakings, disturbed sleep, morning sore throat</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">GERD vs. heart pain</p>
        <p>Reflux and cardiac pain can overlap. If pain is new, severe, or with breathlessness, sweating, or radiating to jaw/arm, treat as cardiac until proven otherwise.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Triggers -->
  <section id="triggers" class="section">
    <h2>Common triggers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Large meals; lying down within 2–3 hours after eating</li>
          <li>Fried/fatty foods; spicy foods; chocolate; mint</li>
          <li>Coffee/tea, cola, alcohol; citrus and tomato products</li>
          <li>Tight belts/waistbands; constipation straining</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Keep a simple <strong>trigger log</strong>: meal, time, symptoms. Adjust one factor at a time for 1–2 weeks to learn your pattern.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>How GERD is diagnosed</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Initial approach</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>History, exam, and a trial of lifestyle changes ± medicines</li>
          <li>Check for red flags that need early endoscopy</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Tests (when needed)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Upper endoscopy (EGD)</strong> for alarm features or long-standing symptoms</li>
          <li><strong>pH monitoring</strong> (with/without impedance) to confirm reflux</li>
          <li><strong>Esophageal manometry</strong> before surgery or if motility issue suspected</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lifestyle foundation</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Smaller, earlier dinners; avoid lying down for 2–3 hours after meals</li>
          <li>Weight reduction if advised; gentle walking after meals</li>
          <li>Elevate the <strong>head of the bed</strong> 6–8 inches (blocks or wedge pillow)</li>
          <li>Stop tobacco; limit alcohol; loosen tight waistbands</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Antacids</strong> for quick relief</li>
          <li><strong>Alginates</strong> (raft-forming) after meals/bedtime for regurgitation</li>
          <li><strong>H2 blockers</strong> (e.g., at night for mild symptoms)</li>
          <li><strong>PPIs</strong> (mainstay for frequent symptoms/esophagitis; take before breakfast)</li>
          <li>Step-down to lowest effective dose once controlled</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Procedures (selected cases)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Laparoscopic fundoplication</strong> or <strong>magnetic sphincter augmentation</strong> when anatomy/symptoms fit</li>
          <li>Discuss benefits/risks; testing guides candidacy</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Long-term PPI use should be <strong>periodically reviewed</strong>. Taper if possible; avoid stopping abruptly to reduce rebound acid.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Night reflux -->
  <section id="sleep" class="section">
    <h2>Night reflux & sleep setup</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dinner at least <strong>3–4 hours</strong> before bed; light snack if needed</li>
          <li>Head-of-bed elevation (blocks under legs or wedge pillow), not extra pillows</li>
          <li>Sleep on the <strong>left side</strong> to reduce nocturnal reflux</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        If cough, wheeze, or voice changes persist at night, ask about <strong>silent reflux</strong> and whether a trial of alginate at bedtime is appropriate.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Diet swaps & hydration</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gentle choices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Oatmeal, bananas, melons, yogurt/curd, lean proteins, steamed veggies</li>
          <li>Whole grains; soups that aren’t tomato-based</li>
          <li>Herbal teas (non-mint), ginger infusions</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Consider limiting</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Deep-fried/fatty foods, chocolate, peppermint</li>
          <li>Tomato/citrus-heavy meals, onion/garlic for some</li>
          <li>Coffee/tea/cola and alcohol if they worsen symptoms</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small" style="margin-top:8px">Hydration: steady sips through the day; reduce large fluid loads late evening if nocturnal symptoms occur.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Seniors -->
  <section id="seniors" class="section">
    <h2>Notes for older adults</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Polypharmacy & interactions</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>PPIs can reduce absorption of <strong>B12</strong>, <strong>iron</strong>, and affect some drugs</li>
          <li>Space <strong>thyroxine</strong>, <strong>bisphosphonates</strong>, and some antibiotics away from antacids</li>
          <li>Ask before combining with clopidogrel or diuretics</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Safety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Frequent night reflux raises <strong>aspiration</strong> risk — elevate bed, left-side sleep</li>
          <li>Persistent difficulty swallowing or food sticking needs evaluation</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Complications -->
  <section id="complications" class="section">
    <h2>Complications (why control matters)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Esophagitis and bleeding; <strong>strictures</strong> (narrowing)</li>
          <li><strong>Barrett’s esophagus</strong> in some long-standing cases</li>
          <li>Chronic cough, asthma flares, dental enamel wear</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card small">
        Related: Hydration & Drinks · Reflux-safe drinks · Weight & activity
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions for your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Do I need endoscopy now or can we try a time-limited PPI course?</li>
        <li>What’s my plan to <strong>step down</strong> medicines once controlled?</li>
        <li>Which triggers matter most for me? Can we review my food log?</li>
        <li>How should I time medicines with my other prescriptions?</li>
        <li>When should I be re-checked or referred for procedure options?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>GERD — quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long to try a PPI?</p>
        <p>Many plans use a <strong>4–8 week</strong> trial; if better, step down to the lowest dose that maintains control.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are alginates different from antacids?</p>
        <p>Yes. They form a <strong>floating raft</strong> on stomach contents to reduce regurgitation — useful after meals and at bedtime.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need lifelong medicine?</p>
        <p>Not always. Many can control symptoms with lifestyle plus the <strong>lowest effective</strong> medication dose or on-demand use.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does weight loss help?</p>
        <p>Even a modest reduction can lower reflux pressure and improve symptoms, especially night reflux.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
