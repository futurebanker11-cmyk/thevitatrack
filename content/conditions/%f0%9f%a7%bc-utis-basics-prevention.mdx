      <details class="section" open>
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">1</span> What is a UTI? <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>A urinary tract infection (UTI) happens when germs grow in the bladder or urinary tract. Typical bladder infection symptoms include burning or pain with urination, going often, strong urgency, and lower abdominal pressure. In older adults, symptoms can be milder or different.</p>
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Common bladder (cystitis) symptoms</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Burning or pain when urinating</li>
                <li>Urgency and frequent small amounts</li>
                <li>Cloudy or strong-smelling urine</li>
                <li>Lower belly pressure or pain</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Upper UTI warning signs</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Fever or chills</li>
                <li>Back/side (flank) pain</li>
                <li>Nausea or vomiting</li>
                <li>Feeling generally unwell</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 2 When to seek care -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> When to seek care (red flags) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="note warn">
            <ul>
              <li>Fever, back/side pain, or vomiting</li>
              <li>Blood in urine</li>
              <li>Symptoms in pregnancy or after certain urologic procedures</li>
              <li>Confusion, weakness, or sudden decline in an older adult</li>
              <li>Symptoms that do not improve within 48–72 hours of treatment</li>
            </ul>
            <p><strong>Emergency:</strong> severe illness, unable to keep fluids down, or signs of sepsis (fever with shaking chills, rapid breathing, confusion)—seek urgent care.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 3 Simple steps that help -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> First steps that help many people <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Fluids & timing</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Drink through the day (clear/pale-yellow urine is a good sign).</li>
                <li>Finish most fluids 2–3 hours before bedtime to reduce night trips.</li>
                <li>Use the bathroom after waking and before bed.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Hygiene & toilet habits</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Wipe front-to-back; avoid harsh soaps or douches.</li>
                <li>Do not “hold it” for long periods; go when you feel the need.</li>
                <li>After bowel movements, ensure full clean; constipation can worsen UTIs.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>After intimacy</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Urinate soon after sexual activity.</li>
                <li>Avoid spermicides if you get frequent UTIs.</li>
                <li>Discuss post-coital antibiotic options if infections are frequent.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 4 Postmenopausal & men -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Postmenopausal & men: special notes <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Postmenopausal changes</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Vaginal dryness and tissue changes can increase UTI risk.</li>
                <li>Ask about local vaginal estrogen options (creams/tablets/ring) if appropriate.</li>
                <li>Consider pelvic floor exercises to improve control and emptying.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Men & prostate</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Slow stream or incomplete emptying may signal prostate enlargement (BPH).</li>
                <li>Night urination, urgency, and UTIs can improve with BPH treatment.</li>
                <li>Discuss persistent or recurrent UTIs with a clinician for evaluation.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 5 Medication basics -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Medication basics (plain guidance) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Antibiotics:</strong> type and duration depend on your symptoms, urine test, allergies, kidneys, and local patterns. Finish the full course unless told otherwise.</li>
            <li><strong>Pain relief:</strong> ask about safe choices (some people use short-term urinary analgesics; urine may turn orange).</li>
            <li><strong>Probiotics / cranberry / D-mannose:</strong> some people find these helpful; discuss with your clinician, especially if on other meds.</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 6 Preventing repeats -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Preventing repeats (checklist) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Daily</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Hydrate evenly through the day.</li>
                <li>Bathroom when you need; avoid long holds.</li>
                <li>Gentle movement to keep bowels regular.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Hygiene</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Front-to-back wiping; simple, unscented soap on the outside only.</li>
                <li>Cotton underwear; avoid tight garments that trap moisture.</li>
                <li>Change pads/briefs regularly; keep skin dry.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Medical follow-up</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Recurrent UTIs (≥2 in 6 months or ≥3 in a year) — ask about preventive options.</li>
                <li>Review medicines that may affect bladder emptying.</li>
                <li>Consider evaluation for bladder emptying and residual urine if needed.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Caregiver notes (home & facility) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Encourage regular bathroom schedules and hydration during the day.</li>
            <li>Track new confusion, falls, fever, or changes in toileting—share with the clinician.</li>
            <li>For catheters: follow sterile technique, keep drainage bag below bladder, avoid kinks, and monitor for fever or color changes.</li>
            <li>Protect skin: gentle cleansing, barrier creams for moisture, frequent brief changes.</li>
          </ul>
