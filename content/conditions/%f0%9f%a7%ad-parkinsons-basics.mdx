      <!-- 2 What is PD -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">1</span> What is Parkinson’s? Key symptoms <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div>
              <p><strong>Parkinson’s disease</strong> affects brain cells that make dopamine, a chemical that helps smooth, coordinated movement. This causes motor symptoms like shaking (tremor), slowness, stiffness, and balance problems. Many people also have <em>non-motor</em> symptoms such as constipation, low mood, sleep issues, loss of smell, and memory or thinking changes.</p>
              <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Motor vs non-motor symptoms">
                <table class="responsive">
                  <thead><tr><th>Motor</th><th>Non-motor</th></tr></thead>
                  <tbody>
                    <tr><td data-col="Motor">Tremor at rest, slowness, stiffness</td><td data-col="Non-motor">Constipation, urinary urgency</td></tr>
                    <tr><td data-col="Motor">Small steps, shuffling, freezing of gait</td><td data-col="Non-motor">Sleep problems (REM behavior, insomnia)</td></tr>
                    <tr><td data-col="Motor">Reduced arm swing, soft voice</td><td data-col="Non-motor">Low mood/apathy, anxiety</td></tr>
                    <tr><td data-col="Motor">Posture changes, balance issues</td><td data-col="Non-motor">Loss of smell, fatigue, thinking changes</td></tr>
                  </tbody>
                </table>
              </div>
            </div>
            <div class="note tip">
              <strong>Important:</strong> Symptoms vary widely. A neurologist confirms the diagnosis and personalizes your plan.
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 3 Daily routine -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> Daily routine (simple wins) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Regular exercise:</strong> Aim most days for walking, stationary bike, tai chi, or amplitude-based therapy ("BIG"-style moves). Short, frequent sessions beat long, rare ones.</li>
            <li><strong>Hydration & fiber:</strong> Warm water in the morning, vegetables, fruit, oats, and adequate fluids help constipation and pill absorption.</li>
            <li><strong>Sleep routine:</strong> Fixed bedtime/wake time; evening wind-down; safe, well-lit path to bathroom.</li>
            <li><strong>Safety first:</strong> Clear floors, non-slip shoes, grab bars in bathroom, raised toilet seat if needed.</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 4 Medicine timing -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Medicine timing (why schedule matters) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Many plans include <strong>carbidopa/levodopa</strong> and sometimes dopamine agonists or other add-on medicines. Doses are often timed evenly through the day. Protein-heavy meals can interfere with levodopa absorption for some people—your clinician may suggest taking pills on an emptier stomach or adjusting protein timing.</p>
          <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Timing tips (general)">
            <table class="responsive">
              <thead><tr><th>Topic</th><th>Simple guidance</th></tr></thead>
              <tbody>
                <tr><td data-col="Topic">Consistency</td><td data-col="Simple guidance">Take at the <em>same times daily</em>—set alarms or use a pill box.</td></tr>
                <tr><td data-col="Topic">Meals</td><td data-col="Simple guidance">If advised, take levodopa 30–60 min before meals or 1–2 h after. Keep protein steady across meals.</td></tr>
                <tr><td data-col="Topic">Wearing-off</td><td data-col="Simple guidance">Note when symptoms return before the next dose ("OFF"). Share this timing with your clinician.</td></tr>
                <tr><td data-col="Topic">Side effects</td><td data-col="Simple guidance">Nausea, lightheadedness, sleepiness, or dyskinesia—report promptly for dose/interval adjustments.</td></tr>
              </tbody>
            </table>

      <!-- 5 Movement & freezing -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Move with confidence (freezing & balance) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Freezing cues:</strong> Count “1-2-3 GO,” step to a beat or metronome, place a target on the floor (laser line/tape), or shift weight side-to-side before stepping.</li>
            <li><strong>Turns:</strong> Take multiple small steps; avoid pivoting on one foot.</li>
            <li><strong>Doorways & crowds:</strong> Look ahead, slow down, widen stance, and cue yourself before entering.</li>
            <li><strong>Balance:</strong> Chair-supported heel-to-toe practice, side steps, and sit-to-stand drills—always within safety.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 6 Speech, swallow, saliva -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Voice, speech, saliva & swallowing <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Speak up:</strong> Practice loud, clear voice (think “big and bold”). Face your listener and slow down.</li>
            <li><strong>Swallow safety:</strong> Sit upright for meals, take small bites/sips, tuck chin slightly, and avoid talking while chewing. Ask your clinician about a speech-language pathology referral.</li>
            <li><strong>Saliva/drooling:</strong> Sugar-free gum or lozenges may help. Discuss medicine options if bothersome.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 7 Common non-motor -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Common non-motor problems (simple fixes) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Constipation</h3>
              <p>Warm fluids on waking, fiber-rich foods, daily walks, and regular bathroom time after meals. Ask about stool softeners if needed.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Sleep & REM behaviors</h3>
              <p>Keep a calm routine, secure the sleep area, and report acting out dreams. Melatonin is sometimes used—only with clinician guidance.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Mood & thinking</h3>
              <p>Low mood and anxiety are common and treatable. Cognitive training, social connection, and good sleep all help. Tell your clinician early.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Blood pressure drops</h3>
              <p>Rise slowly, pump ankles before standing, drink water through the day, and ask about compression socks. Review medicines if dizzy.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 8 When to see specialist / devices -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> When to see a specialist; devices & surgery <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Frequent OFF periods, troublesome dyskinesia, or persistent freezing</li>
            <li>Severe tremor despite medicines</li>
            <li>Questions about <strong>deep brain stimulation (DBS)</strong> or pump/patch therapies</li>
            <li>Swallowing problems, weight loss, or repeated pneumonia</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 9 Caregiver plan -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Caregiver & family plan <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Post the <strong>medicine schedule</strong> where it’s visible; use alarms and refill reminders.</li>
            <li>Set up home safety: remove loose rugs, add night lights, install grab bars, use sturdy chairs with arms.</li>
            <li>Encourage social time, safe exercise, and outdoor walks for mood and energy.</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 10 Myths -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">9</span> Myths to ignore <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>“Nothing helps Parkinson’s.”</strong> Movement, timing your medicines, and specialist care can make a big difference.</li>
            <li><strong>“Rest is best.”</strong> Daily activity is therapy. Find safe, enjoyable movement you can repeat.</li>
            <li><strong>“Tremor is the only sign.”</strong> Many people have few tremors but do have slowness, stiffness, or balance issues.</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 11 FAQs + Multiplex -->
      <details class="section" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/FAQPage">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">10</span> FAQs <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Is Parkinson’s the same as “Parkinsonism”?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">No. “Parkinsonism” means a group of conditions with similar movement symptoms. Atypical forms progress differently and need specialist care to sort out.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Can diet cure Parkinson’s?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">There is no known cure. A steady, fiber-rich diet, hydration, and protein timing (when advised) can support medicine effect and overall health.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">When should we consider DBS?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">If medicines help but you have disabling fluctuations, dyskinesia, or tremor, ask a movement-disorders specialist about DBS evaluation.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
