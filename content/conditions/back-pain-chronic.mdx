  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is-chronic" class="section">
    <h2>What is chronic back pain?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Back pain lasting <strong>3+ months</strong>. In older adults it’s commonly due to arthritis, spinal stenosis, muscle deconditioning, or nerve irritation. Most cases improve with a steady plan: movement, targeted exercises, safe meds, and home setup.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good to know:</strong> Imaging is not always needed first; the story and exam guide whether scans will change treatment.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>New weakness in a leg/foot, foot drop, or trouble walking</li>
        <li>Loss of bladder/bowel control, severe numbness in the groin (saddle anesthesia)</li>
        <li>Fever with back pain, or back pain after major trauma</li>
        <li>Unexplained weight loss or history of cancer with new severe back pain</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies (possible nerve compression, infection, fracture). Seek immediate care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Patterns -->
  <section id="patterns" class="section">
    <h2>Pain patterns & clues</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Mechanical/arthritic:</strong> Worse with activity, better with rest; morning stiffness</li>
          <li><strong>Spinal stenosis:</strong> Leg ache/tingle with walking; relief when leaning forward or sitting</li>
          <li><strong>Radicular (sciatica):</strong> Sharp, shooting pain down leg; numbness/tingling</li>
          <li><strong>Myofascial/strain:</strong> Sore, tight bands; improves with heat and gentle movement</li>
          <li><strong>Compression fracture:</strong> Sudden pain after minor strain; height loss (ask about bone health)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Assessment -->
  <section id="assessment" class="section">
    <h2>How clinicians evaluate</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>History (pattern, triggers, red-flags), exam (strength, reflexes, sensation, gait)</li>
        <li>Imaging only when needed: red-flags, persistent radicular symptoms, or when results change treatment</li>
        <li>Bone health review (osteoporosis risk, height loss, fractures)</li>
        <li>Medication review (sedatives, long-term steroids, anticoagulants)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Most chronic back pain improves with guided movement and lifestyle changes even without early scans.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options (stepwise)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Non-drug basics</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Heat 15–20 min, 2–3×/day; gentle daily walking</li>
          <li>PT-guided core, hip, and posture training; graded activity</li>
          <li>CBT-style pain coping and relaxation skills; pacing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Topicals & simple meds</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Topical NSAID gel (if safe), lidocaine patches, capsaicin</li>
          <li>Acetaminophen within safe daily limits</li>
          <li>Oral NSAIDs only if appropriate (heart/kidney/stomach review first)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Targeted options</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>For nerve pain: selected agents as advised; address sleep</li>
          <li>Injections (facet, SI, epidural) for selected cases</li>
          <li>Bracing short term for fracture; bone density treatment if osteoporotic</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Surgery</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Reserved for red-flags, severe stenosis with poor function, or cases not improving with comprehensive care</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small note" style="margin-top:12px">Plans are individualized; medicine safety varies with heart, kidney, stomach, and bleeding risk.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Exercises -->
  <section id="exercises" class="section">
    <h2>Starter exercises (gentle)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily mobility (2–3 rounds)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pelvic tilts (supine), 10 slow reps</li>
          <li>Knee-to-chest (single then both), 5–10 breaths</li>
          <li>Seated spinal tall-sit + shoulder rolls, 10 rolls</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Core & hip support</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Abdominal bracing with breath, 5×10 seconds</li>
          <li>Bridges, 8–12 reps (pause at top)</li>
          <li>Standing hip abduction holding a counter, 10 reps/side</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small calm" style="margin-top:10px">Move within comfort; slight soreness is common, sharp or spreading leg pain is a stop signal — check with your clinician or PT.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Sleep & ergonomics -->
  <section id="sleep" class="section">
    <h2>Sleep positions & daily ergonomics</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sleep</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Side-lying with pillow between knees</li>
          <li>Back-lying with pillow under knees</li>
          <li>Avoid stomach sleeping if it worsens pain</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daytime setup</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hip-hinge for bending; keep items at waist height</li>
          <li>Change position every 30–45 minutes; micro-walks</li>
          <li>Supportive chair with armrests; small lumbar pillow if helpful</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Self care -->
  <section id="selfcare" class="section">
    <h2>Heat, topicals, and pacing</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heat & cold</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Warm pack before activity; brief ice after flare-provoking tasks</li>
          <li>Protect skin; avoid sleeping on heat sources</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Topicals</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>NSAID gels (if safe), lidocaine patches, or capsaicin cream</li>
          <li>Wash hands after application; follow dosing on label</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Pacing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Break chores into chunks; alternate light and heavier tasks</li>
          <li>Plan rest before pain spikes; keep a brief movement log</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Steady, gentle activity outperforms long bed rest. If pain escalates despite these steps, request a review.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions for your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>What seems to be the main source of my pain (joint, muscle, nerve, fracture)?</li>
        <li>Which exercises should I start and which should I avoid?</li>
        <li>Are topical options or acetaminophen suitable for me? What’s my safe daily limit?</li>
        <li>Do I need imaging now, or only if symptoms change?</li>
        <li>Would PT, pain psychology, or injections be useful in my case?</li>
        <li>How do we monitor bone health to prevent fractures?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is bed rest helpful?</p>
        <p>Short rest during flares is fine, but extended bed rest slows recovery. Gentle, frequent movement helps most people.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When do I need an MRI?</p>
        <p>When red-flags are present, significant nerve symptoms persist, or when results will change treatment (e.g., surgery or injection planning).</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are NSAIDs safe for me?</p>
        <p>Depends on heart, kidney, stomach, and bleeding risks. Many seniors start with topicals and acetaminophen; ask about your safest plan.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What about braces?</p>
        <p>Short-term use can help after a fracture. For chronic pain without fracture, rely more on core/hip strengthening.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
