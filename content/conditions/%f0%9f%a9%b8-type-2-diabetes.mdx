      <details class="section" open>
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">1</span> Overview: what’s happening in Type 2 <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>With Type 2 diabetes (T2D), the body doesn’t use insulin well (“insulin resistance”) and may not make enough over time. Glucose builds up in the blood and quietly harms blood vessels and nerves. The good news: small daily steps—<em>done consistently</em>—can lower glucose, protect eyes/kidneys/feet, and boost energy.</p>
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Core tools:</strong> calm eating pattern, gentle movement after meals, sleep routine, stress management, and medications when needed.</li>
            <li><strong>Trends over single readings:</strong> A1C (3-month average) + home checks tell the real story.</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 5 Daily eating (Plate Method) -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Daily eating: The simple Plate Method <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>1) Half the plate: non-starchy veggies</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Broccoli, greens, cauliflower, peppers, carrots, salad</li>
                <li>Flavor with herbs, lemon, garlic—taste before salting</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>2) Quarter: protein</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Fish, chicken, eggs, tofu, beans/lentils (watch portions if CKD)</li>
                <li>Choose grilled/roasted; limit breaded or salty sauces</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>3) Quarter: smart carbs</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Oats, brown rice, quinoa, whole-grain bread, corn, sweet potato</li>
                <li>Start with modest portions; pair with protein/veggies</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
          <h3>Easy swaps</h3>
          <ul>
            <li>White rice → cauliflower rice + half-portion brown rice mix</li>
            <li>Sugary drinks → water with lemon; unsweet tea</li>
            <li>Fried foods → baked/air-fried; sauces on the side</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="note">Kidney or heart failure? Your protein/sodium/fluid targets may differ. Follow your clinician’s plan.</div>

      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> A gentle, realistic day plan <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Morning</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Finger-stick before breakfast (if advised)</li>
                <li>Breakfast: oats + nuts + berries <em>or</em> eggs + veggies + whole-grain toast</li>
                <li>5–10 minute walk after breakfast</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Midday/Afternoon</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Lunch: Plate Method; water first</li>
                <li>Short walk after meals; stretch break each hour</li>
                <li>Plan dinner early to avoid last-minute takeout</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Evening</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Dinner: half veggies, quarter protein, quarter carb</li>
                <li>Wind-down routine; limit screens before bed</li>
                <li>Prep meds, meter, and log for the morning</li>
              </ul>

      <!-- 7 Medications overview -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> Medication basics (plain language) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Many seniors need medicine plus daily habits. Your prescriber chooses based on A1C, kidney/heart health, weight, and side-effect risks.</p>
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Common groups</h3>
              <ul>
                <li><strong>Metformin:</strong> lowers liver sugar output; stomach upset possible; often first-line</li>
                <li><strong>GLP-1 agonists:</strong> help the body release insulin when needed; often aid weight and heart risk; may cause nausea early</li>
                <li><strong>SGLT2 inhibitors:</strong> help kidneys release extra glucose in urine; heart/kidney benefits for many; watch genital/UTI risk and hydration</li>
                <li><strong>DPP-4 inhibitors:</strong> modest glucose help, generally well-tolerated</li>
                <li><strong>Insulin:</strong> when needed for stronger control or during illness/surgery</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="note tip">
              <strong>Don’t stop suddenly.</strong> If you feel dizzy, overly tired, or have repeated lows/highs, call your clinician. Bring a 3–7 day log.
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 8 Hypo & hyper fixes -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Lows & highs: quick fixes (safety) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Low sugar (hypoglycemia)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Shaky, sweaty, hungry, confused</li>
                <li><strong>15-15 rule:</strong> 15g fast sugar (glucose tabs/gel, 4 oz juice), wait 15 min, recheck; repeat if still low</li>
                <li>Eat a small snack if next meal is far away</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>High sugar (hyperglycemia)</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Thirst, frequent urination, blurry vision, fatigue</li>
                <li>Hydrate (if allowed), easy walk, check meds/food portions</li>
                <li>Call if very high readings persist or you feel ill</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="note warn" style="margin-top:10px"><strong>Emergency:</strong> Confusion, severe drowsiness, vomiting, or breathing changes—seek urgent care now.</div>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 9 Feet, eyes, kidneys -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">9</span> Protect feet, eyes, and kidneys <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Feet</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Check daily: cuts, blisters, redness</li>
                <li>Moisturize (not between toes); well-fitting shoes</li>
                <li>Report wounds or numbness/tingling</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Eyes</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Yearly dilated eye exam (or as advised)</li>
                <li>Report new floaters or vision changes promptly</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Kidneys</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Urine albumin and eGFR checks as scheduled</li>
                <li>Ask about ACEi/ARB if albumin is elevated</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 10 Weight & activity -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">10</span> Weight, waist, and gentle activity <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <p>Even a modest drop in waist size or weight can improve glucose and blood pressure. Focus on <em>repeatable</em> habits over perfection.</p>
          <ul>
            <li>5–10 minute walks after meals (most days)</li>
            <li>Chair stands, light hand weights, or bands 2–3×/week</li>
            <li>Balance practice near a counter (safety first)</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 11 Special situations -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">11</span> Special notes (65+, kidney, heart, surgery/illness) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Age 65+:</strong> Avoid lows—targets may be slightly higher to protect from falls/confusion.</li>
            <li><strong>Kidney disease:</strong> Ask about SGLT2 benefits and safe pain relievers; review potassium/salt guidance.</li>
            <li><strong>Heart disease/heart failure:</strong> Daily weight, sodium awareness, and medication timing matter.</li>
            <li><strong>Illness/surgery (“sick day” plan):</strong> Keep hydration, check glucose more often, know when to pause certain meds—get a written plan.</li>
          </ul>

        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">12</span> FAQs <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Can Type 2 diabetes improve with routine?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Yes—consistent eating patterns, post-meal movement, sleep, stress tools, and the right medicines can lower A1C and protect organs. Your plan is personal.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Do I need to avoid all carbs?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">No. Choose smart portions of higher-fiber carbs and pair them with protein/veggies. Check your meter to learn which meals work best for you.</p>
            </div>
