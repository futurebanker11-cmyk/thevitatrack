      <!-- 1 Quick start -->
      <details class="section" open>
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">1</span> Quick start: the 5-point posture checklist <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Sit quietly 5 minutes</strong> (no talking). Back supported, feet flat, legs uncrossed, bladder empty.</li>
            <li><strong>Upper-arm cuff</strong> on bare skin. <em>Correct size matters</em> (see section 3).</li>
            <li><strong>Arm supported at heart level</strong> (on a table/pillow). Palm up, relaxed shoulders.</li>
            <li><strong>No caffeine, nicotine, or hard exercise 30 minutes</strong> before checking.</li>
            <li><strong>Take 2–3 readings 1 minute apart</strong>. Record the average (top and bottom numbers).</li>
          </ol>

      <!-- 2 Best times -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> Best times to check (and how often) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Morning:</strong> before food, pills, or coffee.</li>
            <li><strong>Evening:</strong> before bed, after resting quietly.</li>
            <li><strong>When starting/changing treatment:</strong> morning and evening for 3–7 days, then share the averages with your clinician.</li>
            <li><strong>Long-term tracking:</strong> a few days each month or as advised, and whenever symptoms change.</li>
          </ul>
          <p class="note tip"><strong>Consistency beats volume.</strong> Same chair, same arm, same times gives a truer trend.</p>

      <!-- 4 Step-by-step routine -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Step-by-step routine (simple & repeatable) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ol>
            <li><strong>Prepare the spot:</strong> quiet chair with back support, a table to rest your forearm, feet flat.</li>
            <li><strong>Place the cuff:</strong> 1–2 cm above the bend of the elbow, tube along inner arm. Snug, not painful.</li>
            <li><strong>Rest 5 minutes:</strong> breathe slowly; don’t talk or check your phone.</li>
            <li><strong>Press start:</strong> keep still, hand relaxed, arm supported at heart level.</li>
            <li><strong>Wait 1 minute</strong> and repeat. Take 2–3 readings and <strong>record the average</strong>.</li>
          </ol>

      <!-- 5 Common errors -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Common errors that raise numbers (and quick fixes) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Talking / texting</strong> → stay quiet during the reading.</li>
            <li><strong>Feet dangling / legs crossed</strong> → feet flat, legs uncrossed.</li>
            <li><strong>Arm hanging down</strong> → support forearm at heart level.</li>
            <li><strong>Cuff over clothing</strong> → place on bare skin.</li>
            <li><strong>Wrong cuff size</strong> → measure arm; use correct cuff.</li>
            <li><strong>Just had coffee/nicotine/exercise</strong> → wait 30 minutes.</li>
            <li><strong>Full bladder</strong> → use the bathroom first.</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 7 How to average & log -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">7</span> Average & log your results (clinic-ready) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ol>
            <li>Write each reading: date/time, top/bottom, pulse, arm used.</li>
            <li>Average the 2–3 readings for that sitting (add, then divide).</li>
            <li>Do morning and evening for 3–7 days; average each column.</li>
            <li>Note context: poor sleep, salty meal, missed dose, pain, stress.</li>
          </ol>

      <!-- 8 Troubleshooting -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Troubleshooting error codes & odd readings <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Error / no reading:</strong> re-seat cuff, relax arm, re-start after 1 minute.</li>
            <li><strong>Very different left vs right arm:</strong> repeat calmly; if persistent and large difference, tell your clinician.</li>
            <li><strong>Irregular pulse message:</strong> rest 5 minutes and retry; if it continues with symptoms (palpitations, dizziness), seek care.</li>
          </ul>
          <p class="note warn"><strong>Emergency signs:</strong> chest pain, severe breathlessness, fainting, or a very high reading with symptoms (for example, ≥180/120). Seek emergency care.</p>
        </div>
      </details>

      <!-- 9 Special situations -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">9</span> Special situations: diabetes, kidney, heart failure, 65+ <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>65+ or dizziness on standing:</strong> take a seated reading, then stand and recheck after 1–3 minutes. Report light-headedness.</li>
            <li><strong>Diabetes / kidney disease:</strong> accurate home trends help protect eyes, nerves, and kidneys—share your averages.</li>
            <li><strong>Heart failure:</strong> keep a morning weight log; sudden gains can signal fluid build-up.</li>
          </ul>

        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">10</span> FAQs <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Which arm should I use?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Use the arm your clinician prefers. If unsure, measure both (calmly) and use the arm with slightly higher numbers for routine checks.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">How tight should the cuff be?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Snug but comfortable; you should slide a fingertip under the top edge without pain. The cuff should not slide down the arm.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
            <h3 itemprop="name">Can I check after walking?</h3>
            <div itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer">
              <p itemprop="text">Rest at least 5 minutes first. Activity, stress, and conversation can all raise the number temporarily.</p>
