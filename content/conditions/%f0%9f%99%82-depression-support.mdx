            <li><strong>Tell your clinician</strong> or schedule a visit; bring a med list (including sleep aids & OTCs).</li>
            <li><strong>Daily outside light + short walk:</strong> 5–15 minutes in daylight helps mood/sleep.</li>
            <li><strong>Simple structure:</strong> Wake time, meals, fresh air, 1 meaningful task, wind-down routine.</li>
            <li><strong>One connection daily:</strong> call, brief visit, or group; ask for practical help.</li>
            <li><strong>Safety check at home:</strong> remove hazards; lock up unused meds & alcohol.</li>

        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Sleep</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Fixed bedtime/waketime; dark, cool, quiet room</li>
                <li>No big late-evening fluids; limit naps to 20–30 min</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Movement</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Short walks, light strength or chair exercises</li>
                <li>Stretch and breathe slowly for 5 minutes</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Food & meds</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Simple meals: veggies/fruit, beans, eggs/fish</li>

              </ul>
            </div>

        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Therapy & medications (what to expect) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Talk therapies:</strong> CBT, problem-solving therapy, and grief/bereavement counseling can help.</li>
            <li><strong>Medicines:</strong> SSRIs/SNRIs are common; benefits build over weeks. Report side effects, falls, or confusion quickly.</li>
            <li><strong>Medical review:</strong> Check thyroid, B-12, anemia, sleep apnea, pain, and hearing/vision.</li>
