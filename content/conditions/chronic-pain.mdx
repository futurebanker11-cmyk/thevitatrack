  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is-chronic-pain" class="section">
    <h2>What is chronic pain?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Pain that persists or returns for <strong>more than 3 months</strong>. It may start after an injury or illness—or without a clear trigger—and can affect sleep, mood, mobility, and daily function.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Goal:</strong> improve function and quality of life using a <em>multimodal</em> plan (movement, skills, medicines when appropriate, and supports).</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>New weakness, numbness in both legs, loss of bladder/bowel control</li>
        <li>Severe, sudden back pain with fever, unexplained weight loss, or cancer history</li>
        <li>Chest pain, shortness of breath, jaw/arm pain, or fainting</li>
        <li>Severe abdominal pain, black/bloody stools, or vomiting blood (especially on NSAIDs)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can indicate emergencies—seek care immediately.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Common areas -->
  <section id="common-areas" class="section">
    <h2>Common pain conditions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Low back pain</strong> (degenerative changes, facet pain, spinal stenosis)</li>
          <li><strong>Osteoarthritis</strong> (knees, hips, hands)</li>
          <li><strong>Neuropathic pain</strong> (diabetic neuropathy, post-herpetic neuralgia, radiculopathy)</li>
          <li><strong>Fibromyalgia</strong> (widespread pain, sleep/mood issues)</li>
          <li>Chronic neck/shoulder pain, tendon problems, osteoporosis fractures (healed but painful)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Evaluation -->
  <section id="evaluation" class="section">
    <h2>Evaluation</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>History & exam: pain pattern, triggers, sleep, mood, daily function</li>
        <li>Screen for red flags and mood symptoms (anxiety/depression)</li>
        <li>Imaging only when indicated by red flags or persistent focal deficits</li>
        <li>Review medicines (interactions, side effects), alcohol, and supplements</li>
        <li>Consider contributing conditions (diabetes, kidney/heart disease, osteoporosis)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">The plan is based on function goals, not imaging alone.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Non-drug -->
  <section id="non-drug" class="section">
    <h2>Non-drug care (core)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Movement & physical therapy</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Gentle aerobic activity most days (walking, cycling, water exercise)</li>
          <li>Targeted strength and flexibility (hips, core, back)</li>
          <li>Balance training to reduce falls</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Skills & supports</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pacing strategies to avoid “boom–bust” cycles</li>
          <li>Relaxation and breathing, mindfulness or CBT-based skills</li>
          <li>Heat/ice, massage, topical rubs, TENS if helpful</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sleep basics</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular schedule; wind-down routine; reduce late caffeine</li>
          <li>Comfortable mattress/pillow; positioning aids</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Consistency beats intensity. Start low, build slow, and track small wins.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="medicines" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines (senior-safety notes)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-describedby="mednotes">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Option</th><th scope="col">Where it helps</th><th scope="col">Senior cautions</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Acetaminophen</strong></td>
          <td>Osteoarthritis, general pain</td>
          <td>Mind total daily dose from all products (liver safety)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Topical NSAIDs</strong></td>
          <td>OA knees/hands, local tendon pain</td>
          <td>Lower systemic risk vs oral; avoid on broken skin</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Oral NSAIDs</strong></td>
          <td>Inflammatory flares</td>
          <td>Kidney/heart/stomach risks ↑; use shortest time at lowest dose if used</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Duloxetine</strong></td>
          <td>OA knee/hip, neuropathic pain, chronic back pain</td>
          <td>Monitor for nausea, sleep changes, BP; interactions review</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Gabapentin/Pregabalin</strong></td>
          <td>Neuropathic pain</td>
          <td>Drowsiness/dizziness/falls; adjust for kidney function; start low</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Tramadol (selected cases)</strong></td>
          <td>Breakthrough pain when others inadequate</td>
          <td>Confusion, constipation, falls; interactions; short courses only if used</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Topical lidocaine/capsaicin</strong></td>
          <td>Localized or nerve pain</td>
          <td>Skin tolerance; follow application limits</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p id="mednotes" class="small">Medicine plans are individualized to your health, other medicines, and goals. Avoid mixing sedating drugs without guidance.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Procedures -->
  <section id="procedures" class="section">
    <h2>Procedures & devices</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Options (selected cases)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Joint injections (e.g., knee OA), bursal injections</li>
          <li>Spine interventions (facet injections, epidurals) when appropriate</li>
          <li>TENS, bracing/assistive devices, canes or walkers for mobility</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Weigh benefits vs. risks and duration of relief; combine with movement and skills.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Pacing -->
  <section id="pacing" class="section">
    <h2>Pacing & flare plan</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily pacing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Break tasks into short blocks; alternate light and light-to-moderate activities</li>
          <li>Set timers; stop before a big spike, then rest briefly and resume</li>
          <li>Use “permission to pause” on higher-pain days</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Flare strategy</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Heat/ice, gentle mobility, relaxation breathing</li>
          <li>Short-term medicine adjustments if part of your plan</li>
          <li>Call if new red flags or if not improving within a few days</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Sleep & mood -->
  <section id="sleep-mood" class="section">
    <h2>Sleep & mood</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Better nights</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular bedtime/wake time; wind-down routine; reduce screens late</li>
          <li>Positioning aids (pillows under knees/hips) to reduce strain</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Mood tools</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Brief daily relaxation practice (5–10 minutes)</li>
          <li>CBT-based skills or group programs; treat anxiety/depression when present</li>
          <li>Connect with peers, family, or support groups</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Lifestyle -->
  <section id="lifestyle" class="section">
    <h2>Weight, diet, and movement</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Weight & joints</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Even small weight changes can reduce knee/hip load</li>
          <li>Focus on steady habits rather than strict diets</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Food patterns</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fiber-rich meals, adequate protein, healthy fats</li>
          <li>Limit excess alcohol; review supplements with your clinician</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Pair food, movement, and sleep steps—together they’re more effective.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions for your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>What is my most likely pain diagnosis (or mix), and what are realistic goals?</li>
        <li>Which exercises and pacing plan fit my condition today?</li>
        <li>Which medicines are safest for me, and what should I avoid?</li>
        <li>Could I benefit from physical therapy, CBT-based pain skills, or a group program?</li>
        <li>Are procedures appropriate for me, and what results should I expect?</li>
        <li>How do we adjust the plan during flares?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is rest best for chronic pain?</p>
        <p>Prolonged rest can worsen stiffness and de-conditioning. Gentle, paced activity usually helps long-term.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need an MRI?</p>
        <p>Only if red flags or persistent deficits suggest a specific problem. Imaging findings often don’t match pain levels.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are opioids recommended?</p>
        <p>They carry risks (falls, confusion, constipation) and are generally avoided or limited in older adults. Other options are preferred.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can mood treatment reduce pain?</p>
        <p>Yes—addressing sleep and mood can meaningfully lower pain intensity and improve function.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
