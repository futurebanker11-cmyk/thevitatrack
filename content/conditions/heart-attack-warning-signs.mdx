  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is a heart attack?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> A heart attack (myocardial infarction) happens when a heart artery is blocked and part of the heart muscle is starved of oxygen. <strong>Minutes matter</strong>—faster treatment saves muscle and life.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Key idea:</strong> Not all heart attacks cause dramatic chest pain. In older adults, women, and people with diabetes, signs can be subtle: breathlessness, nausea, unusual fatigue, or jaw/arm/back discomfort.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Call now -->
  <section id="call-now" class="section">
    <h2>Call now: emergency signs</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Chest <strong>pressure, tightness, squeezing, or heaviness</strong> lasting more than a few minutes or coming and going</li>
        <li>Pain/discomfort spreading to <strong>arm (often left), shoulder, back, neck, or jaw</strong></li>
        <li><strong>Shortness of breath</strong> at rest or with minimal activity</li>
        <li>Cold sweat, nausea/vomiting, light-headedness, sudden fatigue, or a sense of doom</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small"><strong>Act now:</strong> Call your local emergency number. Do not drive yourself.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Typical & atypical symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Typical</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Central chest pressure or pain, often with exertion</li>
          <li>Pain into left arm/shoulder, neck, jaw, or back</li>
          <li>Sweating, shortness of breath, nausea</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Atypical (more common in older adults, women, diabetes)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>New or unusual <strong>breathlessness</strong> or sudden fatigue</li>
          <li>Indigestion-like pressure, burning, or upper stomach discomfort</li>
          <li>Jaw, back, or shoulder ache without obvious chest pain</li>
          <li>Light-headedness or fainting</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Heart attack vs reflux -->
  <section id="vs-reflux" class="section">
    <h2>Heart attack vs. heartburn</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Feature</th><th scope="col">More like heart attack</th><th scope="col">More like heartburn (GERD)</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Sensation</td>
          <td>Pressure, squeezing, heaviness; may not change with position</td>
          <td>Burning that rises from stomach; sour taste</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Triggers</td>
          <td>Exertion or stress; wakes from sleep; not clearly linked to meals</td>
          <td>Large/late meals, lying down, spicy/acidic foods</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Relief</td>
          <td>Rest may help; <em>not reliably</em> improved by antacids</td>
          <td>Often improves with antacids or sitting upright</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Other signs</td>
          <td>Breathlessness, cold sweat, nausea, faintness</td>
          <td>Throat irritation, chronic cough at night</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">If unsure, treat as cardiac and seek urgent care.</div>
  </section>

  <!-- What to do now -->
  <section id="do-now" class="section">
    <h2>What to do right now</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Call emergency services immediately</li>
          <li>Sit or lie in a comfortable position; unlock the door; keep phone nearby</li>
          <li>Gather current medicines and allergies list for responders</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Aspirin?</strong> Take only if the emergency operator or a clinician instructs you (they will check for allergy, bleeding risk, or recent use of blood thinners).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Risk -->
  <section id="risk" class="section">
    <h2>Who is at higher risk</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Older age, family history of early heart disease</li>
          <li>High blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes</li>
          <li>Smoking or past smoking; kidney disease; obesity</li>
          <li>Autoimmune/inflammatory conditions; physical inactivity</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        People with diabetes or neuropathy may have <strong>quieter symptoms</strong>. New breathlessness, fatigue, or nausea can be the main clue.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- After hospital -->
  <section id="after" class="section">
    <h2>After the hospital</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines you may see</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Antiplatelets (e.g., aspirin ± second agent), statin</li>
          <li>Beta blocker, ACEi/ARB/ARNI as appropriate</li>
          <li>Diabetes, BP, and cholesterol optimization</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Recovery & rehab</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cardiac rehabilitation (supervised exercise, education, support)</li>
          <li>Gradual activity plan and symptom diary</li>
          <li>Follow-up for stent/cath surgery care and medication timing</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Prevention basics</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily habits</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Walk most days as advised; pace and build gradually</li>
          <li>Heart-friendly plate: vegetables, fruit, whole grains, legumes, fish</li>
          <li>Reduce salt and ultra-processed foods; hydration plan</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Numbers to know</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Blood pressure, LDL cholesterol, A1C if you have diabetes</li>
          <li>Weight trend; smoke-free status; vaccine updates (flu, COVID, pneumonia)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small" style="margin-top:8px">Care is individualized. Follow your clinician’s plan.</p>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can a heart attack be “silent”?</p>
        <p>Yes. Some people—especially older adults and those with diabetes—may have minimal chest pain and present with breathlessness, fatigue, or nausea.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If symptoms stop, am I safe?</p>
        <p>No. Symptoms can come and go. If you had concerning signs, seek urgent care even if you feel better now.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I drive myself to the hospital?</p>
        <p>No. Call emergency services so treatment can start en route and driving risks are avoided.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does antacid relief rule out heart attack?</p>
        <p>No. Heart symptoms can temporarily improve for many reasons. If in doubt, get checked.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
