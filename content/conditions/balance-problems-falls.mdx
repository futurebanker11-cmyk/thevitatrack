  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is-balance" class="section">
    <h2>What are balance problems?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Balance problems include unsteadiness, veering while walking, frequent tripping, or feeling “pulled” to one side. Falls happen when strength, sensation, vision, inner ear, blood pressure, or medicines aren’t in sync.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Reduce fall risk while maintaining independence — by tuning movement, home setup, vision/hearing, hydration, and medicines.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Signs of stroke: face droop, arm weakness, speech trouble, sudden severe imbalance</li>
        <li>Head injury with confusion, vomiting, severe headache, or loss of consciousness</li>
        <li>Severe vertigo with double vision, slurred speech, or new weakness</li>
        <li>Black/tarry stools, chest pain, or shortness of breath after a fall</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Call local emergency services right away for these symptoms.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Unsteady gait, frequent near-falls, or needing furniture to steady</li>
          <li>Dizziness on standing; “graying out” or light-headedness</li>
          <li>Spinning sensation (vertigo), especially with head turns</li>
          <li>Numb feet (sensory loss), weak legs, or knee buckling</li>
          <li>Blurred vision or poor night vision</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Causes & risks -->
  <section id="causes-risks" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & risk factors</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Body systems involved</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Inner ear (vestibular):</strong> BPPV, labyrinthitis</li>
          <li><strong>Blood pressure:</strong> orthostatic drops, dehydration</li>
          <li><strong>Nerves & muscles:</strong> neuropathy, weakness, arthritis pain</li>
          <li><strong>Brain & spine:</strong> prior stroke/TIA, Parkinsonism, spinal stenosis</li>
          <li><strong>Senses:</strong> poor vision/hearing; unsafe footwear</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Risk boosters</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Past falls, fear of falling, or walking without aids when needed</li>
          <li>Multiple medicines (especially sedating or BP-lowering)</li>
          <li>Home hazards: loose rugs, poor lighting, clutter, slippery bathrooms</li>
          <li>Alcohol, poor sleep, or acute illness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines table -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines linked to falls</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Medicine class</th><th scope="col">How it raises risk</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Sedatives/sleep pills, benzodiazepines</td>
          <td>Drowsiness, slowed reaction, poor balance</td>
          <td>Consider tapering/substitutions with clinician</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Strong pain meds (opioids)</td>
          <td>Dizziness, sedation, confusion</td>
          <td>Use lowest effective dose; review constipation/falls plan</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Antidepressants & antipsychotics</td>
          <td>Sedation, orthostatic drops</td>
          <td>Benefits vs risks should be reviewed regularly</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Blood pressure pills (some)</td>
          <td>Orthostatic hypotension</td>
          <td>Adjust timing/dose if needed; hydrate as advised</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Anticholinergics (bladder, allergy meds)</td>
          <td>Blurred vision, confusion, dry mouth</td>
          <td>Look for safer alternatives</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Diabetes meds (hypoglycemia risk)</td>
          <td>Low blood sugar → dizziness, falls</td>
          <td>Monitor and match meals/meds; adjust plan as needed</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Never change medicines on your own — discuss options with your clinician.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Assessment -->
  <section id="assessment" class="section">
    <h2>How clinicians assess risk</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>History: prior falls, near-falls, dizziness, vertigo, foot numbness, vision changes, shoe type</li>
        <li>Vitals including orthostatic BP; heart rhythm; hydration status</li>
        <li>Gait & balance tests (e.g., Timed Up & Go, chair stand, tandem stance)</li>
        <li>Foot exam (sensation, footwear), joint range and strength</li>
        <li>Vision/hearing screen; medication review; labs only when indicated</li>
        <li>Targeted tests (vestibular maneuvers, B12, imaging) when the history suggests</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Findings guide a focused plan: exercise, meds review, home safety, and supports.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment options -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Exercise & therapy</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Balance training (tandem stance, heel-to-toe, single-leg near counter)</li>
          <li>Strength (sit-to-stand sets, calf raises, hip abductor work)</li>
          <li>Vestibular rehab for positional vertigo; gait training with PT/OT</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Medicines & hydration</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Review and simplify fall-risk medicines where possible</li>
          <li>Adequate daytime fluids; slow position changes; compression socks if advised</li>
          <li>Treat reversible causes (B12 deficiency, infections, anemia)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Supports & footwear</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Proper cane or walker fit; non-slip, supportive shoes</li>
          <li>Night-lights; reachable items at waist height</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">4) Specific inner-ear care</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Repositioning maneuvers (for BPPV) performed by trained clinician</li>
          <li>Short-term symptom relief only when appropriate</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small note" style="margin-top:12px">Plans are individualized to causes and goals. Safety first; build slowly and consistently.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily care & home safety -->
  <section id="daily-care" class="section">
    <h2>Daily care & home safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Home setup</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Clear walkways; remove loose rugs; tidy cords</li>
          <li>Bright lighting in halls and stairs; night-lights to the bathroom</li>
          <li>Bathroom: grab bars, non-slip mat, raised seat if needed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily routines</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stand slowly; pause a moment before walking</li>
          <li>Short walks after meals; brief balance practice twice daily</li>
          <li>Eat enough protein for strength (as advised for kidneys/heart)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Vision & hearing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Update glasses; use single-vision for walking if bifocals cause missteps</li>
          <li>Address cataracts/hearing loss — improves stability and awareness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        If you fall: check for injury, move slowly, and call for help. Report falls to your clinician even if you feel okay — prevention plans improve after each review.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is it safe to exercise if I’m unsteady?</p>
        <p>Yes — with support. Practice near a counter or with a therapist. Short, frequent sessions work best.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Why am I dizzy when I stand up?</p>
        <p>Blood pressure can drop on standing (orthostatic). Hydration, slow rising, and medicine timing may help — ask your clinician.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do canes or walkers make me dependent?</p>
        <p>Used correctly, they improve confidence, reduce falls, and can increase activity.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can inner-ear vertigo be fixed?</p>
        <p>Many positional vertigo cases improve with repositioning maneuvers done by trained clinicians or therapists.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
