  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What AMD is & why it matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> <strong>Age-related macular degeneration (AMD)</strong> affects the <em>macula</em>, the small area in the back of the eye that gives sharp, central vision used for reading, faces, and driving. AMD does <em>not</em> cause total blindness, but can make central vision blurry or distorted—early action helps protect sight.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good to know:</strong> Regular dilated eye exams and prompt treatment for certain types (injections for “wet” AMD) can slow vision loss. Good lighting and magnification tools make daily life easier.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to get urgent eye care</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Sudden or recent central vision loss in one eye</li>
        <li>Straight lines look wavy or a dark/blank spot appears</li>
        <li>Rapid change in reading vision or face recognition</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be signs of <strong>wet AMD</strong> or other eye emergencies. Call your eye specialist promptly—earlier treatment works better.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Signs -->
  <section id="signs" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Blurry or <strong>distorted central vision</strong> (straight lines look bent)</li>
          <li>Fading contrast; needing brighter light to read</li>
          <li>Difficulty recognizing faces or reading street signs</li>
          <li>A central “smudge,” dark spot, or missing letters</li>
          <li>Slow recovery when moving from bright to dim rooms</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Types -->
  <section id="types" class="section">
    <h2>Types of AMD</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Types of AMD">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Type</th><th>What it means</th><th>Care</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dry (atrophic)</strong></td>
          <td>More common; gradual thinning of macula with drusen deposits; slower vision change.</td>
          <td>Regular monitoring, Amsler grid checks, good lighting, low-vision aids; some patients may benefit from <strong>AREDS2</strong> vitamins (ask your eye doctor).</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Wet (neovascular)</strong></td>
          <td>Less common; fragile new vessels leak fluid/blood causing faster central vision loss.</td>
          <td><strong>Urgent evaluation.</strong> Often treated with anti-VEGF eye injections to reduce leakage and protect vision.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

          <td>Lifestyle</td>

        </tr>

          <td>Nutrition</td>
          <td>Low intake of lutein/zeaxanthin, omega-3</td>

        </tr>

  <!-- Screen -->
  <section id="screen" class="section">
    <h2>Simple self-check (Amsler grid)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How to use (weekly)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Wear your reading glasses. Hold the grid at reading distance in good light.</li>
          <li>Cover one eye. Focus on the center dot. Do lines look <strong>wavy</strong> or <strong>missing</strong>?</li>
          <li>Repeat with the other eye. If new distortion or a blank spot appears, <strong>call your eye doctor</strong>.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Print an Amsler grid or use your clinic’s version; mark any changes with date.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>AREDS2 vitamins:</strong> Some with intermediate AMD may benefit. Not for everyone. <em>Ask your eye specialist</em>, especially if you smoke or take other supplements/meds.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily plan -->
  <section id="daily-plan" class="section">
    <h2>Daily vision-safety plan</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">At home</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Light the task:</strong> Adjustable lamp on the side of your better eye; use warm-white LEDs with diffusers.</li>
          <li><strong>High contrast:</strong> Dark placemats with white plates; bold pens; large-print labels.</li>
          <li><strong>Reduce glare:</strong> Matte finishes; close blinds; use anti-glare screen filters.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Eye health</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Don’t smoke; walk daily; manage BP, diabetes, cholesterol.</li>
          <li>Eat leafy greens, colorful veg, nuts; fish 1–2×/week if allowed.</li>
          <li>Keep all <strong>injection</strong> or follow-up appointments if treated for wet AMD.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">Small changes in lighting and contrast often help immediately.</div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can AMD be prevented?</p>
        <p>We can’t guarantee prevention, but <strong>not smoking</strong>, managing blood pressure, and eating leafy greens and fish support eye health. Regular eye exams catch changes early.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need injections forever?</p>
        <p>For wet AMD, many need a series of <strong>anti-VEGF injections</strong>. Frequency depends on how your eye responds; your specialist will tailor the plan.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will I go completely blind?</p>
        <p>AMD affects <strong>central</strong> vision; side (peripheral) vision usually remains. Low-vision aids and rehab can keep you independent.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I take AREDS2?</p>
        <p>Only some AMD stages benefit. <strong>Ask your eye doctor</strong> before starting—important if you smoke or take other supplements/meds.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
