  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is glaucoma?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> A group of eye conditions that damage the <strong>optic nerve</strong>, usually from eye pressure that’s too high for your nerve. Vision loss is often <strong>silent at first</strong>, starting at the edges (peripheral vision) — so regular screening matters.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Lower eye pressure and protect the optic nerve to keep vision stable over the long term.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>Urgent red flags</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Severe eye pain with <strong>headache, nausea, halos</strong> around lights</li>
        <li>Sudden vision loss or very blurred vision</li>
        <li>Red, hard eye with worsening pain</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can signal <strong>acute angle-closure</strong>. Seek urgent eye care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Typical symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Often <strong>none</strong> early on (open-angle type)</li>
          <li>Slowly narrowing side vision; bumping into objects</li>
          <li>Needing more light; glare or halos (especially at night)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Who’s at higher risk?</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Age &ge;60, family history, certain ethnic backgrounds</li>
          <li>High eye pressure, thin corneas, severe nearsightedness/farsightedness</li>
          <li>Long-term steroid use, diabetes, vascular issues</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Types -->
  <section id="types" class="section">
    <h2>Main types</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Type</th><th scope="col">What happens</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Primary open-angle</strong></td>
          <td>Drainage becomes less efficient → slow pressure rise</td>
          <td>Most common; painless and gradual</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Angle-closure</strong> (narrow angle)</td>
          <td>Drain angle closes; pressure can rise quickly</td>
          <td>Can be sudden & painful; needs urgent care</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Normal-tension</strong></td>
          <td>Optic nerve damage even at “normal” pressures</td>
          <td>Blood flow and nerve vulnerability may play roles</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Secondary</strong></td>
          <td>From other causes (inflammation, trauma, steroids)</td>
          <td>Treat the trigger plus pressure control</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>How it’s diagnosed & monitored</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Key tests</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Eye pressure</strong> (tonometry)</li>
          <li><strong>Optic nerve exam</strong> + photos/OCT imaging</li>
          <li><strong>Visual field</strong> testing for side vision</li>
          <li><strong>Gonioscopy</strong> (checks the drain angle)</li>
          <li>Corneal thickness (pachymetry)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Follow-up schedule</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>New diagnosis: visits every 1–3 months until stable</li>
          <li>Stable: typically every 3–6 months with periodic fields/OCT</li>
          <li>After treatment change: earlier re-check to confirm pressure goal</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Eye drops (first-line)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Prostaglandin analogs</strong> (once nightly)</li>
          <li><strong>Beta-blockers</strong>, <strong>alpha-agonists</strong>, <strong>carbonic anhydrase inhibitors</strong></li>
          <li>Often combined for target pressure; watch for side effects</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Laser</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>SLT</strong> (selective laser trabeculoplasty) for open-angle</li>
          <li><strong>Peripheral iridotomy</strong> for narrow/angle-closure</li>
          <li>May reduce or delay need for drops</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Procedures</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>MIGS</strong> (minimally invasive glaucoma surgery) often with cataract surgery</li>
          <li><strong>Trabeculectomy</strong> or <strong>tube shunt</strong> for advanced cases</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        The plan is individualized: your eye doctor sets a <strong>target pressure</strong> based on nerve health and visual field.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Eye drops technique -->
  <section id="drops" class="section">
    <h2>Eye drop technique</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Step-by-step</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Wash hands; tilt head back, look up</li>
          <li>Pull lower lid to make a small pocket</li>
          <li>One drop only; don’t touch the bottle tip to eye/lashes</li>
          <li><strong>Press inner corner</strong> of eye (punctal occlusion) for 1–2 minutes to keep medicine in</li>
          <li>Wait 5–10 minutes between different drops</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make it easier</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use a mirror or lie down</li>
          <li>Color-code caps or use a checklist</li>
          <li>Ask about once-daily options or combination bottles</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily care -->
  <section id="daily" class="section">
    <h2>Daily care & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Take drops exactly as prescribed; set phone reminders</li>
          <li>Bring all bottles to each visit; note any side effects (wheezing, slow pulse with beta-blockers)</li>
          <li>Protect eyes from injury; wear sunglasses for glare</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Keep floors clear and lighting bright; peripheral vision loss can increase trip risk — especially at night.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Driving & low-vision -->
  <section id="driving" class="section">
    <h2>Driving & low-vision help</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Ask about legal vision requirements where you live</li>
          <li>Avoid night driving if glare/halos are significant</li>
          <li>Use larger mirrors; scan side-to-side more often</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Low-vision aids</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>High-contrast labels, magnifiers, task lighting</li>
          <li>Orientation & mobility training if fields are reduced</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions for your eye doctor</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>What type of glaucoma do I have and what is my <strong>target pressure</strong>?</li>
        <li>How often should I have pressure checks, OCT, and visual fields?</li>
        <li>Are drops, laser, or surgery best for me right now?</li>
        <li>Could any of my other medicines affect eye pressure?</li>
        <li>What side effects should I watch for with my drops?</li>
        <li>Can we simplify my drop schedule?</li>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Glaucoma — quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can glaucoma be cured?</p>
        <p>There’s no cure yet, but controlling pressure can <strong>slow or stop</strong> further damage and preserve sight.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If my pressure is normal, can I still have glaucoma?</p>
        <p>Yes — normal-tension glaucoma exists. Nerve testing and visual fields guide treatment even when pressure numbers look “normal.”</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do cataracts affect glaucoma?</p>
        <p>Cataract surgery can sometimes <strong>lower pressure</strong> and improve vision quality. In some cases, MIGS can be added at the same time.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What happens if I miss drops?</p>
        <p>Pressure can drift up. Use reminders; if doses are frequently missed, ask about laser or simpler regimens.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
