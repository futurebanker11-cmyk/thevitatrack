  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What pneumonia is & why it matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> <strong>Pneumonia</strong> is an infection of the lungs (bacterial, viral, or rarely fungal) that makes air sacs fill with fluid. In adults 60+, it can worsen quickly—especially with heart, lung, kidney disease, or diabetes—so early recognition and treatment are essential.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good to know:</strong> “Walking pneumonia” can look mild at first. In older adults, confusion, weakness, or low appetite may be bigger clues than high fever or severe cough.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Breathing fast, struggling for breath, blue/gray lips or fingertips</li>
        <li>New confusion, fainting, or severe weakness</li>
        <li>Chest pain with breathing; oxygen device readings consistently low</li>
        <li>High fever with chills, or very low temperature in a frail adult</li>
        <li>Worsening symptoms despite 48–72 hours of treatment</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Call emergency services or go to urgent care now. Bring a list of medications and recent health issues.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cough (dry or with yellow/green/rust-colored phlegm)</li>
          <li>Shortness of breath, fast breathing, or wheeze</li>
          <li>Fever or feeling very cold/sweaty; body aches</li>
          <li>Chest pain worse with deep breaths or cough</li>
          <li>Low appetite, low energy, or new confusion (common in seniors)</li>
        </ul>

    <h2>Diagnosis & tests</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What clinicians may do</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Exam & oxygen level; chest <strong>X-ray</strong> to look for infection</li>
          <li>Blood tests (infection markers), sputum test in some cases</li>
          <li>COVID/flu/RSV swabs during respiratory seasons</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Home oxygen monitors:</strong> If you use one, track readings several times daily while ill and share numbers with your clinician.
      </div>

    <h2>Treatment overview</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Treatment options">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Type</th><th>Examples</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Antibiotics (bacterial)</td>
          <td>Choice depends on history, allergies, and severity</td>
          <td>Take exactly as prescribed; do not stop early even if better</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Antivirals (viral)</td>
          <td>Influenza, COVID-19, RSV (selected patients)</td>
          <td>Work best when started <strong>early</strong>; eligibility varies</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Supportive care</td>
          <td>Fluids, rest, fever reducers, bronchodilators if wheezy</td>
          <td>Watch for side effects; avoid duplicate meds (e.g., multiple acetaminophen products)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Hospital care</td>
          <td>Oxygen, IV antibiotics/fluids, monitoring</td>
          <td>Needed if oxygen is low, blood pressure drops, or confusion is significant</td>
        </tr>

  <!-- Home care -->
  <section id="home-care" class="section">
    <h2>Home care & recovery plan</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Day-by-day basics</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Hydrate:</strong> Sip water, warm soups, oral rehydration as allowed</li>
          <li><strong>Pacing:</strong> Short activities with rest; avoid overexertion</li>
          <li><strong>Position:</strong> Sleep propped up; side-lying may ease coughing</li>
          <li><strong>Humidify:</strong> Warm showers or humidifier to loosen mucus</li>
          <li><strong>Medicines:</strong> Take on time; use a checklist to avoid missed doses</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Recovery timeline:</strong> Fever 2–4 days; cough & fatigue may last 2–4+ weeks. Call if not clearly improving by day 3–4 or if any red flag appears.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Breathing -->
  <section id="breathing" class="section">
    <h2>Breathing exercises</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Pursed-lip breathing (as needed)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Inhale gently through the nose for <strong>2</strong> counts</li>
          <li>Exhale slowly through puckered lips for <strong>4</strong> counts</li>
          <li>Repeat 5–10 cycles; use during shortness of breath</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Airway clearance</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Incentive spirometer if prescribed: 5–10 breaths, 4–6×/day</li>
          <li>“Huff cough”: deep breath → say “huff” to move mucus up</li>
          <li>Walk short distances indoors to help expand the lungs</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Prevention & vaccines</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Prevention and vaccines">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Why it helps</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Vaccines</td>
          <td>Lower risk of serious lung infections</td>
          <td><strong>Flu</strong> yearly; <strong>pneumococcal</strong> per schedule; <strong>COVID-19</strong> per current guidance; ask about <strong>RSV</strong> for eligible seniors.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Hand/respiratory hygiene</td>
          <td>Cuts spread of viruses/bacteria</td>
          <td>Wash hands; cover coughs; wear masks during community surges</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Aspiration prevention</td>
          <td>Prevents food/liquid entering lungs</td>
          <td>Upright meals, small bites, check dentures, manage reflux</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Lifestyle</td>
          <td>Stronger lungs & immunity</td>
          <td>Quit smoking, daily walks as tolerated, good sleep and nutrition</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long does recovery take?</p>
        <p>Energy often returns over 2–4 weeks, but cough may linger longer. Call if not improving by day 3–4 or if any red flag appears.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I prevent pneumonia?</p>
        <p>Vaccines (flu, pneumococcal, COVID-19; ask about RSV), hand hygiene, smoke-free living, and managing reflux/swallowing risks all help.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I always need antibiotics?</p>
        <p>No. Viral pneumonias don’t respond to antibiotics. Clinicians decide based on exam, tests, and risk factors.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When to return to activity?</p>
        <p>Increase slowly: short walks at home, then outdoors as breathing allows. Stop and rest if dizzy or very short of breath.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
