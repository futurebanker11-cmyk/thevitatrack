  <!-- What it is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What it is</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Frailty and deconditioning mean <strong>lower strength, endurance, and reserve</strong> — often after illness, inactivity, or poor nutrition. The result: slower walking, weak grip, weight loss, and higher fall risk. The good news: a steady plan can <strong>reverse</strong> much of it.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Regain function for daily life — transfers, stairs, shopping, and social activities — while staying safe.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>Urgent red flags</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Rapid unintentional <strong>weight loss</strong> (≥5% in 6–12 months)</li>
        <li>New <strong>confusion</strong>, chest pain, severe breathlessness, or fainting</li>
        <li>Repeated <strong>falls</strong> or head injury on blood thinners</li>
        <li>Inability to stand from a chair even with arms, or new inability to walk household distances</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Seek urgent assessment if any of these are present.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="signs" class="section">
    <h2>Common signs</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Slow walking, shuffling, narrow steps, or stopping to rest</li>
          <li>Weak grip; trouble opening jars or carrying groceries</li>
          <li>Fatigue after short activity; daytime napping</li>
          <li>Weight loss or loose clothing; reduced appetite</li>
          <li>Difficulty with stairs, getting up from low chairs or the toilet</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Why it happens</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common drivers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Recent illness, hospitalization, or long bed rest</li>
          <li>Low protein intake, poor appetite, dehydration</li>
          <li>Pain, depression, or social isolation lowering activity</li>
          <li>Uncontrolled chronic conditions (heart, lung, diabetes, kidney)</li>
          <li>Medicines causing drowsiness, dizziness, or low blood pressure</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Fixing <strong>root causes</strong> (nutrition, medicines, pain, mood) + a <strong>graded movement plan</strong> is the fastest route back.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-checks -->
  <section id="self-check" class="section">
    <h2>Quick self-checks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Chair stand (30-second)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>From a chair with arms, stand up and sit down as many times as safe in <strong>30 seconds</strong></li>
          <li>Use arms if needed at first; note your number and improve weekly</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gait & balance</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Time your usual walk for <strong>4 meters</strong> (or one room length)</li>
          <li>Practice <strong>tandem stand</strong> (heel-to-toe) near a counter, 10–20 seconds</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Nutrition & hydration</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Daily protein target: about <strong>1.0–1.2 g/kg</strong> body weight (ask your clinician for kidney-safe goals)</li>
          <li>Hydration: pale yellow urine goal unless fluid-restricted</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card tip">
        Track in a notebook: chair stands, minutes walked, protein portions, and water glasses. Small wins add up.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Plan -->
  <section id="plan" class="section">
    <h2>4-part daily plan</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Protein at each meal</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Options: eggs, yogurt/curd, milk, tofu/paneer, fish, chicken, beans/lentils, nut butters</li>
          <li>Distribute across breakfast, lunch, dinner for best muscle response</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Hydration routine</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Small, frequent sips; keep a bottle within reach</li>
          <li>Limit evening fluids if nocturia; discuss with clinician if on fluid restriction</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Strength & balance (5–15 min)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Sit-to-stands</strong>, <strong>heel/toe raises</strong>, <strong>counter marches</strong></li>
          <li><strong>Tandem stand</strong> and gentle <strong>side steps</strong></li>
          <li>2–3 non-consecutive days add light <strong>resistance band</strong> work</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">4) Walking plan</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Start with <strong>5 minutes</strong> twice daily; add 1–2 minutes every few days</li>
          <li>Break after meals helps blood sugar and digestion</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Safe progression</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-describedby="progress-note">

          <td>5 min × 2/day</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>2</td>
          <td>8–12 sit-to-stands (light arms)</td>
          <td>Tandem 15 s × 2; side steps</td>

        <tr>
          <td>3</td>
          <td>12–15 sit-to-stands (no arms)</td>
          <td>Single-leg stand 5–10 s each</td>
          <td>10–12 min × 2/day</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>4</td>
          <td>Add band rows/curls (1–2 sets)</td>
          <td>Head turns while standing</td>
          <td>15 min × 2/day</td>
        </tr>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & aids</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Environment</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Clear rugs/clutter; night lights to bathroom</li>
          <li>Grab bars in bath; sturdy chair with arms</li>
          <li>Rise slowly; pause 3 breaths before walking</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Devices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Canes/walkers fitted to wrist height</li>
          <li>Non-slip shoes with closed heel</li>
          <li>Consider alert button if living alone</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card small">

  <!-- See a clinician -->
  <section id="see-clinician" class="section">
    <h2>When to see a clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Unintentional weight loss or poor appetite</li>
        <li>Multiple falls, dizziness on standing, or near-falls</li>
        <li>Breathlessness, chest pain, or new swelling</li>
        <li>Concern for depression, memory changes, or medication side effects</li>
        <li>No improvement after 3–4 weeks of steady effort</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians check</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Assessment</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Vitals, weight, orthostatic blood pressure</li>
          <li>Gait speed, chair stand test, grip strength</li>
          <li>Vision, hearing, feet/footwear, nutrition screen</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Tests (as needed)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Blood tests (anemia, thyroid, vitamin D/B12, kidney)</li>
          <li>Medication review for sedating or blood-pressure-lowering effects</li>
          <li>Referral to physical therapy, dietitian, or social support</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregiver -->
  <section id="caregiver" class="section">
    <h2>Caregiver tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Set a <strong>daily routine</strong> with short walks and a simple exercise block</li>
          <li>Prepare easy protein foods; keep water visible and within reach</li>
          <li>Celebrate small wins; track progress on a fridge chart</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Ask for a <strong>home safety evaluation</strong> and PT-guided program after any hospitalization.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How fast can I improve?</p>
        <p>Many notice better stamina in 2–4 weeks with daily practice; strength and confidence build over months.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need protein shakes?</p>
        <p>Food first is fine; shakes help if appetite is low. Choose options aligned with kidney/diabetes plans.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is it safe to exercise with pain?</p>
        <p>Mild muscle soreness is okay; sharp or joint pain means modify or rest. Ask PT for alternates.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if I’m scared of falling?</p>
        <p>Start seated/at a counter with supervision. Build balance with support, then step away gradually.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
