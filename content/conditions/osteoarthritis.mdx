  <!-- What it is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What OA is & why it matters</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> <strong>Osteoarthritis (OA)</strong> is age-related joint wear affecting cartilage, bone, and surrounding tissues. It causes pain, stiffness, and swelling—often in knees, hips, hands, and spine. Good routines can reduce pain and keep you moving and independent.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good to know:</strong> Motion is medicine. <em>Regular, gentle activity</em> protects joints better than complete rest. Build habits you can sustain.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD ===== -->
  

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to get urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Hot, very swollen joint with fever or feeling unwell (possible joint infection)</li>
        <li>Sudden severe hip or knee pain after a fall or twist; inability to bear weight</li>
        <li>Locking, giving way, or new numbness/weakness in the leg</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Seek urgent care. If on blood thinners or with severe swelling/redness, do not delay.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Signs -->
  <section id="signs" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Morning stiffness (usually &lt;30–45 minutes) easing with gentle movement</li>
          <li>Aching pain with activity; relief at rest (later may ache at night)</li>
          <li>Swelling, bony enlargements, or grinding/creaking (crepitus)</li>
          <li>Reduced range of motion; difficulty with stairs or getting up</li>
          <li>Hands: thumb base pain, finger knobbiness; Knees/Hips: walking tolerance drops</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

    <h2>Causes & risk factors</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="OA risk factors">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Area</th><th>Examples</th><th>What helps</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Age & family</td>
          <td>Age 50+, family history, female sex (post-menopause)</td>
          <td>Early strengthening, vitamin D if deficient, joint-protective habits</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Joint load</td>
          <td>Excess body weight, heavy or repetitive work, past injuries</td>
          <td>Weight reduction (even 5–10%), pacing, proper footwear/shock absorption</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Alignment</td>
          <td>Knock-knee/bow-leg, flat feet</td>
          <td>Bracing/orthotics, targeted physio to balance muscle support</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Whole-body health</td>
          <td>Diabetes, low activity, poor sleep</td>
          <td>Walk most days, protein with meals, sleep routine</td>
        </tr>

  <!-- Self-check -->
  <section id="screen" class="section">
    <h2>Simple self-check</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3 quick questions (past 2 weeks)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Do your joints feel stiff in the morning &gt;15–30 minutes?</li>
          <li>Does pain limit stairs, getting up from a chair, or walking &gt;10 minutes?</li>
          <li>Does swelling or grinding worsen after activity?</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">If “yes” to two or more, consider an evaluation and a physio-guided plan.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Rule-outs:</strong> Red, hot joints or fevers suggest other causes (gout, infection). Seek care promptly.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily plan -->
  <section id="daily-plan" class="section">
    <h2>Daily joint-care plan</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Core habits</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Move every hour:</strong> 1–2 minutes of gentle range-of-motion</li>
          <li><strong>Heat before, ice after:</strong> Warm pack 10–15 min before activity; ice 10 min after flares</li>
          <li><strong>Protein + hydration:</strong> Support muscle repair and joint lubrication</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Weekly anchors</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>3×/week strength</strong> (legs, hips, core, hands)</li>
          <li><strong>Most days:</strong> 20–30 min low-impact cardio (walk, cycle, swim)</li>
          <li><strong>Weight goals:</strong> Small, steady loss reduces knee/hip load</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">Pace yourself: “little and often” beats boom-and-bust activity.</div>
  </section>

  <!-- Pain relief -->
  <section id="pain" class="section">
    <h2>Pain relief options (talk to your clinician)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Pain options">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Option</th><th>How it may help</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Topicals</td>
          <td>Diclofenac gel, capsaicin cream</td>
          <td>Lower whole-body risk than pills; wash hands after; avoid broken skin</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Oral pain relievers</td>
          <td>Acetaminophen; NSAIDs (doctor-guided)</td>
          <td>NSAIDs can affect stomach, kidneys, heart—use lowest dose/shortest time</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Injections</td>
          <td>Corticosteroid (short-term flare relief)</td>
          <td>Not frequent; discuss risks and timing around surgery/diabetes</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Therapies</td>
          <td>Physical/occupational therapy</td>
          <td>Form check, graded strengthening, joint protection training</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Procedures</td>
          <td>Joint replacement (advanced cases)</td>
          <td>Consider when pain and function limit life despite best conservative care</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Moves -->
  <section id="moves" class="section">
    <h2>Safe exercise & movement</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Strength ideas</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Knee:</strong> Sit-to-stand from a chair, wall sits, straight-leg raises</li>
          <li><strong>Hip:</strong> Side-lying leg lifts, bridges, step-ups</li>
          <li><strong>Hands:</strong> Putty squeezes, thumb opposition, gentle finger extensions</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Form first:</strong> Slow, controlled reps; pain ≤3/10 during, gone by next day. If not, reduce range/sets and try again.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Devices -->
  <section id="devices" class="section">
    <h2>Braces & assistive devices</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make movement easier</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Knee unloader or simple sleeve brace; thumb CMC splint</li>
          <li>Cane in the <strong>opposite</strong> hand of the painful knee/hip</li>
          <li>Shock-absorbing shoes/insoles; raised toilet seat; reacher tools</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Home setup:</strong> Higher chairs with armrests, stair rails both sides, non-slip mats, task lighting in kitchen/bath.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support & pacing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Encourage short, frequent walks and exercises rather than long sessions</li>
          <li>Track pain/function on a simple weekly chart; celebrate small wins</li>
          <li>Arrange rides for therapy; prep high-protein, anti-inflammatory meals</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Watch-outs</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>New hot/swollen joint, fever, or sudden inability to bear weight</li>
          <li>Side effects from pain medicines (dizziness, stomach upset, confusion)</li>
          <li>Home fall risks—loose rugs, poor lighting, cluttered walkways</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will exercise wear out my joints?</p>
        <p>No—<strong>appropriate</strong> exercise strengthens muscles that protect joints. Avoid high-impact during flares; build up gradually.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need an MRI?</p>
        <p>Usually not. History, exam, and simple X-rays are often enough. Imaging rarely changes first-line care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What’s the best diet for OA?</p>
        <p>Aim for steady weight loss if needed, adequate protein, and colorful plants (Mediterranean-style). Stay hydrated.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When is surgery considered?</p>
        <p>When pain and function limit daily life despite optimized conservative care and injections/therapy haven’t helped.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
