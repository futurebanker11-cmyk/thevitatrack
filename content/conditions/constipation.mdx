  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is-constipation" class="section">
    <h2>What is constipation?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Constipation means infrequent, hard, or difficult-to-pass stools, often with straining or the feeling of incomplete emptying. In older adults, it’s common and usually improves with steady habits and, when needed, safe laxatives.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Targets:</strong> comfortable, regular bowel movements (your personal “normal”), minimal straining, and prevention of complications like hemorrhoids or fissures.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Severe abdominal pain, vomiting, or inability to pass gas or stool</li>
        <li>Unexpected <strong>blood in stool</strong> or black, tarry stools</li>
        <li>Unintentional weight loss, fever, or new anemia</li>
        <li>Sudden change in bowel habit after age 50</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These could signal blockage or other serious conditions. Seek care promptly.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hard, dry, or lumpy stools</li>
          <li>Straining or pain with bowel movements</li>
          <li>Feeling of incomplete emptying</li>
          <li>Fewer than 3 bowel movements per week</li>
          <li>Bloating, lower abdominal discomfort</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Related topics</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hydration & Drinks</li>
          <li>Daily Living Routines</li>
          <li>Fiber-Rich Recipes</li>
          <li>When to Call a Doctor</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after symptoms) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & medicines</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lifestyle & medical</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Low fiber, low fluids, limited physical activity</li>
          <li>Ignoring the urge, irregular routines (travel, hospitalization)</li>
          <li>Diabetes, hypothyroidism, Parkinson’s disease, prior stroke</li>
          <li>Pelvic floor dysfunction (trouble coordinating muscles)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines that can constipate</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Opioids (pain medicines)</li>
          <li>Iron supplements, some antacids, calcium supplements</li>
          <li>Some antidepressants, anticholinergics, Parkinson’s meds</li>
          <li>Some blood pressure medicines (e.g., calcium channel blockers)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Never stop a prescribed medicine on your own—ask for alternatives or bowel plans.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>Evaluation & simple tests</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>History & exam (diet, medicines, routine, red flags)</li>
        <li>Basic labs in some cases (blood count, thyroid, glucose, calcium)</li>
        <li>Stool tests or colon cancer screening per age/risk and local guidance</li>
        <li>Specialized tests only when needed (anorectal manometry, transit studies)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Plans are tailored to symptoms, risks, and age-appropriate screening.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after tests) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Bowel routine -->
  <section id="routine" class="section">
    <h2>Bowel routine (step-by-step)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily rhythm</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pick a regular time (often 20–30 minutes after breakfast)</li>
          <li>Sit with feet supported (small footstool), lean forward, relax belly</li>
          <li>Practice slow belly breathing; avoid straining</li>
          <li>If no urge in 10–15 minutes, try again later—consistency wins</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gentle stimulators</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Warm drink in the morning</li>
          <li>Short walk after meals</li>
          <li>Abdominal massage clockwise if comfortable</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Laxatives -->
  <section id="laxatives" class="section">
    <h2>Laxatives: options & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common options (examples)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Bulk-forming:</strong> psyllium/ispaghula (add slowly; needs fluids)</li>
          <li><strong>Osmotic:</strong> polyethylene glycol (PEG), lactulose</li>
          <li><strong>Stool softener:</strong> docusate</li>
          <li><strong>Stimulant:</strong> senna or bisacodyl (often bedtime, short courses or as part of a plan)</li>
          <li><strong>Suppository/enema:</strong> for occasional rescue if oral steps fail</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Safety notes:</strong> Plans differ if you have kidney, heart, or bowel disease. For opioid-related constipation, specific prescription options may be considered. Get personalized guidance.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Fiber, fluids, movement</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fiber goals</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Increase gradually (oats, fruit, vegetables, beans/lentils, whole grains)</li>
          <li>Consider psyllium if diet alone isn’t enough</li>
          <li>If you follow a special diet (kidney/diabetes), choose fiber within those limits</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fluids & movement</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Steady sips through the day unless on a fluid restriction</li>
          <li>Limit dehydrating drinks if they bother you</li>
          <li>Daily light walking; gentle core/hip exercises support bowel movement</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Special situations -->
  <section id="special" class="section">
    <h2>Special situations</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Travel & routine changes</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pack your fiber and any usual laxatives</li>
          <li>Keep mealtimes and morning bathroom time similar if possible</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Hemorrhoids & fissures</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Prevent straining; use stool softeners as needed</li>
          <li>Warm sitz baths can soothe; seek care for bleeding or severe pain</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Pelvic floor dysfunction</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Symptoms: prolonged straining, feeling blocked</li>
          <li>Ask about pelvic floor therapy (biofeedback) if suspected</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Aim for comfort, not force. A calm routine works better than straining.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- When to see -->
  <section id="when-to-see" class="section">
    <h2>When to see your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Constipation lasts &gt; 3 weeks despite steady routine</li>
        <li>Needs frequent rescue measures or severe bloating/pain</li>
        <li>New symptoms after a medicine change</li>
        <li>Any red flags (bleeding, weight loss, anemia, vomiting)</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions to ask</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>What’s the likely cause of my constipation?</li>
        <li>Which fiber amount and laxatives are right for me?</li>
        <li>Can any of my medicines be adjusted?</li>
        <li>Do I need tests or age-appropriate cancer screening now?</li>
        <li>What’s a safe “rescue” plan if I go several days without a BM?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How much fiber should I aim for?</p>
        <p>Many adults do well increasing gradually toward 20–30 g/day from foods and/or psyllium—adjust for your medical conditions.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are stimulant laxatives safe?</p>
        <p>They can be used safely as part of a plan (often short courses or intermittent). Long-term daily use needs clinician guidance.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What about probiotics?</p>
        <p>Some people find them helpful, but results vary. Diet, fluids, movement, and fiber are the foundation.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can iron tablets cause constipation?</p>
        <p>Yes. Ask whether the dose, type, or schedule can be adjusted—or whether alternatives are suitable for you.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
