  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>Why falls happen</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Falls often come from a mix of <strong>balance or leg weakness, medicines, vision problems, low blood pressure on standing, foot issues,</strong> and <strong>home hazards</strong>. One fall increases the risk of another—<em>but risk can be lowered</em> with targeted steps.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Goal:</strong> prevent injury and fear of falling while keeping as much independence and activity as possible.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Head injury, loss of consciousness, <strong>new confusion</strong>, or severe headache</li>
        <li>Severe pain, inability to bear weight, deformity (possible fracture)</li>
        <li>New one-sided weakness, facial droop, slurred speech (possible stroke/TIA)</li>
        <li>Chest pain, palpitations, or <strong>unexplained fainting</strong></li>
        <li>On blood thinners with any head strike, or bleeding that won’t stop</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">When in doubt, seek urgent care to check for head injury or fractures.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- First aid -->
  <section id="first-aid" class="section">
    <h2>If a fall just happened</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Check safety first</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>If injured or can’t get up, call for help. Keep warm while waiting.</li>
          <li>If no severe pain or dizziness, roll to your side, <strong>get on hands and knees</strong>, crawl to a sturdy chair, and rise slowly using armrests.</li>
          <li>After standing, <strong>pause 3 breaths</strong> to steady before walking.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Tell your clinician</strong> after any fall—especially with head strike, new weakness, or if falls are recurring.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Common causes & risks</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Area</th><th scope="col">Examples</th><th scope="col">Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Balance & strength</strong></td>
          <td>Leg weakness, neuropathy, Parkinson’s, stroke after-effects</td>
          <td>Shuffling steps, difficulty rising from chair, unsteady turns</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Blood pressure / heart</strong></td>
          <td>Orthostatic hypotension, arrhythmia</td>
          <td>Dizziness on standing, palpitations, near-faint</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Vision & hearing</strong></td>
          <td>Cataracts, glaucoma, poor lighting, hearing loss (balance input)</td>
          <td>Recent vision changes, difficulty in dim rooms</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Feet & footwear</strong></td>
          <td>Painful feet, numbness, unstable or slippery shoes</td>
          <td>Trips on edges, avoids walking due to discomfort</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medicines</strong></td>
          <td>Sedatives/sleep aids, opioids, some BP meds, alcohol</td>
          <td>Sleepiness, dizziness after doses, recent dose changes</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Home hazards</strong></td>
          <td>Loose rugs, clutter, cords, no grab bars, poor night lighting</td>
          <td>Falls in bathroom/at night, stumbles on thresholds</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>

  <!-- Home safety -->
  <section id="home-safety" class="section">
    <h2>Home safety checklist</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Bathroom</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Install <strong>grab bars</strong> by toilet and in shower; use non-slip mats.</li>
          <li>Consider a shower chair and hand-held shower head.</li>
          <li>Keep essentials within reach to avoid bending/twisting.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Bedroom & night path</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Motion night-lights</strong> from bed to bathroom.</li>
          <li>Keep a sturdy chair or walker nearby; remove cords/clutter.</li>
          <li>Stand slowly; sit to dress; avoid rushing for the phone/door.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Floors & stairs</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Remove loose rugs or tape edges; secure thresholds.</li>
          <li>Add <strong>two railings</strong> on stairs; high-contrast step edges.</li>
          <li>Keep pathways wide and clear; tidy daily.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Tip:</strong> reorganize daily items to <strong>waist height</strong>—avoid step stools.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Strength -->
  <section id="strength" class="section">
    <h2>Strength & balance plan</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily basics (5–10 minutes)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Chair sit-to-stands</strong> (2–3 sets of 5–10, as able)</li>
          <li><strong>Heel raises</strong> and <strong>toe raises</strong> (10–15 each)</li>
          <li><strong>Standing balance</strong> near counter: feet together → semi-tandem → tandem (hold 10–30 sec)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Weekly extras</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Short walks</strong> after meals (5–10 min)</li>
          <li><strong>Light resistance</strong> (bands) for hips and thighs</li>
          <li><strong>Tai chi</strong> or gentle yoga for coordination and posture</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Start at your level; hold a counter for safety. A <strong>physiotherapist</strong> can tailor a program and check gait.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Footwear -->
  <section id="footwear" class="section">
    <h2>Footwear & equipment</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Shoes with <strong>firm heel cup</strong>, non-slip sole, low heel; replace worn soles.</li>
          <li>Avoid loose slippers and backless sandals.</li>
          <li>Consider orthotics if foot pain or deformity affects balance.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use a <strong>cane or walker</strong> sized to your wrist crease; ask for a professional fit.</li>
          <li><strong>Personal alarm</strong> or phone within reach; avoid carrying heavy loads while walking.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine review</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">May increase fall risk</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sedatives/sleep aids, benzodiazepines, opioids</li>
          <li>Some blood pressure medicines (especially dose changes)</li>
          <li>Alcohol; some antidepressants; Parkinson’s medicines</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Do not stop medicines on your own.</strong> Ask about dose timing (e.g., bedtime), alternatives, or deprescribing where safe.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">Purpose</th><th scope="col">Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Find causes & risk</td>
          <td>Orthostatic vitals, heart rhythm, feet/neuropathy check, vision, gait (Timed Up & Go)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Labs</strong></td>
          <td>Rule out contributors</td>
          <td>CBC (anemia), electrolytes, kidney/thyroid, B12, glucose</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging</strong></td>
          <td>Injury assessment</td>
          <td>Hip/wrist X-ray after fall; head CT if indicated</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Plan</strong></td>
          <td>Reduce future risk</td>
          <td>Physio referral, vision update, footwear/orthotics, home safety visit, med adjustments, vitamin D if appropriate</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Date/time, location (bathroom, stairs, outside), shoes used</li>
          <li>What you were doing (standing up, carrying items, rushing at night)</li>
          <li>Symptoms before the fall (dizzy on standing, tripped, feet numb)</li>
          <li>Any injury, help needed to get up, and medicine changes that week</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        A simple log helps target the <strong>one or two biggest risks</strong> to fix first.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are falls a normal part of aging?</p>
        <p>No. Risk rises with age, but <em>many falls are preventable</em> with strength, safer homes, and medicine review.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I rest after a fall?</p>
        <p>Rest if injured, but prolonged inactivity weakens legs. Resume gentle activity once cleared and pain allows.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need a walker?</p>
        <p>Use the least assistance that keeps you safe. A professional fit ensures the right height and style.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What about vitamin D?</p>
        <p>Some people benefit; dosing varies by health history. Ask your clinician—don’t self-dose high amounts.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
