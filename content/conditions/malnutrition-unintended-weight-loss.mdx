  <!-- What it is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What it is</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Unintended weight loss and malnutrition happen when the body isn’t getting enough energy or protein. This raises the risk of weakness, falls, pressure sores, infections, and longer hospital stays—but a clear plan usually helps.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Rule of thumb:</strong> Losing <strong>≥5% of body weight in 6–12 months</strong> without trying is a signal to act.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Inability to keep food/fluids down; signs of dehydration (very dry mouth, no urine for 8+ hours, dizziness when standing)</li>
        <li>Rapid weight loss over weeks with weakness, confusion, or fainting</li>
        <li>Black/bloody stools, vomiting blood, severe abdominal pain</li>
        <li>Choking on foods or repeated pneumonia</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies. Call your local emergency number or seek immediate care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

          <li>Weakness, fatigue, reduced walking speed, more falls</li>
          <li>Dry skin, hair thinning, mouth sores, slow wound healing</li>

  <!-- Screening -->
  <section id="screen" class="section">
    <h2>Simple screening</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Quick check (home)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Lost ≥5% weight in past 6–12 months without trying?</li>
          <li>Poor appetite or eating <strong>&lt;2 meals/day</strong> for &gt;2 weeks?</li>
          <li>Clothes noticeably looser; weakness climbing a flight of stairs?</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">If “yes” to any, share with your clinician and request a nutrition assessment.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Clinic tools:</strong> Screening such as MNA-SF or MST and grip strength or walking-speed checks help identify risk early.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>Useful tests</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Basics:</strong> CBC, electrolytes, kidney/liver function, blood sugar, thyroid (TSH)</li>
        <li><strong>Nutrient checks:</strong> vitamin B12, folate, vitamin D, iron studies (if indicated)</li>
        <li><strong>Inflammation/infection:</strong> CRP/ESR, urinalysis, chest imaging if symptoms</li>
        <li><strong>Swallow assessment</strong> by speech–language pathologist if coughing with meals</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Testing is individualized. Your clinician will choose based on history and exam.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Plan -->
  <section id="plan" class="section">
    <h2>Nutrition plan (calories, protein, fluids)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily targets (typical starting points)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Calories:</strong> ~25–30 kcal per kg body weight (adjust for goals/conditions)</li>
          <li><strong>Protein:</strong> ~1.0–1.2 g per kg (1.2–1.5 g/kg if rebuilding muscle; check kidney status)</li>
          <li><strong>Fluids:</strong> steady sips to maintain pale-yellow urine unless fluid-restricted</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Practical boosts</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Add <strong>powdered milk, nut butters, olive oil, ghee, cheese, eggs</strong> to meals</li>
          <li>Use <strong>between-meal snacks</strong>: yogurt, paneer, hummus & crackers, trail mix, protein smoothies</li>
          <li><strong>Small, frequent meals</strong> every 2–3 hours if early fullness</li>
          <li>Consider <strong>oral nutrition supplements</strong> (ready-to-drink) if advised</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">Individuals with kidney, liver, or heart conditions may need tailored protein or fluid limits—follow your clinician’s plan.</div>
  </section>

  <!-- Swallowing -->
  <section id="swallow" class="section">
    <h2>Swallowing & dental tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Moisten foods with broth, yogurt, gravies; choose softer textures</li>
          <li>Alternate bites and sips; sit upright during and 30 minutes after meals</li>
          <li>Manage <strong>dry mouth</strong>: sugar-free lozenges, saliva substitutes, frequent sips</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Dentures & teeth:</strong> Ensure good fit; painful chewing lowers intake. Schedule dental review if sores or pressure spots occur.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medication review</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Ask which medicines may reduce appetite or cause nausea/constipation</li>
        <li>Time medicines away from meals if they upset the stomach (when safe)</li>
        <li>Be careful with appetite stimulants—benefits and risks vary; clinician guidance is essential</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make eating easier</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Serve small portions on a small plate; add second helpings</li>
          <li>Create a calm, well-lit eating space; reduce distractions</li>
          <li>Offer favorite foods at the best appetite time of day</li>
          <li>Weigh weekly; track meals/snacks and symptoms</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>When to call the clinic:</strong> Continued loss despite efforts, swallowing issues, repeated infections, new confusion, or signs of dehydration.
      </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should older adults avoid weight loss?</p>
        <p>Planned loss may be appropriate in limited cases with close supervision, but <strong>unplanned loss</strong> is a concern and needs evaluation.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are supplements necessary?</p>
        <p>Not always. Start with food-first strategies. Consider ready-to-drink supplements if intake remains low or per clinician advice.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How fast should weight improve?</p>
        <p>Gradual gain of ~0.25–0.5 kg per week is typical when a plan works. Track weekly and adjust with your care team.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Any exercise?</p>
        <p>Yes—light strength and balance training with adequate protein supports muscle and prevents falls. Start slowly and progress.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
