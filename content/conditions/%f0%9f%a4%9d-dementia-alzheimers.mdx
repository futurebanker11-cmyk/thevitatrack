      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">1</span> What is dementia? Types & common symptoms <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div>
              <p><strong>Dementia</strong> is a decline in memory and thinking that affects daily life. Alzheimer’s disease is the most common type. Others include vascular dementia, Lewy body dementia, and frontotemporal dementia. Many people have mixed causes.</p>
              <div class="table-wrap" role="region" aria-label="Common symptoms">
                <table class="responsive">

                    <tr><td data-col="Domain">Language</td><td data-col="Examples">Word-finding trouble, shorter sentences</td></tr>
                    <tr><td data-col="Domain">Behavior</td><td data-col="Examples">Mood shifts, poor judgment, apathy, anxiety</td></tr>

            </div>
            <div class="note tip">
              <strong>Normal aging vs dementia:</strong> Misplacing keys but finding them later is common. Dementia affects daily function—bills, cooking, medications, getting lost in familiar places.

      <!-- 3 Getting evaluated -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">2</span> Getting evaluated (what to bring, tests) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Bring a brief timeline, medication list (including OTC/herbals), and examples of safety issues (stove, money, driving).</li>
            <li>Expect memory/thinking tests, mood screening, labs (thyroid, B-12, infections), and sometimes brain imaging.</li>
            <li>Some conditions are <strong>treatable</strong> (depression, sleep apnea, medication side effects, vitamin problems).</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 4 Daily life & communication -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">3</span> Daily life & communication that works <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Communication</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>One-step directions, calm tone, eye level, use names</li>
                <li>Offer two simple choices; avoid quizzing or arguing</li>
                <li>Use memory aids: labels, large calendars, pill boxes</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Routine</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Consistent wake/meal/med times; gentle daytime activity</li>
                <li>Break tasks into small steps; celebrate small wins</li>
                <li>Reduce clutter and noise; keep lighting even</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Meaningful activity</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Familiar music, photo albums, simple crafts or gardening</li>
                <li>Short walks, safe chores (fold towels, set table)</li>
                <li>Social connection: brief friendly calls/visits</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>

      <!-- 5 Behavior changes -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">4</span> Behavior changes (agitation, wandering, sleep) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="two">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Agitation / anxiety</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>First check for pain, constipation, infection, hunger, or too much stimulation</li>
                <li>Redirect with a calm task (folding, snack, music) rather than confront</li>
                <li>Keep routines; limit caffeine; avoid scary TV news</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Wandering / getting lost</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Door alarms, motion lights, ID bracelet; recent photo available</li>
                <li>Daily walks with a buddy; purposeful activities reduce restlessness</li>
                <li>Alert neighbors; consider GPS-based safety devices</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="box">
            <h3>Sleep & sundowning</h3>
            <ul>
              <li>Daylight exposure and daytime movement; limit long naps</li>
              <li>Quiet, well-lit evening routine; night-lights to reduce shadows</li>
              <li>Consistent bedtime; avoid late heavy meals and alcohol</li>
            </ul>
          </div>

      <!-- 6 Medications overview -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">5</span> Medicines (benefits & cautions) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Cognitive symptoms:</strong> Some people try cholinesterase inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine) or memantine. Benefits are modest; side effects and drug interactions matter.</li>
            <li><strong>Behavioral symptoms:</strong> Non-drug steps first. Medicines (for mood, sleep, or severe agitation) are individualized and monitored closely.</li>
            <li><strong>Medication safety:</strong> Use a single pharmacy if possible; keep an updated list; review anticholinergic drugs that can worsen thinking.</li>
          </ul>

      <!-- 7 Safety at home & driving -->
      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">6</span> Safety: home, kitchen, falls, and driving <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <div class="three">
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Home & kitchen</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Stove auto-shutoff, kettle with auto-off, appliance locks if needed</li>
                <li>Remove loose rugs; add grab bars and non-slip mats</li>
                <li>Lock up cleaners/meds; label rooms and doors</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Falls</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Well-fitting shoes, night-lights, clear paths</li>
                <li>Balance exercises; review meds that cause dizziness</li>
                <li>Use cane/walker correctly; schedule vision/hearing checks</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="box">
              <h3>Driving</h3>
              <ul>
                <li>Red flags: getting lost, fender-benders, slow reactions</li>
                <li>Ask clinician about a road safety evaluation</li>
                <li>Plan alternatives early (rides, family schedule)</li>
              </ul>
            </div>

            <li>Organize passwords, bills, and automatic payments; simplify bank accounts.</li>

      <details class="section">
        <summary class="head"><span class="badge">8</span> Caregiver well-being (burnout is real) <span class="chev">▾</span></summary>
        <div class="content">
          <ul>
            <li>Set a simple routine that includes <strong>your</strong> breaks; accept help from friends/family.</li>
            <li>Use respite services, adult day programs, or short-term home care when needed.</li>
            <li>Watch for red flags: constant exhaustion, anger, sadness, or health decline—talk to your clinician.</li>

  (function(){
    const openAll=document.getElementById('openAll');
    const closeAll=document.getElementById('closeAll');
    const sections=[...document.querySelectorAll('details.section')];
    openAll && openAll.addEventListener('click', ()=> sections.forEach(d=> d.open=true));
    closeAll && closeAll.addEventListener('click', ()=>{
      sections.forEach((d,i)=> d.open=(i===0));
      sections[0].scrollIntoView({behavior:'smooth', block:'start'});
    });
  })();
