  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What anxiety can look like in older adults</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Anxiety may show up as restlessness, worry, chest fluttering, poor sleep, muscle tension, stomach upset, or trouble concentrating. In later life, it often overlaps with <strong>medical issues, pain, grief, or medication effects</strong>.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Common patterns:</strong> generalized worry most days, sudden panic episodes, anxiety tied to breathlessness or heart sensations, or anxiety linked to memory changes.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD ===== -->
  

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Thoughts of harming yourself or others</li>
        <li>New chest pain, shortness of breath, fainting, or fast irregular heartbeat</li>
        <li>Severe agitation/confusion, or sudden behavior change</li>
        <li>Possible alcohol/benzodiazepine withdrawal (tremor, sweating, high pulse, confusion)</li>
        <li>Panic-like symptoms with heart risk factors (treat as heart problem until ruled out)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Call your local emergency number. If in emotional crisis, contact your country’s suicide/crisis hotline.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Calming steps -->
  <section id="calm-steps" class="section">
    <h2>Fast calming steps</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Breathing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>4-6 breathing:</strong> inhale 4 seconds, exhale 6 seconds, 10 cycles</li>
          <li><strong>Pursed-lip breathing</strong> if breathless: in through nose, out through pursed lips</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Grounding</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>5-4-3-2-1:</strong> name 5 things you see, 4 feel, 3 hear, 2 smell, 1 taste</li>
          <li>Hold a cool glass, feel feet on the floor, slow shoulder rolls</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Thought tools</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>“<strong>Worry window</strong>” (e.g., 4–4:15 pm). Note worries and return only then</li>
          <li>Write 3 actions you <em>can</em> do today; postpone the rest</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Connection & movement:</strong> brief outdoor walk, call a friend, or light chores—10 minutes can shift the state.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Sleep -->
  <section id="sleep" class="section">
    <h2>Sleep support</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular <strong>wake time</strong>; wind-down routine with dim lights</li>
          <li>Caffeine cut-off ~<strong>6 hours</strong> before bed; light snack if needed</li>
          <li>Bedroom cool, dark, quiet; reserve bed for sleep</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Insomnia + anxiety:</strong> short daytime naps (≤20 min) if needed; avoid long afternoon naps. If no sleep for 2–3 nights with worsening anxiety, contact your clinician.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine cautions & options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Use with caution</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Benzodiazepines</strong> (e.g., lorazepam): fall risk, confusion, dependence; avoid long-term use in older adults when possible</li>
          <li><strong>Diphenhydramine/hydroxyzine:</strong> anticholinergic effects (confusion, dry mouth, constipation)</li>
          <li>Decongestants, high-dose caffeine, some bronchodilators can worsen jitteriness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Common first-line prescriptions</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>SSRIs/SNRIs</strong> (start low, go slow); several weeks to full effect</li>
          <li><strong>Buspirone</strong> for generalized anxiety (non-sedating)</li>
          <li><strong>Beta-blockers</strong> for performance-type physical symptoms (per clinician)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Never stop benzodiazepines suddenly.</strong> Taper only with clinician guidance.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

          <td><strong>Screening tools</strong></td>
          <td>Severity and co-occurring mood issues</td>

          <td>ECG, ambulatory monitoring, chest assessment if indicated</td>

          <td><strong>Plan</strong></td>
          <td>Non-drug + drug mix, follow-up</td>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Time of day, location, and <strong>what was happening</strong> before symptoms</li>
          <li>Sleep hours, caffeine/alcohol, medicines taken and timing</li>
          <li>Pulse or BP during episodes (if you monitor)</li>
          <li>Which calming steps helped and how quickly</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Bring a 1-page log to visits—this guides treatment and speeds relief.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is new anxiety a medical problem?</p>
        <p>Often there’s a medical or medication trigger (thyroid, infection, breathing/heart issues, stimulants). New or worsening anxiety deserves a medical review.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do deep-breathing exercises really help?</p>
        <p>Yes—slow exhalations lower sympathetic arousal. Try 10 cycles of 4-6 breathing and repeat as needed.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are benzodiazepines safe for seniors?</p>
        <p>They can cause falls, memory problems, and dependence. Safer long-term choices are usually SSRIs/SNRIs plus skills training.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long until medicine helps?</p>
        <p>Most first-line medicines take 2–6 weeks to show full effect. Early check-ins help adjust the dose and address side effects.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
