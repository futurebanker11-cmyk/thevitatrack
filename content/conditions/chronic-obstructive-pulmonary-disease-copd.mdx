  <!-- What is COPD -->
  <section id="what-is-copd" class="section">
    <h2>What is COPD?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> COPD is a long-term lung condition that makes it harder to breathe due to <strong>airflow limitation</strong> that isn’t fully reversible (often from chronic bronchitis and/or emphysema). Goals: reduce symptoms, prevent flare-ups, keep you active and independent.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good to know:</strong> Stopping smoking, correct inhaler use, pulmonary rehab, and vaccinations are the most effective steps for many people.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to seek urgent help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Severe breathlessness at rest or trouble speaking in full sentences</li>
        <li>Blue lips or fingers, confusion, or extreme drowsiness</li>
        <li>Chest pain, new fast heartbeat, or swelling of legs with worsening breathlessness</li>
        <li>High fever with shaking chills and thick green/bloody sputum</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may signal a serious flare or another urgent problem. Seek emergency care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Symptoms -->
  <section id="symptoms" class="section">
    <h2>Common symptoms</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Shortness of breath (especially on exertion)</li>
          <li>Chronic cough with or without sputum</li>
          <li>Chest tightness, wheeze</li>
          <li>Fatigue, reduced stamina, frequent winter “chest colds”</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Causes & risk factors</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cigarette smoking (current or past)</li>
          <li>Indoor biomass fuel exposure (wood/coal chulhas), secondhand smoke</li>
          <li>Outdoor air pollution and occupational dusts/chemicals</li>
          <li>Genetic factors (e.g., alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency — less common)</li>
          <li>Frequent childhood respiratory infections</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Key step:</strong> If you smoke, stopping is the most powerful treatment at any stage.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diagnosis -->
  <section id="diagnosis" class="section">
    <h2>Diagnosis & spirometry</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Clinical history (breathlessness, cough, exposures)</li>
        <li><strong>Spirometry:</strong> airflow limitation confirmed when post-bronchodilator <strong>FEV<sub>1</sub>/FVC &lt; 0.70</strong></li>
        <li>Severity estimated by FEV<sub>1</sub> % predicted and symptom/flare history</li>
        <li>Chest X-ray or CT sometimes used to assess emphysema or complications</li>
        <li>Pulse oximetry ± arterial blood gases in advanced disease or flares</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Always pair numbers with how you feel and how often you flare.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Staging -->
  <section id="staging" class="section">
    <h2>GOLD staging (overview)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">

        <tr><th scope="col">Grade</th><th scope="col">FEV<sub>1</sub> % predicted</th><th scope="col">Typical notes</th></tr>

        <tr><td><strong>GOLD 2</strong></td><td>50–79%</td><td>Moderate; breathlessness with activity</td></tr>
        <tr><td><strong>GOLD 3</strong></td><td>30–49%</td><td>Severe; limited stamina, frequent flares</td></tr>
        <tr><td><strong>GOLD 4</strong></td><td>&lt; 30%</td><td>Very severe; daily symptoms and risks higher</td></tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Your treatment also depends on symptom burden and past exacerbations, not grades alone.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment (inhalers, rehab, oxygen)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Core steps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Smoking cessation</strong> support (counseling, medicines as advised)</li>
          <li><strong>Correct inhaler technique</strong> and adherence; spacer if needed</li>
          <li><strong>Pulmonary rehabilitation:</strong> exercise + education improves breathlessness and quality of life</li>
          <li><strong>Vaccines:</strong> seasonal flu, pneumococcal, and others as recommended</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Inhalers (examples; individualized)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Relievers:</strong> short-acting bronchodilators (SABA/SAMA) for quick relief</li>
          <li><strong>Controllers:</strong> long-acting bronchodilators (LABA and/or LAMA)</li>
          <li><strong>Inhaled corticosteroid</strong> may be added in selected patients (e.g., frequent exacerbations with certain lab patterns)</li>
          <li>Some people use <strong>triple therapy</strong> as advised by their clinician</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Oxygen & devices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Long-term home oxygen for those who meet criteria (resting low oxygen)</li>
          <li>Portable concentrators improve mobility when prescribed</li>
          <li>Non-invasive ventilation in selected advanced cases per specialist</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Medicines and devices are individualized. Bring your inhalers to visits to review technique.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Flare-up -->
  <section id="flare" class="section">
    <h2>Flare-up (exacerbation) action plan</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Know your early signs</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>More breathless than usual, walking less distance</li>
          <li>Increased cough or thicker/darker sputum</li>
          <li>Needing rescue inhaler more often</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Typical steps (personalized plan)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Start rescue inhaler as directed; add spacers/nebulizer if prescribed</li>
          <li>Short course of <strong>steroids ± antibiotics</strong> may be pre-planned by your clinician</li>
          <li>Hydration, airway clearance techniques; monitor oxygen if you use it</li>
          <li>Seek care if not improving within 24–48 hours or if red flags develop</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small calm" style="margin-top:10px">Ask for a written, step-by-step action plan you can keep at home.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Breathing -->
  <section id="breathing" class="section">
    <h2>Breathing techniques & energy saving</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Breathe smarter</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Pursed-lip breathing:</strong> in through nose 2 counts → out through pursed lips 4 counts</li>
          <li><strong>Forward-lean positions</strong> to ease breathlessness during tasks</li>
          <li>Airway clearance if advised (huff cough, active cycle techniques)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Conserve energy</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pace tasks; sit for grooming/cooking; keep items at waist height</li>
          <li>Break chores into short blocks with rest</li>
          <li>Use rolling carts or backpacks instead of carrying</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Vaccines -->
  <section id="vaccines" class="section">
    <h2>Vaccines & prevention</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Annual influenza vaccine</li>
        <li>Pneumococcal vaccines as recommended for your age/health</li>
        <li>Other vaccines per local guidance and clinician advice</li>
        <li>Handwashing and avoiding sick contacts during outbreaks</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Daily tips -->
  <section id="daily" class="section">
    <h2>Daily tips & home setup</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Home environment</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Good ventilation; avoid smoke, incense, and strong fumes</li>
          <li>Keep rescue inhaler accessible; label by time and purpose</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Movement & strength</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Daily gentle walking; add light strength exercises as tolerated</li>
          <li>Stop and use breathing techniques if breathless; restart when settled</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Nutrition & hydration</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Small, frequent meals if a full stomach worsens breathing</li>
          <li>Stay hydrated unless on a fluid limit; discuss weight goals if underweight</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Keep an up-to-date medicines list and your written action plan on the fridge or inside your inhaler pouch.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Questions -->
  <section id="questions" class="section">
    <h2>Questions for your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>What is my current COPD severity and flare risk?</li>
        <li>Which inhalers should I use daily vs. only when needed?</li>
        <li>Can we review my inhaler technique with a spacer or demo device?</li>
        <li>Am I a candidate for pulmonary rehabilitation or home oxygen?</li>
        <li>What vaccines are recommended for me this year?</li>
        <li>Can you give me a written flare-up action plan?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can COPD be reversed?</p>
        <p>Airway damage cannot be fully reversed, but symptoms and flare-ups can be reduced with the right plan.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do inhaled steroids help everyone?</p>
        <p>No. They are used selectively (for frequent exacerbations/selected lab patterns) and usually combined with long-acting bronchodilators.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is oxygen always needed?</p>
        <p>Only if your oxygen is low at rest or with activity based on testing and criteria. Many people with COPD never need oxygen.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will exercise make me worse?</p>
        <p>Done safely, exercise and pulmonary rehab improve stamina and breathlessness for many people.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
