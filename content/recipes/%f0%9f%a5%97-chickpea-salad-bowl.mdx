  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>1 can (400 g) <strong>chickpeas</strong>, drained and rinsed well*</li>
          <li>1 cup <strong>cucumber</strong>, diced</li>
          <li>1 cup <strong>tomato</strong>, diced (or cherry halves)</li>
          <li>¼ cup <strong>red onion</strong>, finely chopped (or spring onion)</li>
          <li>¼ cup <strong>fresh herbs</strong>: parsley/coriander/mint (chopped)</li>
          <li>2 tbsp <strong>extra-virgin olive oil</strong></li>
          <li>2–3 tbsp <strong>lemon juice</strong> (fresh), plus zest</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>ground cumin</strong>; pinch of black pepper</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">*Rinse canned chickpeas under running water for 30–60 seconds to lower sodium.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>¼ cup <strong>bell pepper</strong>, diced</li>
          <li>2 tbsp <strong>feta</strong> or paneer, crumbled (optional)</li>
          <li>1 tbsp <strong>tahini</strong> or 2 tbsp plain <strong>Greek yogurt</strong> (for a creamy dressing)</li>
          <li>2 tbsp <strong>toasted nuts/seeds</strong> (almond, walnut, pumpkin)</li>
          <li>Leafy base: 2 cups <strong>lettuce/spinach</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prep</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Drain and rinse chickpeas thoroughly; pat dry.</li>
          <li>Chop cucumber, tomato, onion, and herbs.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Dress & toss</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>In a bowl, whisk olive oil, lemon juice, cumin, pepper (and a <em>tiny</em> pinch of salt only if allowed).</li>
          <li>Add chickpeas and veggies; toss gently. Taste and adjust lemon or herbs.</li>
          <li>Top with nuts/seeds or a spoon of yogurt/tahini, if desired. Serve over greens.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">Texture tip: let the bowl rest 5–10 minutes for flavors to meld.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Portion chickpeas to <strong>½–¾ cup</strong> per serving; add extra non-starchy veg and greens.</li>
          <li>Healthy fat helps: olive oil + nuts/seeds; consider a spoon of plain yogurt for protein.</li>
          <li>Skip sweet dressings; rely on lemon, herbs, and spice.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>no-salt-added</strong> chickpeas or rinse well.</li>
          <li>Keep cheese optional/minimal; prefer nuts and olive oil.</li>
          <li>Herb-heavy, lemon-forward dressing for flavor without salt.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Check your <strong>potassium/phosphorus</strong> goals. Limit portion to ½ cup chickpeas if advised.</li>
          <li>Balance with lower-potassium veg (cucumber/lettuce); use lemon and herbs generously.</li>
          <li>Ask your clinician/dietitian about cheese and tahini amounts.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Go lighter on raw onion; swap with spring onion greens or briefly soak onion in water.</li>
          <li>Use lemon to taste but avoid very sour bowls; add a spoon of yogurt to soften acidity.</li>
          <li>Eat slowly and sit upright for 30 minutes after.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Fiber tip:</strong> If beans cause gas, start with ¼–½ cup, rinse very well, and increase gradually. A warm tea (ginger/fennel) can help comfort.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make-ahead</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Prep veggies and rinse chickpeas up to 2 days ahead.</li>
          <li>Keep dressing separate; toss just before eating for best crunch.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Storage</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Refrigerate assembled salad up to 24 hours; add greens/nuts at serving.</li>
          <li>Leftovers may need a squeeze of lemon to brighten.</li>
        </ul>

  <!-- Tips -->
  <section id="tips" class="section">
    <h2>Flavor boosters & dressings</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Spice route: cumin + paprika + pepper; or chaat masala (light).</li>
          <li>Herb route: parsley + mint + dill with lemon zest.</li>
          <li>Creamy: whisk 1 tbsp tahini or 2 tbsp yogurt with lemon and a spoon of water.</li>
          <li>Crunch: add toasted almonds or pumpkin seeds just before serving.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Salt-smart: try lemon, herbs, toasted spices, and a drizzle of olive oil before adding any salt.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Dried vs canned chickpeas?</p>
        <p>Dried (soaked/cooked) are great if you batch-cook; canned are faster—just rinse well to lower sodium.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gas or bloating?</p>
        <p>Increase amount gradually, rinse well, and add ginger/fennel. Some prefer smaller, more frequent portions.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Protein boost?</p>
        <p>Add yogurt, grilled chicken/fish (if you eat meat), or extra seeds/nuts as advised.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gluten-free?</p>
        <p>Chickpeas are naturally gluten-free; ensure add-ins like spice mixes are GF if needed.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
