    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (makes ~1 liter)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>1 L <strong>cold water</strong> (still or lightly sparkling)</li>
          <li>½–1 small <strong>lemon</strong>, thinly sliced (or 2–3 tbsp juice)</li>
          <li>6–10 fresh <strong>mint leaves</strong>, lightly bruised</li>
          <li>Ice as desired</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>2–3 slices <strong>cucumber</strong> or a few basil leaves</li>
          <li>1–2 slices <strong>ginger</strong> (mild warmth)</li>
          <li>1–2 drops <strong>vanilla</strong> or a pinch of cinnamon (sugar-free aroma)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">For gentler citrus, use lemon <em>peel strips</em> (less acidity) instead of juice.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Infuse (5–10 min)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>In a pitcher, add water, lemon slices/juice, and mint. Gently bruise mint leaves between fingers first.</li>
          <li>Stir and let sit 5–10 minutes (or chill up to 2–4 hours for stronger flavor).</li>
          <li>Serve over ice. Remove lemon/mint after 4 hours to prevent bitterness.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Serving ideas:</strong> sip with meals, during a short walk, or keep a bedside bottle (if not on fluid restriction).
      </div>

  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep it <strong>unsweetened</strong>. If needed, a few drops non-nutritive sweetener.</li>
          <li>Pair with protein-rich snacks to avoid grazing on sweets.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>No added salt or salty mixers.</li>
          <li>Use plain water or sodium-free sparkling water.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>lemon slices</strong> or 1–2 tbsp juice per liter; avoid concentrated lemonades.</li>
          <li>Follow any fluid limits given by your clinician.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>If citrus triggers symptoms, use <strong>peel strips</strong> or mint-cucumber only.</li>
          <li>Skip fizzy water if it worsens reflux.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Hydration tip:</strong> Aim for steady sipping across the day (unless on fluid restriction).
      </div>
    </div>

  <section id="tips" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & smart sipping</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Citrus is acidic—after drinking, <strong>rinse mouth with plain water</strong> to protect tooth enamel.</li>
          <li>For sensitive stomachs, start with fewer lemon slices or use just mint/cucumber.</li>
          <li>On fluid limits (heart/kidney): pre-measure daily allowance in bottles.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        If you have frequent heartburn, try mint-cucumber water first or talk to your clinician about safe flavorings.
      </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need to peel the lemon?</p>
        <p>Not required if well washed. For less bitterness, remove seeds and thick pith; or use peel strips for aroma with less acidity.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I sweeten it?</p>
        <p>Best unsweetened. If needed, use a few drops of a non-nutritive sweetener rather than sugar or honey.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long to infuse?</p>
        <p>5–10 minutes for light flavor; up to 2–4 hours chilled for stronger taste. Remove solids after 4 hours.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sparkling or still?</p>
        <p>Either. If reflux or bloating, choose still water.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
