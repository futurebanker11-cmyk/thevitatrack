    <h2>Ingredients & portions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Fish fillets:</strong> salmon or tilapia, skin-on or skinless — <strong>2 × 120–150 g</strong></li>
          <li><strong>Olive oil:</strong> <strong>2 tsp</strong> total</li>
          <li><strong>Lemon:</strong> ½–1 lemon (zest + juice), a few thin slices for topping</li>
          <li><strong>Herbs:</strong> dill/parsley/coriander (small handful), optional</li>
          <li><strong>Seasoning:</strong> black pepper or paprika pinch (no added salt)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Salmon has more omega-3s; tilapia is milder and leaner. Choose boneless fillets for ease.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional sides</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Steamed veggies: beans, carrots, broccoli, zucchini</li>
          <li>Small portion: brown rice, millet, or quinoa (½ cup cooked)</li>
          <li>Lemon yogurt dip: 2 tbsp plain yogurt + lemon + dill</li>
        </ul>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step (easy bake)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ol class="list">
          <li><strong>Heat oven</strong> to <strong>200°C / 400°F</strong>. Line a tray with baking paper or lightly oil.</li>
          <li><strong>Prep fish:</strong> Pat dry. Rub with olive oil, lemon zest, and pepper/paprika.</li>
          <li><strong>Arrange:</strong> Place fillets skin-side down (if skin-on). Top with thin lemon slices and herbs.</li>
          <li><strong>Bake</strong> 10–14 minutes (tilapia) or 12–16 minutes (salmon), depending on thickness.</li>
          <li><strong>Finish:</strong> Squeeze fresh lemon. Rest 2 minutes; serve with veggies and small whole-grain side if desired.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Senior-friendly texture:</strong> Bake until <em>just</em> opaque and flakes easily with a fork. Add 1–2 tsp water to tray for extra moisture if needed.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pair with non-starchy veggies; keep grains to <strong>½ cup cooked</strong>.</li>
          <li>Avoid sugary glazes. Lemon + herbs give bright flavor without carbs.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>No added salt.</strong> Use lemon zest/juice, garlic powder (not salted), and herbs.</li>
          <li>Choose salmon (omega-3s) more often; keep oil to <strong>~2 tsp for 2 servings</strong>.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Protein portion: <strong>~120 g cooked</strong> (per plan). Avoid salty marinades.</li>
          <li>Balance phosphorus/potassium across the day; coordinate with your renal dietitian.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>mild lemon</strong> (zest and a small squeeze). Skip heavy spices and avoid late-night meals.</li>
          <li>Bake (not pan-fry) to reduce smoke and oil.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Chewing/swallowing ease</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Choose <strong>boneless fillets</strong>; check for pin bones.</li>
          <li>Serve flaked fish with soft sides (steamed carrots, mashed peas).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety, bones & doneness</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Internal temperature:</strong> about <strong>63°C / 145°F</strong> at the thickest part, or flesh flakes easily and is opaque.</li>
          <li><strong>Bones:</strong> Run a finger along the fillet and remove pin bones with tweezers before cooking.</li>
          <li><strong>Storage:</strong> Refrigerate leftovers within 2 hours; use within 1–2 days. Reheat gently to avoid dryness.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Allergy alert:</strong> If fish allergy is suspected, do not prepare; discuss safe protein alternatives with your clinician.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I cook this in a pan?</p>
        <p>Yes. Use a non-stick pan with 1–2 tsp olive oil on medium heat, 3–5 minutes per side depending on thickness. Keep seasoning simple.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Skin on or off?</p>
        <p>Either. Skin-on helps keep salmon moist; remove skin after baking if preferred.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What herb works best?</p>
        <p>Dill and parsley are classic. Coriander works too. Avoid salty mixes.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if fish smells strong?</p>
        <p>Fresh fish should smell clean. Rinse, pat dry, and cook the same day. If odor persists, do not use.</p>
      </div>
