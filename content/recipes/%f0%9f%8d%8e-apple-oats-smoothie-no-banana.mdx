  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients & portions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (per serving ~300–330 ml)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Rolled oats</strong> (not instant): <strong>3 tbsp (≈ 20–25 g)</strong></li>
          <li><strong>Plain yogurt/curd</strong> (unsweetened) <em>or</em> milk (dairy/lactose-free/fortified plant): <strong>½ cup (120 ml)</strong></li>
          <li><strong>Cold water</strong>: <strong>½–¾ cup (120–180 ml)</strong> to thin</li>
          <li><strong>Apple</strong>, peeled if needed: <strong>1 small–medium</strong>, cored & chopped</li>
          <li>Flavor (optional): <strong>cinnamon</strong> pinch, <strong>ginger</strong> tiny piece</li>
          <li><em>No added sugar</em>; sweetness comes from apple</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">For extra creaminess without banana, soak the oats and use chilled ingredients.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Soak (improves texture)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cover oats with warm water for <strong>10 minutes</strong> (quick) or <strong>overnight</strong> in the fridge.</li>
          <li>Drain excess water before blending (keep a few spoons to adjust thickness).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Soak & blend steps</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ol class="list">
          <li><strong>Prep oats:</strong> Soak as above. Drain (save a little liquid to adjust).</li>
          <li><strong>Blend base:</strong> Add soaked oats + yogurt/milk + half the water. Blend smooth.</li>
          <li><strong>Add apple & spice:</strong> Add chopped apple + pinch cinnamon or tiny ginger. Blend again.</li>
          <li><strong>Adjust:</strong> Add more cold water for sip-able texture. Avoid ice if reflux or sensitive teeth.</li>
          <li><strong>Serve:</strong> Pour into a glass. Optional sprinkle of cinnamon on top.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Tip:</strong> For a thinner, easier-to-sip smoothie (great for mornings), use up to <strong>½ cup extra water</strong> and blend briefly to avoid foam.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep to <strong>1 small apple</strong> and <strong>3 tbsp oats</strong>; no sugar/honey.</li>
          <li>Prefer <strong>unsweetened dairy</strong> or fortified unsweetened soy/almond milk.</li>
          <li>Drink with a meal or pair with protein (e.g., egg, paneer) for steadier response.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>low-fat yogurt</strong> or skim milk; avoid salt in any form.</li>
          <li>Cinnamon adds flavor without sugar.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Oats and dairy add potassium & phosphorus—<strong>keep portion modest</strong> and follow renal diet advice.</li>
          <li>Consider <strong>rice flakes (poha)</strong> or lower-potassium milk alternatives if needed (dietitian-guided).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>room-cool</strong> liquids; avoid ice and large night servings.</li>
          <li>Skip ginger if spicy; choose cinnamon only if tolerated.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lactose sensitivity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>lactose-free milk/yogurt</strong> or fortified plant milks (unsweetened).</li>
          <li>Fermented curd may be better tolerated than milk for some.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & consistency</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Texture:</strong> Blend fully for a <strong>smooth, sip-able</strong> drink (dentures-friendly).</li>
          <li><strong>Timing:</strong> Best in the morning or mid-day. Large late-evening portions may add to night urination.</li>
          <li><strong>Medicines:</strong> Space dairy a few hours from certain antibiotics (ask your clinician/pharmacist).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Oats thicken over time. If storing for a few hours, <strong>stir and add a splash of water</strong> before serving.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I make it creamy without banana?</p>
        <p>Soak rolled oats, use chilled yogurt/milk, and blend well. A pinch of cinnamon adds dessert-like feel without sugar.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I skip yogurt?</p>
        <p>Yes. Use dairy or fortified plant milk (unsweetened). Protein is higher with dairy or soy milk.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I add nuts?</p>
        <p>Optional 1 tsp <strong>finely ground</strong> almonds or peanuts for protein—avoid whole pieces if chewing/swallowing is hard.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long can it sit?</p>
        <p>Refrigerate up to 12–24 hours. Stir and thin with water before serving as oats thicken.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
