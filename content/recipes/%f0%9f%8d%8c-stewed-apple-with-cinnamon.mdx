    <h2>Ingredients & portions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Apples</strong> (sweet-tart): <strong>2 medium</strong>, peeled if needed, cored & chopped small</li>
          <li><strong>Water</strong>: <strong>½ cup (120 ml)</strong></li>
          <li><strong>Cinnamon</strong>: <strong>¼–½ tsp</strong> ground (or small stick)</li>
          <li><strong>Lemon</strong>: a few drops (optional) to brighten</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">No added sugar. Sweetness comes from the apples. Peel only if skins are tough for you.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins (choose 1)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Raisins</strong> 1 tbsp (if diabetes controlled)</li>
          <li><strong>Ginger</strong> a tiny sliver for warmth</li>
          <li><strong>Vanilla</strong> a drop at the end</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Stovetop steps</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ol class="list">
          <li><strong>Prep apples:</strong> Wash, core, and chop small (½–1 cm). Peel if skins are hard to chew.</li>
          <li><strong>Simmer:</strong> Add apples + water to a small pan. Bring to a gentle simmer.</li>
          <li><strong>Flavor:</strong> Stir in cinnamon (and ginger if using). Cover and cook on low <strong>6–10 minutes</strong> until fork-tender.</li>
          <li><strong>Finish:</strong> Mash lightly for a chunky texture or blend briefly for a smoother spoonable purée. Add a few drops lemon if desired.</li>
          <li><strong>Serve warm</strong> or cool. Thin with a spoon of water if too thick.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Senior-friendly texture:</strong> For dentures or swallowing ease, blend <em>very smooth</em> and serve warm, not hot.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep to <strong>½–1 apple per serving</strong>; no sugar or honey.</li>
          <li>Pair with protein (curd, paneer, egg) or have with a meal for steadier response.</li>
          <li>Skip raisins if glucose runs high.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Zero added salt; cinnamon adds flavor.</li>
          <li>Serve with unsweetened <strong>low-fat yogurt</strong> if dairy fits your plan.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Apple is <strong>lower potassium</strong> than many fruits — portion still counts toward daily limit.</li>
          <li>Keep fluid additions modest if on restrictions.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Serve <strong>warm-not-hot</strong>; avoid large late-night bowls.</li>
          <li>Use a small pinch of cinnamon; skip ginger if it triggers you.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lactose sensitivity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Enjoy plain or with <strong>lactose-free</strong> yogurt/curd.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & texture</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Temperature:</strong> Let cool briefly to avoid mouth burns.</li>
          <li><strong>Consistency:</strong> Add a spoon of water if it thickens on cooling; stir well.</li>
          <li><strong>Storage:</strong> Refrigerate within 2 hours; use within 2 days. Reheat gently with a splash of water.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Medication timing:</strong> If you take medicines that interact with high-fiber meals, separate by a few hours when advised.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

        </ul>

      <div class="card ok">

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Which apple is best?</p>
        <p>Any sweet-tart apple that cooks down well (Gala, Fuji, Pink Lady). Very sour apples may need longer cooking for softness.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I add sugar or jaggery?</p>
        <p>Skip added sugars for most seniors. If absolutely needed for taste, use a <em>very small</em> amount and check glucose goals.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I freeze it?</p>
        <p>Yes. Portion into small containers; freeze up to 2 months. Thaw in the fridge; reheat with a spoon of water.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">No cinnamon?</p>
        <p>Use a drop of vanilla or a pinch of cardamom, if tolerated.</p>
      </div>
