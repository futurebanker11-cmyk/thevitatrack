  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients & portions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (3–4 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Dal:</strong> ½ cup (100 g) <em>yellow moong</em> or <em>red masoor</em>, rinsed well</li>
          <li><strong>Water:</strong> 3–4 cups (adjust for thickness)</li>
          <li><strong>Vegetables (chopped small):</strong> 1–1½ cups total — carrots, bottle gourd (lauki), zucchini, pumpkin, tomato (½ small)</li>
          <li><strong>Aromatics:</strong> ½ tsp grated ginger, ¼ tsp turmeric, ¼ tsp cumin powder</li>
          <li><strong>Fat:</strong> 2 tsp olive oil or neutral oil</li>
          <li><strong>Lemon:</strong> wedges to finish; fresh coriander to garnish (optional)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Keep onions/garlic mild or skip if reflux; avoid chili powders for a gentle profile.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional boosters</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Protein:</strong> 2 tbsp roasted chana dal (blend in) or 2 tbsp Greek yogurt/curd stirred in after cooking</li>
          <li><strong>Fiber:</strong> 1 tbsp oats while simmering (thickens smoothly)</li>
          <li><strong>Herb oil:</strong> ½ tsp olive oil + lemon zest for aroma (no salt)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Pressure cooker / IP -->
  <section id="steps-pressure" class="section">
    <h2>Pressure cooker / Instant Pot</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ol class="list">
          <li><strong>Sauté (optional):</strong> Warm oil on sauté mode/medium heat. Add ginger, turmeric, cumin for 30–40 sec.</li>
          <li><strong>Add:</strong> Rinsed dal, chopped veggies, and water (start with 3½ cups).</li>
          <li><strong>Cook:</strong> Pressure cook 6–8 minutes (IP: manual high 6 min); natural release 10 min.</li>
          <li><strong>Blend:</strong> Use an immersion blender to your preferred smoothness. Add hot water to thin if needed.</li>
          <li><strong>Finish:</strong> Squeeze lemon, garnish lightly with coriander; no added salt.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Smooth & sip-able:</strong> Blend fully for an even texture. Strain once if a silkier finish is desired.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Stovetop -->
  <section id="steps-stovetop" class="section">
    <h2>Stovetop method</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ol class="list">
          <li>In a pot, warm oil on medium-low. Bloom ginger, turmeric, and cumin briefly.</li>
          <li>Add dal, veggies, and water. Bring to a gentle boil; skim foam.</li>
          <li>Cover and simmer <strong>25–30 minutes</strong> until dal and veggies are very soft.</li>
          <li>Blend smooth; thin with hot water for a soup consistency.</li>
          <li>Lemon to taste; keep spices mild.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>For dentures/swallowing:</strong> Cook a few extra minutes before blending; serve warm, not hot.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stick to ~<strong>¾–1 cup</strong> soup alongside protein (egg, curd) and non-starchy veg.</li>
          <li>Favor moong over masoor if you notice gentler glucose response.</li>
          <li>Add oats for fiber; avoid bread sides if watching carbs.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>No added salt.</strong> Lemon, cumin, and coriander give flavor.</li>
          <li>Keep oil to ~2 tsp total; avoid salty stock cubes.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use smaller portions of high-potassium veg; favor bottle gourd/zucchini over tomato/pumpkin.</li>
          <li>Portion soup per your protein/potassium plan; discuss dal type with your clinician if unsure.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skip chili and heavy onion/garlic. Use ginger, turmeric, cumin only.</li>
          <li>Serve warm, not hot; avoid large late-night bowls.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Chewing/swallowing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Blend fully; thin with warm water or unsalted veggie water.</li>
          <li>Test thickness with a spoon; aim for smooth pour, not gloopy.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & texture</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Temperature:</strong> Let soup cool a minute before serving to avoid mouth burns.</li>
          <li><strong>Consistency:</strong> Whisk with hot water if it thickens after resting.</li>
          <li><strong>Storage:</strong> Refrigerate within 2 hours; keep 2–3 days. Reheat gently, add water as needed.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card danger">
        <strong>Allergy/cautions:</strong> If sensitive to legumes, confirm with your clinician before using dal. Avoid salty packaged stocks.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Serve -->
  <section id="serve" class="section">
    <h2>How to serve</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>As a light dinner with a small bowl of curd or a boiled egg</li>
          <li>With a side of sautéed bottle gourd or carrots</li>
          <li>With a lemon wedge and a few coriander leaves on top</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Portion cue:</strong> ~<strong>¾–1 cup</strong> per person as a starter or light meal; adjust based on appetite and glucose goals.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Which dal is gentlest?</p>
        <p>Yellow moong cooks quickly and blends very smooth. Red masoor is also soft and mild.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I skip oil?</p>
        <p>Yes. You can dry-toast spices in a non-stick pot, then add water and proceed.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I freeze it?</p>
        <p>Yes. Freeze in single portions up to 2 months. Thaw in the fridge and reheat gently with water.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Creaminess without dairy?</p>
        <p>Blend in 1 tbsp oats while simmering or a spoon of cooked rice before blending.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
