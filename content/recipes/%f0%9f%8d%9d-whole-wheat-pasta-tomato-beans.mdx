  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2–3 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>200 g <strong>whole-wheat pasta</strong> (penne/fusilli)</li>
          <li>1 tbsp <strong>olive oil</strong></li>
          <li>2 cloves <strong>garlic</strong>, minced (or ½ tsp powder)</li>
          <li>1 small <strong>onion</strong>, finely chopped (optional; skip if reflux)</li>
          <li>1 can (400 g) <strong>no-salt-added crushed tomatoes</strong> (or 3 fresh ripe tomatoes, chopped)</li>
          <li>1 cup cooked <strong>beans</strong> (cannellini/chickpeas/kidney), rinsed if canned</li>
          <li>½ tsp <strong>oregano</strong> or mixed Italian herbs</li>
          <li>Pinch <strong>black pepper</strong>; <strong>salt</strong> to taste (optional)</li>
          <li>2 tbsp chopped <strong>parsley/basil</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>chili flakes</strong> (skip for reflux)</li>
          <li>2 tbsp <strong>grated cheese</strong> (parmesan/paneer crumbs)</li>
          <li>½ cup <strong>capsicum/spinach</strong>, finely chopped</li>
          <li>1 tsp <strong>olive oil</strong> (extra) to finish</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Tip: choose low-sodium/canned “no salt added” tomatoes and rinse beans to cut salt.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Boil pasta (8–10 min)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add pasta; cook until just tender (<em>al dente+</em> for easier chewing).</li>
          <li>Reserve ½ cup cooking water, then drain.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make the sauce (10–12 min)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Warm olive oil in a pan. Soften onion (if using) 3–4 min; add garlic for 30 sec.</li>
          <li>Add crushed tomatoes, herbs, and pepper. Simmer 6–8 min.</li>
          <li>Stir in beans and ¼ cup pasta water; simmer 2–3 min to a gentle, spoon-coating consistency.</li>
          <li>Toss in pasta. Use more reserved water for a silkier sauce. Finish with herbs and a drizzle of olive oil.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">Chew-soft: cook pasta 1–2 min longer and chop any chunky veg small.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Portion: ~70–90 g dry pasta per person (1–1½ cups cooked).</li>
          <li>Use whole-wheat; keep beans at ½ cup per serving.</li>
          <li>Add protein (tofu/paneer/egg) and non-starchy veg for fullness.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>no-salt-added</strong> tomatoes; rinse beans well.</li>
          <li>Flavor with herbs/garlic; go easy on cheese.</li>
          <li>Olive oil total 1–2 tbsp for the whole recipe.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Beans and tomatoes add potassium—<strong>mind portions</strong> (¼–½ cup beans/serving).</li>
          <li>Consider half-bean, half-veggie sauce (more zucchini/capsicum).</li>
          <li>Keep sodium low; discuss potassium targets with your clinician.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skip onion/chili; cook tomatoes longer and use a pinch of sugar or milk splash to soften acidity.</li>
          <li>Small, earlier meals; sit upright 30–45 min after eating.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Chew-soft tip:</strong> Choose short shapes (penne/spirals), cook slightly beyond al dente, and keep sauce moist.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="make-ahead" class="section">
    <h2>Make-ahead & storage</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prep once</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cook sauce (tomato + beans) up to 3 days in advance; reheat gently.</li>
          <li>Boil pasta fresh or undercook by 1 min if reheating later.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Leftovers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Refrigerate airtight 2–3 days. Add a splash of water when reheating.</li>
          <li>Freeze sauce (without pasta) up to 2 months.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tips -->
  <section id="tips" class="section">
    <h2>Texture & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Reserve pasta water to keep the sauce silky and easy to swallow.</li>
          <li>Dice veg small; mash part of the beans for a smoother texture.</li>
          <li>Food safety: cool and refrigerate within 2 hours; reheat to steaming hot.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        For dentures/sensitive teeth: choose smaller pasta shapes and cook slightly softer; avoid sharp chili flakes.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Which beans work best?</p>
        <p>Cannellini and chickpeas are creamy and hold shape; kidney beans add color and heartiness.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Too acidic?</p>
        <p>Simmer longer, add a pinch of sugar or splash of milk, and skip chili/onion. Try half tomato + half finely diced zucchini.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gluten-free?</p>
        <p>Use GF pasta (brown rice/chickpea). Check package times—avoid overcooking.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Extra protein ideas?</p>
        <p>Tofu/paneer cubes, tuna, or a boiled egg. Or sprinkle 2 tbsp grated parmesan.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
