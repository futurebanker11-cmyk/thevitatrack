  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (for 1 hearty serving)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>2 large <strong>eggs</strong> (or 3 egg whites, or 1 egg + 2 whites)</li>
          <li>1 tsp <strong>olive oil</strong> or 1 tsp butter</li>
          <li>¾–1 cup <strong>soft-cut veggies</strong> (choose 2–3): bell pepper, zucchini, mushrooms, spinach, tomato (seeded), onions/green onions</li>
          <li>1–2 tbsp <strong>milk or water</strong> (for softer curds; optional)</li>
          <li>Pinch of <strong>salt-free seasoning</strong> (pepper, garlic/onion powder, herbs)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>1–2 tbsp <strong>grated cheese</strong> (low-sodium if needed)</li>
          <li>Fresh herbs: cilantro, parsley, dill</li>
          <li>1 tbsp <strong>paneer/cottage cheese</strong> (crumbled) or 2 tbsp cooked beans for extra protein</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Fine-dice veggies (pea-size) for faster cooking and easier chewing.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Pan prep (2–3 min)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Beat eggs with milk/water and seasonings until slightly frothy.</li>
          <li>Warm a <strong>nonstick pan</strong> on medium; add oil/butter.</li>
          <li>Soften veggies 2–4 minutes (add spinach last, 30–60 sec).</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Scramble (3–5 min)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Pour in eggs; let set 10–15 seconds.</li>
          <li>Using a spatula, <strong>slowly push from edges to center</strong>, forming soft curds.</li>
          <li>Cook until <strong>no liquid egg remains</strong> and curds are moist, not runny. Fold in cheese/herbs if using.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">Food safety: cook eggs until firm throughout. Avoid runny eggs for vulnerable immune systems.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pair with a <strong>small whole-grain</strong> side (½ slice toast) or veggies only—keep carbs modest.</li>
          <li>Skip large cheese amounts; consider 1 tbsp nuts/seeds or cottage cheese for protein.</li>
          <li>Keep portion to 2 eggs or 1 egg + 2 whites.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cook in <strong>olive oil</strong>; avoid processed meats.</li>
          <li>Use herbs/pepper; <strong>avoid added salt</strong> or choose low-sodium cheese.</li>
          <li>Add potassium-rich veg judiciously per clinician advice if on certain meds.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Favor lower-potassium veg: bell pepper, zucchini, mushrooms.</li>
          <li>Limit big portions of tomato/spinach if potassium-restricted.</li>
          <li>Choose <strong>egg whites</strong> more often if protein restrictions require.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Go for <strong>smaller portions</strong>; avoid late-night eating.</li>
          <li>Use mild seasonings; skip large onions/chilies if they trigger you.</li>
          <li>Serve with non-acidic sides (steamed veg) instead of citrus/tomato.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Texture tip:</strong> Add 1–2 tbsp water or milk to eggs and cook gently for <em>soft curds</em>—easier to chew and swallow.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="make-ahead" class="section">
    <h2>Make-ahead & storage</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prep once</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pre-dice veggies (2–3 days in fridge); store in airtight container.</li>
          <li>Beat eggs in a jar (same day) for quick pour in the morning.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Leftovers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Best fresh. If needed, refrigerate up to 1 day; reheat gently on low with 1 tsp water.</li>
          <li>Avoid freezing scrambled eggs—they get watery/rubbery.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Tips -->
  <section id="tips" class="section">
    <h2>Cook tips & texture</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use a <strong>silicone spatula</strong> and low–medium heat for soft curds.</li>
          <li>For dental/chewing issues: chop veg small and cook until tender; add 1 tbsp cottage cheese for moisture.</li>
          <li>For extra calories (unintentional weight loss): swirl in 1 tsp olive oil at the end.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Food safety: Refrigerate eggs and use within date. Wash hands and utensils after handling raw eggs. Cook until set (no runny liquid).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I make this without dairy?</p>
        <p>Yes—use water instead of milk and skip cheese. Flavor with herbs and pepper.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Whole eggs or egg whites?</p>
        <p>Whole eggs add nutrients and flavor; whites reduce fat and phosphorus. Mix to fit your plan.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best pan size?</p>
        <p>An 8–10 inch nonstick pan for 2 eggs gives soft, fluffy curds and easy stirring.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What to serve it with?</p>
        <p>Side of fruit or tomato-cucumber salad; a small slice of whole-grain toast if carbs are desired.</p>
      </div>
