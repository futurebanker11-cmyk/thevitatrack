  <!-- Ingredients -->
  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2–3 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>1½ cups cooked <strong>beans</strong> (chickpeas, kidney, or cannellini); well-rinsed if canned</li>
          <li>½ cup <strong>cucumber</strong>, diced small</li>
          <li>½ cup <strong>tomatoes</strong> (cherry/roma), diced</li>
          <li>¼ cup <strong>red onion</strong>, very finely chopped (optional; skip if reflux)</li>
          <li>¼–⅓ cup <strong>olives</strong>, sliced (rinse to reduce salt)</li>
          <li>2–3 tbsp chopped <strong>parsley</strong> or cilantro</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Dressing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>2 tbsp <strong>extra-virgin olive oil</strong></li>
          <li>1–2 tbsp <strong>lemon juice</strong> or mild vinegar (apple/cider)</li>
          <li>½ tsp <strong>roasted cumin</strong> or dried oregano</li>
          <li>Pinch <strong>black pepper</strong></li>
          <li><strong>Salt</strong> only if needed (olives add salt)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">GERD-friendly: use vinegar or lemon <em>peel strips</em> for aroma if citrus triggers symptoms.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Mix (8–10 min)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>In a large bowl, combine beans, cucumber, tomatoes, onion (if using), olives, and herbs.</li>
          <li>Whisk olive oil, lemon/vinegar, cumin/oregano, and pepper. Taste the olives first, then add a <em>pinch</em> of salt only if needed.</li>
          <li>Pour dressing over salad and toss gently. Rest 5 minutes for flavors to meld.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Serve:</strong> as a light meal with whole-grain roti/toast, or a side for grilled fish/chicken/Paneer Tikka.
      </div>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use ¾–1 cup beans per serving + non-starchy veggies.</li>
          <li>Add protein (egg/tofu/paneer) for fullness; avoid breaded sides.</li>
          <li>Dress with olive oil + vinegar; no sugar.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Rinse olives and canned beans</strong> well to reduce sodium.</li>
          <li>Go easy on cheese; flavor with herbs, lemon peel, vinegar.</li>
          <li>Olive oil 2 tbsp total across the bowl.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Beans have potassium — <strong>mind portion</strong> (½–¾ cup).</li>
          <li>Choose lower-sodium olives; rinse thoroughly.</li>
          <li>Discuss protein/ potassium targets with your clinician.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skip raw onion and strong chili. Use cucumber, herbs, and mild vinegar.</li>
          <li>Try lemon peel aroma instead of juice if citrus triggers.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Chew-soft tip:</strong> Mash some beans with a fork and dice veggies small; this gives a moist, easy-to-chew bite.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tips -->
  <section id="tips" class="section">
    <h2>Texture & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rinse canned beans and olives well to reduce sodium and gas-forming compounds.</li>
          <li>For dentures/sensitive teeth: dice small and mash part of the beans.</li>
          <li>Food safety: keep chilled; avoid leaving out &gt;2 hours.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Gas/comfort tip: start with smaller portions if you’re new to beans; increase slowly over a week and drink water through the day.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Which beans work best?</p>
        <p>Chickpeas and cannellini hold shape and have a creamy bite. Kidney beans add color and heartiness.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Too salty?</p>
        <p>Rinse olives and beans; use fewer olives and no added salt. Add herbs, lemon peel, or vinegar for brightness.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I add grains?</p>
        <p>Yes—mix in ½ cup cooked quinoa or brown rice to make it a fuller meal.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">No onion?</p>
        <p>Use extra herbs and a spoon of minced cucumber or bell pepper for crunch.</p>
      </div>
