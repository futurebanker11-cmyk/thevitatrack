    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2–3 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>500 g <strong>potatoes</strong>, peeled and cut into 2–3 cm cubes (waxy or all-purpose)</li>
          <li>1½ tbsp <strong>oil</strong> (mustard/peanut/neutral/olive)</li>
          <li>1½ tsp <strong>cumin seeds</strong> (jeera)</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>turmeric</strong> powder</li>
          <li>½–¾ tsp <strong>salt</strong> (or less; see swaps)</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>black pepper</strong> (freshly ground)</li>
          <li>1–2 tbsp <strong>lemon</strong> juice</li>
          <li>2 tbsp <strong>cilantro</strong>, chopped</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>½ tsp <strong>grated ginger</strong></li>
          <li>½ tsp <strong>coriander</strong> powder</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>amchur</strong> (dry mango) or extra lemon for tang</li>
          <li>Pinch <strong>hing</strong> (asafoetida)</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>red chili</strong> flakes (skip for reflux)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Texture note: cut pieces evenly for even cooking; smaller dice = softer, quicker cook.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Parboil (soft centers, less oil)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add potato cubes and cook 5–7 minutes until just tender at the edges (knife meets slight resistance).</li>
          <li>Drain well; let steam-dry 2–3 minutes. This helps crisp later with less oil.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Temper spices</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Heat oil in a wide non-stick pan over medium.</li>
          <li>Add cumin seeds; let sizzle 20–30 seconds until fragrant. Add ginger/hing if using; stir briefly.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Pan-roast & finish</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Add potatoes, turmeric, and pepper. Toss gently to coat. Cook 6–9 minutes, turning every 1–2 minutes for even golden spots.</li>
          <li>Sprinkle salt toward the end; add coriander powder if using. Finish with lemon juice and cilantro. Taste and adjust tang/pepper.</li>
        </ol>
        <div class="info small">Safety: keep heat moderate to avoid smoking oil. Use a wide pan so cubes brown, not steam.</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Portion ~¾ cup cooked potatoes; pair with a protein/veg (curd, dal, sautéed greens).</li>
          <li>Use extra lemon/cumin/coriander for flavor; avoid added sugar in meals.</li>
          <li>Consider half-potato + half-cauliflower florets for lower carbs.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep salt ≤½ tsp across the whole dish; boost with lemon, pepper, and cumin.</li>
          <li>Use 1–1¼ tbsp oil total; non-stick pan helps reduce oil.</li>
          <li>Serve with potassium-rich greens/veg if not restricted.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Leach potassium: cube potatoes, soak 2–3 hours in water (change once), then parboil and drain before pan-roast.</li>
          <li>Use lemon and herbs for flavor; avoid salt substitutes unless your clinician approves.</li>
          <li>Watch portion size per your plan.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skip red chili; go easy on ginger if sensitive.</li>
          <li>Avoid very late-night portions; sit upright 30–45 minutes after eating.</li>
          <li>Keep oil moderate; avoid greasy texture.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Texture tip:</strong> Parboil slightly longer for extra-soft centers; press gently with a spatula for “smashed” edges that crisp quickly.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Make-ahead, storage & reheating</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prep ahead</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Parboil and chill potatoes up to 2 days (drain well). Pan-roast to serve fresh.</li>
          <li>Pre-measure spices in a small jar for quick cooking.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Store & reheat</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Refrigerate cooked potatoes up to 3 days.</li>
          <li>Reheat in a non-stick pan with a tsp of oil or splash of water, medium heat, 3–4 minutes.</li>
          <li>Microwave 40–60 sec, then crisp briefly in pan if desired.</li>
        </ul>

    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Bloom cumin well — aroma = flavor without extra salt.</li>
          <li>Lemon + pepper at the end brighten the dish and reduce salt need.</li>
          <li>For extra crunch with less oil, finish uncovered for the last 2 minutes.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

        <strong>Hydration-friendly:</strong> Pair with a bowl of thin dal/rasam or warm water with lemon. Sip, don’t chug, if night urination is an issue.

  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Boil first or cook raw?</p>
        <p>Parboiling gives soft centers and uses less oil. Raw cubes work but take longer and may stick.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I keep potato skins?</p>
        <p>Yes, scrub well — adds fiber and minerals. For very soft texture, peel.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">No lemon at home?</p>
        <p>Use a pinch of amchur or ½ tsp vinegar to brighten flavors without extra salt.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make it oil-light?</p>
        <p>Use a good non-stick pan, parboil well, and spread cubes out. A light spray of oil helps browning.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
