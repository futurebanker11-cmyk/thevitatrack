    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (1 large mug)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>1 <strong>chamomile tea bag</strong> (or 1 tbsp loose flowers in an infuser)</li>
          <li>3–4 thin slices fresh <strong>ginger</strong> (peeled)</li>
          <li>1½ cups <strong>water</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>½–1 tsp <strong>honey</strong> or jaggery (or non-nutritive sweetener)</li>
          <li>1–2 strips <strong>lemon peel</strong> (aroma without extra acidity)</li>
          <li>Pinch <strong>cinnamon</strong> or cardamom</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Tip: lemon <em>peel</em> adds fragrance with less bite than juice (friendlier for reflux).</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Stovetop (best infusion)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Bring water just to a boil. Add ginger; simmer gently 3–4 minutes.</li>
          <li>Turn off heat. Add chamomile (bag/infuser) and steep 3–5 minutes.</li>
          <li>Remove chamomile and ginger. Sweeten lightly if desired; add lemon peel for 1 minute and discard.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Kettle/Microwave (fast)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Heat water until steaming. Add ginger slices and chamomile to the mug.</li>
          <li>Cover the mug (saucer) and steep 5–6 minutes for fuller flavor.</li>
          <li>Remove solids; sweeten to taste.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">Safety: beverage will be hot—use a stable mug and sit while drinking.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Flavor tweaks</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Extra-calm</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>½ tsp dried lavender + chamomile (steep 3–4 min)</li>
          <li>Skip lemon and spices</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Digestive-gentle</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>More ginger (1–2 extra slices), add a pinch of cinnamon</li>
          <li>Sweeten with ½ tsp honey only if needed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Iced version</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Brew double-strength; cool and pour over ice</li>
          <li>Add mint leaf (skip if reflux-triggering)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use no sweetener or a non-nutritive option.</li>
          <li>Pair with a small protein snack if needed (curd/yogurt).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep sodium negligible (no added salt). Plain water base.</li>
          <li>If you retain fluids, count this toward daily fluid limits.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Usually low in potassium/sodium—still <strong>track fluids</strong> if restricted.</li>
          <li>Avoid large amounts of herbal concentrates unless cleared by your clinician.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Favor lemon <em>peel</em> over juice; skip mint/chili.</li>
          <li>Sip warm (not very hot). Avoid drinks close to bedtime if reflux-prone.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Allergy note:</strong> Chamomile is related to ragweed—avoid if you’ve had reactions to ragweed/marigold/daisy-family plants.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Tips -->
  <section id="tips" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & serving</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Medication cautions: large amounts of chamomile or ginger may not suit everyone. If you use blood thinners, have bleeding disorders, or upcoming surgery, confirm with your clinician before regular use.</li>
          <li>Start mild: 3–4 min steep; adjust strength slowly.</li>
          <li>Temperature safety: sip warm, not scalding, to protect mouth and throat.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Bedtime tip: finish your cup 1–2 hours before sleep if night bathroom trips are an issue.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fresh vs powdered ginger?</p>
        <p>Fresh gives gentle warmth and aroma. If using powder, start with a tiny pinch and whisk—powder is stronger.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I add milk?</p>
        <p>It’s usually taken without milk. If you like creaminess, add a spoon of warm milk after steeping.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Caffeine-free?</p>
        <p>Yes—chamomile is naturally caffeine-free.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best time to drink?</p>
        <p>Evenings or after meals. If reflux-prone, avoid very large volumes close to bedtime.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
