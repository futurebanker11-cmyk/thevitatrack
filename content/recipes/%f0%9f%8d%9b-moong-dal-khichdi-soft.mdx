  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2 servings, soft texture)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>½ cup <strong>split yellow moong dal</strong> (washed)</li>
          <li>½ cup <strong>rice</strong> (parboiled or short-grain; rinse well)</li>
          <li>3–3½ cups <strong>water</strong> (more for softer texture)</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>turmeric</strong></li>
          <li>Optional: ½ tsp <strong>cumin seeds</strong>, 1 tsp <strong>ghee</strong> or oil</li>
          <li>Pinch of <strong>salt</strong> (keep low or skip for BP/CKD)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Rinsing dal/rice improves digestion and reduces starch surface.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gentle add-ins (choose any)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>¼ cup finely chopped <strong>carrot</strong> or <strong>zucchini</strong></li>
          <li>2 tbsp <strong>peas</strong> (optional; skip if potassium restricted)</li>
          <li>½ tsp grated <strong>ginger</strong> (optional)</li>
          <li><strong>Coriander</strong> to garnish (optional)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Pressure cooker / Instant Pot (softer, faster)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Rinse dal and rice until water runs clearer.</li>
          <li>In the pot: dal, rice, water (3½ cups for very soft), turmeric, and salt (if using). Add veggies if desired.</li>
          <li>Pressure cook: 2–3 whistles on stovetop or <strong>IP: 8–10 min High</strong>, natural release.</li>
          <li>Mash lightly for extra-soft texture. Temper (optional): warm ghee/oil, splutter cumin, stir in.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Stovetop (saucepan)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Bring water to boil; add rinsed dal+rice, turmeric, and optional veggies.</li>
          <li>Simmer on low, covered, 25–30 min; stir occasionally and add hot water for softer porridge-like khichdi.</li>
          <li>Finish with optional cumin temper and a tiny ghee drizzle if allowed.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">Texture tip: add hot water and whisk briefly to reach spoon-soft consistency.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Portion: ~¾–1 cup cooked per meal; pair with non-starchy veg or salad.</li>
          <li>Use <strong>more dal than rice</strong> (e.g., ⅔ cup dal : ⅓ cup rice) or consider brown rice (well-cooked).</li>
          <li>Keep ghee minimal; add protein side (curd/yogurt) if tolerated.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Low-salt</strong> or no added salt; lean on turmeric, cumin, and ginger for flavor.</li>
          <li>Use minimal oil/ghee (½–1 tsp total for 2 servings).</li>
          <li>Add carrots/zucchini for fiber; avoid pickles/tangy high-salt sides.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Watch <strong>potassium</strong>: keep peas small or skip; portion khichdi as advised.</li>
          <li>Season lightly; discuss protein targets for dal with your clinician/dietitian.</li>
          <li>Use water (not milk); avoid high-sodium broths.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep spices gentle; avoid chili. Ginger in small amounts is usually comfortable.</li>
          <li>Eat slowly, warm (not very hot), and sit upright for 30–45 minutes after.</li>
          <li>Prefer soft consistency to reduce strain while eating.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Hydration tip:</strong> serve with warm water or light herbal tea. Avoid large fluid chugs at night if nocturia is an issue.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Flavor boosters & serving ideas</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dry roast cumin seeds before tempering for a nutty aroma.</li>
          <li>Grate a tiny bit of ginger into the pot for gentle warmth.</li>
          <li>For extra protein, increase dal slightly and reduce rice a little.</li>
          <li>Keep chili and garam masala out for a senior-friendly, stomach-easy bowl.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Salt-smart: brighten with lemon (a few drops), ginger, and cumin before adding any salt. Taste first.

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Which rice works best?</p>
        <p>Short-grain or parboiled rice cooks softer. Rinse well; use more water for a porridge-like texture.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I skip ghee?</p>
        <p>Yes. Use a few drops of oil or go fat-free. If you include ghee, keep it minimal for heart health.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gas or bloating?</p>
        <p>Rinse dal well; cook very soft; start with smaller portions and add ginger. Split yellow moong is typically gentler.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Brown rice?</p>
        <p>Possible, but cook longer with extra water for softness; many prefer white rice here for digestibility.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
