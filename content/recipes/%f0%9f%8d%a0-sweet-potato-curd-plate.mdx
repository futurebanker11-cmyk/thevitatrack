  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (1–2 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>1 medium <strong>sweet potato</strong> (250–300 g), scrubbed</li>
          <li>¾–1 cup <strong>plain curd/yogurt</strong> (regular or Greek; lactose-free if needed)</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>roasted cumin</strong> powder (optional)</li>
          <li>Pinch <strong>black pepper</strong> (optional)</li>
          <li><strong>Salt</strong> to taste (or skip for BP/CKD)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional toppings/add-ons</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>1 tsp <strong>ghee/olive oil</strong> for richness (optional)</li>
          <li>Finely chopped <strong>mint/cilantro</strong></li>
          <li>1 tbsp <strong>roasted peanuts</strong> or pumpkin seeds (crushed)</li>
          <li>½ tsp <strong>lemon juice</strong> (skip if reflux)</li>
          <li>Side: cucumber slices or simple raita</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Budget tip: steam 2–3 sweet potatoes at once; use over 2–3 days.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Cook the sweet potato (10–15 min)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li><strong>Microwave:</strong> Pierce 5–6 times; microwave 5–8 min, flipping once, until very tender.</li>
          <li><strong>Steam/Boil:</strong> 12–18 min until a fork slides in easily.</li>
          <li>Cool slightly; peel and <strong>mash</strong> with a fork. Add a tiny pinch of salt/pepper and cumin if using.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Plate & serve (2–3 min)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Spoon warm mashed sweet potato on one side, <strong>curd</strong> on the other.</li>
          <li>Drizzle 1 tsp ghee/olive oil if desired; sprinkle herbs/seeds.</li>
          <li>Serve as a light meal or hearty snack.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">Chew-soft: mash to a smooth, lump-free texture; thin curd with a spoon of water if too thick.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Portion sweet potato to <strong>½–¾ cup</strong> mash.</li>
          <li>Use <strong>Greek yogurt</strong> (½–¾ cup) for extra protein.</li>
          <li>Avoid honey/jaggery; add nuts/seeds for satiety.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>No added salt.</strong> Flavor with cumin, pepper, herbs.</li>
          <li>Choose olive oil; keep ghee small (1 tsp max).</li>
          <li>Pair with a raw veg side for fiber.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sweet potato is higher in potassium—<strong>mind portion</strong> (½ cup mash) if restricted.</li>
          <li>Leach method: cube, boil, and discard water before mashing.</li>
          <li>Use regular yogurt (smaller portion) if protein-restricted; keep sodium low.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skip lemon, raw onion/chili. Use mild herbs and cumin.</li>
          <li>Small, earlier meals; sit upright 30–45 minutes after.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Chew-soft tip:</strong> Mash thoroughly; add a spoon of warm water or curd to reach smooth puree if needed.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Make-ahead & storage</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prep once</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Steam/boil 2–3 sweet potatoes; refrigerate up to 3 days.</li>
          <li>Mash just before eating; add a splash of hot water to rewarm.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Leftovers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep mashed sweet potato and yogurt <strong>separate</strong> in the fridge.</li>
          <li>Reheat mash covered (microwave 40–60 sec). Stir and check temperature before serving.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Tips -->
  <section id="tips" class="section">
    <h2>Texture & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use a fork or potato masher for a smooth, lump-free mash.</li>
          <li>For extra calories (if unintentional weight loss): add 1 tsp ghee/olive oil or use fuller-fat yogurt.</li>
          <li>On fluid limits (heart/kidney): note yogurt counts toward daily fluid in some plans.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Food safety: Use pasteurized yogurt; refrigerate within 2 hours. Reheat only the mash—keep yogurt cold.
      </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I keep the sweet potato skin?</p>
        <p>Yes, if tender and well washed—it adds fiber. Peel for a smoother mash.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Greek or regular curd?</p>
        <p>Greek adds more protein and thickness; regular is lighter and easier to mix.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best time to eat?</p>
        <p>Great for lunch or early dinner. If reflux, avoid eating within 3 hours of bedtime.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Plant-based option?</p>
        <p>Use unsweetened, fortified plant yogurt; add 1 tbsp peanut butter for protein.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
