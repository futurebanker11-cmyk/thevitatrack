    <h2>Ingredients & ratios</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (per serving ~250 ml)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Plain yogurt/curd</strong> (unsweetened, low-fat or regular): <strong>½ cup (120 ml)</strong></li>
          <li><strong>Cold water</strong>: <strong>1–1¼ cups (240–300 ml)</strong> for a 1:2–1:2.5 <em>yogurt:water</em> ratio</li>
          <li><strong>Turmeric</strong> (tiny pinch) or <strong>roasted cumin</strong> (pinch), optional</li>
          <li><strong>Fresh mint</strong> 2–3 small leaves, finely chopped (optional)</li>
          <li><em>No sugar, no added salt</em></li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Thinner = easier to sip. Add ice only if it doesn’t trigger reflux.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What to use</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Set curd</strong> or <strong>Greek-style thinned with water</strong></li>
          <li><strong>Lactose-free yogurt</strong> if sensitive</li>
          <li><strong>Probiotic yogurt</strong> (plain) is a good option</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Whisk method (no blender)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Whisk yogurt until smooth.</li>
          <li>Add cold water gradually (1–1¼ cups), whisking to a light, frothy drink.</li>
          <li>Add a <strong>pinch roasted cumin</strong> or <strong>tiny pinch turmeric</strong> if desired.</li>
          <li>Serve chilled. Avoid added salt/sugar.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Blender method</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Combine yogurt and water. Pulse briefly—avoid over-foaming.</li>
          <li>Stir in optional <strong>mint (fine)</strong>. Skip mint if it triggers reflux.</li>
          <li>Pour into a glass; sip slowly.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">For a very light “chaas,” use up to a 1:3 ratio (½ cup yogurt : 1½ cups water).</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>No sugar.</strong> If sweetness is needed, a drop or two of stevia can be used—optional.</li>
          <li>Keep to ~<strong>200–250 ml</strong> per sitting and pair with a meal or high-fiber snack.</li>
          <li>Greek yogurt thinned gives a little more protein per sip.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Do not add salt.</strong> Flavor with cumin/mint instead.</li>
          <li>Use <strong>low-fat</strong> yogurt to keep saturated fat lower.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dairy adds potassium & phosphorus. Keep portions <strong>small</strong> as advised (e.g., 100–200 ml).</li>
          <li>Avoid salty seasonings; discuss yogurt frequency with your renal dietitian.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Serve cool, not icy. <strong>Skip mint/pepper</strong> if they trigger symptoms.</li>
          <li>Avoid close to bedtime; sip slowly.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lactose sensitivity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>lactose-free yogurt</strong> or try small amounts first.</li>
          <li>Fermented curd is often better tolerated than milk.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Hydration note:</strong> Helpful in hot weather—spread intake across the day rather than large glasses at night.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Timing & safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Timing & safety tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Best time:</strong> mid-morning or afternoon; avoid large glasses late evening if night urination is an issue.</li>
          <li><strong>Medicine spacing:</strong> Dairy can interfere with some antibiotics (e.g., quinolones/tetracyclines). Keep a few hours apart.</li>
          <li><strong>Cold-chain:</strong> Keep yogurt refrigerated; discard if left out too long.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        For added flavor without salt/sugar: a <strong>pinch roasted cumin</strong>, squeeze of lemon (if tolerated), or <strong>tiny ginger</strong>.
      </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is unsweetened lassi OK for diabetes?</p>
        <p>Yes—keep portions modest (≈200–250 ml) and avoid sugar. Pair with meals or fiber for steadier response.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Salted chaas vs this version?</p>
        <p>This recipe <strong>skips salt</strong> to support BP/kidneys. Use cumin/mint/ginger for flavor.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I add ice?</p>
        <p>Small amounts are fine if reflux isn’t triggered. Cool (not icy) is easier on sensitive stomachs.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long does it keep?</p>
        <p>Refrigerate up to 24 hours. Stir before serving; discard if sourness or separation seems off.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
