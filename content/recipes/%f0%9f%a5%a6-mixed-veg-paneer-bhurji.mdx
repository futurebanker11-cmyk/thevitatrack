  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients & portions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2–3 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Paneer:</strong> 200–250 g, crumbled (fresh, low-salt if possible)</li>
          <li><strong>Vegetables (finely chopped):</strong> 1–1½ cups total — capsicum (¼), carrots (½), tomatoes (½ small, optional), zucchini/bottle gourd (½ cup)</li>
          <li><strong>Aromatics:</strong> ½ tsp grated ginger; ¼ tsp turmeric; ¼ tsp cumin powder</li>
          <li><strong>Oil:</strong> 2 tsp olive or neutral oil</li>
          <li><strong>Finish:</strong> black pepper to taste; lemon juice (½ lemon); coriander (optional)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Keep spices mild; skip chilies for a reflux-friendly dish.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Fiber:</strong> 1 tbsp oats sprinkled in with veg for extra softness</li>
          <li><strong>Protein blend:</strong> 1 egg scrambled in with paneer (if suitable)</li>
          <li><strong>Herb aroma:</strong> dill or coriander at the end</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Stovetop steps (soft texture)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ol class="list">
          <li><strong>Warm pan:</strong> Heat a non-stick pan on medium-low. Add oil.</li>
          <li><strong>Aromatics:</strong> Sauté ginger 20–30 sec; add turmeric and cumin powder, stir briefly.</li>
          <li><strong>Veg first:</strong> Add finely chopped veg; cook 3–5 min until soft. Sprinkle 2–3 tbsp water and cover for 1–2 min if needed.</li>
          <li><strong>Paneer in:</strong> Crumbled paneer goes in. Fold gently 2–3 min until warmed through.</li>
          <li><strong>Finish:</strong> Lemon and black pepper. No added salt required.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Extra-soft option:</strong> Add 1–2 tbsp water, cover 1 minute, and lightly mash to a spoonable texture.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Paneer provides protein; pair with non-starchy veg sides.</li>
          <li>Keep roti/rice portions modest; skip bread if watching carbs closely.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>No added salt.</strong> Use lemon, pepper, and herbs for flavor.</li>
          <li>Keep oil ~2 tsp; avoid salty sauces.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Paneer contains phosphorus and sodium; choose low-salt paneer and follow your portion plan.</li>
          <li>Favor lower-potassium veg (bottle gourd, zucchini) over tomato/capsicum if advised.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skip chilies/onion if sensitive; stick to ginger and mild spices.</li>
          <li>Serve warm, not hot; avoid large late meals.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Chewing/swallowing</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Grate or crumble paneer finely; cook veg very soft and mash lightly.</li>
          <li>Moisten with a spoon of water or curd for a softer texture.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & storage</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Temperature:</strong> Let steam settle to avoid mouth burns.</li>
          <li><strong>Hygiene:</strong> Use fresh paneer; refrigerate leftovers promptly.</li>
          <li><strong>Storage:</strong> Refrigerate within 2 hours; use within 2 days. Reheat gently with a splash of water.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card danger">
        <strong>High-sodium additions:</strong> Avoid ketchup, soy sauce, and stock cubes unless sodium fits your plan.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Serve -->
  <section id="serve" class="section">
    <h2>How to serve</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>With a small whole-wheat roti or millet roti</li>
          <li>Over sautéed bottle gourd/zucchini for a low-carb plate</li>
          <li>As a soft filling in a chapati roll (light curd smear)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Portion cue:</strong> ~<strong>¾–1 cup</strong> per person as a main; adjust for protein needs.
      </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I use tofu instead of paneer?</p>
        <p>Yes. Firm tofu crumbled works well; press briefly to remove extra water and season with lemon and pepper.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How to keep it moist?</p>
        <p>Add 1–2 tbsp water and cover for 30–60 seconds before serving, or stir in 1 tbsp curd off heat.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is onion/garlic okay?</p>
        <p>Use lightly sautéed if tolerated. Skip for reflux-friendly version; rely on ginger and lemon.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best veg for a soft texture?</p>
        <p>Bottle gourd, zucchini, and finely grated carrots soften quickly and blend well with paneer.</p>
      </div>
