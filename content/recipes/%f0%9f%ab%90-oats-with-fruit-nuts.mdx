  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (1 large bowl)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>½ cup <strong>old-fashioned rolled oats</strong> (or steel-cut quick version)</li>
          <li>1 cup <strong>water</strong> (or ½ cup water + ½ cup low-fat milk/unsweetened fortified plant milk)</li>
          <li>1 small <strong>banana</strong> or ½ cup <strong>berries</strong> (fresh/frozen)</li>
          <li>1–2 tbsp <strong>nuts</strong> (almonds/walnuts/pecans), chopped</li>
          <li>½ tsp <strong>cinnamon</strong> or cardamom</li>
          <li>Optional: 1 tsp <strong>chia</strong> or ground flaxseed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>1–2 tsp <strong>peanut/almond butter</strong></li>
          <li>1 tsp <strong>honey</strong> or maple (or non-nutritive sweetener if advised)</li>
          <li>Pinch of <strong>salt</strong> (skip for BP/CKD)</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>vanilla</strong> extract</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Tip: keep nuts pre-chopped in a small jar for easy mornings.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Stovetop (creamiest)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Bring water/milk to a gentle simmer.</li>
          <li>Stir in oats and cinnamon; cook 4–6 minutes, stirring.</li>
          <li>Off heat: mash in banana or fold in berries.</li>
          <li>Top with nuts and seeds. Taste and add a drizzle of honey only if needed.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Microwave (fast)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>In a deep bowl: oats + liquid + cinnamon.</li>
          <li>Microwave 2½–3 minutes (watch for boil-over).</li>
          <li>Stir, then add fruit and nuts.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">Safety: bowl will be hot; use oven mitts.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>steel-cut</strong> or old-fashioned oats; avoid instant flavored packets.</li>
          <li>Protein boost: 2 tbsp nut butter or ½ cup plain Greek yogurt (stir in after cooking).</li>
          <li>Fruit: prefer <strong>berries</strong> or ½ banana; keep total fruit to ~½ cup.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>No added salt; sweeten with fruit/cinnamon first.</li>
          <li>Choose <strong>walnuts/almonds</strong> for healthy fats (1–2 tbsp).</li>
          <li>Use low-fat milk or fortified unsweetened plant milk.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Portion oats to ½ cup dry; limit high-potassium fruits if advised.</li>
          <li>Prefer <strong>berries</strong> over banana if potassium restricted.</li>
          <li>Ask your clinician/dietitian about milk type/amount for phosphorus/potassium needs.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Avoid large late-night bowls; enjoy earlier in the day.</li>
          <li>Limit mint/chocolate toppings; cinnamon is generally gentler.</li>
          <li>Keep portions comfortable; sit upright 30–45 minutes after eating.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Hydration tip:</strong> have a small glass of water or herbal tea alongside; avoid chugging large volumes if night urination is an issue.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="make-ahead" class="section">
    <h2>Make-ahead, storage & reheating</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Overnight oats (no-cook)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Jar: ½ cup oats + ½ cup milk + ¼ cup yogurt + cinnamon.</li>
          <li>Chill overnight; add fruit and nuts in the morning.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Cook once, eat twice</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cook 2–3 servings; refrigerate up to 3 days.</li>
          <li>Reheat with a splash of water/milk; stir well.</li>
          <li>Add fresh fruit/nuts just before eating for crunch.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tips -->
  <section id="tips" class="section">
    <h2>Taste boosters & safe sweetening</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Cinnamon + cardamom + vanilla = sweet taste without sugar.</li>
          <li>Toast nuts lightly for extra flavor.</li>
          <li>Zest of orange or lemon brightens bowls.</li>
          <li>If you use honey/maple, start with <strong>1 tsp</strong>; or try a non-nutritive sweetener if recommended.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        If you monitor glucose, check your pattern with different fruit portions and timing. Pairing protein/fat with oats helps steady rises.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Old-fashioned vs instant?</p>
        <p>Old-fashioned and steel-cut are less processed and more satisfying; instant often has added sugar and salt.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Frozen fruit ok?</p>
        <p>Yes — great budget choice. Warm berries right in the pot to release juices.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Nut allergy?</p>
        <p>Skip nuts; use seeds (pumpkin/chia/flax) or a dollop of yogurt for texture and protein.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gluten-free?</p>
        <p>Choose oats labeled <strong>gluten-free</strong> if you require GF products.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
