  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>1 cup <strong>thick poha</strong> (flattened rice)</li>
          <li>¾–1 cup <strong>water</strong> (for rinsing + sprinkling)</li>
          <li>1–1½ tsp <strong>oil</strong> (peanut/neutral) or 1 tsp ghee</li>
          <li>½ tsp <strong>mustard seeds</strong></li>
          <li>6–8 <strong>curry leaves</strong> (optional but lovely)</li>
          <li>1 small <strong>onion</strong>, finely chopped (optional)</li>
          <li>½ cup <strong>green peas</strong> (fresh/frozen)</li>
          <li>½ cup finely diced <strong>carrot</strong> (or capsicum)</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>turmeric</strong> powder</li>
          <li>½–¾ tsp <strong>salt</strong> (to taste; see swaps)</li>
          <li>½ <strong>lemon</strong> (wedges to serve)</li>
          <li>2 tbsp chopped <strong>cilantro</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>2 tbsp <strong>peanuts</strong> or roasted chana dal (protein/crunch)</li>
          <li>1 small <strong>green chili</strong>, slit (or a pinch of red chili—optional)</li>
          <li>1 pinch <strong>hing</strong> (asafoetida)</li>
          <li>1 tsp <strong>sugar</strong> (traditional in some regions; optional)</li>
          <li>2 tbsp <strong>grated coconut</strong> for garnish (optional)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Tip: For extra softness, keep a small spray bottle of water to mist the poha while cooking.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Rinse & rest (softer bite)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Place poha in a sieve. Rinse under running water 10–15 seconds while gently fluffing with fingers.</li>
          <li>Drain well and rest 5–8 minutes. Sprinkle 2–3 tbsp water if it still feels dry. Avoid soaking.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Temper & cook veggies</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Heat oil in a non-stick pan. Add mustard seeds; let crackle. Add hing, curry leaves, and chili (if using).</li>
          <li>Add onion; sauté 2–3 minutes till translucent. Add peas and carrots; cook 3–4 minutes till tender.</li>
          <li>Stir in turmeric and salt.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Add poha & steam</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Add rinsed poha (and peanuts if using). Toss very gently to coat yellow.</li>
          <li>Mist/sprinkle 1–2 tbsp water around the pan. Cover 2 minutes on low to steam.</li>
          <li>Switch off heat. Rest 1 minute. Finish with lemon juice and cilantro. Fluff and serve warm.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">Safety: keep flame low after adding poha to prevent sticking or drying.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Portion: ~¾ cup cooked per person; load up veggies (1–1½ cups).</li>
          <li>Add protein: 2 tbsp roasted peanuts/chana dal, or fold in ½ cup sprouts at the end.</li>
          <li>Keep lemon, herbs, and spices for flavor—skip sugar.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use 1–1½ tsp oil total; dry-roast peanuts if using.</li>
          <li>Go easy on salt; boost with lemon, cilantro, and a pinch of cumin.</li>
          <li>Add extra peas/carrots for fiber and potassium balance (if not restricted).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Mind portion of peas, peanuts, and coconut if potassium/phosphorus limited.</li>
          <li>Season with herbs and lemon; discuss salt substitutes with your clinician.</li>
          <li>Aim smaller portions with extra low-potassium veggies as advised.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skip green chili; use mild spice and more veggies.</li>
          <li>Eat slowly; avoid late-night servings. Sit upright 30–45 minutes after.</li>
          <li>Use minimal oil; avoid large portions.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Texture tip:</strong> For dentures/sensitive teeth, dice veggies small and steam 1–2 extra minutes for a softer bite.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="make-ahead" class="section">
    <h2>Make-ahead, storage & reheating</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prep ahead</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Chop onion/veggies and store 1–2 days in the fridge.</li>
          <li>Dry-roast peanuts and keep in a jar (sprinkle at serving).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Store & reheat</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Refrigerate cooked poha up to 2 days.</li>
          <li>Reheat on low with a splash of water; cover to steam 1–2 minutes. Or microwave 45–60 sec, stirring once.</li>
          <li>Add lemon and cilantro fresh to brighten.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Thick vs thin poha?</p>
        <p>Use <strong>thick</strong> poha for this recipe—it holds shape after rinsing. Thin poha can turn mushy; use for chivda-style snacks instead.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">No onion/peanut?</p>
        <p>Skip onion and add more peas/carrots. Replace peanuts with roasted chana dal or seeds, or omit for a softer texture.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Too dry / too mushy?</p>
        <p>Dry: mist 1–2 tbsp water and steam covered. Mushy: reduce rinsing time and avoid soaking; handle gently.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Microwave version?</p>
        <p>Temper mustard/curry leaves in 1 tsp oil (microwave-safe bowl, 40–60 sec), add veggies and microwave 2 min, fold in rinsed poha + turmeric + salt, mist water, cover, microwave 1 min, rest 1 min, finish with lemon/cilantro.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
