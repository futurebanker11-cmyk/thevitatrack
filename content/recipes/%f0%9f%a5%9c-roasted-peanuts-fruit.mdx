  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients & portions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (1 serving)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Roasted peanuts</strong>, unsalted: <strong>2 tbsp (≈ 16–18 g)</strong> <span class="small">(~1 small handful)</span></li>
          <li><strong>Fresh fruit</strong> (choose one): <strong>¾–1 cup</strong> sliced apple, pear, papaya, banana, or berries</li>
          <li>Optional: ½ tsp <strong>roasted cumin</strong> or <strong>cinnamon</strong> for dusting</li>
          <li>Optional soft swap: <strong>peanut butter</strong> (unsalted, no added sugar): <strong>1 tbsp</strong></li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Scale for 2 servings: double fruit, keep peanuts at 2–3 tbsp total if watching calories.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What to buy</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Dry-roasted, unsalted peanuts</strong> (or roast at home).</li>
          <li>Fruit that’s easy to chew and not overripe-slippery.</li>
          <li>For spreads: natural peanut butter, <em>no added sugar or hydrogenated oil</em>.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Roasting methods</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Air fryer</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Preheat to <strong>160–170°C</strong>.</li>
          <li>Add raw peanuts in a single layer; cook <strong>6–9 min</strong>, shake halfway.</li>
          <li>Cool completely; store airtight. Add spices after cooling.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Skillet (dry roast)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Warm a heavy pan on low–medium.</li>
          <li>Roast peanuts <strong>8–12 min</strong>, stirring often till fragrant/lightly browned.</li>
          <li>Cool fully before storing; dust with cumin/cinnamon if desired.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Oven</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Preheat to <strong>170°C</strong>. Spread peanuts on a tray.</li>
          <li>Bake <strong>10–14 min</strong>, stirring once. Watch edges.</li>
          <li>Cool completely; store airtight up to 2 weeks.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Make-ahead:</strong> Roast a small weekly batch; portion into 2 tbsp snack bags for easy grab-and-go.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Pairing -->
  <section id="pairing" class="section">
    <h2>Best fruit pairings</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Apple or pear</strong> wedges + cinnamon dust</li>
          <li><strong>Papaya</strong> cubes + squeeze of lime</li>
          <li><strong>Banana</strong> slices (smaller portion for diabetes)</li>
          <li><strong>Mixed berries</strong> (strawberry/blueberry)</li>
          <li><strong>Orange</strong> segments (if not sensitive to acid)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Soft-texture alternatives</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Peanut butter <strong>1 tbsp</strong> spread on soft apple/pear slices</li>
          <li>Fruit + <strong>peanut powder</strong> sprinkle</li>
          <li>Fruit + <strong>yogurt</strong> + crushed peanuts (fine chop)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">For dentures or chewing trouble, choose softer fruit and <strong>finely chop or grind</strong> peanuts.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep peanuts at <strong>2 tbsp</strong>; choose lower-GI fruit (berries, apple, pear).</li>
          <li>Pair with <strong>plain yogurt/curd</strong> for extra protein.</li>
          <li>Banana/orange portions smaller; eat slowly and monitor response.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>unsalted</strong> peanuts; avoid fried or honey-coated options.</li>
          <li>Flavor with cinnamon/cumin instead of salt.</li>
          <li>Mind portions (calorie-dense); combine with high-fiber fruit.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Peanuts have potassium & phosphorus—<strong>stick to 1–2 tbsp</strong> if restricted.</li>
          <li>Pick lower-potassium fruits (apples, berries) if advised.</li>
          <li>Discuss nut frequency with your renal dietitian.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Choose non-acidic fruit (banana, papaya, ripe pear).</li>
          <li>Keep portions moderate; avoid eating right before bed.</li>
          <li>Skip spicy dustings; use mild cinnamon only if tolerated.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Protein boost:</strong> Add 2 tbsp cottage cheese/curd alongside for a more filling snack without extra nuts.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & choking prevention</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card danger">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Allergy:</strong> Avoid if you have any <strong>peanut allergy</strong> (or unclear history). Do not “test” at home.</li>
          <li><strong>Choking risk:</strong> If you have dentures, swallowing issues, or Parkinson’s—<strong>finely chop or use peanut butter</strong>. Sit upright; take small bites; sip water.</li>
          <li><strong>Salt & sugar coatings:</strong> Skip salted, candied, or masala-coated nuts.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Keep nuts in <strong>small containers</strong> to avoid mindless handfuls. Measure 2 tbsp into a bowl, then seal the pack.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are peanuts good daily?</p>
        <p>In small portions (2 tbsp), unsalted peanuts can fit many plans. Rotate with other nuts/seeds for variety.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Peanut butter vs whole peanuts?</p>
        <p>Both work. Peanut butter may be easier to chew/swallow. Choose <strong>unsalted, no-sugar-added</strong> jars.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best time to have this snack?</p>
        <p>Mid-morning or mid-afternoon. For diabetes, spacing snacks evenly helps; pair with protein (yogurt/curd).</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Storage tips?</p>
        <p>Keep roasted peanuts in an airtight jar in a cool, dark place for up to 2 weeks. Refrigerate in warm weather.</p>
      </div>
