  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients (saag + roti)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Saag (2 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>300 g mixed greens <strong>(spinach + mustard/ bathua*</strong> if available) — washed, chopped</li>
          <li>1 small <strong>onion</strong>, finely chopped (optional)</li>
          <li>1 small <strong>tomato</strong>, chopped (optional; gentler for GERD)</li>
          <li>1 tsp <strong>ginger</strong> paste (or grated)</li>
          <li>1 small <strong>green chili</strong>, slit (optional; skip for reflux)</li>
          <li>1–1½ tsp <strong>oil</strong> or 1 tsp ghee</li>
          <li>½ tsp <strong>cumin seeds</strong> (jeera)</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>turmeric</strong></li>
          <li>½ tsp <strong>coriander</strong> powder (optional)</li>
          <li>½–¾ tsp <strong>salt</strong> total (or less; see swaps)</li>
          <li>2–4 tbsp <strong>water</strong> or low-sodium stock as needed</li>
          <li>Optional finish: 1 tbsp <strong>curd</strong> or 1–2 tsp <strong>makki atta</strong> (cornmeal) for body</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">*If mustard/bathua aren’t available, use all spinach + a handful of methi or kale (softer-cook).</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Besan Roti (makes 3–4 small rotis)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>½ cup <strong>besan</strong> (chickpea flour)</li>
          <li>½ cup <strong>whole-wheat flour</strong> (atta) — adjust for softer, flexible rotis</li>
          <li>½ tsp <strong>oil</strong> (in dough) + 1 tsp for cooking (optional)</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>salt</strong> (optional)</li>
          <li>~⅓–½ cup warm <strong>water</strong> (as needed)</li>
          <li>Optional: ½ tsp <strong>ajwain</strong> or <strong>jeera</strong> seeds; 1–2 tbsp <strong>curd</strong> for extra softness</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Ratio tip: 1:1 (besan:atta) gives softness + protein; use 2:1 (atta:besan) for the softest rotis if chewing is a concern.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Saag (creamy, gentle spice)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Heat oil in a pan (medium). Add cumin to sizzle 20–30 sec. Add onion; sauté 2–3 min till soft.</li>
          <li>Add ginger (and chili if using) 30 sec. Add tomato, turmeric, and coriander powder; cook 2–3 min till soft.</li>
          <li>Add chopped greens with 2–4 tbsp water; cover and cook 5–7 min till wilted and soft.</li>
          <li>Mash lightly with a spoon/masher for a rustic texture. For extra body, stir in curd off heat or simmer 2 min with 1–2 tsp cornmeal.</li>
          <li>Season with minimal salt. Rest 2 min; adjust lemon/curd to taste.</li>
        </ol>
        <div class="info small">Texture: cook a few minutes longer and chop greens small for easier chewing. Avoid high heat to keep flavors gentle.</div>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Besan roti (soft & pliable)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Mix besan, atta, salt (optional), and seeds. Add oil and warm water gradually to form a soft dough. Rest 10–15 min.</li>
          <li>Divide into 3–4 balls. Roll gently with light dry flour.</li>
          <li>Cook on a medium-hot tawa: 30–45 sec each side, then 30–45 sec more with light pressing. Brush with a few drops of oil or ghee if desired.</li>
          <li>Keep rotis covered in a cloth-lined container to stay soft.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">Safety: use moderate heat to avoid hard/dry rotis. Besan browns faster—don’t overcook.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Portion: 1–1½ small besan rotis + generous saag.</li>
          <li>Boost protein: add ½ cup paneer/tofu cubes to saag or serve curd on the side.</li>
          <li>Keep rotis thinner; avoid extra ghee. Skip sugar in any sides.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use 1–1½ tsp oil in the whole meal; skip extra salt—use lemon and cumin for flavor.</li>
          <li>Prefer curd finish over butter. Add chopped coriander for aroma.</li>
          <li>Whole-wheat:besan at 2:1 for more fiber if tolerated.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Spinach/mustard are potassium-rich—<em>mind portions</em>; balance with lower-K sides as per plan.</li>
          <li>Keep salt minimal; avoid salt substitutes unless approved.</li>
          <li>If phosphorus restricted, limit besan quantity or use higher atta ratio (2:1 atta:besan).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skip chili; go easy on ginger/tomato if sensitive. Cook greens well for tenderness.</li>
          <li>Small, unhurried bites; remain upright 30–45 minutes after eating.</li>
          <li>Keep rotis soft (use curd in dough) and avoid very late dinners.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Hydration tip:</strong> Sip warm water or thin chaas alongside; avoid chugging large volumes if night urination is an issue.
      </div>
    </div>

  <section id="make-ahead" class="section">
    <h2>Make-ahead, storage & reheating</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prep ahead</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Wash/chop greens and refrigerate 1–2 days (in a box lined with paper towel).</li>
          <li>Mix dry flours for roti in advance; store airtight.</li>
          <li>Cooked saag can be refrigerated 2 days; reheat gently.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Store & reheat</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Saag: reheat on low with 1–2 tbsp water; avoid repeated reheats.</li>
          <li>Rotis: keep wrapped; reheat on tawa 20–30 sec per side or microwave 10–15 sec wrapped in a damp towel.</li>
          <li>Freeze: Saag freezes fairly well up to 1 month; thaw in fridge and reheat gently.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Tips -->
  <section id="tips" class="section">
    <h2>Taste boosters & texture comfort</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Lemon at the end lifts flavor without more salt.</li>
          <li>For extra softness, blend half the saag briefly (not fully smooth) and mix back.</li>
          <li>Add 1–2 tbsp curd into roti dough for tender, pliable rotis.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Allergy note:</strong> Besan is chickpea flour (legume). If you have legume allergies, use 100% whole-wheat rotis instead.
      </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Mustard greens too strong?</p>
        <p>Use more spinach and a little methi/kale. Cook longer and finish with curd for a milder, creamy taste.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Rotis cracking?</p>
        <p>Add a bit more warm water and ½ tsp oil or curd. Rest dough longer and roll smaller.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gluten-free?</p>
        <p>Make besan-only rotis as small pancakes on a greased non-stick pan (thicker batter), or use certified GF flours.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Low-sodium tips?</p>
        <p>Bloom cumin well, finish with lemon and coriander, and keep portions flavorful with herbs instead of extra salt.</p>
      </div>
