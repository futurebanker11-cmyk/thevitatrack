  <section id="ingredients" class="section">
    <h2>Ingredients (base + options)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Base (2–3 servings)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>1 cup <strong>plain yogurt</strong> (low-fat or Greek; lactose-free if needed)</li>
          <li>1 medium <strong>cucumber</strong>, grated (peel/seed if desired)</li>
          <li>2–3 tbsp <strong>water</strong> to thin (as preferred)</li>
          <li>¼–½ tsp <strong>roasted cumin powder</strong></li>
          <li>Pinch <strong>black pepper</strong></li>
          <li><strong>Salt</strong> to taste (or <em>no added salt</em> for BP/CKD)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Optional add-ins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>1–2 tbsp <strong>finely chopped mint</strong> or cilantro</li>
          <li>1–2 tbsp <strong>finely chopped onion</strong> (skip if reflux)</li>
          <li>A pinch <strong>chaat masala</strong> or lemon juice (for tang)</li>
          <li>½ tsp <strong>grated ginger</strong> (gentle warmth)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Hydration tip: lightly squeeze cucumber after grating if you prefer thicker raita.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <div class="grid">

        <p class="kicker">Mix (5–7 min)</p>

          <li>In a bowl, whisk yogurt with 2 tbsp water until smooth.</li>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>Greek yogurt</strong> for more protein and fullness.</li>
          <li>Keep portion to ~½–¾ cup; pair with non-starchy mains.</li>
          <li>Avoid added sugar; flavor with cumin, mint, lemon.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>No added salt</strong> or use a tiny pinch; rely on herbs/lemon.</li>
          <li>Choose low-fat yogurt to reduce saturated fat.</li>
          <li>Skip boondi and salty masalas.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Check protein/potassium targets with your clinician/dietitian.</li>
          <li>Use <strong>regular yogurt</strong> (not large Greek portions) if protein-restricted.</li>
          <li>Mind sodium—prefer no-salt raita and fresh spices.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skip raw onion/chili. Use mint + cumin + mild lemon only if tolerated.</li>
          <li>Keep portions modest; avoid eating close to bedtime.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Chew-soft tip:</strong> Peel and <em>finely grate</em> cucumber; whisk yogurt smooth for a gentle, spoonable texture.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Make-ahead & storage</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prep once</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Grate cucumber up to 4 hours ahead; lightly squeeze and chill.</li>
          <li>Whisk yogurt separately; mix just before serving for best texture.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Leftovers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Refrigerate airtight up to 24 hours. Stir before serving.</li>
          <li>If watery, add a spoon of yogurt to thicken.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tips -->
  <section id="tips" class="section">
    <h2>Texture & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Peel for softer texture; seed to reduce wateriness.</li>
          <li>Use pasteurized yogurt; keep cold—avoid leaving out &gt;2 hours.</li>
          <li>For extra calories (if unintended weight loss): stir in 1 tbsp thick yogurt or 1 tsp olive oil.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Food safety: Wash cucumber and herbs well. Use clean grater/board, especially if also preparing raw meats nearby (separate boards).
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Greek vs regular yogurt?</p>
        <p>Greek is thicker and higher in protein; regular is lighter. Thin either with a little water to your preferred texture.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lactose-free option?</p>
        <p>Use lactose-free or plant-based unsweetened yogurt; season well with herbs, cumin, and lemon.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I add spices?</p>
        <p>Yes—roasted cumin is classic. Add mild chili only if tolerated; avoid for reflux.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Serving ideas?</p>
        <p>With khichdi, pulao, paratha, grilled fish/chicken, or as a veggie dip.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
