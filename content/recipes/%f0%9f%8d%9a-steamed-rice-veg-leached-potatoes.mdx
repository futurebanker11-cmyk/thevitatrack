          <li>1 cup <strong>raw white rice</strong> (short/medium grain or basmati)</li>
          <li>1¼–1½ cups <strong>water</strong> for rice (see method)</li>
          <li>1½ cups mixed <strong>vegetables</strong>, bite-size (carrot, beans, zucchini, cauliflower)</li>
          <li>2 cups <strong>potatoes, peeled & cubed</strong> (for leaching)</li>
          <li>1–1½ tsp <strong>oil</strong> or 1 tsp ghee (optional, for veg gloss)</li>
          <li>½–¾ tsp <strong>salt</strong> total (optional; see swaps)</li>
          <li><strong>Lemon</strong> wedges and fresh <strong>cilantro</strong> to finish</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gentle seasonings (optional)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>cumin</strong> seeds or powder</li>
          <li>1 small <strong>bay leaf</strong> (for rice aroma)</li>
          <li>¼ tsp <strong>pepper</strong> or ½ tsp <strong>ginger</strong> paste (skip if triggers reflux)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">CKD note: the <em>leaching method</em> below helps reduce potassium in potatoes. Always follow your dietitian’s plan.</p>
      </div>

  <!-- Steps -->
  <section id="steps" class="section">
    <h2>Step-by-step</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">1) Leach potatoes (CKD-friendly)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li><strong>Peel & cube</strong> potatoes (2–3 cm). Rinse.</li>
          <li><strong>Soak</strong> in a large bowl of room-temp water for <strong>2–4 hours</strong>, changing the water halfway.</li>
          <li><strong>Parboil</strong> in fresh boiling water for <strong>5–10 minutes</strong> until just tender at edges. <strong>Drain and discard</strong> the water. Set aside to steam-dry.</li>
        </ol>
        <p class="small">Leaching reduces potassium; exact reduction varies. Use as directed by your clinician/dietitian.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">2) Steam rice & veg</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li><strong>Rinse</strong> rice 2–3 times until water runs clearer. Soak 15 minutes; drain.</li>
          <li>Cook by preferred method:
            <ul class="list">
              <li><em>Rice cooker/steamer:</em> rice + 1¼–1½ cups water + bay leaf (optional). Steam until tender; rest 5 minutes.</li>
              <li><em>Stovetop:</em> bring rice + water to boil, then cover on low 10–12 minutes; rest 5 minutes.</li>
            </ul>
          </li>
          <li><strong>Steam vegetables</strong> separately until soft but not mushy (5–8 minutes). For gloss, toss with 1 tsp oil and a pinch of cumin/pepper.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">3) Warm potatoes & assemble</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>In a non-stick pan, warm ½ tsp oil (optional). Add leached potatoes; sprinkle a pinch of salt (if using) and cumin. Toss 2–3 minutes to heat through.</li>
          <li>Serve a plate/bowl of <strong>steamed rice + mixed veg + leached potatoes</strong>. Finish with lemon and cilantro.</li>
        </ol>
        <div class="info small">Safety: keep handles turned in; avoid overcooking rice or veg for easier chewing and digestion.</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Condition swaps -->
  <section id="condition-swaps" class="section">
    <h2>Swaps by condition</h2>
    <div class="three">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Diabetes (Type 2)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Portion: ~½–¾ cup cooked rice + extra non-starchy veg.</li>
          <li>Consider <strong>half rice + half cauliflower rice</strong> or switch to small portion of brown rice if tolerated.</li>
          <li>Add protein (dal, tofu, grilled fish/chicken) to steady glucose.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & BP</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep salt minimal; build flavor with lemon, herbs, cumin.</li>
          <li>Use 1–1½ tsp oil total across veg/potatoes.</li>
          <li>Choose rice portions mindful of weight goals; add more veg volume.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">CKD (Kidney)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Use leached potatoes</strong> as above; avoid using the soaking/boiling water.</li>
          <li>Prefer lower-potassium veg (carrot, cabbage, cauliflower, zucchini) per your plan.</li>
          <li>Confirm rice portion and total potassium with your dietitian.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Reflux (GERD)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skip chili; go light on pepper/ginger if sensitive.</li>
          <li>Eat slowly; sit upright 30–45 minutes after meals.</li>
          <li>Keep portions comfortable; avoid very late dinners.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Texture tip:</strong> Cook rice slightly softer and dice veg small for easier chewing if dentures or jaw fatigue are issues.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Make-ahead -->
  <section id="make-ahead" class="section">
    <h2>Make-ahead, storage & reheating</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Prep ahead</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Leach potatoes earlier in the day; refrigerate up to 24 hours after parboil and drain.</li>
          <li>Wash/chop veg and store airtight 2–3 days.</li>
          <li>Rinse/soak rice just before cooking for best texture.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Store & reheat</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Refrigerate cooked rice/veg/potatoes separately up to 2 days.</li>
          <li>Reheat rice with a splash of water; cover to steam (microwave 60–90 sec or stovetop low heat).</li>
          <li>Warm potatoes in a non-stick pan 2–3 min; avoid repeated reheating.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers (FAQ)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does leaching remove nutrients?</p>
        <p>Some vitamins/minerals are reduced along with potassium. Use leaching only if recommended, and balance the day with other allowed foods.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Brown rice or white?</p>
        <p>Either can work. White rice is gentler for some stomachs; brown rice adds fiber but may feel heavier. Choose what fits your plan and chewing comfort.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I add spices?</p>
        <p>Yes—mild cumin, bay leaf, or a small ginger piece. Skip chili if reflux or CKD sodium/fluid goals discourage spicy, thirst-inducing meals.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Rice too dry or sticky?</p>
        <p>Dry: add 1–2 tbsp water while reheating, cover. Sticky: rinse rice well and use correct water ratio; rest covered 5 minutes before fluffing.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
