  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What lower back pain can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Most lower back pain improves within a few weeks. In seniors, it often involves <strong>muscle strain, facet joint arthritis, spinal stenosis,</strong> or <strong>osteoporosis-related fractures</strong>. Watch for the red flags below.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Goals:</strong> keep gently active, protect the back with good body mechanics, manage pain safely, and address contributing factors (weak core/hips, stiff hamstrings, prolonged sitting).</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>New trouble controlling <strong>bladder or bowels</strong>, numbness in the groin/saddle area</li>
        <li>Severe weakness in a leg or foot “foot-drop”</li>
        <li>Back pain with <strong>fever, chills, unexplained weight loss</strong>, or recent infection</li>
        <li>Pain after a <strong>fall</strong> or in known osteoporosis; sudden, severe mid-back pain</li>
        <li>Back pain with chest pain, shortness of breath, or abdominal “tearing” sensation</li>
        <li>History of cancer with new persistent back pain</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can indicate spinal cord compression, fracture, infection, vascular problems, or cancer.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes in later life</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Cause</th><th scope="col">Clues</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Muscle/ligament strain</strong></td>
          <td>After lifting/twisting; sore with movement, better at rest</td>
          <td>Usually improves in 1–3 weeks with gentle activity</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Facet joint osteoarthritis</strong></td>
          <td>Achy low back; worse on extension/standing long; better with sitting</td>
          <td>Common with aging; posture and core/hip strength help</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Spinal stenosis</strong></td>
          <td>Back/leg pain, numbness with walking; relief when leaning forward or sitting</td>
          <td>“Shopping cart sign”; consider PT, flexion-biased exercises</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Disc degeneration/herniation</strong></td>
          <td>Back +/- leg pain (sciatica), worse with bending/coughing</td>
          <td>Most improve without surgery; watch for weakness/red flags</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Compression fracture</strong></td>
          <td>Sudden severe mid/low back pain after minor strain/fall</td>
          <td>More likely with osteoporosis or steroid use</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Referred pain</strong></td>
          <td>From kidneys, aorta, pancreas, pelvis</td>
          <td>Consider if back exam doesn’t match pain</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Self-care (first 48–72 hours)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Activity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Avoid bed rest; take short walks every 2–3 hours while awake</li>
          <li>Use log-roll technique to get in/out of bed; avoid heavy lifting</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Comfort</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Heat or cold 15–20 minutes, up to a few times daily</li>
          <li>Pillow support: side-lying with pillow between knees, or supine with pillow under knees</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Call soon</strong> if pain doesn’t start easing in a week, or limits basic activities.
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Breathing</strong> with longer exhale and gentle shoulder rolls can reduce muscle guarding.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Posture -->
  <section id="posture" class="section">
    <h2>Posture & ergonomics</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sit smarter</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Feet flat, hips/knees ~90°, small towel roll at low back</li>
          <li>Stand and stretch hourly: back extension, shoulder blades down/back</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lift & carry</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep load close, bend at hips/knees, exhale as you lift</li>
          <li>Avoid twisting while carrying; pivot feet instead</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Movement Plan -->
  <section id="movement" class="section">
    <h2>Gentle movement plan</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily (if comfortable)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Pelvic tilts</strong> x10</li>
          <li><strong>Knee-to-chest (single)</strong> hold 5–10 sec each side x5</li>
          <li><strong>Hamstring stretch (seated)</strong> 10–15 sec x3 each</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Most days</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Walking</strong> 10–20 minutes; break into short bouts if needed</li>
          <li><strong>Core/hip</strong>: mini bridges, side steps with band (as tolerated)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        Stop and seek advice if pain shoots down the leg with numbness/weakness.
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Progress gradually—many backs prefer frequent, small sessions to long ones.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine cautions & options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Cautions in seniors</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>NSAIDs</strong> (ibuprofen/naproxen): stomach bleed, kidney risk, BP effects—use only if clinician approves</li>
          <li><strong>Muscle relaxants</strong> (e.g., cyclobenzaprine): sedation, falls—often avoided</li>
          <li><strong>Opioids</strong>: constipation, confusion, falls—reserve for short, carefully supervised use</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Often-used options</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Acetaminophen</strong> within safe daily limits (consider total from all products)</li>
          <li>Topicals: <strong>lidocaine patches</strong>, <strong>NSAID gel</strong> (per clinician), heat rubs</li>
          <li>For nerve-type pain: options like duloxetine/gabapentin may be considered</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small">Medicine plans depend on kidney/heart history, other meds, and cause of pain.</p>
  </section>

          <td>Restore mobility, strength, mechanics</td>

    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">

          <li>Morning stiffness vs. evening fatigue pattern</li>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Bed rest or movement?</p>
        <p>Short gentle walks and frequent position changes beat bed rest for recovery.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need an MRI right away?</p>
        <p>Not usually unless red flags or persistent leg weakness/severe symptoms. Many cases improve with conservative care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What pillow setup helps?</p>
        <p>Side-lying with pillow between knees, or on back with pillow under knees to reduce lumbar strain.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heat or ice?</p>
        <p>Either can help—use what feels better. 15–20 minutes per session, skin protected.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
