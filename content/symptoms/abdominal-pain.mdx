  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What abdominal pain can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Abdominal pain ranges from mild cramps to severe, sudden pain. In older adults, symptoms can be subtle even with serious causes. Pay attention to <strong>new, worsening, or persistent</strong> pain and any warning signs below.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Common sources:</strong> stomach/intestinal issues (reflux, gastritis, constipation, infection), gallbladder, pancreas, urinary tract, kidney stones, hernias, blood vessel problems, medications, or referred pain from chest or back.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Severe, sudden pain (“worst ever”), or pain with a rigid/board-like belly</li>
        <li>Pain with chest pain, shortness of breath, sweating, or fainting</li>
        <li>Vomiting blood, black/tarry stools, or bright-red blood in stool</li>
        <li>Fever with shaking chills, or persistent vomiting preventing fluids</li>
        <li>Yellow skin/eyes, dark urine with upper-right pain (possible bile duct blockage)</li>
        <li>New severe pain while on blood thinners, or after a fall/trauma</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can signal emergencies like perforation, obstruction, heart or vascular problems, severe infection, or bleeding.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Pain map: common causes by area</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Area</th><th scope="col">More likely causes</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Upper-right (RUQ)</strong></td>
          <td>Gallstones/biliary colic, cholecystitis, liver congestion, shingles (before rash)</td>
          <td>Pain after fatty meals; fever or jaundice needs urgent care</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Upper-middle (epigastric)</strong></td>
          <td>GERD/gastritis, ulcer, pancreatitis, heart-related pain</td>
          <td>Worse after NSAIDs/alcohol; chest-like pain → rule out heart</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Upper-left (LUQ)</strong></td>
          <td>Gastritis/ulcer, enlarged spleen, constipation/gas</td>
          <td>Fullness after small meals; check for infection history</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Right-lower (RLQ)</strong></td>
          <td>Appendicitis, constipation, hernia, kidney stone</td>
          <td>Fever, loss of appetite, rebound tenderness → urgent</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Mid-lower (suprapubic)</strong></td>
          <td>UTI, bladder issues, constipation</td>
          <td>Burning/urgency/frequency → test urine</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Left-lower (LLQ)</strong></td>
          <td>Diverticulitis, constipation, gas, hernia</td>
          <td>Fever with steady LLQ pain → seek timely care</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Diffuse/widespread</strong></td>
          <td>Gastroenteritis, bowel obstruction/ileus, ischemia, medications, electrolyte issues</td>
          <td>Severe bloating with no gas/stool passage → urgent assessment</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Self-care that’s usually safe</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Short trial at home (if no red-flags)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rest; small sips of water or oral rehydration solution</li>
          <li>Simple foods as tolerated: bananas, rice, applesauce, toast, yogurt</li>
          <li>Warm compress/heating pad (low setting) 10–15 minutes at a time</li>
          <li>For gas/constipation causes: gentle walking; fiber increase gradually if not obstructed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Call your clinician soon if</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pain lasts >24–48 hours, keeps returning, or disrupts sleep/appetite</li>
          <li>Unintended weight loss, anemia, or pain that wakes you at night</li>
          <li>New pain with a history of ulcers, gallstones, kidney stones, or vascular disease</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Avoid routine NSAIDs</strong> (ibuprofen, naproxen) if ulcer/bleeding risk, kidney disease, or on blood thinners—ask first</li>
          <li>Acetaminophen can help pain/fever (mind total daily dose from all products)</li>
          <li>Antacids or acid-reducers may help upper-stomach burning; persistent symptoms need evaluation</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Constipation meds:</strong> overuse of stimulant laxatives may cramp; osmotic options (PEG) are gentler. Suspected obstruction → do not use laxatives—seek care.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Food & hydration tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Helpful choices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Small, frequent meals; avoid large, heavy, or high-fat meals during flares</li>
          <li>Low-acid, low-spice options if reflux/ulcer symptoms</li>
          <li>Fiber from oats, fruits, vegetables; add slowly and drink fluids</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Limit/avoid during pain</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Alcohol, very fatty or fried foods, large amounts of caffeine</li>
          <li>For lactose sensitivity: try lactose-free dairy or alternatives</li>
          <li>During diarrhea: hold raw roughage, resume gradually as symptoms improve</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

          <td><strong>Basic tests</strong></td>

    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Start time, location, and character (crampy, burning, sharp)</li>
          <li>What worsens/relieves it (meals, position, bowel movement, medicines)</li>
          <li>Fever, vomiting, stool/urine changes, weight change</li>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When is stomach pain an emergency?</p>
        <p>Severe, sudden pain; rigid abdomen; blood in vomit or stool; fever with chills; or pain with chest symptoms needs urgent care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is constipation a common cause?</p>
        <p>Yes—especially with low fiber/fluids or new medicines (iron, some pain pills). New severe bloating without gas/stool is concerning; seek care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can gas pain mimic heart issues?</p>
        <p>Upper abdominal pain can overlap with heart symptoms. If unsure—or if pain spreads to chest, jaw, arm—treat as possible heart problem.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What helps nausea?</p>
        <p>Small sips of clear fluids, ginger tea, rest. Persistent vomiting or dehydration signs need assessment.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
