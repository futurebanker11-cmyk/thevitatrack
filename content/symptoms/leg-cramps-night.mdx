  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What night cramps can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Sudden, painful tightening of calf or foot muscles that wakes you from sleep is common with aging. Often benign, it can be linked to <strong>dehydration</strong>, <strong>overuse</strong>, <strong>electrolyte shifts</strong>, <strong>medicines</strong>, or nerve/circulation issues. Pattern and triggers guide the plan.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p>Good news: Most people improve with <strong>stretching before bed</strong>, smart fluids, footwear, and reviewing medicines.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>One leg becomes <strong>swollen, red, warm, and painful</strong> (possible blood clot)</li>
        <li>Severe cramp with <strong>numbness/weakness</strong>, foot drop, or new incontinence</li>
        <li>Painful, tight leg after <strong>injury, immobility, surgery, or long travel</strong></li>
        <li>Cramp plus <strong>chest pain, shortness of breath, or fainting</strong></li>
        <li>Skin becomes <strong>pale/cold</strong> with lost pulses (urgent circulation issue)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can signal circulation or nerve emergencies. Seek urgent care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & contributors</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Area</th><th scope="col">Examples</th><th scope="col">Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Fluids & electrolytes</strong></td>
          <td>Dehydration; low magnesium/potassium/calcium (rare without other illness)</td>
          <td>Darker urine, daytime heat/illness, diuretics, vomiting/diarrhea</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Muscle overuse</strong></td>
          <td>New walking program, hills, poor shoe support</td>
          <td>Sore calves, worse after active days</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Nerves & circulation</strong></td>
          <td>Peripheral neuropathy, spinal stenosis, peripheral artery disease, varicose veins</td>
          <td>Numbness/tingling, cramp with walking relieved by rest or leg elevation</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medicines</strong></td>
          <td>Diuretics, some statins, beta-agonists, high-dose thyroid meds, quinine (not recommended)</td>
          <td>New/worsened cramps after a med change</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Other</strong></td>
          <td>Pregnancy history, thyroid/kidney issues, low activity, prolonged sitting</td>
          <td>Cramp at night or with pointing toes</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- First steps -->
  <section id="first-steps" class="section">
    <h2>First steps & immediate relief</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When a cramp strikes</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Gently stretch</strong> the calf: pull toes toward your nose (use a towel strap if needed).</li>
          <li><strong>Heel-walk</strong> a few steps or press foot flat to the floor.</li>
          <li>Massage the muscle; apply <strong>warmth</strong> (heating pad) for 5–10 minutes.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">After it eases</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sip water; if sweating/ill, consider an oral rehydration solution.</li>
          <li>Do a <strong>light repeat stretch</strong> before going back to bed.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Call your clinician</strong> if cramps are frequent, severe, new, or if you have weakness, numbness, or leg swelling/redness.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Stretch routine -->
  <section id="stretch" class="section">
    <h2>Nightly stretch routine</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Calf wall stretch (knee straight)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hands on wall, one foot back with heel down, knee <strong>straight</strong>. Lean forward until you feel a stretch in the back calf.</li>
          <li>Hold 20–30 sec, relax; repeat 3 times each side.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Soleus stretch (knee bent)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Same stance, but bend the back knee slightly while keeping heel down.</li>
          <li>Hold 20–30 sec, repeat 3 times each side.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Seated strap stretch</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sit with leg extended. Loop a towel/strap over the ball of your foot.</li>
          <li>Gently pull toes toward you; hold 20–30 sec x 3.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Do the sequence <strong>even on days without cramps</strong>, ideally after a short evening walk or warm shower.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Hydration -->
  <section id="fluids" class="section">
    <h2>Hydration & electrolytes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Drink steadily in daytime; taper fluids 2–3 hours before bed to limit night trips.</li>
          <li>Hot weather/illness: add an <strong>oral rehydration solution</strong> or lightly salted soup if allowed.</li>
          <li>Balanced meals with vegetables, legumes, dairy/yogurt, nuts, and fruits support electrolytes naturally.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Magnesium or potassium supplements</strong> only with clinician guidance—can interact with kidney or heart conditions and medicines.</li>
          <li><strong>Quinine</strong> is generally avoided due to side effects and risks.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicine factors -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine factors</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">May contribute</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Diuretics</strong> (water pills): shift electrolytes and fluids.</li>
          <li><strong>Statins</strong>: rarely cause muscle symptoms—report persistent pain/weakness.</li>
          <li>High-dose thyroid meds; beta-agonists; some antidepressants.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Do <strong>not</strong> stop medicines on your own. Ask about timing, dose, or alternatives if cramps started after a change.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevent" class="section">
    <h2>Prevention during the day</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Walk or do gentle ankle pumps hourly if you sit a lot.</li>
          <li>Choose <strong>supportive footwear</strong> with adequate arch and heel support.</li>
          <li>Avoid prolonged toe-pointing (e.g., tucked-in sheets that force feet down).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Consider <strong>evening calf massage</strong> and warm shower.</li>
          <li>If you have leg swelling, ask about brief evening leg elevation or compression (if appropriate).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Time of cramps, which leg/muscle, severity (0–10), duration</li>
          <li>Day’s activity, footwear, fluids, heat exposure</li>
          <li>New medicines or dose changes</li>
          <li>What relieved it (stretch, heat, fluids) and how fast</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        A 2–3 week diary clarifies triggers and shows whether the <strong>nightly stretch routine</strong> is working.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are night cramps from low magnesium?</p>
        <p>Sometimes, but not always. Supplements can interact with kidney/heart issues—ask your clinician before using.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do bananas fix cramps?</p>
        <p>Potassium foods help overall balance, but cramps have many causes. A complete plan (stretching + fluids + footwear + med review) works best.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I use quinine?</p>
        <p>Generally avoided due to risks (heart/blood side effects). Safer options are preferred.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Why do they happen at night?</p>
        <p>Muscles rest in a shortened position and small fluid/electrolyte shifts occur. Gentle <em>pre-bed stretching</em> helps prevent tightening.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
