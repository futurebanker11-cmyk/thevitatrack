  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What dark urine can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Urine normally ranges from pale yellow to amber. <strong>Dark urine</strong> often means <strong>dehydration</strong>, but can also signal <strong>medication/food effects</strong>, <strong>liver or bile duct problems</strong> (tea/cola color), <strong>blood</strong> (red, pink, cola), or <strong>muscle breakdown</strong> (rhabdomyolysis—cola/brown).</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Look for:</strong> jaundice (yellow skin/eyes), pale/clay-colored stools, fever, pain with urination, back/flank pain, new medicines, heavy exercise/heat exposure, or dark urine despite drinking well.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Dark/cola-colored urine with <strong>severe muscle pain/weakness</strong>, confusion, or after a fall/long lie/heat exposure (possible rhabdomyolysis)</li>
        <li><strong>Unable to urinate</strong>, very low output, or severe dehydration (dizziness, confusion, very dry mouth)</li>
        <li>Dark urine with <strong>yellow skin/eyes</strong>, <strong>pale stools</strong>, or severe abdominal pain/fever</li>
        <li>Visible blood or <strong>blood clots</strong> in urine, especially with back/flank pain or fever</li>
        <li>Dark urine in someone on high-risk meds (e.g., statin + new antibiotic, high-dose diuretics) with new weakness or kidney pain</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can indicate muscle breakdown with kidney risk, bile duct blockage or hepatitis, stones/infection, or significant dehydration.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="colors" class="section">
    <h2>Color guide & common causes</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Urine color guide">

        <tr><th>Color/appearance</th><th>More likely causes</th><th>Clues</th></tr>

        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dark yellow/amber</strong></td>
          <td>Dehydration, diuretics, high-sweat days</td>
          <td>Improves with fluids; urine concentrated, strong smell</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Orange</strong></td>
          <td>Dehydration; meds like <em>phenazopyridine</em>, <em>rifampin</em>, some laxatives/vitamins</td>
          <td>Check new meds; may stain clothing</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Tea/cola brown</strong></td>
          <td>Bilirubin from liver/bile problems; severe dehydration; rhabdomyolysis</td>
          <td>Jaundice, pale stools, RUQ pain/fever; or muscle pain/weakness after exertion/fall</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Red/pink</strong></td>
          <td>Blood (stones, UTI, prostate issues, tumors); foods (beets), dyes</td>

        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Brown/black</strong></td>
          <td>Old blood, certain meds (metronidazole), myoglobin</td>
          <td>Often with other symptoms—seek evaluation</td>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Self-care that’s usually safe</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If no red flags</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Drink fluids steadily (water, oral rehydration, broths) aiming for <strong>pale-yellow</strong> urine</li>
          <li>Limit alcohol and very caffeinated drinks until color improves</li>
          <li>Review new foods (beets, blackberries) and vitamins (B-complex, carotene)</li>
          <li>Monitor for pain, fever, burning, or jaundice—seek care if present</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>UTI clues:</strong> burning, urgency, frequency, fever, new confusion. <em>Dark urine alone</em> doesn’t prove infection—testing helps decide.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medication & supplement notes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can darken urine (benign but notable)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Phenazopyridine</strong> (orange), <strong>rifampin</strong> (orange–red), <strong>metronidazole</strong> (brown), <strong>chloroquine</strong>, some laxatives, multivitamins/B-complex</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">When to review urgently</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>On a <strong>statin</strong> with new muscle pain/weakness or cola-colored urine</li>
          <li>Recent med change plus dark urine, jaundice, abdominal pain, or severe fatigue</li>
          <li>High-dose diuretics with dizziness, low output, or faintness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet & hydration -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Hydration & diet tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sip ~½ cup every 20–30 minutes while awake unless on fluid restriction</li>
          <li>Use <strong>oral rehydration</strong> if sweating/diarrhea (electrolytes help absorption)</li>
          <li>Include watery foods: soups, yogurt, fruits (melons, citrus)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Fluid limits:</strong> If you have heart or kidney disease with a prescribed fluid limit, follow that plan and call your clinician if urine stays dark.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Clinical evaluation">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Purpose</th><th>Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Identify causes & risks</td>
          <td>Hydration, meds/supplements, jaundice, muscle pain, fever, urinary symptoms</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Urinalysis ± culture</strong></td>
          <td>Check blood, infection, myoglobin</td>
          <td>RBCs, nitrites/leukocytes, protein, specific gravity, myoglobin/hemoglobin distinctions</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Blood tests</strong></td>
          <td>Assess organs & muscle</td>
          <td>Electrolytes, kidney function (BUN/creatinine), liver panel & bilirubin, CK (muscle), CBC</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging</strong></td>
          <td>Look for obstruction or stones</td>
          <td>Ultrasound or CT if pain, fever, or persistent hematuria</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Treatment targets the cause—fluids/electrolytes, antibiotics for infection, stone/obstruction care, statin/medication adjustments, or liver/bile duct management.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Urine color & <strong>frequency</strong>; any visible blood or clots</li>
          <li><strong>Fluid intake</strong> (cups/day) and sweating/heat exposure</li>
          <li>New meds/supplements in last 2–3 weeks</li>
          <li>Fever, pain with urination, back/flank pain, jaundice, muscle pain/weakness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Bring this log (and photos if helpful) to appointments—speeds diagnosis and reduces unnecessary tests.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support with dignity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Offer fluids the person enjoys; place water within reach; consider a timed reminder</li>
          <li>Watch for new confusion, decreased urination, fever, or jaundice</li>
          <li>Maintain an up-to-date medication/supplement list (share at visits)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call the clinician for:</strong> persistent dark urine despite fluids, cola-colored urine with muscle symptoms, visible blood/clots, fever/back pain, or jaundice.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How dark is “too dark”?</p>
        <p>If urine stays darker than <strong>amber</strong> after steady fluids, or turns tea/cola colored—get medical advice.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can vitamins change urine color?</p>
        <p>Yes—B-complex can make bright yellow; some meds (phenazopyridine, rifampin) make it orange/red. New jaundice or pain still needs evaluation.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is red urine always blood?</p>
        <p>No—foods (beets) and dyes can mimic. But any <strong>red or bloody</strong> urine, especially with pain/fever, should be checked.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What is rhabdomyolysis?</p>
        <p>Muscle breakdown causing <strong>cola-colored</strong> urine, muscle pain/weakness, and kidney risk—needs urgent care, especially after a fall, heat, or new drug interactions.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
