  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is edema?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Swelling of the feet/ankles/lower legs from extra fluid in the tissues. Shoes feel tight, socks leave marks, and skin may look shiny. Edema is a symptom — the cause can be the <strong>veins, heart, kidneys, liver,</strong> medicines, or simple dependent swelling from sitting long hours.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Why it matters:</strong> Untreated causes may increase fall risk, skin breakdown, or infection (cellulitis). The right plan often improves comfort quickly.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>Urgent warning signs</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Shortness of breath</strong>, chest pain, rapid weight gain (≥2 kg in 2–3 days)</li>
        <li><strong>One-sided</strong> painful swelling, redness, or warmth (possible blood clot)</li>
        <li>Skin blisters, weeping fluid, fever, or spreading redness</li>
        <li>Confusion, very low urine, or severe abdominal swelling</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies — seek urgent care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after Red flags) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & patterns</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Pattern</th><th scope="col">Clues</th><th scope="col">Often related to</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Both feet/ankles</strong>, worse by evening</td>
          <td>Improves overnight, sock marks</td>
          <td>Venous insufficiency, long sitting, heat</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Both legs + breathlessness</strong>, weight gain</td>
          <td>Swelling up to calves/thighs, fatigue</td>
          <td>Heart failure fluid overload</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>One-sided</strong>, tender/warm</td>
          <td>Calf pain, recent immobility or surgery</td>
          <td>Deep vein thrombosis (DVT)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Puffy face + legs</strong>, low urine</td>
          <td>Foamy urine, morning swelling</td>
          <td>Kidney disease or nephrotic syndrome</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Abdomen + legs</strong>, thin arms</td>
          <td>Jaundice or easy bruising</td>
          <td>Liver disease/low albumin</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Non-pitting</strong>, firm skin</td>
          <td>Thickened skin, usually chronic</td>
          <td>Lymphedema</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Quick checks -->
  <section id="checks" class="section">
    <h2>Quick self-checks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Track weight & swelling</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Weigh daily on waking after bathroom; log changes</li>
          <li>Note shoe fit and sock marks morning vs evening</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Press test</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Press shin/top of foot for 5 seconds — a lasting dent = <strong>pitting</strong> edema</li>
          <li>If skin feels firm with little dent, think <strong>lymphedema</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Sudden changes, pain, or one-sided swelling → get medical advice promptly.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Salt & fluid -->
  <section id="salt-fluid" class="section">
    <h2>Salt & fluids</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Limit <strong>added salt</strong> and high-sodium foods (soups, pickles, chips, processed meats)</li>
          <li>Flavor with lemon, herbs, pepper instead of salt</li>
          <li>Spread fluids through the day; avoid large late-evening drinks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        If you have <strong>heart failure or kidney disease</strong>, follow your personalized <strong>fluid and sodium</strong> plan exactly. Do not change salt/fluids without clinician guidance.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after Salt & fluid) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Movement & elevation -->
  <section id="move-elevate" class="section">
    <h2>Movement & elevation</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Circulation boosters</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Short <strong>5–10 minute walks</strong> after meals</li>
          <li>Hourly <strong>ankle pumps</strong> and <strong>calf raises</strong></li>
          <li>Don’t sit with legs down for long; stand/stretch breaks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Leg elevation</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Feet up to heart level for 15–20 minutes, 2–3×/day</li>
          <li>At night: a small pillow under calves (not knees) can help</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Combine <strong>low-salt eating + walking + elevation</strong> for the biggest day-to-day impact.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Compression & footwear -->
  <section id="compression" class="section">
    <h2>Compression & footwear</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Compression basics</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Graduated stockings</strong> 15–20 mmHg for mild swelling; 20–30 mmHg if advised</li>
          <li>Put on in the morning before swelling builds</li>
          <li>Consider <strong>zippered</strong> or <strong>open-toe</strong> styles for easier use</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Shoes & skin care</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Roomy, supportive shoes; avoid tight straps</li>
          <li>Moisturize dry skin; inspect daily for cracks or sores</li>
          <li>Call if skin weeps fluid or shows spreading redness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines to review</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Group</th><th scope="col">Examples</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>BP medicines</td>
          <td><strong>Amlodipine</strong> and other dihydropyridine calcium-channel blockers</td>
          <td>Can cause ankle swelling; dosing/time changes may help</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Anti-inflammatories</td>
          <td>NSAIDs (e.g., ibuprofen, diclofenac)</td>
          <td>May worsen fluid retention and kidneys</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Hormonal/other</td>
          <td>Steroids, thiazolidinediones (pioglitazone), some antidepressants</td>
          <td>Review risk/benefit with clinician</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Never stop medicines on your own — ask for a medication review and written plan.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after Medicines) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>How it’s checked</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Clinic evaluation</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>History, exam, <strong>pitting vs non-pitting</strong>, skin checks</li>
          <li>Vitals, weight trend, oxygen level if breathless</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Tests (as needed)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Bloods: kidney/liver, electrolytes, thyroid, albumin</li>
          <li>Urine protein; <strong>BNP</strong> if heart failure suspected</li>
          <li>Leg ultrasound for suspected <strong>DVT</strong></li>
          <li>Echo/chest X-ray if cardiac cause suspected</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lifestyle + supports</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Low-salt eating, walking plan, scheduled elevation</li>
          <li>Compression stockings; leg/ankle exercises</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines (when indicated)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Diuretics</strong> for fluid overload (heart, kidney, liver) — monitored</li>
          <li>Adjust or switch meds that cause swelling</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        The best plan targets the cause. Keep a simple <strong>weight + swelling log</strong> to guide follow-ups.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- When to call -->
  <section id="when-to-call" class="section">
    <h2>When to contact your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>New or rapidly worsening swelling</li>
        <li>One-sided swelling, calf pain, or warmth</li>
        <li>Breathlessness, chest pain, or sudden weight gain</li>
        <li>Skin breakdown, weeping fluid, or signs of infection</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are diuretics always needed?</p>
        <p>No. They help in fluid overload (e.g., heart failure) but aren’t for every cause. Many improve with salt control, movement, and compression.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What time to put on compression?</p>
        <p>In the morning before swelling builds. Take breaks if uncomfortable and replace worn-out pairs.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can hot weather worsen swelling?</p>
        <p>Yes — veins relax in heat. Use elevation, indoor cool breaks, and extra ankle pumps.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Which salt swaps help?</p>
        <p>Lemon, herbs, spices, pepper, garlic, vinegar. Rinse canned foods and pick lower-sodium labels.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
