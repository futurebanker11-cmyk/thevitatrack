  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What nosebleeds can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Most nosebleeds in adults come from fragile vessels in the <strong>front</strong> of the nose and stop with pressure. In seniors, dryness, blood thinners, high blood pressure, and nose picking/blowing are common triggers. A minority come from the <strong>back</strong> of the nose and bleed more heavily.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Typical companions:</strong> nasal dryness/crusting, colds/allergies, recent COVID/upper-respiratory infection, deviated septum, or trauma (even minor).</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD ===== -->
  

  <!-- Stop now -->
  <section id="stop" class="section">
    <h2>How to stop a nosebleed (now)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do this immediately</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li><strong>Sit up, lean forward</strong> slightly—<em>don’t</em> lie back (prevents swallowing blood).</li>
          <li><strong>Pinch the soft part</strong> of the nose (below the bone) with thumb and index finger.</li>
          <li><strong>Hold constant pressure for 10–15 minutes</strong> by the clock—no peeking. Breathe through your mouth.</li>
          <li>If available, spray <strong>oxymetazoline</strong> (decongestant) 2–3 sprays in the bleeding side before pinching.</li>
          <li>After 10–15 minutes, gently release. If still bleeding, repeat once.</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">What to avoid</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>No tilting head back or packing with tissue (sticks to clot)</li>
          <li>No hot drinks, smoking, heavy lifting, or blowing the nose for 24–48 hours</li>
          <li>Avoid aspirin/NSAIDs unless prescribed for another condition (see cautions)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small">This guide is educational—not a diagnosis. Seek care for the red-flags below.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Bleeding that <strong>doesn’t stop after 20 minutes</strong> of firm pressure (twice)</li>
        <li>Heavy bleeding down the throat or coughing up large clots</li>
        <li>Nosebleed after <strong>head/facial injury</strong>, or suspected broken nose</li>
        <li>On <strong>blood thinners</strong> (warfarin, apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, heparin) with ongoing bleeding</li>
        <li>Significant weakness, lightheadedness, chest pain, or shortness of breath</li>
        <li>Frequent bleeds with <strong>easy bruising</strong> or gum bleeding (possible bleeding disorder)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may require cautery, packing, reversal of anticoagulation, or treatment of a posterior source.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Common causes & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Nosebleed causes and clues">

        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dry air, winter, CPAP/oxygen</strong></td>
          <td>Nasal dryness/cracking (anterior)</td>
          <td>Humidifier, saline spray/gel, petroleum-free emollient</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Blood thinners/antiplatelets</strong></td>
          <td>Prolonged/recurrent bleeding</td>
          <td>Warfarin/DOACs, aspirin, clopidogrel—review dosing and interactions</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Allergies/infection</strong></td>
          <td>Inflamed mucosa, frequent blowing</td>
          <td>Saline, gentle antihistamine/steroid sprays (rinse mouth after steroids)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>High blood pressure episode</strong></td>
          <td>More brisk bleeding</td>
          <td>Control BP; seek care if repeated spikes or other symptoms</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Unilateral blockage, foul discharge, or mass</strong></td>
          <td>Polyp, foreign body, rarely tumor</td>
          <td>ENT exam/endoscopy recommended</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hereditary bleeding disorder or liver disease</strong></td>
          <td>Easy bleeding at multiple sites</td>
          <td>Ask family history; may need labs and specialist care</td>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Aftercare & self-care</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">First 48 hours (if bleeding has stopped)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Apply a small amount of <strong>saline gel</strong> or petroleum-free emollient to front of nostrils 2–3×/day</li>
          <li>Use <strong>saline sprays</strong> every few hours; run a <strong>cool-mist humidifier</strong> at night</li>
          <li>Sleep with head slightly elevated; avoid hot showers, spicy foods, alcohol</li>
          <li>If you must sneeze, do it with mouth open; avoid nose blowing</li>
          <li>For small re-bleed: oxymetazoline spray + firm pinch 10–15 min</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Call your clinician soon if</strong> nosebleeds are frequent (e.g., ≥3/week), you see scabs/crusts that won’t heal, have new one-sided blockage, or you are on anticoagulants with recurrent bleeds.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Anticoagulants/antiplatelets:</strong> never stop on your own. Ask about dose checks (e.g., INR for warfarin), interactions, or timing adjustments.</li>
          <li><strong>NSAIDs/aspirin:</strong> can worsen bleeding. Avoid for pain unless prescribed for your heart/stroke plan.</li>
          <li><strong>Nasal steroid sprays:</strong> helpful for allergies, but aim nozzle <em>outward</em> (away from septum) and use lowest effective dose.</li>
          <li><strong>Decongestant sprays:</strong> Oxymetazoline can help acutely; don’t use >3 days in a row to avoid rebound congestion.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Keep an up-to-date <strong>medicine list</strong> (including OTCs and supplements like fish oil, ginkgo) and bring it to visits.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Prevention tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Daily <strong>saline spray</strong> or gel; bedside <strong>humidifier</strong> in dry seasons</li>
          <li>Trim nails; avoid picking; blow nose gently one side at a time</li>
          <li>Treat allergies; manage blood pressure</li>
          <li>Protect nose from trauma (mask/guard if on CPAP or during activities)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Small habits (saline + humidity + gentle technique) prevent most recurrences.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians/ENT may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">Purpose</th><th scope="col">Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Focused exam</strong></td>
          <td>Find the source</td>
          <td>Anterior septum vs posterior; blood pressure check; look for crusts, telangiectasias</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Labs (selected)</strong></td>
          <td>Assess bleeding risk</td>
          <td>CBC, INR/PTT if on warfarin or bleeding concerns; liver tests if indicated</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Cautery</strong></td>
          <td>Seal bleeding vessel</td>
          <td>Silver nitrate for anterior sites; topical anesthetic first</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Nasal packing</strong></td>
          <td>Control persistent bleed</td>
          <td>Anterior/posterior packs or balloons; prophylactic antibiotics sometimes used with posterior packs</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Endoscopy / imaging</strong></td>
          <td>Evaluate recurrent/atypical cases</td>
          <td>Nasal endoscopy; CT if mass/structural concern</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Definitive therapy</strong></td>
          <td>Prevent recurrence</td>
          <td>Arterial ligation/embolization for severe posterior epistaxis; adjust anticoagulation plan</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Treatment depends on bleed location, severity, and your medicines/health conditions.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Date/time, side (left/right/both), and estimated duration</li>
          <li>Triggers (dry air, allergy flare, nose blowing, trauma)</li>
          <li>Blood thinner/aspirin use and doses that day</li>
          <li>Blood pressure at the time (if known)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Bring this log to appointments—helps decide on cautery, packing, or med adjustments.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I tilt my head back?</p>
        <p>No. Sit up and lean forward while pinching the soft nose for 10–15 minutes.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if I’m on a blood thinner?</p>
        <p>Use the same pressure steps and seek care sooner if bleeding persists. Do <em>not</em> stop your medicine without guidance.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Why does it taste like blood but I don’t see it?</p>
        <p>Posterior bleeds can drain backward into the throat. If heavy or persistent, seek urgent care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I prevent the next one?</p>
        <p>Daily saline spray/gel, humidifier, gentle nose care, manage allergies/BP, and avoid picking/blowing hard.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
