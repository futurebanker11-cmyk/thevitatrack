  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>Why seniors itch more</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> With age, skin makes less oil and the barrier thins, so water escapes more easily. Dry air, long hot showers, soaps, and some medicines worsen the dryness. Itching can also signal medical issues such as <strong>thyroid disease, diabetes, chronic kidney or liver problems, iron deficiency,</strong> or <strong>infection/infestation</strong> like scabies.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Good news:</strong> Most itching improves with the right <em>routine</em> (gentle cleansing + thick moisturizer within 3 minutes of bathing) and by removing triggers.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency / urgent care</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Hives with lip/tongue swelling</strong>, wheeze, or trouble breathing</li>
        <li>Widespread rash + <strong>fever</strong>, painful skin, or blisters</li>
        <li>Yellow eyes/skin (<strong>jaundice</strong>) or very dark urine with severe itching</li>
        <li>Rapidly spreading redness, warmth, pus, or <strong>signs of skin infection</strong></li>
        <li>Unexplained <strong>weight loss, night sweats,</strong> or persistent whole-body itch</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Possible anaphylaxis, severe drug reaction, cellulitis, liver blockage, or systemic illness.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Home care -->
  <section id="home-care" class="section">
    <h2>Home care that actually helps</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Moisturizers (pick at least one)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Ointments:</strong> petrolatum jelly, mineral oil blends — most sealing</li>
          <li><strong>Creams with ceramides</strong> or <strong>urea 5–10%</strong> / <strong>lactic acid</strong> for very dry areas (avoid open skin)</li>
          <li>Fragrance-free, dye-free only; tub or pump better than thin lotions</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Itch relief</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Short course of <strong>1% hydrocortisone</strong> for small, inflamed patches (up to 7 days)</li>
          <li><strong>Menthol or pramoxine</strong> lotions can cool/soothe</li>
          <li>Cold compress 10 minutes; keep <strong>nails short</strong>; cotton gloves at night if scratching</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Avoid triggers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Limit hot water; switch to <strong>lukewarm</strong> 5–10 minute showers</li>
          <li>Fragrance-free soap only (or gentle cleanser just for armpits/groin/feet)</li>
          <li>Soft cotton layers; avoid scratchy wool; use fragrance-free laundry detergent</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">Environment & routines</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Humidifier in bedroom (target 40–50%)</li>
          <li>Drink water steadily in the day; modest evening fluids if nocturia</li>
          <li>After bathing, <strong>pat dry</strong> and apply thick moisturizer within <strong>3 minutes</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Bath routine -->
  <section id="bath" class="section">
    <h2>Bath & moisturizer routine (step-by-step)</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">What to do</th><th scope="col">Why it helps</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>1) Short, warm shower</strong></td>
          <td>5–10 minutes, gentle cleanser, no harsh scrubbing</td>
          <td>Prevents oil loss and irritation</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>2) Pat, don’t rub</strong></td>
          <td>Leave skin slightly damp</td>
          <td>Water on skin boosts moisturizer effect</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>3) Seal within 3 minutes</strong></td>
          <td>Apply thick cream/ointment head-to-toe</td>
          <td>Locks in moisture and repairs barrier</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>4) Target hot spots</strong></td>
          <td>Hydrocortisone 1% thin layer to itchy red patches (≤7 days)</td>
          <td>Reduces inflammation/itch cycle</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">If patches don’t improve in a week, or worsen, seek care to rule out infection or other causes.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine cautions & options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines that can worsen itch/dryness</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Diuretics</strong> (water pills), some <strong>statins</strong>, <strong>opioids</strong>, <strong>ACE inhibitors</strong>, <strong>calcium-channel blockers</strong></li>
          <li>Harsh topical antiseptics, frequent alcohol-based sanitizers</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Sedating antihistamines</strong> (diphenhydramine, hydroxyzine) can raise fall/confusion risk in seniors. Prefer daytime non-sedating options only if allergies are a trigger, and confirm with your clinician.
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When prescription help is needed</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Topical steroids</strong> of appropriate strength for eczema/dermatitis</li>
          <li><strong>Scabies therapy</strong> (permethrin/ivermectin) for confirmed infestations</li>
          <li><strong>Bile-acid resins</strong> or other agents for cholestatic itch; <strong>gabapentin</strong> for neuropathic itch (specialist-guided)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Daily prevention habits</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep showers short & lukewarm; avoid bubble baths</li>
          <li>Moisturize <strong>twice daily</strong> (after shower and at bedtime)</li>
          <li>Use cotton clothing and bedding; avoid rough seams</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Sleep better by keeping the room cool, nails trimmed, and using a bland emollient on itchy areas before bed.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best type of moisturizer?</p>
        <p>Thick <strong>creams or ointments</strong> (petrolatum/ceramides). Avoid fragranced lotions.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can hot showers cause itching?</p>
        <p>Yes—heat strips oils and worsens dryness. Use warm water and keep it brief.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Whole-body itch but skin looks normal?</p>
        <p>Ask about labs for thyroid, kidney, liver, diabetes, and iron levels; review medicines.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Itch worse at night?</p>
        <p>Moisturize at bedtime, keep room cool, try menthol/pramoxine lotions; check for scabies if others at home are itchy.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
