  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What chest pain can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Chest pain or pressure may come from the heart, lungs, esophagus/stomach, muscles/bones, anxiety, or even the aorta. In older adults, symptoms can be <strong>atypical</strong> (indigestion, breathlessness, fatigue, jaw/arm/back pain). Treat new or worsening pain as potentially serious until proven otherwise.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Heart-related causes:</strong> angina, heart attack, pericarditis, aortic emergencies. <strong>Lung-related:</strong> pulmonary embolism, pneumonia, pneumothorax (collapsed lung), pleurisy. <strong>Other common:</strong> GERD/acid reflux, esophageal spasm, costochondritis (chest wall strain), anxiety/panic.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Chest pressure, tightness, heaviness, or pain lasting <strong>>5 minutes</strong>, especially at rest</li>
        <li>Pain spreading to arm, jaw, neck, back, or with <strong>shortness of breath</strong>, sweating, nausea, or fainting</li>
        <li>Sudden, severe “tearing” chest/back pain (possible aortic problem)</li>
        <li>Chest pain with <strong>fast heartbeat</strong>, lightheadedness, or low oxygen</li>
        <li>Recent long travel/immobility with sharp pain and breathlessness (possible clot)</li>
        <li>Known heart disease, diabetes, kidney disease, or on blood thinners with new chest pain</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Do <strong>not</strong> drive yourself. Call emergency services for evaluation.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Types -->
  <section id="types" class="section">
    <h2>Common patterns & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Chest pain patterns">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Pattern</th><th scope="col">More likely</th><th scope="col">Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Pressure/heaviness</strong> with exertion, improves with rest</td>
          <td>Angina (reduced heart blood flow)</td>
          <td>Risk factors: age, diabetes, high BP, cholesterol; may radiate to arm/jaw</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Crushing, persistent pressure</strong> not relieved by rest</td>
          <td>Heart attack</td>
          <td>Shortness of breath, sweating, nausea; call emergency</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sharp pain worse with deep breath/cough</strong></td>
          <td>Pleurisy, pneumonia, pulmonary embolism</td>
          <td>Fever/cough or recent immobility/clot risks</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Burning behind breastbone</strong> after meals/lying down</td>
          <td>GERD/acid reflux</td>
          <td>Bitter taste/regurgitation; improves with antacids</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Tender to touch</strong> over ribs/sternum</td>
          <td>Costochondritis/muscle strain</td>
          <td>Pain reproducible with movement or pressure</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sudden, tearing pain</strong> to back/abdomen</td>
          <td>Aortic dissection</td>
          <td>Blood pressure difference between arms; immediate emergency</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Chest tightness with panic</strong></td>
          <td>Anxiety/panic attack</td>
          <td>Rapid breathing, tingling; still consider heart causes in older adults</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- Do now -->
  <section id="do-now" class="section">
    <h2>What to do right now</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If symptoms suggest a heart problem</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Stop activity. Sit or lie with head elevated.</strong></li>
          <li><strong>Call emergency services.</strong> Do not drive yourself.</li>
          <li>If you have <strong>prescribed nitroglycerin</strong>, use it as directed while waiting.</li>
          <li>If not allergic and no bleeding risk, many clinicians advise <strong>chewing aspirin</strong> (160–325 mg) <em>only if instructed by an emergency operator/clinician.</em></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">If pain fully resolves and seems non-urgent</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Arrange a <strong>prompt clinic visit</strong>, especially if symptoms were new, intense, or you have heart risk factors.</li>
          <li>Note triggers (exertion, meals, position), duration, and any associated symptoms.</li>
          <li>Avoid heavy exertion until evaluated.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small">This page is educational and not a diagnosis. When in doubt, seek urgent care.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care (after resolution) -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>If symptoms fully resolve</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Helpful steps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Small, non-heavy meals; avoid late-night large meals if reflux suspected</li>
          <li>Elevate head of bed for nighttime reflux; consider antacids/H2 blocker if previously advised</li>
          <li>Gentle activity as tolerated; avoid over-the-chest strain until assessed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Important:</strong> Even if pain stops, new chest pain in older adults often needs <strong>ECG and troponin testing</strong> to rule out heart injury.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>NSAIDs</strong> (ibuprofen, naproxen) may raise heart/stomach risks; avoid for undiagnosed chest pain unless told otherwise.</li>
          <li><strong>Antacids/acid reducers</strong> can help reflux; seek care if symptoms persist or mimic heart pain.</li>
          <li><strong>Nitroglycerin</strong> is only for those prescribed it; can cause low blood pressure/headache.</li>
          <li><strong>Do not start/stop heart medicines</strong> (beta-blockers, blood thinners) without clinician advice.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Keep an updated list of <strong>all medicines and supplements</strong> and bring it to appointments.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">Purpose</th><th scope="col">Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Assess cardiac, lung, GI, and aortic risks</td>
          <td>Risk factors, vital signs, oxygen level, chest wall tenderness</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>ECG & troponin</strong></td>
          <td>Detect heart injury/ischemia</td>
          <td>ECG on arrival; repeat troponin over hours</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging</strong></td>
          <td>Look for lung/aorta issues</td>
          <td>Chest X-ray; CT angiography for clots or aorta when indicated; echocardiogram</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Other tests</strong></td>
          <td>Clarify reflux or chronic angina</td>
          <td>Stress test, coronary CT, endoscopy if GI cause suspected</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Treatment</strong></td>
          <td>Stabilize and treat cause</td>
          <td>Oxygen, aspirin if appropriate, nitroglycerin, anticoagulation for clots, PCI for heart attack, antibiotics for pneumonia</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">The plan depends on severity, age, medical history, and exam/testing results.</p>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is chest “pressure” the same as pain?</p>
        <p>Yes—pressure, heaviness, tightness, or an “elephant on the chest” sensation can all be heart symptoms, especially in older adults.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If antacids help, is it just reflux?</p>
        <p>Not always. Heart pain can briefly improve with rest or medicines; new or recurrent symptoms still need medical evaluation.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I take aspirin?</p>
        <p>Only if not allergic and if advised by a clinician or emergency operator for suspected heart attack. Otherwise, wait for medical guidance.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When can I resume activity?</p>
        <p>After a clinician evaluates the cause. Avoid heavy exertion until cleared, especially if symptoms were exertional.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
