  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What is orthostatic dizziness?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Light-headedness, dimming vision, or unsteadiness within minutes of standing. Often due to a drop in blood pressure when moving from lying/sitting to standing.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Typical definition:</strong> fall in systolic ≥ <strong>20 mmHg</strong> or diastolic ≥ <strong>10 mmHg</strong> within 3 minutes of standing (or during tilt-table testing).</p>
      <p><strong>Why it matters:</strong> Raises fall risk and can signal dehydration, medication effects, or nervous system conditions — all addressable once identified.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>Urgent warning signs</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Fainting, head injury, chest pain, palpitations, or shortness of breath</li>
        <li>New weakness, trouble speaking, or vision loss</li>
        <li>Severe dehydration signs: very little urine, confusion, extreme thirst</li>
        <li>Black/bloody stools, fever with severe dizziness</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be emergencies — seek care immediately.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & triggers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">More common</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dehydration or low salt intake</li>
          <li>Blood pressure medicines (especially dose changes)</li>
          <li>Prolonged bed rest, recent illness, hot showers or weather</li>
          <li>Post-meal dips (post-prandial hypotension)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Other contributors</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Diabetes-related nerve changes, Parkinson’s disease</li>
          <li>Anemia, thyroid or adrenal issues</li>
          <li>Alcohol, dehydration from diarrhea/vomiting</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Routine -->
  <section id="routine" class="section">
    <h2>Rise-slow routine & counter-moves</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Rise-slow (do this every time)</p>
        <ol class="list">
          <li>Sit at the bed’s edge for 30–60 seconds; <strong>ankle pumps</strong> and deep breaths</li>
          <li>Stand up slowly; hold a stable surface, pause for 3 slow breaths</li>
          <li>If woozy, sit back down; repeat ankle/calf pumps and try again</li>
        </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Quick counter-maneuvers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Calf raises</strong>, <strong>leg-cross & squeeze</strong>, <strong>buttock clench</strong>, or <strong>hand-grip</strong> for 30 seconds</li>
          <li>Wear <strong>waist-high compression</strong> stockings (20–30 mmHg) or an abdominal binder if advised</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Head of bed up 10–20° can lessen overnight fluid shifts and morning dizziness.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Hydration & salt -->
  <section id="hydration" class="section">
    <h2>Hydration & salt</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Drink regularly through the day; extra caution in hot weather</li>
          <li>A morning glass of water before standing may help</li>
          <li>Small, frequent meals to avoid big post-meal dips</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        If you have <strong>heart failure or kidney disease</strong>, follow your fluid and salt plan exactly — do not increase salt or fluids without guidance.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines to review</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Group</th><th scope="col">Examples</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>BP-lowering</td>
          <td>Diuretics, alpha-blockers, nitrates, some beta-blockers</td>
          <td>May need timing/dose review</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Neuro/Psych</td>
          <td>Parkinson’s meds, tricyclics, antipsychotics, sedation meds</td>
          <td>Can worsen standing BP drop</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Other</td>
          <td>Alcohol, high-dose sildenafil/tadalafil</td>
          <td>Discuss safe use and timing</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Never stop medicines on your own — ask for a review and a stepwise plan.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>How it’s checked</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Orthostatic vitals</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Measure BP/heart rate after 5 minutes lying, then at 1 and 3 minutes standing</li>
          <li>Some clinics use <strong>tilt-table</strong> testing if unclear</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Other tests (as needed)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Blood tests: electrolytes, kidney function, blood count, B12/thyroid if indicated</li>
          <li>ECG; medication review; hydration assessment</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lifestyle first</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rise-slow routine, counter-maneuvers, compression garments</li>
          <li>Spread fluids through the day; cautious salt per plan</li>
          <li>Smaller meals; limit alcohol and hot environments</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines (if needed)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Midodrine</strong> (raises standing BP)</li>
          <li><strong>Fludrocortisone</strong> (expands volume)</li>
          <li><strong>Droxidopa</strong> in select cases</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">Used when non-drug steps aren’t enough and after careful evaluation.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Most people improve with routine + hydration + medication review. Track symptoms and share a log at follow-ups.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Home safety & falls</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use night lights; clear pathways; avoid loose rugs</li>
          <li>Sturdy, non-slip footwear; handrails for stairs and bathroom grab bars</li>
          <li>Keep frequently used items at waist height</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If you feel faint</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sit or lie down immediately; elevate legs if possible</li>
          <li>Try counter-maneuvers; sip water once steady</li>
          <li>Report any falls or near-falls to your clinician</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- When to call -->
  <section id="when-to-call" class="section">
    <h2>When to contact your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Dizziness persists despite rise-slow, hydration, and compression</li>
        <li>New medicines started or doses changed with worse symptoms</li>
        <li>Any fainting, fall, head injury, or red-flag symptom</li>
        <li>Morning episodes becoming frequent or affecting daily activities</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is this the same as vertigo?</p>
        <p>No. Orthostatic dizziness is a blood-pressure drop on standing, not a spinning sensation from the inner ear.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does coffee help or hurt?</p>
        <p>Large amounts can cause dehydration in some. Moderate intake is usually fine; avoid near bedtime.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are compression socks enough?</p>
        <p>They help, especially waist-high styles. Best results come with the rise-slow routine and medication review.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Why worse after meals?</p>
        <p>Blood shifts to the gut (post-prandial hypotension). Try smaller, more frequent meals and avoid very hot rooms after eating.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
