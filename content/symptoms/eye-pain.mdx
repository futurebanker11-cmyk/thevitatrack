  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What eye pain can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Eye pain ranges from surface irritation to deep, aching pain. Common causes include <strong>dry eye</strong>, <strong>corneal abrasion</strong> (scratch), <strong>conjunctivitis</strong>, <strong>eyelid problems</strong> (stye/blepharitis), and <strong>sinus/headache</strong> referred pain. Pain with <strong>vision changes, light sensitivity, halos, nausea, or trauma</strong> needs urgent attention.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Describe:</strong> sudden vs gradual, one or both eyes, vision changes, discharge, contact lens use, injury/foreign body, headache, nausea, and light sensitivity.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD ===== -->
  

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Sudden vision loss</strong>, new dense floaters/flashes, or a dark curtain over vision</li>
        <li><strong>Chemical splash</strong> to the eye—<em>immediately irrigate with clean water for 15+ minutes</em> then seek care</li>
        <li>Severe eye pain with <strong>headache, halos around lights, nausea/vomiting</strong> (possible acute angle-closure glaucoma)</li>
        <li>High-speed or penetrating eye injury, metal/grit in eye, or post-surgery pain</li>
        <li>Eye pain with <strong>fever and swelling</strong> around the eye or inability to move the eye (orbital cellulitis)</li>
        <li>Shingles rash on forehead/eyelid or tip of nose with eye pain (urgent ophthalmology)</li>
        <li>Red, painful eye in a <strong>contact lens</strong> wearer—risk of corneal infection</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can indicate retinal problems, glaucoma attack, corneal ulcer, serious infection, or vision-threatening injury.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Eye pain causes">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Condition</th><th>Typical features</th><th>Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dry eye / keratoconjunctivitis sicca</strong></td>
          <td>Burning, gritty feeling, fluctuating vision, worse with screens/wind</td>
          <td>Improves with artificial tears, humid air, blinking breaks</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Corneal abrasion / foreign body</strong></td>
          <td>Sharp pain, tearing, light sensitivity; feels like sand in eye</td>
          <td>History of rubbing, yardwork, or lens use; relief with anesthetic at clinic</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Conjunctivitis</strong></td>
          <td>Redness, discharge (watery or sticky), mild irritation</td>
          <td>Allergic: itchy/watery; viral: watery + URI; bacterial: thicker discharge</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Blepharitis / stye (hordeolum)</strong></td>
          <td>Eyelid tenderness, crusting, localized painful bump</td>
          <td>Often recurrent; better with warm compress and lid hygiene</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Uveitis (iritis)</strong></td>
          <td>Deep ache, light sensitivity, blurred vision</td>
          <td>Often needs steroid drops under ophthalmology guidance</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Acute angle-closure glaucoma</strong></td>
          <td>Severe pain, halos, headache, nausea, mid-dilated pupil</td>
          <td>Emergency treatment to reduce eye pressure</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sinus / cluster headache</strong></td>
          <td>Pain around/behind eye, tearing, nasal symptoms</td>
          <td>Neurology/ENT input if recurrent or severe</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Shingles (ophthalmic)</strong></td>
          <td>Burning pain → blistery rash on forehead/eyelid</td>
          <td>Early antivirals; eye exam to prevent complications</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Self-care that’s usually safe</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If no red flags</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>preservative-free artificial tears</strong> 3–6×/day for dryness/irritation</li>
          <li><strong>Warm compress</strong> 5–10 minutes for stye/blepharitis; clean lids with diluted baby shampoo or lid wipes</li>
          <li>Follow the <strong>20–20–20</strong> rule for screens (every 20 minutes, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds)</li>
          <li>Wear sunglasses outdoors; use a humidifier and avoid direct fan/AC on eyes</li>
          <li>If you suspect a <strong>foreign body</strong>, rinse with clean water/saline—do not rub</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Do not:</strong> use leftover antibiotic or <strong>steroid eye drops</strong> without medical advice, wear contact lenses during pain/redness, or patch the eye after injury unless instructed.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medication & drop cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Avoid frequent use of “<strong>redness reliever</strong>” drops—can worsen redness (rebound)</li>
          <li>Contact lens wearers: switch to <strong>glasses</strong> until cleared; never sleep in lenses unless prescribed</li>
          <li>Share a full list of <strong>meds</strong> (antihistamines, diuretics, antidepressants can worsen dry eye)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Allergy relief:</strong> Lubricating tears first; consider antihistamine/mast-cell stabilizer eye drops if allergic symptoms—ask your clinician which is safe for you.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Comfort & prevention tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep hands and lenses clean; replace lens cases every 3 months</li>
          <li>Use protective eyewear for yardwork/DIY; avoid rubbing eyes</li>
          <li>Adjust lighting and font size to reduce eye strain</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Systemic health:</strong> Good control of <em>diabetes, autoimmune disease, and thyroid conditions</em> supports eye comfort and vision.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Clinical evaluation for eye pain">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Purpose</th><th>Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Vision & pupil check</strong></td>
          <td>Baseline vision & neurologic screen</td>
          <td>Snellen chart, pupil reactions, visual fields</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Slit-lamp exam</strong></td>
          <td>Surface & anterior eye assessment</td>
          <td>Look for abrasions, ulcers, inflammation; <em>fluorescein staining</em></td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Eye pressure (tonometry)</strong></td>
          <td>Rule out glaucoma attack</td>
          <td>Measured intraocular pressure</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dilation & fundus exam</strong></td>
          <td>View retina/optic nerve</td>
          <td>Retinal tears, detachment, optic neuritis signs</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging / labs</strong></td>
          <td>Complications or atypical cases</td>
          <td>Orbital CT for cellulitis; labs for autoimmune uveitis; corneal cultures if ulcers</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Treatment targets the cause—lubricants and lid care; antibiotics/antivirals for infections; pressure-lowering meds for glaucoma; anti-inflammatories under eye-specialist guidance.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Onset time, <strong>which eye</strong>, and pain severity (0–10)</li>
          <li>Triggers: screens, wind, contact lenses, makeup, yardwork</li>
          <li>Vision changes, light sensitivity, discharge color, fever</li>
          <li>All eye drops/meds used and response; contact lens wear schedule</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Bring this log to visits—it speeds diagnosis and helps protect vision.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support with dignity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Help with timely appointments for <strong>new vision changes</strong> or severe pain</li>
          <li>Assist with eye-drop schedules and hygiene (hand washing, drop spacing)</li>
          <li>Encourage protective eyewear for chores and consistent glasses use during flares</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call the clinician for:</strong> worsening pain/redness, vision changes, fever/swelling around the eye, shingles rash, or any painful red eye in contact lens users.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is eye pain with halos an emergency?</p>
        <p>Yes—especially with headache and nausea. This may be <strong>acute angle-closure glaucoma</strong>; seek emergency care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can dry eye really cause pain?</p>
        <p>It can cause burning, gritty discomfort, and fluctuating vision. Lubricating drops, warm compresses, and blinking breaks often help.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I use leftover antibiotic or steroid drops?</p>
        <p>No. Some conditions worsen with steroids, and the wrong antibiotic can delay care. Get an eye exam first.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When can I wear contacts again?</p>
        <p>Wait until pain/redness fully resolve and a clinician clears you. Always replace/open new lenses after infections.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
