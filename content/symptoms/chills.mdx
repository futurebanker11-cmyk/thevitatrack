  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What chills can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Chills are a sudden feeling of cold with shivering. They often occur with <strong>fever</strong> from infection, but can also follow exposure to cold, certain <strong>medicines</strong>, or hormone/metabolic issues. In seniors, chills deserve attention—serious infection can show few other signs.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Goals:</strong> identify red flags early, measure temperature correctly, hydrate, and seek timely evaluation when needed.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Rigors</strong> (uncontrolled shaking) with fever and <strong>confusion</strong> or extreme weakness</li>
        <li>Fever/chills with <strong>shortness of breath, chest pain,</strong> or oxygen drop (if you monitor)</li>
        <li>Chills with <strong>stiff neck, severe headache, new rash</strong> (especially purple spots)</li>
        <li>Chills  + <strong>burning urination</strong>, flank pain, or inability to pass urine</li>
        <li>Recent <strong>surgery, catheter, wound, or device</strong> and feeling acutely unwell</li>
        <li>On <strong>chemotherapy, high-dose steroids, or immunosuppressants</strong></li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may signal sepsis, meningitis, pneumonia, pyelonephritis, endocarditis, or other urgent problems.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- First steps -->
  <section id="first-steps" class="section">
    <h2>Safe first steps & fever care</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Check & note</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Measure temperature correctly (oral/tympanic). Write down time and reading.</li>
          <li>Note other symptoms: cough, burning urination, rash, confusion, chest pain.</li>
          <li>Drink small, frequent fluids unless on a fluid restriction.</li>
          <li>Layer light clothing/blanket; avoid overheating once shaking stops.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">At-home comfort</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rest, but move around every couple of hours if able.</li>
          <li>Cool cloth on forehead if fever high; tepid (not ice-cold) sponge if uncomfortable.</li>
          <li>Consider home tests (influenza/COVID-19) during respiratory season.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Call your clinician soon</strong> if fever ≥38.3°C (101°F), lasts >24–48 hours, or if you feel worse—especially with chronic heart, lung, or kidney disease.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fever reducers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Acetaminophen</strong> within safe total daily limits (consider combination products).</li>
          <li><strong>NSAIDs</strong> (ibuprofen/naproxen) can irritate stomach, affect kidneys, and raise BP—use only if your clinician agrees.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When on blood thinners</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Avoid starting additional NSAIDs unless specifically advised.</li>
          <li>Report fever with bleeding/bruising or black stools promptly.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Do not take leftover antibiotics</strong> without guidance—this can delay correct diagnosis and cause resistance.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can you have chills without a fever?</p>
        <p>Yes—early infection, environmental cold, medication effects, or hormonal/metabolic issues can cause chills without a high temperature.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When should I test for COVID-19/flu?</p>
        <p>During respiratory season or after exposure—especially if you also have cough, sore throat, or body aches.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What is a “rigor”?</p>
        <p>Intense shaking chills with teeth chattering. In older adults, rigors plus feeling very unwell deserve urgent assessment.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Warm bath for chills?</p>
        <p>Lukewarm bathing can help comfort, but avoid very hot water with fever or dizziness. Hydrate and rest.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
