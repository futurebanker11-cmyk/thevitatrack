  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What tremor can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Tremor is a rhythmic shaking of a body part (often hands). In older adults, common causes include <strong>essential tremor</strong>, <strong>Parkinson’s disease</strong> (resting tremor), <strong>medications</strong>, <strong>anxiety/caffeine</strong>, <strong>thyroid problems</strong>, <strong>low blood sugar</strong>, and <strong>alcohol use or withdrawal</strong>. Less commonly, cerebellar or neurologic disease is involved.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Key observation:</strong> Does it happen <em>at rest</em>, when <em>holding a posture</em> (e.g., holding a cup), or during <em>movement</em> (e.g., touching finger to nose)? This guides the cause.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Shaking with <strong>confusion, fainting, chest pain, or shortness of breath</strong></li>
        <li>Sudden <strong>one-sided weakness</strong>, face droop, or speech trouble (possible stroke)</li>
        <li>New severe headache, fever with stiffness/confusion, or head injury</li>
        <li>Continuous whole-body shaking with <strong>loss of awareness</strong> (possible seizure)</li>
        <li>Known diabetes with tremor plus <strong>sweats, shakiness, or confusion</strong> (possible low blood sugar)</li>
        <li>Alcohol/benzodiazepine withdrawal symptoms (agitation, fast heart, high BP, hallucinations)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may require urgent treatment—do not drive yourself.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Types -->
  <section id="types" class="section">
    <h2>Tremor types & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Tremor types and clinical clues">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Type</th><th scope="col">When it shows</th><th scope="col">Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Resting tremor</strong></td>
          <td>At rest, less with movement</td>
          <td>“Pill-rolling” hands; slowness/stiffness → consider Parkinson’s</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Postural tremor</strong></td>
          <td>Holding a position (arms outstretched, holding a cup)</td>
          <td>Essential tremor, anxiety, hyperthyroid, medication or caffeine</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Action/kinetic tremor</strong></td>
          <td>During movement</td>
          <td>Essential tremor common; improves with <em>small</em> alcohol amounts (not a treatment)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Intention tremor</strong></td>
          <td>Worse as finger nears a target</td>
          <td>Cerebellar issues; look for imbalance, wide-based gait</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Orthostatic tremor</strong></td>
          <td>Legs shake on standing, better when walking/sitting</td>
          <td>Rare; feels like “unsteady standing”</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dystonic/psychogenic</strong></td>
          <td>Variable, may change with posture or distraction</td>
          <td>Irregular pattern; neuro evaluation helpful</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes by clue</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Causes of tremor with clinical clues">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Clue</th><th scope="col">Consider</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Family history; both hands; with action</strong></td>
          <td>Essential tremor</td>
          <td>Common; head/voice tremor possible; often improves with small alcohol but avoid reliance</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Rest tremor, slowness, stiffness, small steps</strong></td>
          <td>Parkinson’s disease</td>
          <td>One side often worse; gait/balance changes</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Recent new meds</strong></td>
          <td>Medication-induced</td>
          <td>Albuterol, stimulants, SSRIs/SNRIs, bupropion, lithium, valproate, antipsychotics, theophylline, amiodarone, cyclosporine/tacrolimus</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Weight loss, heat intolerance, fast heart</strong></td>
          <td>Hyperthyroidism</td>
          <td>Check thyroid labs (TSH/FT4)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Diabetes; sweats, hunger, confusion</strong></td>
          <td>Hypoglycemia</td>
          <td>Check glucose; follow hypoglycemia plan</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Shaking in morning or after cutting down</strong></td>
          <td>Alcohol withdrawal</td>
          <td>May escalate; seek care if severe</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Unsteady, clumsy, slurred speech</strong></td>
          <td>Cerebellar disease</td>
          <td>Consider stroke, meds, or degeneration</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Do now -->
  <section id="do-now" class="section">
    <h2>What to do right now</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If shaking is bothersome (no red-flags)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Sit, breathe slowly</strong> (4–6 breaths/min) to reduce adrenaline-driven tremor</li>
          <li><strong>Limit stimulants</strong> today: caffeine, energy drinks, decongestants</li>
          <li><strong>Review recent meds</strong> or dose changes (see table)—note anything new</li>
          <li>If diabetic and symptomatic, <strong>check glucose</strong> and follow your low-sugar plan</li>
          <li>Hydrate and eat a small snack if you haven’t eaten</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Call your clinician soon if</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Tremor <strong>worsens over weeks</strong>, interferes with eating, writing, dressing, or walking</li>
          <li>You have associated <strong>slowness, stiffness, balance issues, or falls</strong></li>
          <li>You started a new medicine around the time the tremor began</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small">This page is educational and not a diagnosis. Seek emergency care for the warning signs above.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>If mild & stable</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily strategies</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Weighted utensils</strong>, lidded cups, and non-slip mats for meals</li>
          <li>Use two hands for cups; rest elbows on table to stabilize</li>
          <li><strong>Occupational therapy</strong> can teach adaptive techniques and tools</li>
          <li>Schedule tasks when tremor is <strong>least</strong> (e.g., after rest, before caffeine/meds)</li>
          <li>Brief <strong>isometric squeeze</strong> (e.g., press hands together) can temporarily reduce essential tremor</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Driving & safety:</strong> If tremor affects steering, feeding, or medication handling, discuss supports or treatment—safety first.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine notes & cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Can worsen tremor:</strong> albuterol, pseudoephedrine, caffeine, theophylline, SSRIs/SNRIs, bupropion, lithium, valproate, antipsychotics, stimulants, amiodarone, cyclosporine/tacrolimus</li>
          <li><strong>Discuss options (doctor-prescribed):</strong> for essential tremor—propranolol or primidone; for Parkinson’s—levodopa, MAO-B inhibitors; others case-by-case</li>
          <li><strong>Do not</strong> start/stop prescription meds on your own; review risks (heart rate, blood pressure, sleepiness, interactions)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Keep an updated <strong>medicine & supplement list</strong> (include nicotine, herbal stimulants). Bring devices (e.g., inhalers) to review technique and dosing.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Lifestyle -->
  <section id="lifestyle" class="section">
    <h2>Caffeine, alcohol & sleep</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Helpful choices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Reduce caffeine</strong> (coffee, tea, cola, energy drinks); switch some to decaf</li>
          <li>Regular meals with <strong>protein + complex carbs</strong> help steady blood sugar</li>
          <li>Consistent <strong>sleep</strong> routine; manage stress with breathing, walks, or meditation</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Limit/avoid</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Heavy alcohol use (withdrawal worsens tremor); if cutting down significantly, <strong>seek a plan</strong> with your clinician</li>
          <li>Decongestants and certain “pre-workout” or weight-loss supplements</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">Purpose</th><th scope="col">Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & neuro exam</strong></td>
          <td>Classify tremor & rule out mimics</td>
          <td>Rest vs action; gait/balance; rigidity/bradykinesia; medication & alcohol review</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Basic labs</strong></td>
          <td>Check metabolic & endocrine causes</td>
          <td>Glucose, electrolytes, kidney/liver function, <strong>TSH/FT4</strong>, B12 ± drug levels (lithium/valproate)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging (selected)</strong></td>
          <td>Evaluate atypical/acute neuro signs</td>
          <td>Brain MRI/CT if new neuro deficits, stepwise onset, or cerebellar signs</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Treatment</strong></td>
          <td>Symptom control & cause-specific care</td>
          <td>Medication adjustment, targeted therapy (e.g., propranolol/primidone, levodopa), therapy referrals</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Procedures (refractory cases)</strong></td>
          <td>Reduce disabling tremor</td>
          <td>Botulinum toxin (head/voice), deep brain stimulation, MR-guided focused ultrasound—specialist centers</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Plans vary by severity, cause, coexisting conditions, and patient goals.</p>
  </section>

  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>When tremor happens (rest, posture, action) and what triggers/improves it</li>
          <li>Impact on daily tasks: writing, eating, dressing, walking</li>
          <li>New medicines, caffeine/alcohol intake, sleep quality</li>
          <li>Any falls, balance issues, stiffness, or slowness</li>
        </ul>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is tremor always Parkinson’s?</p>
        <p>No. <strong>Essential tremor</strong> is more common and occurs with action/posture. Parkinson’s tremor is classically at rest and comes with slowness and stiffness.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will coffee make it worse?</p>
        <p>Often yes. Caffeine and some decongestants/stimulants can amplify tremor. Reducing them usually helps.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do tiny amounts of alcohol help?</p>
        <p>Essential tremor may temporarily improve, but alcohol isn’t a treatment and can cause dependence and worse withdrawal tremor. Discuss safer options.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What treatments exist?</p>
        <p>Depending on cause: medication adjustments, propranolol/primidone for essential tremor, Parkinson’s therapy, therapy devices, and in select cases <strong>deep brain stimulation</strong> or focused ultrasound.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
