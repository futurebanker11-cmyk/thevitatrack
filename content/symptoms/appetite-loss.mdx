  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What appetite loss can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> <strong>Appetite loss (poor appetite)</strong> is common in older adults and may follow illness, pain, low mood, or medication changes. While short dips can be normal, persistent loss can lead to <strong>weight loss, weakness, falls,</strong> and slower recovery.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Look for patterns:</strong> new taste/smell changes, mouth/dental pain, nausea or early fullness, constipation, loneliness at meals, or recent new medicines.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Inability to keep fluids down, signs of <strong>dehydration</strong> (very dark urine, dizziness, confusion)</li>
        <li>Black/tarry stools, vomiting blood, severe belly or chest pain</li>
        <li>Rapid, unintentional weight loss (≥5% body weight in 6–12 months) with weakness</li>
        <li>Severe trouble swallowing, choking, or repeated aspiration</li>
        <li>High fever, persistent vomiting, or new confusion</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can signal bleeding, infection, obstruction, stroke, or other urgent problems.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>Common causes & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Appetite loss causes">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Category</th><th>Examples</th><th>Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Digestive</td>
          <td>GERD/gastritis/ulcer, constipation, gallbladder or pancreas problems</td>
          <td>Burning pain, early fullness, nausea, black stools, pale stools/jaundice</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Infections</td>
          <td>Flu/COVID, UTI, pneumonia</td>
          <td>Fever, cough, burning urination, fatigue/confusion</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Mood & brain</td>
          <td>Depression, anxiety, grief, dementia</td>
          <td>Low interest, sleep/appetite change, forgetting meals</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Mouth & senses</td>
          <td>Poorly fitting dentures, mouth sores, dry mouth, taste/smell changes</td>
          <td>Pain with chewing, avoiding certain textures, bland taste</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Medical conditions</td>
          <td>Thyroid, kidney/liver disease, heart failure, cancer</td>
          <td>Swelling, shortness of breath, night sweats, persistent fatigue</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Medications</td>
          <td>Some antibiotics, digoxin, opioids, SSRIs/SNRIs, metformin, chemo, anticholinergics</td>
          <td>Nausea, metallic taste, constipation or sedation after a med change</td>
        </tr>

  <!-- Self-check -->
  <section id="self-check" class="section">
    <h2>Simple self-check</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Ask yourself (past month)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Have you <strong>lost weight</strong> without trying? (check clothes/rings fit)</li>
          <li>Do meals feel like a chore or taste different?</li>
          <li>Any <strong>nausea, pain, constipation,</strong> or trouble swallowing?</li>
          <li>Any <strong>new medicines</strong> started or doses changed?</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">If yes to any, build the plan below and contact your clinician to review causes.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Protein & strength first:</strong> Loss of appetite can quickly reduce muscle. Prioritize protein and light activity to protect strength.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Safe appetite boosters</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Make eating easier</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Small, frequent meals/snacks</strong> (every 2–3 hours)</li>
          <li><strong>Protein first:</strong> eggs, yogurt, cheese, tofu, fish, beans, nut butters</li>
          <li><strong>Flavor & warmth:</strong> herbs, spices, lemon, warm foods if smells help</li>
          <li><strong>Social meals:</strong> eat with a friend/video call; pleasant background music</li>
          <li><strong>Light activity</strong> (10–15 min walk) before meals to stimulate appetite</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">Easy add-ons</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep <strong>ready-to-eat</strong> options: soups, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, fortified cereals</li>
          <li><strong>Enrich foods:</strong> olive oil, avocado, powdered milk in oatmeal/soups, nut toppings</li>
          <li><strong>Oral care:</strong> brush/floss/denture care; sip water or use saliva substitutes for dry mouth</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Protein, hydration & constipation</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Diet support">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Area</th><th>Targets</th><th>Tips</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Protein</td>
          <td>About <strong>1.0–1.2 g/kg/day</strong> spread over meals</td>
          <td>Examples: 2 eggs + yogurt; lentil soup + cheese toast; tuna + crackers</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Hydration</td>
          <td>Aim for <strong>pale-yellow</strong> urine (unless fluid-restricted)</td>
          <td>Broths, milk, water, herbal tea; sip throughout the day</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Constipation</td>
          <td>Regular soft stools</td>
          <td>Fiber foods + fluids; gentle walking; use clinician-advised laxatives if needed</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Medication review</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Make an up-to-date <strong>medication list</strong> (prescriptions, OTCs, herbs)</li>
          <li>Ask if any can cause <strong>nausea, taste change, dry mouth, sedation, or constipation</strong></li>
          <li>Do <strong>not</strong> stop medicines on your own—request a review or timing/dose adjustments</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Appetite stimulants:</strong> Medicines to “boost appetite” are not one-size-fits-all and can have side effects. Treat the cause first; consider dietitian referral.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Weekly weight</strong> and belt/waist changes</li>
          <li><strong>3-day food & fluid diary</strong> (times, amounts, symptoms)</li>
          <li>Mood, sleep, pain, bowel habits, nausea</li>
          <li>New meds or illnesses around the time appetite changed</li>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support with dignity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Offer <strong>small, appealing portions</strong> on a schedule; don’t force large meals</li>
          <li>Make meals social and unhurried; reduce distractions if tiring</li>
          <li>Set out ready snacks at eye level: yogurt, cheese, nuts, fruit cups</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call the clinician for:</strong> rapid weight loss, dehydration signs, persistent vomiting, black stools, painful swallowing, or new confusion/weakness.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What’s one quick win?</p>
        <p>Eat <strong>every 2–3 hours</strong> and lead with protein (e.g., yogurt or eggs). A short pre-meal walk can also help spark appetite.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are nutrition shakes okay?</p>
        <p>They can help if whole foods aren’t enough—use between meals, not to replace meals, and choose protein-forward options.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Could depression cause appetite loss?</p>
        <p>Yes. Low mood commonly reduces appetite. Screening and treatment often improve eating and energy.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When should I get checked?</p>
        <p>Any <strong>unintentional weight loss</strong>, appetite loss lasting more than 1–2 weeks, trouble swallowing, persistent nausea, or dehydration signs warrants prompt evaluation.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
