  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What shortness of breath can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Shortness of breath (dyspnea) can come from <strong>lungs</strong> (asthma/COPD, pneumonia, pulmonary embolism), <strong>heart</strong> (heart failure, heart attack), <strong>blood</strong> (anemia), <strong>fluids/electrolytes</strong>, infections (including COVID/flu), anxiety, or medication effects.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Watch for patterns:</strong> breathlessness with <em>exertion</em>, when <em>lying flat</em>, with <em>wheeze</em>, <em>fever/cough</em>, <em>chest pain</em>, <em>leg swelling</em>, or <em>black stools</em> (anemia/bleeding).</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Severe breathlessness at rest or unable to speak full sentences</li>
        <li>Chest pain/pressure, sweating, nausea, or pain to arm/jaw/Back</li>
        <li>Blue/gray lips or fingertips, <strong>confusion</strong>, or very drowsy</li>
        <li>Worsening <strong>oxygen levels</strong> (home SpO₂ &lt; 92% or &lt; 88% if you use oxygen unless told otherwise)</li>
        <li>New <strong>one-sided leg swelling</strong>, coughing blood, or sudden chest pain (possible clot)</li>
        <li>Rapid weight gain &gt;2 kg in 2 days with swelling/orthopnea (possible fluid overload/heart failure)</li>
        <li>Known COPD/asthma with <strong>no relief</strong> after rescue inhaler/nebulizer</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can indicate heart attack, pulmonary embolism, severe infection, or decompensated lung/heart disease.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Patterns -->
  <section id="patterns" class="section">
    <h2>Common patterns & causes</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Shortness of breath patterns and clues">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Pattern</th><th scope="col">More likely</th><th scope="col">Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sudden at rest</strong></td>
          <td>Pulmonary embolism, pneumothorax, heart attack, arrhythmia</td>
          <td>Chest pain, fast heart, risk of DVT/immobility, one-sided chest pain</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>With exertion</strong></td>
          <td>Heart failure, COPD/asthma, anemia, interstitial lung disease</td>
          <td>Fatigue, wheeze, cough, paleness, low exercise tolerance</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Worse when lying flat (orthopnea) / wakes at night (PND)</strong></td>
          <td>Heart failure</td>
          <td>Leg swelling, rapid weight gain, need for extra pillows</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Fever, cough, sputum</strong></td>
          <td>Pneumonia, COVID/flu, bronchitis</td>
          <td>Chills, chest crackles, exposure history</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Wheeze, chest tightness</strong></td>
          <td>Asthma/COPD flare</td>
          <td>Viral trigger, smoke exposure, relief with bronchodilator</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Pale, dizzy, black stools</strong></td>
          <td>Anemia/bleeding</td>
          <td>Fatigue, fast heartbeat, low energy</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>After new meds/sedatives</strong></td>
          <td>Medication effect</td>
          <td>Opiates, benzodiazepines, beta-blockers (asthma), fluid-retaining drugs</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">
      <strong>Home checks:</strong> If available, note temperature, pulse oximeter readings, heart rate, and any swelling or weight change.
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Do now -->
  <section id="do-now" class="section">
    <h2>What to do right now</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If you feel breathless</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Sit upright</strong> (tripod position) and <strong>pursed-lip breathe</strong>: in through nose 2 counts, out through lips 4 counts</li>
          <li>Use your <strong>rescue inhaler/nebulizer</strong> as prescribed (e.g., albuterol); spacer helps</li>
          <li>Check <strong>SpO₂</strong> if available; start prescribed home oxygen per your plan</li>
          <li>Loosen tight clothing; cool the room; stop exertion</li>
          <li>Call emergency for the red flags above—<em>don’t drive yourself</em></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Call your clinician soon if</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>New or worsening breathlessness over days, or limits walking/sleep</li>
          <li>Need your rescue inhaler more than every 4 hours or more than usual</li>
          <li>Recent infection with lingering cough/breathlessness &gt;2–3 weeks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small">This page is educational and not a diagnosis. Seek urgent care for the warning signs above.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>If mild & stable</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily steps</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Practice <strong>pursed-lip</strong> and <strong>diaphragmatic</strong> breathing 5–10 minutes twice daily</li>
          <li>Use <strong>inhalers correctly</strong> (with spacer if MDI); rinse mouth after steroid inhalers</li>
          <li>Gradual activity: short walks, seated/standing intervals, pulmonary rehab if offered</li>
          <li>For heart failure: follow sodium/fluid guidance; elevate legs to reduce swelling</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Oxygen safety:</strong> Never smoke near oxygen; keep away from open flames; use the flow your clinician prescribed (not higher unless instructed).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine notes & cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Medicines affecting breathlessness">
          <thead>
            <tr><th scope="col">Medicine</th><th scope="col">Effect</th><th scope="col">Notes for seniors</th></tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Rescue bronchodilators</strong> (albuterol/levalbuterol)</td>
              <td>Open airways during flares</td>
              <td>Use with spacer; watch for tremor/fast heart</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Inhaled steroids/LABAs/LAMAs</strong></td>
              <td>Control asthma/COPD</td>
              <td>Daily adherence prevents flares; rinse mouth</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Diuretics</strong> (e.g., furosemide)</td>
              <td>Reduce fluid overload in heart failure</td>
              <td>Monitor weight, electrolytes; don’t self-change dose without plan</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Antibiotics/antivirals</strong></td>
              <td>Treat pneumonia/COVID/flu when indicated</td>
              <td>Use when prescribed; complete the course</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Sedatives/opioids</strong></td>
              <td>Can depress breathing</td>
              <td>Caution; avoid mixing with alcohol/other sedatives</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>NSAIDs</strong></td>
              <td>May cause fluid retention</td>
              <td>Use cautiously in heart failure/kidney disease</td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Keep an updated <strong>medicine list</strong> and bring devices (inhalers, spacer, oximeter) to visits for technique checks.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet & Breathing -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Breathing, salt & fluid tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Helpful choices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Small, frequent meals to avoid stomach fullness pushing on the diaphragm</li>
          <li>Hydration: regular fluids unless on restriction; warm drinks can loosen mucus</li>
          <li>For heart failure: <strong>reduce sodium</strong> (limit packaged/restaurant foods)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Limit/avoid</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Smoking/vaping and secondhand smoke</li>
          <li>Very salty foods if fluid retention is an issue</li>
          <li>Outdoor exertion during poor air quality; use a mask if advised</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

          <td><strong>Tests</strong></td>

          <td><strong>Treatment</strong></td>
          <td>Stabilize & prevent</td>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Prevention & safety</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stay current on <strong>vaccines</strong>: flu, COVID, and pneumococcal per guidelines</li>
          <li>Use inhalers/CPAP exactly as prescribed; check technique regularly</li>
          <li>Exercise within limits; consider <strong>pulmonary or cardiac rehab</strong></li>
          <li>Stop smoking; seek support programs and nicotine replacement if needed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Early recognition of fluid gain, infection, or worsening exercise tolerance prevents hospital visits—track your numbers.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Daily <strong>SpO₂</strong> (resting and with a short walk) if you have a pulse oximeter</li>
          <li><strong>Rescue inhaler</strong> use (times/day) and symptom triggers</li>
          <li><strong>Weight</strong> each morning (HF): call if ↑ &ge;1–2 kg in 2 days</li>
          <li>Temperature, cough/sputum color, exposures (for infection)</li>
          <li>All medicines, doses, and any recent changes</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Bring this log to visits—it speeds diagnosis and a targeted treatment plan.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When is breathlessness an emergency?</p>
        <p>Severe rest breathlessness, chest pain, low oxygen (SpO₂ &lt;92%), blue lips, confusion, coughing blood, or sudden one-sided leg swelling—seek emergency care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I use my inhaler more often?</p>
        <p>Use your rescue inhaler as prescribed. If you need it more than usual (e.g., every few hours), contact your clinician—this signals a flare.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need antibiotics?</p>
        <p>Only for certain bacterial infections. Many flares are viral or inflammatory; evaluation guides treatment.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can anemia cause shortness of breath?</p>
        <p>Yes—especially with fatigue and pale color, or if you have black stools (bleeding). A simple blood test can check.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
