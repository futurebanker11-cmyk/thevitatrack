  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What easy bruising can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Bruises form when small blood vessels break under the skin. With age, <strong>skin thins</strong> and protection from fat decreases, so minor bumps cause bigger marks—especially on forearms and hands (<em>senile purpura</em>). But new or worsening bruising can also signal <strong>medication effects or bleeding problems</strong> that need review.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Watch for:</strong> bruises without clear injury, very large or painful bruises, nose/gum bleeding, blood in urine/stool, or easy bleeding after dental care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Head injury or fall with <strong>blood thinners</strong> (warfarin, DOACs) on board—even if you feel okay</li>
        <li>New <strong>widespread bruising</strong>, <strong>petechiae</strong> (tiny red/purple dots), or large rapidly growing bruise</li>
        <li>Bruising with <strong>weakness, pallor, fever, or weight loss</strong></li>
        <li>Bleeding that won’t stop, blood in vomit/stool/urine, or coughing up blood</li>
        <li>Severe abdominal, back, or joint pain with a bruise after injury (possible deep bleed)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can signal internal bleeding, very low platelets, liver disease, or a clotting disorder.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common patterns & causes</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Patterns & causes">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Pattern</th><th>More likely causes</th><th>Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Forearms/hands from light bumps</strong></td>
          <td>Sun/age-related skin thinning (<em>senile purpura</em>)</td>
          <td>Flat, purple patches; fade to brown; recur in same areas</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>After starting or raising dose of a medicine</strong></td>
          <td>Anticoagulants (warfarin, apixaban, rivaroxaban, etc.), antiplatelets (aspirin, clopidogrel), steroids, SSRIs/SNRIs, NSAIDs</td>
          <td>Bleeding gums/nose, easier bruising, prolonged bleeding from cuts</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Small pinpoint dots (petechiae)</strong></td>
          <td>Low platelets, infections, medication reactions, vasculitis</td>
          <td>Often on legs; don’t blanch with pressure; new fevers worsen concern</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Large deep muscle bruises/bleeds</strong></td>
          <td>Anticoagulants, clotting-factor problems, trauma</td>
          <td>Swollen painful limb or joint; limited movement</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Easy bleeding + jaundice or swelling</strong></td>
          <td>Liver disease or severe malnutrition</td>
          <td>Yellow eyes/skin, abdominal swelling, spider veins</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Frequent nose/gum bleeding, heavy menses (if applicable), family history</strong></td>
          <td>Platelet disorders or von Willebrand disease</td>
          <td>Bleeding since youth, relatives with similar issues</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Self-care that’s usually safe</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">For minor bruises (no red flags)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Cold pack</strong> 10–15 minutes at a time during first 24–48 hours</li>
          <li><strong>Rest & elevate</strong> the area; gentle compression if comfortable</li>
          <li>Switch to <strong>warmth</strong> after 48 hours to help reabsorb</li>
          <li>Pain: prefer <strong>acetaminophen</strong>; avoid extra NSAIDs unless advised</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call your clinician soon if:</strong> individual bruises are >5–7 cm, you bruise without injury, or bruising is new/worsening after a medication change.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medication & supplement cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Do not stop</strong> blood thinners or antiplatelets on your own—ask first</li>
          <li>Review for <strong>doubling up</strong> on blood thinners (e.g., aspirin + NSAID)</li>
          <li>Warfarin users: keep INR checks up to date; report new bruising or diet/med changes</li>
          <li>Alcohol increases bleeding risk with many meds—limit or avoid</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">Common supplements that increase bleeding</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fish oil/omega-3, <strong>ginkgo</strong>, garlic, ginger, ginseng, turmeric, high-dose vitamin E</li>
          <li>Tell your clinician and dentist about all <strong>OTCs and herbs</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Skin protection -->
  <section id="skin" class="section">
    <h2>Skin protection & prevention</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Wear <strong>long sleeves</strong> or soft forearm guards for house/yard work</li>
          <li>Moisturize daily; treat dry/cracked skin early to reduce tears</li>
          <li>Declutter walkways, add padding to sharp furniture edges</li>
          <li>Sun protection (clothing/sunscreen) to reduce skin thinning over time</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Diet basics:</strong> Balanced meals with adequate <strong>protein</strong> and vitamin C–rich foods (citrus, berries, peppers) support skin and vessel health. Avoid extreme diets.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

          <td>Identify pattern, triggers, family history</td>

        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Labs</strong></td>
          <td>Check blood & clotting</td>

          <td><strong>Imaging</strong></td>

    <p class="small">Treatment targets the cause—e.g., medication adjustments, correcting deficiencies, or treating liver/platelet disorders.</p>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Photos with a coin for size, and <strong>location</strong> of each bruise</li>
          <li>Any <strong>trigger</strong> (bump, fall, new exercise) or none</li>
          <li>Bleeding from gums/nose, urine/stool color, unusual fatigue</li>
          <li>All <strong>medications & supplements</strong>, dose changes, and alcohol intake</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Bring a short log and photos to appointments—this speeds evaluation and safer med adjustments.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support with dignity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Review medication/supplement list together; organize in a weekly pillbox</li>
          <li>Pad sharp furniture edges; add night-lights and clear walkways</li>
          <li>Encourage long sleeves for chores and moisturizing to protect skin</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call the clinician for:</strong> new widespread bruising, petechiae, persistent nose/gum bleeds, black/tarry stools, or any head injury on blood thinners.
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is bruising more common with age?</p>
        <p>Yes. Skin thins and blood vessels are more fragile. Forearm/hand bruises after minor bumps are common, but sudden changes still deserve review.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Which medicines commonly worsen bruising?</p>
        <p>Blood thinners (warfarin, apixaban, rivaroxaban), antiplatelets (aspirin, clopidogrel), NSAIDs, steroids, and some antidepressants (SSRIs/SNRIs).</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do vitamins help?</p>
        <p>A balanced diet with enough protein and vitamin C supports skin/vessels. Avoid high-dose supplements (like vitamin E) that can increase bleeding without advice.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long should a bruise last?</p>
        <p>Most fade within 1–3 weeks, changing from purple/blue to green/yellow/brown. Bruises that grow, stay very painful, or don’t fade should be checked.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
