  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What balance trouble can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Feeling <strong>unsteady, dizzy, or veering</strong> is common with aging but not “just age.” Vision, inner ear, nerves, muscles, joints, blood pressure, and medicines all play a role—tuning each can reduce falls.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Words matter:</strong> “Spinning” (vertigo) suggests inner ear; “lightheaded” when standing suggests blood pressure; “feet feel numb” points to neuropathy; “legs weak or heavy” may be strength or nerve issues.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>FAST stroke signs:</strong> face droop, arm weakness, speech trouble, sudden severe headache</li>
        <li>Sudden imbalance with <strong>double vision</strong>, new severe headache, trouble speaking/swallowing</li>
        <li>Head injury with loss of consciousness, worsening headache, vomiting, confusion, or blood thinners on board</li>
        <li>Chest pain, palpitations, fainting, or shortness of breath with dizziness</li>
        <li>New inability to walk without support or rapidly worsening weakness/numbness</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can signal stroke/TIA, brain bleed, heart rhythm problems, or other emergencies.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Causes of unsteadiness">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Category</th><th>Examples</th><th>Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Inner ear (vestibular)</td>
          <td>BPPV, vestibular neuritis, Ménière’s</td>
          <td>Spinning with head turns/rolling in bed; nausea; ear fullness or ringing</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Blood pressure/heart</td>
          <td>Orthostatic hypotension, arrhythmias, dehydration, anemia</td>
          <td>Lightheaded on standing, “graying out,” fast heartbeats</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Nerves & feet</td>
          <td>Peripheral neuropathy (diabetes/B12), foot pain/deformity</td>
          <td>Numb soles, burning, poor position sense; worse in the dark</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Brain & movement</td>
          <td>Stroke/TIA, Parkinson’s, cerebellar disorders, normal-pressure hydrocephalus</td>
          <td>Shuffling gait, tremor, slurred speech, wide-based or ataxic walk</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Vision & proprioception</td>
          <td>Cataracts, macular degeneration, poor lighting/contrast</td>
          <td>Trouble in dim light/glare; misses steps/curbs</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Muscle & joints</td>
          <td>Leg weakness, hip/knee arthritis, deconditioning</td>
          <td>Difficulty rising from chair, stairs, or carrying items</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Medications</td>
          <td>Sedatives, sleep aids, opioids, anticholinergics, some BP meds, alcohol</td>
          <td>Worse after dose changes or at night; daytime sleepiness</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-checks -->
  <section id="self-check" class="section">
    <h2>Simple self-checks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">At home (if no red flags)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stand up slowly. If dizzy, sit and try again later; sip water</li>
          <li>Check shoes: low heel, firm heel cup, non-slip sole; avoid backless slippers</li>
          <li>Do a <strong>chair stand</strong> test: from a chair w/o using hands—how many in 30 seconds? Track weekly</li>
          <li>Note triggers: rolling in bed (BPPV), standing quickly (orthostatic), dark rooms (vision/neuropathy)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Bring to visits:</strong> list of falls/near-falls, medication list (including sleep aids), BP readings (sitting & standing), and any ear/vision changes.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Home safety -->
  <section id="home" class="section">
    <h2>Home safety upgrades</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Immediate fixes</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Light the path</strong> bed → bathroom with motion night-lights</li>
          <li>Remove <strong>throw rugs</strong>, secure cords, clear clutter</li>
          <li>Install <strong>grab bars</strong> (not towel racks) and non-slip mats in bathroom</li>
          <li>Mark stair edges with high-contrast tape; rails on both sides</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Vision boosts:</strong> brighter, diffuse lighting; high-contrast switches/handles; routine eye/hearing checks.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Exercises -->
  <section id="exercise" class="section">
    <h2>Exercises that help</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Weekly mix</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Balance daily:</strong> heel-to-toe walk, single-leg stands near counter, weight shifts</li>
          <li><strong>Strength 2–3×/wk:</strong> chair stands, wall pushups, step-ups, resistance bands</li>
          <li><strong>Gait & posture:</strong> brisk walks (with device if needed), short bouts after meals</li>
          <li><strong>Programs:</strong> Otago, Tai Chi, or PT-guided vestibular rehab for vertigo</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Safety first:</strong> Practice near a counter or sturdy chair. Stop for chest pain, severe breathlessness, sudden weakness, or new neuro symptoms.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Devices -->
  <section id="devices" class="section">
    <h2>Assistive devices & footwear</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Choosing help</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Cane:</strong> held in opposite hand from weak/painful leg; elbow slightly bent</li>
          <li><strong>Walker:</strong> four-point or rollator for more support; adjust height so shoulders stay relaxed</li>
          <li>Add <strong>non-slip tips</strong>; keep paths wide enough for device</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Footwear:</strong> closed-back shoes, thin firm soles, good traction; avoid thick memory-foam insoles if they reduce stability.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds & BP -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Blood pressure & medication tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Orthostatic check (at home)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Measure BP/heart rate after 5 min sitting → stand and recheck at 1 and 3 minutes</li>
          <li>Drops in BP with symptoms suggest orthostatic hypotension—share numbers with your clinician</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medication review</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Ask about tapering <strong>sedatives/sleep aids</strong>, daytime opioids, and strong anticholinergics</li>
          <li>Time blood pressure meds to avoid morning lightheadedness (per clinician)</li>
          <li>Stay hydrated unless on fluid restrictions; rise slowly from bed/chair</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Clinical evaluation for unsteadiness">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Purpose</th><th>Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Clarify dizziness type & fall risks</td>
          <td>Gait, strength, neuropathy signs, cerebellar tests, ear/eye exam</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Orthostatics & ECG</strong></td>
          <td>Check BP changes & rhythm</td>
          <td>Standing BP/HR, ECG; labs for dehydration/anemia</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Vestibular tests</strong></td>
          <td>Identify BPPV/inner-ear causes</td>
          <td>Dix–Hallpike maneuver; Epley repositioning if positive</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Labs</strong></td>
          <td>Reversible causes</td>
          <td>B12, thyroid (TSH), A1c, CBC/CMP; medication level checks when relevant</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging</strong></td>
          <td>Neurologic/structural causes</td>
          <td>CT/MRI brain if neuro deficits, new severe headache, or trauma</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Referrals</strong></td>
          <td>Targeted therapy</td>
          <td>Physical therapy (balance/vestibular), neurology, cardiology, ENT, optometry</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Treatment depends on cause—e.g., Epley for BPPV, medication adjustments, PT balance program, vision/hearing updates, or cardiac/neurologic care.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Fall diary:</strong> date, time, what you were doing, shoes/device used, lighting</li>
          <li>Symptoms: spinning vs lightheaded; triggers (standing, rolling, dark rooms)</li>
          <li>BP readings (sitting/standing), new meds or dose changes</li>
          <li>Exercise completed (balance/strength) and any near-falls</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Share notes and videos of your walk with your clinician or PT—they help tailor treatment quickly.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support with dignity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Encourage slow position changes and device use; model the setup</li>
          <li>Pair meds/meal times with brief walks and simple balance drills</li>
          <li>Keep paths lit and clear; check footwear and sock traction</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call the clinician for:</strong> any fall with head hit, new neuro symptoms, fainting spells, chest pain, or rapidly worsening unsteadiness.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What’s one quick fall-risk reducer?</p>
        <p>Light the bed-to-bathroom path with motion night-lights and remove throw rugs today.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Could my pills be the cause?</p>
        <p>Yes—sedatives, sleep aids, some BP meds, and opioids commonly worsen balance. Ask for a medication review before stopping anything.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I know if it’s vertigo?</p>
        <p>Spinning triggered by rolling in bed or looking up/down suggests BPPV—often treated with the Epley maneuver by a clinician or PT.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do canes/walkers make people weaker?</p>
        <p>No—when fitted correctly, they prevent falls and let you move more, which maintains strength.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
