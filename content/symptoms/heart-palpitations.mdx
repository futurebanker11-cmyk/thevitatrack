  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What palpitations can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> <strong>Palpitations</strong> are feelings that the heart is racing, skipping, pounding, or fluttering. Many are harmless (extra beats with stress or caffeine). In older adults, however, palpitations can signal <strong>atrial fibrillation (AFib)</strong>, <strong>supraventricular tachycardia (SVT)</strong>, <strong>thyroid or anemia</strong>, dehydration, medication effects, or heart disease.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Describe:</strong> sudden vs gradual start, regular vs irregular, how long it lasts, triggers (caffeine, alcohol, exertion, stress), and symptoms like chest discomfort, shortness of breath, dizziness, or fainting.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Palpitations with <strong>chest pain/pressure</strong>, shortness of breath, or fainting</li>
        <li>Very fast heart rate (&gt;150 bpm) or persistent racing with <strong>dizziness/weakness</strong></li>
        <li>New palpitations after a <strong>heart attack</strong> or in known heart failure</li>
        <li>Palpitations with <strong>stroke symptoms</strong> (face droop, arm weakness, speech trouble)</li>
        <li>Palpitations after taking new stimulants, illicit drugs, or excess alcohol</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can indicate dangerous rhythms, AFib with rapid rate, ischemia, or low blood pressure/oxygen.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Palpitations causes">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Cause</th><th>Typical features</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Premature beats</strong> (PACs/PVCs)</td>
          <td>“Skip” or “thump,” brief flutters; often at rest or after caffeine/stress</td>
          <td>Usually benign; reduce stimulants, manage stress</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Atrial fibrillation</strong> (AFib)</td>
          <td>Irregularly irregular pulse, fatigue, breathlessness</td>
          <td>Stroke risk—needs evaluation for rate control and anticoagulation</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>SVT</strong> (supraventricular tachycardia)</td>
          <td>Sudden start/stop, regular rapid beat (often 150–200 bpm)</td>
          <td>Vagal maneuvers may help; medical care if persistent</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Ventricular arrhythmias</strong></td>
          <td>Rapid pounding with faintness/chest pain, often serious</td>
          <td>Emergency—especially with heart disease history</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Thyroid, anemia, fever</strong></td>
          <td>Racing heart with heat intolerance/weight change (thyroid), pallor (anemia)</td>
          <td>Blood tests (TSH, CBC) guide treatment</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medications & stimulants</strong></td>
          <td>Decongestants, inhalers, some antidepressants, caffeine, alcohol</td>
          <td>Review label doses; avoid energy drinks</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dehydration/electrolytes</strong></td>
          <td>After illness, heat, or diuretics; cramps, dizziness</td>
          <td>Fluids and electrolyte checks (K, Mg)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Anxiety & stress</strong></td>
          <td>Palpitations with worry, tremor, hyperventilation</td>
          <td>Breathing techniques, counseling, rule out medical causes</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Structural heart disease</strong></td>
          <td>History of heart attack, valve disease, cardiomyopathy</td>
          <td>Often needs echo and cardiology input</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-checks -->
  <section id="self-check" class="section">
    <h2>Simple self-checks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">At home (if no red flags)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>When you feel palpitations, <strong>count pulse</strong> for 30 sec × 2; note <em>regular vs irregular</em></li>
          <li>Record triggers: caffeine/alcohol, stress, exertion, dehydration, poor sleep</li>
          <li>Check blood pressure/heart rate if you monitor; note oxygen level if you have a pulse oximeter</li>
          <li>Review new meds: decongestants, inhalers, thyroid dose, supplements</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Bring to visits:</strong> a symptom log with time of day, duration, pulse rate, and associated symptoms (chest discomfort, dizziness, shortness of breath).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Safe steps at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Hydrate</strong> steadily; avoid heavy alcohol or energy drinks</li>
          <li>Limit caffeine (coffee/tea/cola) and nicotine; avoid decongestants with <em>pseudoephedrine</em> unless advised</li>
          <li>Practice <strong>slow breathing</strong> (inhale 4 sec, exhale 6 sec for 2–3 minutes)</li>
          <li>If episodes are sudden and regular, a clinician may teach <strong>vagal maneuvers</strong> (e.g., Valsalva) to try safely</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Heart-smart habits:</strong> consistent sleep, light daily activity, and limiting excess salt (if you have blood pressure or heart failure guidance).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medication & stimulant cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Review with your clinician: <strong>decongestants</strong>, inhaled bronchodilators, thyroid meds, some antidepressants, and high-dose diuretics</li>
          <li>Avoid sudden <strong>stimulant</strong> use (energy drinks, weight-loss pills)</li>
          <li>Ask before starting <strong>herbals</strong> (bitter orange, yohimbine) or high-dose caffeine tablets</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Anticoagulation in AFib:</strong> If diagnosed, your team may discuss blood thinners to reduce stroke risk—do not stop/start without guidance.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Clinical evaluation for palpitations">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Purpose</th><th>Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Characterize rhythm issue & risks</td>
          <td>Onset/offset, regularity, meds/stimulants, thyroid/anemia symptoms</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>ECG</strong></td>
          <td>Identify rhythm during symptoms</td>
          <td>AFib/AFlutter, SVT, PVCs, pre-excitation, ischemic changes</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Ambulatory monitor</strong></td>
          <td>Catch intermittent episodes</td>
          <td>Holter (24–48h), event monitor, patch, or wearable ECG</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Labs</strong></td>
          <td>Find reversible causes</td>
          <td>CBC (anemia), CMP/electrolytes, Mg/K, TSH, troponin if indicated</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Echocardiogram</strong></td>
          <td>Assess structure/function</td>
          <td>Valve disease, heart failure, cardiomyopathy</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Stress testing / imaging</strong></td>
          <td>Ischemia evaluation</td>
          <td>Exercise ECG, nuclear or echo stress if symptoms with exertion</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Management</strong></td>
          <td>Treat rhythm & risks</td>
          <td>Rate/rhythm meds, anticoagulation for AFib, ablation referral for SVT/AFib</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Treatment depends on the cause and your overall heart/bleeding risk profile.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Date/time, duration, and <strong>pulse rate</strong> during episodes</li>
          <li>Regular vs irregular rhythm; triggers and activities</li>
          <li>Symptoms: chest discomfort, breathlessness, dizziness, fainting</li>
          <li>Alcohol, caffeine, and new medications/supplements</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Share logs (and device ECG strips if you have them) at appointments—this speeds diagnosis and avoids unnecessary tests.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support with dignity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Encourage hydration and limit evening alcohol/caffeine</li>
          <li>Help organize medication lists and timing (especially thyroid and inhalers)</li>
          <li>Know the <strong>red flags</strong> and how to check a pulse calmly</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call the clinician for:</strong> increasing frequency, prolonged episodes, new chest pain or fainting, or if an at-home ECG flags AFib or high-risk rhythm.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are palpitations always dangerous?</p>
        <p>No. Many are harmless extra beats. But palpitations with chest pain, fainting, breathlessness, or an irregular rapid pulse should be checked promptly.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What can I do during an episode?</p>
        <p>Sit or lie down, breathe slowly, sip water. If your clinician has taught a safe vagal maneuver and the rhythm is regular and rapid, you may try it. Seek care for red flags.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do caffeine and alcohol matter?</p>
        <p>Yes—both can trigger palpitations in some people. Try limiting and see if episodes decrease.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What is AFib and why is it important?</p>
        <p>Atrial fibrillation is an irregular rhythm that increases stroke risk. Management may include rate/rhythm control and blood thinners depending on your risk.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
