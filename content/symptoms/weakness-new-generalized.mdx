  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What new weakness can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> “Weakness” may be <strong>true loss of strength</strong> (hard to move a limb) or <strong>fatigue</strong> (low energy). New or worsening weakness deserves attention because it can signal infection, dehydration, <strong>stroke/TIA</strong>, heart or lung problems, <strong>electrolyte</strong> or blood sugar shifts, <strong>anemia</strong>, low <strong>thyroid</strong>, medication effects, or malnutrition.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p>Clues: one-sided weakness or facial droop (neurologic), fever/cough (infection), black stools (bleeding), palpitations/chest pain (heart), confusion or thirst (glucose/electrolytes), tingling/numbness (nerve), or new medicines (sedatives, blood pressure, diabetes pills).</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Stroke signs:</strong> face droop, arm/leg weakness or numbness on one side, slurred speech, sudden trouble seeing, sudden severe headache, new imbalance.</li>
        <li>Chest pain/pressure, severe shortness of breath, fainting, or a heart rate that is very fast or very slow.</li>
        <li>Fever with confusion, severe dehydration (very dry mouth, little urine), or rapidly worsening weakness.</li>
        <li>Black/tarry stools or vomiting blood; new inability to stand or frequent falls.</li>
        <li>Very high or very low blood sugar with confusion, shaking, or sweating.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Time-sensitive conditions need urgent care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Cause</th><th scope="col">Typical features</th><th scope="col">Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Infections</strong> (UTI, pneumonia, COVID, cellulitis)</td>
          <td>Weakness with fever, cough, burning urine, or redness/swelling</td>
          <td>Often sudden; may cause confusion or falls in older adults</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dehydration / electrolytes</strong> (Na/K/Mg)</td>
          <td>Thirst, dizziness, cramps, low urine</td>
          <td>Vomiting/diarrhea, diuretics, heat, poor intake</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Glucose problems</strong> (hypo-/hyperglycemia)</td>
          <td>Shaking, sweating, confusion or excessive thirst/urination</td>
          <td>Diabetes meds changes, infections, missed meals</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Anemia</strong></td>
          <td>Fatigue, shortness of breath, paleness, rapid pulse</td>
          <td>Bleeding (GI), poor diet, B12/folate deficiency, kidney disease</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Thyroid disorders</strong> (low or high)</td>
          <td>Low: cold, constipation, slow pulse; High: tremor, heat intolerance</td>
          <td>TSH abnormal; medication dose changes</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Cardiac / pulmonary</strong> (HF, COPD flare)</td>
          <td>Breathlessness, swelling, low exercise tolerance</td>
          <td>Weight gain/edema, wheeze/cough, low oxygen</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Neurologic</strong> (stroke/TIA, neuropathy, spinal)</td>
          <td>One-sided weakness, speech/vision changes, gait issues</td>
          <td>Sudden onset or progressive; back/neck pain with deficits</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medications</strong></td>
          <td>Drowsy, dizzy, low BP, falls</td>
          <td>New sedatives, opioids, benzodiazepines, some BP meds, anticholinergics</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Malnutrition / vitamin deficiency</strong></td>
          <td>Weight loss, frailty, muscle loss</td>
          <td>Low protein, B12, vitamin D; poor appetite, dental issues</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Depression / sleep problems</strong></td>
          <td>Low motivation, poor sleep or apnea</td>
          <td>Snoring, daytime sleepiness, mood changes</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- First steps -->
  <section id="first-steps" class="section">
    <h2>First steps at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Safety first</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use a sturdy chair with arms to rise; clear paths; avoid ladders and slippery surfaces.</li>
          <li>If one side feels weak or vision/speech changes appear, seek emergency care.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Check basics</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>If you monitor BP, pulse, oxygen, and temperature—write them down.</li>
          <li>Check blood sugar if you have diabetes; treat lows per your plan.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">Fluids & fuel</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Small sips of water or oral rehydration; a light snack if you’ve missed meals.</li>
          <li>Hold alcohol; limit caffeine if jittery or dehydrated.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Call your clinician</strong> for new weakness lasting more than a day, fever, weight loss, black stools, medication changes, or repeated falls.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">Purpose</th><th scope="col">Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Vitals & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Identify urgency and likely source</td>
          <td>BP (sitting/standing), pulse, oxygen, temp; heart/lungs; focused neuro exam</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Labs</strong></td>
          <td>Check blood, electrolytes, endocrine</td>
          <td>CBC (anemia), CMP (Na/K/creatinine), glucose/A1C, TSH, B12/folate, vitamin D; CK if muscle injury</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Urine tests</strong></td>
          <td>Look for infection/dehydration</td>
          <td>Urinalysis ± culture; specific gravity/ketones</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Heart/lung tests</strong></td>
          <td>Cardiopulmonary causes</td>
          <td>ECG, chest X-ray, BNP, echocardiogram as indicated</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging (if neurologic)</strong></td>
          <td>Rule out stroke/structural issues</td>
          <td>CT/MRI brain; spine imaging if cord/root signs</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medication review</strong></td>
          <td>Remove contributors</td>
          <td>Deprescribe or adjust sedatives, anticholinergics, high-dose BP meds, etc.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Treat the cause</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fluids/electrolyte correction, antibiotics for confirmed infections, iron/B12/folate for anemia, thyroid replacement or adjustment.</li>
          <li>Optimize glucose, review and simplify medicines.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Rebuild strength safely</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Physical therapy for balance/strength; progressive walking plan.</li>
          <li>Protein at each meal (eggs, beans, fish, yogurt), vitamin D if low.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Supportive care</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Treat pain, sleep apnea evaluation if daytime sleepiness, mood support for depression.</li>
          <li>Home safety: grab bars, night lights, shoe review, assistive devices.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Plans are individualized—follow your clinician’s guidance and share your symptom diary.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Onset time, triggers (illness, new medicines), fever or pain.</li>
          <li>Daily weight, fluids, meals, and a simple activity log.</li>
          <li>If available: BP (sitting/standing), pulse, oxygen, and glucose.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Share the log and medication list at visits—this speeds diagnosis and safer treatment.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is weakness the same as fatigue?</p>
        <p>Not always. True weakness is loss of strength (e.g., can’t lift a limb); fatigue is low energy. Both matter and can occur together.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can medicines cause sudden weakness?</p>
        <p>Yes—sedatives, opioids, benzodiazepines, some blood pressure or bladder medicines, and drug interactions can all contribute.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What food helps?</p>
        <p>Regular meals with protein at each sitting, fluids during the day, and correction of deficiencies (iron, B12, vitamin D) as advised.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When is imaging needed?</p>
        <p>If weakness is one-sided, sudden, or accompanied by speech/vision changes, severe headache, or spinal red flags—seek urgent imaging.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
