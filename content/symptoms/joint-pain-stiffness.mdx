  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What joint pain can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Joint pain and stiffness are common with age. <strong>Osteoarthritis</strong> (cartilage wear) is most frequent, but <strong>inflammatory arthritis</strong> (e.g., rheumatoid), <strong>crystal arthritis</strong> (gout/pseudogout), injuries, infections, and medication side effects can also cause symptoms.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Clues:</strong> Morning stiffness lasting &gt;30 minutes suggests inflammation; pain worse with activity and better with rest points to osteoarthritis. A <strong>single red, hot, swollen joint</strong> can be gout or infection and needs timely care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Fever</strong> with a red, hot, very painful joint (possible septic arthritis)</li>
        <li>Severe <strong>trauma</strong> with deformity, inability to bear weight, or numbness/blue color</li>
        <li>New <strong>weakness</strong>, loss of bowel/bladder control with back pain (possible spinal emergency)</li>
        <li>Uncontrolled pain with swelling that rapidly worsens</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may require urgent imaging, joint aspiration, antibiotics, or surgery.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Patterns -->
  <section id="patterns" class="section">
    <h2>Patterns & common causes</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Joint pain patterns and causes">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Pattern</th><th scope="col">More likely</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Single joint (acute)</strong></td>
          <td>Gout/pseudogout, infection, injury</td>
          <td><em>Very</em> tender, hot, swollen → urgent eval; crystals vs infection often needs aspiration</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Wear-and-tear joints</strong> (knees, hips, spine, thumbs)</td>
          <td>Osteoarthritis</td>
          <td>Pain worse later in day/after activity; brief morning stiffness; creaking</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Symmetric small joints of hands/feet</strong></td>
          <td>Rheumatoid or other inflammatory arthritis</td>
          <td>Morning stiffness &gt;30–60 min, swelling, warmth; may need rheumatology</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Shoulder/hip aching with stiffness</strong></td>
          <td>Polymyalgia rheumatica (PMR)</td>
          <td>Age &gt;50; worse in morning; sometimes with headaches/vision issues (see care)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Back pain with shooting leg pain</strong></td>
          <td>Sciatica/nerve irritation</td>
          <td>Worse with cough/sneeze; numbness/weakness → timely eval</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medication-related</strong></td>
          <td>Aromatase inhibitors, statins (muscle ache), fluoroquinolones (tendons)</td>
          <td>Review timing vs start date; do not stop meds without guidance</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">
      <strong>Call your clinician soon if:</strong> pain lasts &gt;2–4 weeks, you notice joint deformity/instability, morning stiffness &gt;60 minutes, or repeated flares of gout-like pain.
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Self-care & movement</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If no red-flags</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Relative rest</strong> 24–48h for flares; then gradual activity</li>
          <li><strong>Ice</strong> for hot/swollen joints (10–15 min at a time); <strong>heat</strong> for stiffness/muscle tightness</li>
          <li><strong>Gentle range-of-motion</strong> daily; short walks, stationary cycling, water exercise</li>
          <li><strong>Support</strong>: cane or walker on the <em>opposite</em> side of a painful hip/knee; braces as advised</li>
          <li><strong>Sleep</strong> with pillows for joint alignment (between knees/under arms)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Special cases</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Gout flare:</strong> rest, ice, elevate; avoid alcohol/red meat/seafood during flare; call about meds (colchicine/NSAID/steroid)</li>
          <li><strong>PMR clues</strong> (shoulder/hip stiffness + fatigue): ask clinician—blood tests and specific treatment help</li>
          <li><strong>Jaw/scalp pain or vision changes</strong> with PMR symptoms → urgent eval (possible giant cell arteritis)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small">This page is educational and not a diagnosis. Seek urgent care for the warning signs above.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine options & cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Medicines for joint pain">
          <thead>
            <tr><th scope="col">Option</th><th scope="col">Helps with</th><th scope="col">Notes for seniors</th></tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Topicals</strong> (diclofenac gel, lidocaine patches)</td>
              <td>Osteoarthritis, localized pain</td>
              <td>Lower systemic risk; apply as directed, avoid on broken skin</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Acetaminophen</strong></td>
              <td>Mild–moderate pain</td>
              <td>Max generally ≤3,000 mg/day (less if liver disease/low weight); check combo products</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>NSAIDs</strong> (ibuprofen/naproxen)</td>
              <td>Pain, inflammation</td>
              <td>Use lowest dose/shortest time; avoid with ulcers/bleeding risk, kidney disease, anticoagulants, or heart failure unless advised</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Gout flare meds</strong></td>
              <td>Acute gout</td>
              <td>Colchicine, NSAID, or short steroid course—prescriber will screen interactions</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Injections</strong></td>
              <td>Knee/shoulder OA or inflammation</td>
              <td>Corticosteroid or hyaluronic acid in selected cases</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Disease-modifying drugs</strong></td>
              <td>Inflammatory arthritis</td>
              <td>Rheumatology-guided (e.g., methotrexate, biologics); monitoring needed</td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Do not</strong> start or stop prescription medicines on your own. If pain persists or function is limited, ask about physical therapy and targeted treatments.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Food & lifestyle tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Helpful choices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Anti-inflammatory pattern:</strong> vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes, nuts, olive oil; fish 1–2×/week</li>
          <li>Adequate <strong>vitamin D & calcium</strong> for bones (diet or supplements as advised)</li>
          <li>Weight management—even 5–10% loss eases knee/hip load</li>
          <li>Stay hydrated; balanced activity-rest cycle</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Limit/avoid</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Excess alcohol (especially during gout flares), high-purine foods if gout-prone (organ meats, large seafood/alcohol servings)</li>
          <li>Smoking—slows healing and worsens bone/joint health</li>
          <li>Prolonged inactivity; long sitting without movement breaks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

          <td>Locate pattern & risks</td>

        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging</strong></td>
          <td>Assess damage/injury</td>
          <td>X-ray for OA; ultrasound for effusion; MRI if soft tissue/ligament concern</td>

        <tr>
          <td><strong>Treatment</strong></td>
          <td>Control pain & preserve function</td>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Protection & prevention</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular <strong>low-impact exercise</strong> (walking, cycling, tai chi, water aerobics)</li>
          <li><strong>Strengthen</strong> hips, thighs, and core; flexibility work for hamstrings/calves</li>
          <li>Use <strong>proper footwear</strong> and shock-absorbing insoles if needed</li>
          <li>Home safety to prevent falls: lighting, clear pathways, grab bars</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Consistency beats intensity—small daily movement maintains joints, balance, and independence.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Pain/stiffness score (0–10), morning stiffness duration</li>
          <li>Which joints are swollen/red and any triggers (foods, activity)</li>
          <li>Meds taken (name/dose/time) and response/side effects</li>
          <li>Function goals: steps, sit-to-stand counts, stairs, sleep quality</li>
        </ul>

      <div class="card calm">
        Bring this log to visits—it speeds diagnosis and tailored treatment.

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heat or ice?</p>
        <p><strong>Ice</strong> for hot, swollen joints (flares). <strong>Heat</strong> for stiffness or muscle tightness. Use 10–15 minutes at a time.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need an X-ray?</p>
        <p>Often for persistent pain or after injury. X-rays show arthritis and fractures; MRI/ultrasound are for soft-tissue or unclear cases.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are glucosamine or turmeric helpful?</p>
        <p>Some people report mild benefit; results are mixed. If you try them, discuss interactions (especially with blood thinners).</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When is surgery considered?</p>
        <p>When pain and disability persist despite optimized therapy (PT, meds, injections) and imaging shows severe joint damage.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
