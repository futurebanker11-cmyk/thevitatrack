  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What runny nose/congestion can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Nasal symptoms are usually from <strong>viral infections</strong> (common cold, influenza, <strong>COVID-19</strong>) or <strong>allergies</strong>. Other causes include <strong>acute or chronic sinusitis</strong>, <strong>nonallergic rhinitis</strong> (irritants, temperature, perfumes), <strong>medication effects</strong> (decongestant spray overuse), <strong>nasal polyps</strong>, and <strong>structural issues</strong> (deviated septum). In older adults, symptoms can be milder but complications (dehydration, sinus infection, pneumonia) are more likely with frailty or chronic illness.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Describe:</strong> onset (sudden vs gradual), fever, facial pain/pressure, sore throat/cough, itchy eyes/sneezing (allergy clues), exposure to sick contacts, seasonality, medication changes, and vaccination status (flu/COVID).</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Shortness of breath, chest pain, <strong>confusion</strong>, or blue lips/face</li>
        <li>Severe <strong>headache with neck stiffness</strong>, or persistent high fever</li>
        <li>Eye swelling/redness with <strong>vision changes</strong> or severe facial swelling (orbital/cellulitis risk)</li>
        <li>Symptoms lasting &gt;10 days <em>without improvement</em> or that <strong>worsen after 5–7 days</strong> (possible bacterial sinusitis)</li>
        <li>Repeated <strong>nosebleeds</strong> or bleeding while on blood thinners that won’t stop</li>
        <li>Clear fluid dripping after head injury (possible CSF leak)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can signal serious infection or complications and need urgent care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Runny nose and congestion causes">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Cause</th><th>Typical features</th><th>Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Viral upper-respiratory infection (cold)</strong></td>
          <td>Runny nose → congestion, sore throat, mild cough</td>
          <td>Peaks day 2–3, improves by day 7–10; low fever or none</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Influenza / COVID-19</strong></td>
          <td>Fever, body aches, cough; may start with runny nose</td>
          <td>Test early; higher risk for complications in elders</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Allergic rhinitis</strong></td>
          <td>Itchy eyes/nose, sneezing, clear discharge</td>
          <td>Seasonal or exposure-related; improves with antihistamine/steroid spray</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Acute bacterial sinusitis</strong></td>
          <td>Facial pain/pressure, thick discharge, reduced smell</td>
          <td>&gt;10 days persistent, or severe (fever, purulent discharge) for ≥3–4 days, or “double-worsening”</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Nonallergic/irritant rhinitis</strong></td>
          <td>Drip or stuffiness without itch/sneeze</td>
          <td>Triggered by smoke, perfume, temp changes, spicy foods</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Rhinitis medicamentosa</strong></td>
          <td>Rebound congestion</td>
          <td>After &gt;3 days of topical decongestant sprays (oxymetazoline)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Nasal polyps / chronic sinusitis</strong></td>
          <td>Long-standing congestion, reduced smell</td>
          <td>Often with asthma/aspirin sensitivity; needs evaluation</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Home care that helps</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If no red flags</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Saline</strong> nasal spray or rinse 2–3×/day (see tips below)</li>
          <li><strong>Humidified air</strong>, warm showers, and head elevation at night</li>
          <li>Fluids, warm soups/teas, and rest</li>
          <li>Honey (1 tsp) can ease cough at night (not for diabetics with poor control or aspiration risk)</li>
          <li>Limit smoke/irritants; mask when dusting/cleaning</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Testing:</strong> Consider <em>COVID-19/flu</em> testing early if fever, body aches, or known exposure—results can guide antivirals and isolation.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medication & OTC notes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Intranasal steroid sprays</strong> (fluticasone, budesonide): daily use for allergies/chronic congestion; allow several days for full effect</li>
          <li><strong>Non-sedating antihistamines</strong> (cetirizine, loratadine): best for allergy itch/sneeze/runny nose</li>
          <li><strong>Ipratropium nasal spray</strong>: reduces watery rhinorrhea (not congestion)</li>
          <li><strong>Topical decongestant sprays</strong> (oxymetazoline): quick relief but <strong>limit to 3 days</strong> to avoid rebound</li>
          <li><strong>Oral decongestants</strong> (pseudoephedrine/phenylephrine): <em>use cautiously</em>—can raise BP/heart rate and worsen glaucoma, insomnia, or urinary retention (BPH)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Avoid/Use with caution in older adults:</strong> combination “cold & flu” syrups with <em>first-generation antihistamines</em> (diphenhydramine, chlorpheniramine) due to <strong>drowsiness, falls, confusion, and urinary retention</strong>. Ask your clinician/pharmacist what’s safe for you.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Comfort -->
  <section id="comfort" class="section">
    <h2>Humidifier & saline tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>isotonic saline</strong> (store-bought spray or rinse). For neti pots, use <strong>distilled/boiled then cooled</strong> water and clean devices after each use.</li>
          <li>Lean over sink, head slightly forward; irrigate each nostril, gently blow, repeat</li>
          <li>Run a <strong>cool-mist humidifier</strong> in bedroom; clean tank daily and dry weekly to prevent mold</li>
          <li>Sleep with head elevated on 1–2 pillows; side-lying can ease one-sided blockage</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Allergy control:</strong> keep windows closed on high-pollen days, rinse hair/face after outdoor time, wash bedding weekly in hot water, and consider dust-mite covers for pillows/mattress.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Clinical evaluation for nasal symptoms">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Purpose</th><th>Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Identify viral vs allergic vs bacterial</td>
          <td>Vitals, nasal/pharynx exam, sinus tenderness, chest/ear check</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Rapid tests</strong></td>
          <td>Guide treatment/isolation</td>
          <td>COVID-19/flu tests; sometimes RSV</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Treatment trial</strong></td>
          <td>Symptom control</td>
          <td>Intranasal steroids/antihistamines, saline, short decongestant course</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Antibiotics</strong></td>
          <td>Only when criteria suggest bacterial sinusitis</td>
          <td>Persistent &gt;10 days, severe fever/purulent discharge ≥3–4 days, or double-worsening</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Further evaluation</strong></td>
          <td>Recurrent/chronic cases or complications</td>
          <td>Allergy testing, nasal endoscopy; sinus CT for chronic/complicated cases</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Imaging isn’t needed for routine colds. Antibiotics don’t help viral infections or allergies.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Onset date, fever, and daily symptom scores (congestion, facial pain, cough)</li>
          <li>Color/consistency of discharge (note changes, but color alone doesn’t prove bacterial infection)</li>
          <li>Medication and saline use; any side effects (palpitations, insomnia, dizziness)</li>
          <li>Exposure history (sick contacts, pollen days) and home humidity</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        If symptoms persist &gt;10 days, worsen after initial improvement, or recur &gt;4×/year, bring your log to a visit—it speeds diagnosis.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support with dignity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Offer fluids, warm soups, and help with saline rinses/humidifier care</li>
          <li>Watch for <strong>confusion, dehydration, fast breathing</strong>, or eye/facial swelling</li>
          <li>Review OTCs to avoid sedating antihistamines and risky decongestants in elders</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call the clinician for:</strong> persistent fever &gt;3 days, worsening facial pain, thick discharge &gt;10 days, chest pain/breathlessness, or new ear pain/hearing loss.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does yellow/green mucus mean I need antibiotics?</p>
        <p>Not by itself. Viral colds can cause colored mucus. Antibiotics are considered when symptoms persist &gt;10 days, are severe for ≥3–4 days, or worsen after initial improvement.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What’s the safest first step for congestion?</p>
        <p><strong>Saline rinses/sprays</strong>, humidified air, rest, and hydration. Add an intranasal steroid for allergies/chronic congestion.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are decongestants safe for seniors?</p>
        <p>Use cautiously. Oral decongestants can raise blood pressure and cause urinary retention or insomnia. Limit nasal decongestant sprays to <strong>3 days</strong>.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When should I test for COVID-19/flu?</p>
        <p>Early in illness—especially with fever, aches, known exposure, or high-risk conditions—so treatment and isolation decisions are timely.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
