  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What numbness/tingling can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> “Pins and needles,” burning, or reduced feeling may come from <strong>nerve irritation or damage</strong>, a <strong>pinched nerve</strong> in the spine or wrist, <strong>vitamin issues</strong> (like B12), <strong>diabetes-related neuropathy</strong>, circulation problems, or medicines. Pattern (where, when, both sides vs one side) helps find the cause.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p>Many causes are manageable with foot protection, blood sugar control, B12 correction if low, wrist/elbow unloading, gentle movement, and targeted therapy.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Sudden <strong>one-sided numbness</strong> with face droop, slurred speech, vision change, or weakness (possible stroke/TIA)</li>
        <li>New numbness with <strong>loss of bladder/bowel control</strong> or severe back pain (possible spinal cord compression)</li>
        <li>Rapidly spreading numbness/weakness, trouble breathing or swallowing</li>
        <li>Severe numbness after <strong>injury</strong> or with a <strong>cold, pale limb</strong> (circulation emergency)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These need urgent medical care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Area</th><th scope="col">Examples</th><th scope="col">Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Peripheral neuropathy</strong></td>
          <td>Diabetes, B12/folate deficiency, thyroid disease, kidney disease, alcohol, chemotherapy</td>
          <td>Glove-and-stocking pattern in feet/hands; burning/tingling; reduced vibration</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Nerve entrapment</strong></td>
          <td>Carpal tunnel (median), ulnar neuropathy (elbow), peroneal (knee), tarsal tunnel</td>
          <td>Numbness in specific fingers/foot areas; worse with positions (wrist bend, elbow flex)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Radiculopathy (pinched nerve in spine)</strong></td>
          <td>Cervical/lumbar disc or arthritis narrowing</td>
          <td>Neck/back pain radiating down arm/leg; cough/sneeze worsens; dermatomal pattern</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Vascular</strong></td>
          <td>Peripheral artery disease, Raynaud’s</td>
          <td>Cold, pale feet; pain with walking that eases with rest; color change to cold</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Other</strong></td>
          <td>Anxiety hyperventilation, shingles, autoimmune, infections</td>
          <td>Episodes with stress/breathing fast; rash on one side (shingles)</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

  <!-- First steps -->
  <section id="first-steps" class="section">
    <h2>First steps at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Check patterns</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Which fingers/toes? One side or both? Constant or position-based?</li>
          <li>Any neck/back pain, recent falls, new shoes or repetitive wrist/elbow pressure?</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">Gentle care</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep feet warm, dry, and protected; avoid walking barefoot.</li>
          <li>Short walks and ankle/hand range-of-motion a few times daily.</li>
          <li>Neutral wrist at night (soft brace if advised); avoid elbow resting on hard edges.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Call your clinician</strong> if symptoms are new, worsening, or affect balance or hand use.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & foot care</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Daily foot check for blisters, cuts, color changes—especially with diabetes.</li>
          <li>Supportive, well-fitting shoes; cushioned socks; trim nails straight across.</li>
          <li>Home fall-proofing: clear cords, night lights, handrails, non-slip mats.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Reduced sensation raises injury risk—protect skin and check inside shoes before wearing.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Address the cause</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Improve blood sugar if diabetic; treat B12/folate deficiency; adjust thyroid levels.</li>
          <li>Unload compressed nerves (wrist/elbow/knee) with splints or ergonomic changes.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Pain & sensation management</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Topicals (lidocaine/capsaicin); selected oral agents for neuropathic pain when appropriate.</li>
          <li>Physical/occupational therapy for balance, hand function, and safe mobility.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Vitamin supplements (B12, folate) only if low or advised. Avoid high-dose B6 unless directed.</li>
          <li>Injections or surgery may be considered for severe entrapment or refractory radiculopathy.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Medicines and procedures are individualized to risks and goals—follow your clinician’s plan.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Location (fingers/toes), timing (night/day), triggers (position, activity).</li>
          <li>Associated symptoms: weakness, pain, back/neck pain, color/temperature change.</li>
          <li>Blood sugar readings (if diabetic), new medicines/supplements.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does numbness always mean nerve damage?</p>
        <p>No—temporary pressure or posture can cause it. Persistent or progressive symptoms need evaluation.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can B12 fix tingling?</p>
        <p>Only if B12 is low or absorption is poor. Your clinician can test levels and advise the right form/dose.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Which wrist brace for hand tingling?</p>
        <p>A soft neutral-splint worn at night helps many with carpal tunnel. Proper fit and posture changes matter too.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is walking safe with numb feet?</p>
        <p>Yes, with protection: supportive shoes, indoor non-slip paths, and balance practice. Ask about PT if unsteady.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
