  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What a sore throat can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Throat pain is most often due to <strong>viral infections</strong> (common cold), <strong>influenza/COVID-19</strong>, or <strong>post-nasal drip</strong> from allergies. Other causes include <strong>strep throat</strong> (less common in seniors), <strong>acid reflux/LPR</strong>, <strong>dry air/irritants</strong> (smoke, perfumes), <strong>thrush</strong> (yeast—more likely with inhaled steroids/antibiotics or diabetes), and less often <strong>abscesses</strong> or <strong>epiglottitis</strong> (emergencies).</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Describe:</strong> fever, cough, nasal symptoms, trouble swallowing, voice changes, rash, sick contacts, recent travel, reflux symptoms, inhaled steroid use, and vaccination/testing for COVID/flu.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Trouble breathing</strong>, noisy breathing, or drooling/inability to swallow</li>
        <li>“<strong>Hot potato</strong>” muffled voice, severe one-sided throat pain, <strong>trismus</strong> (can’t open mouth)</li>
        <li>High fever with severe neck stiffness, confusion, or rapidly worsening swelling</li>
        <li>Severe dehydration (very low urine, dizziness, fainting)</li>
        <li>Throat pain after <strong>choking</strong> or chemical/thermal exposure (pill esophagitis, burns)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can signal <em>epiglottitis</em>, <em>peritonsillar/retropharyngeal abscess</em>, airway compromise, or serious infection—seek urgent care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Sore throat causes">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Cause</th><th>Typical features</th><th>Clues</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Viral pharyngitis</strong></td>
          <td>Scratchy/burning throat, cough, runny nose</td>
          <td>Improves in 3–7 days; supportive care</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Influenza / COVID-19</strong></td>
          <td>Fever, aches, cough ± sore throat</td>
          <td>Test early to guide antivirals/isolation</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Strep throat</strong></td>
          <td>Sudden pain, fever, swollen tender neck nodes</td>
          <td>Usually no cough; may have red “strawberry” throat or scarlatiniform rash</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Post-nasal drip (allergy/sinus)</strong></td>
          <td>Morning soreness, throat clearing</td>
          <td>Itchy eyes/sneeze; improves with saline/steroids</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Reflux / LPR</strong></td>
          <td>Hoarseness, throat clearing, worse after meals/lying down</td>
          <td>Heartburn or sour taste; night symptoms</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Thrush (candida)</strong></td>
          <td>White patches, burning, altered taste</td>
          <td>Risk: inhaled steroids, antibiotics, diabetes, dentures</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Peritonsillar abscess</strong></td>
          <td>Severe unilateral pain, fever, muffled voice</td>
          <td>Trismus, uvula deviation—urgent evaluation</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Irritants/dry air</strong></td>
          <td>Scratchy throat without fever</td>
          <td>Smoke, perfumes, mouth-breathing, dehydration</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medication effects</strong></td>
          <td>Dry mouth/irritation, cough</td>
          <td>ACE inhibitors (cough), anticholinergics (dryness)</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Soothing home care</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If no red flags</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Warm salt-water gargles</strong> 3–4×/day (½ tsp salt in 1 cup warm water)</li>
          <li><strong>Fluids</strong>: warm tea/broth or cold options (popsicles) as tolerated</li>
          <li><strong>Humidified air</strong>, rest your voice, avoid smoke/irritants</li>
          <li><strong>Honey</strong> 1 tsp for cough/throat (avoid if poorly controlled diabetes)</li>
          <li>For reflux: small meals, avoid late-night eating, elevate head of bed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call your clinician</strong> if pain lasts &gt;3–5 days, you have fever &gt;38.3°C (101°F), painful/swollen neck nodes, rash, or exposure to strep/COVID/flu.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medication & OTC notes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Acetaminophen</strong> can reduce pain/fever (mind total daily dose from all products)</li>
          <li><strong>NSAIDs</strong> may help short-term but can irritate stomach/kidneys or interact with blood thinners—ask first</li>
          <li><strong>Lozenges/sprays</strong> with menthol or local anesthetic provide brief relief; avoid excessive benzocaine (rare methemoglobinemia)</li>
          <li><strong>Allergy/post-nasal drip</strong>: intranasal steroids + non-sedating antihistamines</li>
          <li><strong>Thrush</strong>: needs antifungal from a clinician; rinse mouth after inhaled steroids</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Antibiotics:</strong> Only if bacterial causes (e.g., strep) are confirmed/suspected—testing guides treatment. They don’t help viral sore throats.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Comfort -->
  <section id="comfort" class="section">
    <h2>Gargle & humidifier tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Gargle for 20–30 seconds, 3–4 times daily; don’t swallow the salt water</li>
          <li>Try <strong>warm water + honey/lemon</strong> or <strong>chamomile/ginger</strong> tea</li>
          <li>Use a <strong>cool-mist humidifier</strong>; clean and dry tank daily to prevent mold</li>
          <li>Consider sugar-free lozenges to stimulate saliva if dry mouth</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Voice rest:</strong> speak softly (not whispering), sip water often, and avoid throat clearing—try a swallow or sip instead.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Clinical evaluation for sore throat">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Purpose</th><th>Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Identify likely cause & severity</td>
          <td>Throat/tonsil look, neck nodes, hydration, airway assessment</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Rapid tests</strong></td>
          <td>Guide treatment & isolation</td>
          <td>Rapid strep test ± culture; COVID-19/flu tests when appropriate</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Targeted labs/imaging</strong></td>
          <td>Complications or atypical cases</td>
          <td>Monospot (rare in elders), CBC; neck ultrasound/CT if abscess suspected</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Treatment</strong></td>
          <td>Relieve symptoms & treat cause</td>
          <td>Analgesics, steroids for severe swelling, antibiotics for confirmed strep/abscess, drainage if abscess</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Most viral sore throats improve within a week. Return sooner for red flags or worsening symptoms.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fever trends, pain level (0–10), ability to swallow fluids/meds</li>
          <li>Presence of cough, runny nose, rash, or swollen neck nodes</li>
          <li>Test results (COVID/flu/strep) and response to OTCs</li>
          <li>Any hoarseness, drooling, or worsening one-sided pain</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Bring this log and a medication list to visits—speeds diagnosis and avoids interactions.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support with dignity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Offer frequent warm/cool fluids and assist with salt-water gargles</li>
          <li>Use a humidifier and avoid smoke/irritants</li>
          <li>Monitor for dehydration, confusion, increasing pain, or breathing/swallowing difficulty</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call the clinician for:</strong> persistent fever &gt;3 days, inability to swallow fluids/meds, severe one-sided pain or muffled voice, or red-flag breathing issues.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need antibiotics?</p>
        <p>Only if bacterial infection (like strep) is confirmed or strongly suspected. Most sore throats are viral and improve with supportive care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What helps the pain fast?</p>
        <p>Warm salt-water gargles, acetaminophen, humidified air, and menthol lozenges/sprays provide short-term relief.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Could it be reflux?</p>
        <p>Yes—especially if hoarseness or morning throat clearing. Try smaller meals, avoid late eating, and elevate the head of the bed.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What about thrush from my inhaler?</p>
        <p>Rinse and spit after each use. If white patches or burning persist, see your clinician for antifungal treatment.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
