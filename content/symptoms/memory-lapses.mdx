  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What memory lapses can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Occasional name-finding trouble or misplacing items can be part of aging. Concerning patterns include <strong>repeating questions, getting lost in familiar places, money or medicine mix-ups,</strong> or personality change. Treatable problems (sleep, depression, infection, hearing, medicines) often worsen memory and are worth checking.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p>Early recognition helps: supports at home, medical review, and planning maintain independence longer.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Sudden confusion, slurred speech, face droop, one-sided weakness, or severe headache (possible stroke/TIA)</li>
        <li>Fever, vomiting, severe dehydration, or sudden behavior change with sleepiness</li>
        <li>Head injury with confusion, worsening headache, or repeated vomiting</li>
        <li>Dangerous behavior (wandering, leaving stove on, getting lost) that cannot be supervised</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Rapid-onset symptoms are medical emergencies.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Comparison -->
  <section id="compare" class="section">
    <h2>Normal aging vs MCI vs dementia</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Pattern</th><th scope="col">Typical clues</th><th scope="col">Daily impact</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Normal aging</strong></td>
          <td>Slower recall; remembers with cues; no major navigation or bill-pay errors</td>
          <td>Independent; routines intact</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI)</strong></td>
          <td>More frequent lapses than peers; objective memory change on testing</td>
          <td>Most activities independent; subtle inefficiencies</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dementia</strong></td>
          <td>Forgetting recent events, repeating, getting lost, word-finding plus planning/judgment changes</td>
          <td>Interferes with daily function (finances, meds, meals, safety)</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Other causes -->
  <section id="other-causes" class="section">
    <h2>Delirium, depression, sleep & medicines</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Delirium (sudden, fluctuating)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hours–days onset; worse at night; inattention; often due to infection, dehydration, new medicines, pain.</li>
          <li>Needs urgent evaluation and treatment of the cause.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Depression & anxiety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Low mood, poor sleep, low energy, reduced concentration can mimic memory loss.</li>
          <li>Improves with counseling, activity, sleep treatment, or medicines.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sleep & hearing/vision</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sleep apnea, insomnia, pain, or poor hearing/vision strain memory and attention.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Medicine review</strong>: sedatives, anticholinergics (bladder, some allergy meds), some pain meds, and alcohol can worsen memory—ask about safer alternatives.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- First steps -->
  <section id="first-steps" class="section">
    <h2>First steps at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Track & share</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Write examples (what was forgotten, how often, impact).</li>
          <li>List all medicines and supplements; note recent changes.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">Hydration, sleep, routine</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular bedtime; morning light; short daytime walks.</li>
          <li>Steady fluids in day; limit caffeine late.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Supports -->
  <section id="supports" class="section">
    <h2>Daily supports that work</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">External memory aids</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>One <strong>central notebook</strong> or phone notes app for tasks and questions.</li>
          <li>Calendar on wall + phone reminders for appointments and pills.</li>
          <li>Place keys/wallet/phone in the same dish by the door.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Simplify choices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Organize the kitchen and medicine area; label shelves/boxes.</li>
          <li>Use a <strong>weekly pillbox</strong> or smart dispenser; keep a written medication list.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Hearing helps memory</strong>: use hearing aids if prescribed, turn on captions, face the speaker, reduce background noise.
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Brain & body activity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Brisk walking or gentle exercise most days.</li>
          <li>Social visits, hobbies, puzzles, simple new learning.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Safety & independence</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Home safety</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stove timers/auto shutoff, medication lockbox if needed, night lights, clear paths, labeled switches.</li>
          <li>Consider a <strong>medical ID</strong> and phone ICE contacts.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Money & driving</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Set up automatic bill pay; review bank/credit alerts.</li>
          <li>For driving: periodic road-safety checks; avoid night driving if vision or attention is reduced.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">Purpose</th><th scope="col">Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & cognitive screen</strong></td>
          <td>Define pattern & impact</td>
          <td>Orientation, recall, attention tests; informant questionnaire</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medication review</strong></td>
          <td>Remove worseners</td>
          <td>Sedatives, anticholinergics, alcohol interactions</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Basic labs</strong></td>
          <td>Reversible causes</td>
          <td>Thyroid, B12, folate, electrolytes, CBC, CMP; consider depression screen</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging (selective)</strong></td>
          <td>Rule out structural issues</td>
          <td>Head CT/MRI if focal signs, rapid decline, head trauma, or atypical features</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hearing/vision & sleep</strong></td>
          <td>Support attention & memory</td>
          <td>Hearing test; sleep apnea assessment if snoring/daytime sleepiness</td>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Address reversible factors</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Treat depression/anxiety, pain, sleep apnea, infections; correct thyroid/B12/folate problems.</li>
          <li>Reduce/replace memory-harming medicines.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Lifestyle & rehab</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Physical activity, social engagement, cognitive stimulation.</li>
          <li>Occupational therapy for routines, home setup, and compensatory strategies.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines (selected cases)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>For diagnosed Alzheimer’s or certain dementias: clinician may discuss cholinesterase inhibitors or memantine (benefits vs side effects vary).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Care is individualized. Goals include safety, quality of life, and supporting caregivers.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Examples of lapses (what, when, where), mood, sleep quality, hydration.</li>
          <li>Medicine changes, hearing aid use, new stressors.</li>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is forgetting names normal?</p>
        <p>Often yes—especially if recall returns with hints. Trouble following steps, paying bills, or getting lost is more concerning.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can poor sleep cause memory issues?</p>
        <p>Yes. Sleep apnea and insomnia reduce attention and recall. Treating sleep often improves memory.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Which medicines harm memory?</p>
        <p>Sedatives, some bladder/allergy medicines with anticholinergic effects, and alcohol can worsen memory. Ask about safer options.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do puzzles help?</p>
        <p>They support engagement, but combining activity, social connection, hearing support, and routine gives the best results.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
