  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What headache can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Many headaches are benign (tension-type, migraine), but in older adults new or different headaches deserve attention. The pattern, suddenness, associated symptoms, medicines, hydration, sleep, and vision changes help find the cause.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>New after age 50</strong>, rapidly worsening, or different from your usual pattern needs medical review even if pain is moderate.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD ===== -->
  

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>“Worst-ever” sudden headache</strong> (peaks within seconds)</li>
        <li><strong>Stroke signs:</strong> face droop, arm weakness, speech trouble, new confusion</li>
        <li><strong>Fever</strong> with stiff neck or rash</li>
        <li><strong>New headache after head injury</strong> or with fainting</li>
        <li><strong>New visual loss</strong>, double vision, jaw pain when chewing, or scalp tenderness (possible temporal arteritis)</li>
        <li>Severe headache with <strong>eye pain/halos, nausea</strong> (possible acute glaucoma)</li>
        <li>Headache with <strong>cancer, immune suppression, or anticoagulants</strong></li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may threaten life or vision. Seek urgent care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes by pattern</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Pattern</th><th scope="col">Clues</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Tension-type</strong></td>
          <td>Band-like pressure, mild–moderate, no nausea, activity okay</td>
          <td>Stress, posture, neck strain; simple measures often help</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Migraine (± aura)</strong></td>
          <td>Pulsing, moderate–severe, worse with activity, ± nausea/light-sound sensitivity</td>
          <td>May start later in life; aura = visual/zap sensations before pain</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medication-overuse</strong></td>
          <td>Near-daily headaches; frequent painkiller use</td>
          <td>Risk if simple pain meds ≥15 d/mo or combos/triptans ≥10 d/mo</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dehydration / missed meals</strong></td>
          <td>Thirst, darker urine, better after fluids/food</td>
          <td>Common in hot weather, illness, diuretics</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sinus-related</strong></td>
          <td>Facial pressure, worse bending forward, nasal congestion</td>
          <td>True sinus infection is less common than assumed</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Neck/cervicogenic</strong></td>
          <td>Pain begins in neck, reduced neck motion</td>
          <td>Posture, arthritis; responds to physio/ergonomics</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Temporal arteritis (GCA)</strong></td>
          <td>New headache >50, scalp tender, jaw pain chewing, vision change</td>
          <td>Urgent tests (ESR/CRP); early treatment protects vision</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Acute angle-closure glaucoma</strong></td>
          <td>Severe eye pain, halos, nausea, hard eye</td>
          <td>Emergency eye pressure treatment needed</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sleep apnea / poor sleep</strong></td>
          <td>Morning headaches, loud snoring, daytime sleepiness</td>
          <td>Sleep study may help; treatable</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- First steps -->
  <section id="first-steps" class="section">
    <h2>Safe first steps & home care</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Comfort & routine</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hydrate (water or oral rehydration); small frequent sips if nauseated.</li>
          <li>Regular meals; add a protein snack if meals were delayed.</li>
          <li>Dim, quiet room; gentle neck/shoulder stretches; heat for tension, cool pack for throbbing.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">Caffeine & triggers</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>A small amount of caffeine <em>early in the day</em> can help some migraines; avoid late-day caffeine.</li>
          <li>Check triggers: poor sleep, bright screens, skipped meals, dehydration, strong smells.</li>
          <li>Practice 20-20-20 for screens and improve posture setup.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Call your clinician</strong> for new or changing headaches, headaches on ≥15 days/month, or if over-the-counter medicines are needed more than a few days per week.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after First steps) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Medicine cautions -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Use carefully</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Acetaminophen</strong> — keep total daily dose within your clinician’s advice (often ≤3,000 mg/day for older adults). Watch for duplicate ingredients in cold/flu products.</li>
          <li><strong>NSAIDs</strong> (ibuprofen, naproxen) — may irritate stomach and kidneys or raise BP; avoid if kidney disease, ulcers, heart failure, or on certain blood thinners.</li>
          <li><strong>Triptans</strong> — migraine-specific; not for some heart/vascular conditions; needs clinician guidance.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Avoid opioids</strong> and barbiturate combinations for headache—higher risk and drive medication-overuse.</li>
          <li>Medication-overuse headache: simple pain meds ≥15 days/month or combos/triptans ≥10 days/month can perpetuate headaches.</li>
          <li>Ask about preventive options if headaches are frequent (sleep, BP meds, anti-CGRP, etc.).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">Purpose</th><th scope="col">Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Identify pattern and risk</td>
          <td>Neurologic exam, BP, neck/jaw/temporal arteries, vision check</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Targeted labs</strong></td>
          <td>Rule out contributors</td>
          <td>CBC (anemia), electrolytes, thyroid, ESR/CRP if GCA suspected</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging</strong></td>
          <td>Red flags or atypical</td>
          <td>CT/MRI for sudden severe, new neuro deficits, new onset >50, cancer/immunosuppression, trauma</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Eye pressure</strong></td>
          <td>Rule out acute glaucoma</td>
          <td>Tonometer if severe eye pain/halos</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Plan</strong></td>
          <td>Relief & prevention</td>
          <td>Acute treatment, trigger management, sleep and hydration plan, preventive meds if frequent</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Start time, duration, location (one/both sides), severity (0–10)</li>
          <li>Associated symptoms: nausea, light/sound sensitivity, visual changes, neck pain</li>
          <li>Sleep, meals, fluids, caffeine; medicines used and effect</li>
          <li>BP readings (if advised), and any new stressors or illnesses</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        A 2–4 week diary helps identify triggers and whether preventive options are needed.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Does high blood pressure cause headaches?</p>
        <p>Most routine headaches are <em>not</em> from mildly elevated BP. Headache with very high BP and other symptoms needs urgent evaluation.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is a new daily headache serious?</p>
        <p>New persistent headaches—especially after age 50—should be assessed. Many causes are treatable.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How often can I use pain relievers?</p>
        <p>Limit to the fewest days per week. Frequent use can cause medication-overuse headaches. Ask about prevention if headaches are common.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When should I see an eye doctor?</p>
        <p>Headache with eye pain, halos, or vision change; or if you haven’t had a recent eye exam—especially with glaucoma/cataract history.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
