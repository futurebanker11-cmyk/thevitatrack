  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What dry mouth can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Dry mouth (<em>xerostomia</em>) is a common problem in seniors. Low saliva can make swallowing, speaking, and wearing dentures harder—and raises the risk of cavities, oral thrush, and bad breath. Medicines are the most frequent cause.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Symptoms:</strong> sticky/dry feeling, thick saliva, sore mouth, cracked lips, thirst, waking at night to drink, taste changes, trouble swallowing dry foods.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD ===== -->
  

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call or seek urgent care</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Signs of <strong>dehydration</strong>: confusion, fainting, very dark urine, minimal urination</li>
        <li><strong>Painful white patches</strong> on tongue/cheeks (possible thrush) with fever or spreading</li>
        <li>Dry mouth with <strong>swollen/tender jaw below ears</strong> (salivary gland infection/stone)</li>
        <li>Dry mouth plus <strong>dry eyes + joint pain</strong> (possible autoimmune condition)</li>
        <li>After starting new medicines with severe dry mouth interfering with eating/drinking</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may need prompt medical or dental treatment.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & triggers</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Common causes of dry mouth">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Category</th><th scope="col">Examples</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medicines</strong></td>
          <td>Anticholinergics (oxybutynin), antihistamines, antidepressants (TCAs, SSRIs), antipsychotics, opioids, decongestants, diuretics, clonidine, muscle relaxants</td>
          <td><em>Most common.</em> Review list for substitutes or dose changes.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medical conditions</strong></td>
          <td>Diabetes, Sjögren’s syndrome, thyroid disease, depression, anxiety</td>
          <td>Consider testing if symptoms are persistent or severe.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Therapies</strong></td>
          <td>Head/neck radiation, chemotherapy</td>
          <td>Can permanently reduce saliva; needs dental prevention plan.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Habits & environment</strong></td>
          <td>Mouth breathing, snoring/untreated sleep apnea, CPAP without humidifier, low room humidity, tobacco, alcohol</td>
          <td>Humidify and address airway issues when possible.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Dehydration</strong></td>
          <td>Low fluid intake, vomiting/diarrhea, diuretics, hot weather</td>
          <td>Increase fluids unless restricted by your clinician.</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Self-care & moisture tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily comfort strategies</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sip water frequently; keep a bottle handy (ice chips can help)</li>
          <li>Chew <strong>sugar-free gum</strong> or suck sugar-free lozenges—xylitol can stimulate saliva</li>
          <li>Use <strong>saliva substitutes</strong> or mouth moisturizers (carboxymethylcellulose gels/sprays)</li>
          <li>Run a <strong>cool-mist humidifier</strong> at night; consider CPAP humidification</li>
          <li>Apply <strong>lip balm</strong> often; nasal saline for nose dryness</li>
          <li>Avoid <strong>alcohol-based mouthwashes</strong>, tobacco, and excessive caffeine/alcohol</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Call your clinician soon if</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Dry mouth lasts &gt;2–4 weeks or causes difficulty chewing/swallowing</li>
          <li>You have frequent <strong>cavities</strong>, gum disease, or <strong>oral thrush</strong></li>
          <li>New medicines started around the time symptoms began</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small">This page is educational and not a diagnosis. Ask your clinician or dentist for tailored advice.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Dental protection -->
  <section id="dental" class="section">
    <h2>Protecting teeth & mouth</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Brush twice daily with <strong>fluoride toothpaste</strong>; consider high-fluoride (e.g., 5000 ppm) if prone to cavities</li>
          <li><strong>Nightly fluoride</strong> gel or rinse if advised; frequent dental checkups (every 3–6 months)</li>
          <li>Clean <strong>dentures</strong> daily; remove at night; ensure proper fit to avoid sores</li>
          <li>Rinse after meals; gentle scraping for tongue coating if comfortable</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Why it matters:</strong> Saliva protects against acid and bacteria. Low saliva → higher risk of cavities, gum disease, thrush, and mouth sores.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine options & cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Medicines for dry mouth">
          <thead>
            <tr><th scope="col">Option</th><th scope="col">What it does</th><th scope="col">Notes for seniors</th></tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Review current meds</strong></td>
              <td>Reduce dryness burden</td>
              <td>Ask about safer alternatives, dose timing, or deprescribing where appropriate</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Pilocarpine / Cevimeline</strong></td>
              <td>Stimulate saliva (if glands still produce)</td>
              <td>Prescription only; may cause sweating, flushing; avoid in certain heart/lung conditions—clinician will screen</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Fluoride therapies</strong></td>
              <td>Prevent cavities</td>
              <td>High-fluoride toothpaste/rinses via dentist when cavity risk is high</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Antifungals</strong></td>
              <td>Treat oral thrush</td>
              <td>Nystatin/clotrimazole as indicated if white patches/soreness are present</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Saliva substitutes</strong></td>
              <td>Coat and moisten mouth</td>
              <td>Gels, sprays, and rinses; use before meals/bedtime</td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Avoid</strong> decongestants and alcohol-based mouthwashes when possible—they can worsen dryness. Do not change prescriptions without guidance.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Food & hydration tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Helpful choices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Sip water throughout the day; sugar-free flavored drops or citrus slices for taste</li>
          <li><strong>Moist foods</strong>: sauces, gravies, soups, stews; dunk dry foods in liquids</li>
          <li><strong>Soft fruits</strong> (melon, berries), yogurt, cottage cheese, eggs, oatmeal</li>
          <li><strong>Xylitol gum/lozenges</strong> after meals to stimulate saliva (avoid in pets)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Limit/avoid</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Alcohol, tobacco, and very salty/spicy dry snacks</li>
          <li>Excess caffeine; acidic sodas that can irritate enamel</li>
          <li>Frequent sugary sipping—raises cavity risk when saliva is low</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">Purpose</th><th scope="col">Examples</th></tr>

          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>

          <td>Look for systemic causes</td>

          <td>Measure saliva / confirm autoimmune disease</td>
          <td>Sialometry, ophthalmology tests for dry eyes; imaging or biopsy if needed</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Treatment plan</strong></td>
          <td>Relieve symptoms & prevent complications</td>
          <td>Medication changes, saliva stimulants/substitutes, antifungals, dental fluoride plan</td>

      </tbody>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will dry mouth damage my teeth?</p>
        <p>Yes—low saliva increases cavities and gum disease risk. Use fluoride, keep dental visits frequent, and avoid frequent sugary drinks.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do dentures make it worse?</p>
        <p>Dentures can irritate a dry mouth. Ensure a good fit, remove at night, and use saliva gels. See your dentist if sore spots develop.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What mouthwash should I use?</p>
        <p>Choose <strong>alcohol-free</strong> mouthwashes designed for dry mouth. Some include xylitol and mild fluoride for extra protection.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can medications help produce saliva?</p>
        <p>Yes—pilocarpine or cevimeline may help selected patients. Your clinician will check for suitability and possible side effects.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
