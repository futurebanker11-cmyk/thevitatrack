  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What fever means</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Fever is a body temperature of <strong>≥38.0°C (100.4°F)</strong>. In older adults, temperatures may be <em>lower</em> even with serious infection—pay attention to new chills, confusion, weakness, or a temperature that’s <strong>≥1.1°C (2°F)</strong> above your usual baseline.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Common symptoms:</strong> chills, sweats, headache, body aches, cough, sore throat, burning urination, belly pain, or skin/wound redness.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Check -->
  <section id="check" class="section">
    <h2>How to check temperature</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best methods</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Oral digital</strong>: under tongue, mouth closed, no hot/cold drinks for 15 min beforehand</li>
          <li><strong>Rectal</strong>: most accurate (use for frail or confused adults when needed)</li>
          <li><strong>Tympanic/temporal</strong>: quick but can vary—follow device instructions, ensure clean probe</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Tips</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Measure at the <strong>same site</strong> each time for comparison</li>
          <li>Record time, method, and medicines taken</li>
          <li>Low oxygen, fast heart rate, or confusion may signal serious illness even if temp is modest</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Fever with <strong>confusion, new slurred speech, or severe weakness</strong></li>
        <li>Shortness of breath, oxygen level &lt; 92% if you monitor at home, or chest pain</li>
        <li>Severe headache/stiff neck, rash that spreads quickly or looks like bruises</li>
        <li>Persistent vomiting, unable to keep fluids down, or signs of dehydration (very little urine, dizziness)</li>
        <li>Fever after recent <strong>surgery, chemotherapy, or while on immune-suppressing drugs</strong></li>
        <li>Temperature &ge; 39.4°C (103°F) or any fever &gt; 48–72 hours with worsening symptoms</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may indicate serious infection (sepsis/meningitis), cardiac/lung problems, or dehydration.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes by clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Fever causes and clues">

      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Cough, sore throat, runny nose</strong></td>
          <td>Viral respiratory infection, influenza, COVID-19</td>
          <td>Consider home test; watch breathing and hydration</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Burning urination, urgency</strong></td>
          <td>Urinary tract infection</td>
          <td>Older adults may present with confusion or falls</td>

        <tr>
          <td><strong>Localized redness/swelling/warmth</strong></td>
          <td>Skin infection (cellulitis), wound infection</td>
          <td>Mark edges; seek care if spreading</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Persistent cough, chest pain, breathlessness</strong></td>
          <td>Pneumonia</td>
          <td>Needs evaluation; low oxygen can be subtle</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Abdominal pain, diarrhea</strong></td>
          <td>Gastroenteritis, diverticulitis</td>
          <td>Severe abdominal tenderness → urgent care</td>
        </tr>

          <td>Call the surgical/clinic team</td>

          <td>Drug fever, C. difficile (if diarrhea)</td>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Cooling & self-care</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If no red-flags</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rest, light clothing; keep the room cool and well-ventilated</li>
          <li>Hydrate with <strong>water or oral rehydration solution</strong>; small, frequent sips</li>
          <li>Lukewarm sponge bath or cool compress to forehead/neck (avoid ice baths)</li>
          <li>Light foods as tolerated (soups, yogurt, fruits)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Call your clinician soon if</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fever lasts &gt;48–72 hours or recurs after improving</li>
          <li>You have heart, lung, kidney disease, or are on steroids/chemotherapy</li>
          <li>New confusion, worsening cough, chest pain, or urinary symptoms develop</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small">This page is educational and not a diagnosis. When in doubt, seek care.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine dosing & cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Antipyretic dosing">
          <thead>
            <tr><th scope="col">Medicine</th><th scope="col">Typical adult dose</th><th scope="col">Max daily dose</th><th scope="col">Notes for seniors</th></tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Acetaminophen</strong> (paracetamol)</td>
              <td>500–650 mg every 6–8 hours</td>
              <td><strong>≤3,000 mg/day</strong> (many clinicians prefer ≤2,000–2,600 mg/day if liver disease or low body weight)</td>
              <td>Check all combo products (cold/flu) to avoid double-dosing</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Ibuprofen</strong> (NSAID)</td>
              <td>200–400 mg every 6–8 hours with food</td>
              <td>≤1,200 mg/day (OTC)</td>
              <td>Avoid with kidney disease, ulcers/bleeding risk, anticoagulants, or heart failure unless advised</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Naproxen</strong> (NSAID)</td>
              <td>220 mg every 8–12 hours</td>
              <td>≤660 mg/day (OTC)</td>
              <td>Similar NSAID cautions; avoid combining with other NSAIDs</td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Do not</strong> alternate multiple fever reducers without a written schedule from a clinician. Avoid aspirin for fever in many older adults due to bleeding risk unless specifically directed.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Fluids & diet tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Helpful choices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Water, ORS, broths; aim for <strong>pale-yellow urine</strong></li>
          <li>Soups, oatmeal, yogurt, fruits (banana, melon), eggs</li>
          <li>Small, frequent meals and snacks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Limit/avoid</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Alcohol (dehydrates), very sugary drinks</li>
          <li>Heavy, greasy meals that worsen nausea</li>
          <li>Over-bundling in heavy blankets—can raise temperature discomfort</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

          <td>Relieve symptoms & treat cause</td>

        </tr>

  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Temperature (time, method), chills/sweats, and symptom changes</li>
          <li>Fluid intake and urine output/color</li>
          <li>Cough, breathing, chest pain, urinary symptoms, rashes</li>
          <li>All medicines taken (name/dose/time), including fever reducers</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Bring this log to appointments—it speeds diagnosis and safe treatment.

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What counts as a fever?</p>
        <p>Generally ≥38.0°C (100.4°F). In seniors, a rise of ≥1.1°C (2°F) over baseline can be significant even if the absolute number is lower.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I always treat a fever?</p>
        <p>Treat if uncomfortable, dehydrated, or with heart/lung strain. The goal is comfort and hydration; medicine does not “cure” the cause.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can I use ibuprofen?</p>
        <p>Possibly, but avoid if you have kidney disease, ulcers/bleeding risk, are on blood thinners, or have heart failure—acetaminophen is often safer. Ask your clinician.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When should I get tested?</p>
        <p>If fever lasts &gt;48–72 hours, you feel worse, or you have concerning symptoms (breathing problems, urinary pain, severe headache, rash, confusion).</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
