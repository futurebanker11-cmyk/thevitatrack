  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What type of cough can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Cough protects the airways. The <strong>sound</strong> (dry vs. phlegm), <strong>duration</strong> (days vs. weeks), and <strong>pairing</strong> with fever, breathlessness, or heart symptoms help identify the cause. In seniors, new or worsening cough—especially at night or with exertion—deserves attention.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Clues:</strong> Dry + tickle (post-nasal drip), heartburn + night cough (reflux), wheeze + chest tightness (asthma/COPD), leg swelling + orthopnea (heart failure), fever + chills (pneumonia/viral).</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Severe breathlessness</strong>, blue lips, confusion, or oxygen saturation &lt; 90% (if you monitor)</li>
        <li><strong>Chest pain/pressure</strong>, especially with sweating, nausea, or radiation to arm/jaw</li>
        <li>Coughing up <strong>large amounts of blood</strong> or pink frothy sputum</li>
        <li>High fever with rigors and weakness, or new confusion</li>
        <li>Known COPD/asthma with rapidly worsening wheeze not responding to your plan</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Possible pneumonia, pulmonary embolism, heart attack, heart failure flare, or severe asthma/COPD exacerbation.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes by pattern -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes by pattern</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Infections</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Viral:</strong> common cold, influenza, COVID-19 (often dry → later phlegm)</li>
          <li><strong>Bacterial:</strong> pneumonia (fever, chest pain, shortness of breath)</li>
          <li><strong>Post-infectious</strong> cough: lingers after colds for weeks</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Airways & lungs</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Asthma/COPD:</strong> wheeze, chest tightness, triggers, morning phlegm</li>
          <li><strong>Bronchiectasis:</strong> chronic productive cough with recurrent infections</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Heart & fluid</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Heart failure:</strong> night cough, breathlessness when lying flat, leg swelling</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Upper airway & reflux</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Post-nasal drip</strong> (allergies/sinus): throat clearing, tickle</li>
          <li><strong>GERD:</strong> heartburn, sour taste, worse after meals or at night</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines & other</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>ACE-inhibitors:</strong> lisinopril/ramipril can cause dry cough</li>
          <li>Environmental smoke/irritants; aspiration with swallowing issues</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- First steps & home care -->
  <section id="first-steps" class="section">
    <h2>Safe first steps & home care</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Check temperature and note <strong>onset</strong>, <strong>triggers</strong>, and <strong>night vs. day</strong> pattern.</li>
          <li>Hydrate; warm fluids or honey-lemon (if not diabetic-restricted) may soothe.</li>
          <li>Use a <strong>humidifier</strong> or steam cautiously; keep bedroom air not too dry.</li>
          <li>Elevate head of bed; avoid eating within 2–3 hours of bedtime (reflux).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">If you have COPD/asthma</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Follow your <strong>rescue inhaler</strong> / spacer plan; check inhaler technique.</li>
          <li>Monitor oxygen if you have a pulse oximeter; seek help if dropping or worsening.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Call your clinician</strong> if cough lasts &gt;3 weeks, keeps you from sleeping, or you have weight loss, night sweats, blood, or repeated pneumonias.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">For symptom relief</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Acetaminophen</strong> for fever/aches within safe daily limits (watch combination products).</li>
          <li><strong>Dextromethorphan</strong> may reduce cough; avoid with certain antidepressants (risk of interactions).</li>
          <li><strong>Guaifenesin</strong> can loosen phlegm with fluids.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Use with care / avoid unless advised</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Codeine / opioid syrups:</strong> sedation, fall risk, constipation; avoid driving.</li>
          <li><strong>NSAIDs</strong> can affect kidneys/BP and irritate stomach; check if on blood thinners.</li>
          <li><strong>ACE-inhibitor cough:</strong> ask about switching class (e.g., to ARB) if persistent.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Antibiotics aren’t needed for most viral coughs.</strong> Seek guidance if high fever, chest pain, shortness of breath, or symptoms persist.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

        <tr>
          <td><strong>Targeted tests</strong></td>
          <td>Confirm/inform treatment</td>

        <tr>
          <td><strong>Further workup</strong></td>
          <td>Chronic/unexplained</td>
          <td>CT chest, echocardiogram, swallow/aspiration studies, allergy/ENT eval</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Treatment</strong></td>
          <td>Cause-directed</td>
          <td>Bronchodilators/steroids for asthma/COPD exacerbation; diuretics for heart failure; antibiotics if bacterial pneumonia; reflux/post-nasal drip therapy</td>
        </tr>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Onset & duration (days/weeks), day vs. night pattern</li>
          <li>Dry vs. phlegm (color/amount), any blood</li>
          <li>Fever, breathlessness, chest pain, heartburn, wheeze</li>
          <li>Exposures (sick contacts, smoke), new medicines (ACE-inhibitors)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        A simple diary (including response to inhalers or cough remedies) helps speed diagnosis.
      </div>
    </div>

  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long does a viral cough last?</p>
        <p>Often 2–3 weeks. If it’s getting worse after week one, brings high fever, or you’re short of breath, seek care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Night cough but no fever?</p>
        <p>Think reflux, post-nasal drip, asthma, or heart failure (if you also have leg swelling and need more pillows).</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Cough with pink frothy sputum?</p>
        <p>Concerning for acute heart failure—seek emergency care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">ACE-inhibitor and new dry cough?</p>
        <p>Common side effect—ask your clinician about alternatives (e.g., ARB).</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
