  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What vertigo can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> <strong>Vertigo</strong> is a false sense of spinning or movement. In older adults, brief spins with certain head positions often point to <strong>BPPV</strong> (inner-ear crystals). Longer-lasting vertigo can follow a <strong>viral inner-ear illness</strong>. Less often, dizziness signals <strong>stroke</strong> or heart rhythm problems—know the red flags below.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Describe your dizziness:</strong> spinning vs. lightheaded, triggered by rolling in bed or looking up, constant vs. brief, with or without hearing changes. These clues guide care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>FAST stroke signs:</strong> face droop, arm weakness, speech trouble, severe sudden headache</li>
        <li>Vertigo with <strong>double vision</strong>, new trouble speaking/swallowing, severe unsteady gait</li>
        <li>Head injury or new severe headache (especially on <strong>blood thinners</strong>)</li>
        <li>Fainting, chest pain, palpitations, or shortness of breath with dizziness</li>
        <li>New inability to walk without support or rapidly worsening symptoms</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may indicate stroke/TIA, brain bleed, heart rhythm problems, or other emergencies.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Vertigo causes">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Condition</th><th>Typical features</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>BPPV</strong> (benign paroxysmal positional vertigo)</td>
          <td>Brief (<1 min) spins triggered by rolling in bed, looking up/down</td>
          <td>Often recurs; relieved by canalith repositioning (Epley) done by clinician/PT</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Vestibular neuritis</strong></td>
          <td>Sudden severe vertigo lasting hours–days, nausea, no hearing loss</td>
          <td>Follows a virus; improves over days with vestibular rehab</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Labyrinthitis</strong></td>
          <td>Vertigo with <strong>hearing loss</strong> or ear fullness/ringing</td>
          <td>Urgent ear/hearing evaluation recommended</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Ménière’s disease</strong></td>
          <td>Recurrent spells (20 min–hours) + fluctuating hearing loss, tinnitus</td>
          <td>Low-salt diet; specialist care</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Vestibular migraine</strong></td>
          <td>Vertigo with/without headache; light/sound sensitivity</td>
          <td>History of migraine; triggers include sleep loss, certain foods</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Central causes</strong></td>
          <td>Vertigo with neuro deficits (double vision, severe ataxia)</td>
          <td>Consider stroke—seek emergency care</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Not vertigo:</strong> lightheaded on standing</td>
          <td>Orthostatic hypotension, dehydration, medications</td>
          <td>Check sitting/standing BP; hydrate if allowed</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-checks -->
  <section id="self-check" class="section">
    <h2>Simple self-checks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">At home (if no red flags)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Note <strong>triggers</strong>: rolling in bed, looking up, sudden standing</li>
          <li>Track <strong>duration</strong>: seconds vs hours; constant vs episodes</li>
          <li>Record ear/hearing changes: fullness, ringing, or loss</li>
          <li>Check BP/heart rate sitting → standing at 1 & 3 minutes if able</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Bring to visits:</strong> symptom diary, medication list (including sleep aids), and any BP/HR readings. A short phone video during an episode can help diagnosis.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Safe steps at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Staying safe</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rise slowly; sit at bedside for a minute before standing</li>
          <li>Use <strong>night-lights</strong>; avoid driving/heights until symptoms settle</li>
          <li>Small, frequent fluids and light meals to reduce nausea</li>
          <li>If episodes are positional/brief, ask a clinician/PT about <strong>Epley</strong> and home <strong>Brandt–Daroff</strong> exercises</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Vestibular rehab:</strong> Targeted balance/eye-head exercises from PT speed recovery after vestibular neuritis and reduce BPPV recurrences.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds & hydration -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medication & hydration tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Use medicines sparingly</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Short-term anti-nausea/vestibular suppressants (e.g., meclizine) may help <em>severe</em> spells but can slow compensation—avoid long-term use</li>
          <li>Review sedatives, sleep aids, and alcohol—these can worsen dizziness and falls</li>
          <li>Ask about ototoxic drugs if new hearing changes (e.g., some antibiotics/diuretics)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Hydration:</strong> Dehydration and low blood pressure increase lightheadedness—sip fluids through the day unless on fluid restriction.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Clinical evaluation for vertigo">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Purpose</th><th>Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Differentiate vertigo vs lightheadedness</td>
          <td>Triggers/duration, neurologic exam, ear exam, orthostatic vitals</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Positional tests</strong></td>
          <td>Diagnose BPPV</td>
          <td>Dix–Hallpike and supine roll tests; canalith repositioning (Epley)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>HINTS exam</strong></td>
          <td>Differentiate central vs peripheral vertigo (trained clinicians)</td>
          <td>Head-impulse, nystagmus, test-of-skew</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hearing tests</strong></td>
          <td>Assess ear causes</td>
          <td>Audiogram if hearing loss/tinnitus</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging</strong></td>
          <td>Rule out central causes</td>
          <td>MRI/CT if red flags or abnormal neuro exam</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Treatment targets the cause—Epley for BPPV, vestibular rehab, migraine management, Ménière’s diet/meds, or stroke/cardiac care if indicated.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>When episodes occur, duration, and head/body positions involved</li>
          <li>Associated symptoms: nausea, hearing changes, headache, vision changes</li>
          <li>BP/HR sitting vs standing (if safe), new meds or dose changes</li>
          <li>Falls or near-falls and circumstances (lighting, footwear)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Share your diary and any videos of eye movements during an episode—this speeds diagnosis.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support with dignity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Provide steady arm support when standing/walking; clear pathways and add night-lights</li>
          <li>Offer small sips and light foods; have a receptacle ready if nausea</li>
          <li>Help schedule PT for vestibular rehab and follow-up hearing tests if needed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Seek urgent care for:</strong> stroke signs, new severe headache, double vision, inability to walk, chest pain/fainting, or head injury on blood thinners.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How do I know if it’s BPPV?</p>
        <p>Spinning that lasts seconds and is triggered by rolling in bed or looking up/down strongly suggests BPPV. It’s often relieved by the Epley maneuver done by a clinician or PT.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do vertigo pills fix the problem?</p>
        <p>They can ease nausea briefly but don’t correct BPPV and may slow recovery if used long term. Repositioning and rehab work better for many causes.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When should I worry about stroke?</p>
        <p>Any vertigo with double vision, slurred speech, severe unsteady walk, new weakness, or worst-ever headache—treat as an emergency.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can dehydration cause dizziness?</p>
        <p>Yes—especially lightheadedness when standing. Sip fluids throughout the day unless you’re on a fluid restriction plan.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
