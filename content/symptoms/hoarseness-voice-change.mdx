  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What hoarseness can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Hoarseness is a change in voice quality (raspy, weak, breathy, strained). In older adults, causes range from mild <strong>viral laryngitis</strong> or <strong>reflux</strong> to <strong>vocal cord paralysis</strong> or, rarely, <strong>throat cancer</strong>. Pay attention to new, persistent, or progressive changes.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Common companions:</strong> sore throat, cough, post-nasal drip, frequent throat clearing, neck lump, trouble swallowing, shortness of breath, or heartburn.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Hoarseness with <strong>noisy breathing/stridor</strong>, drooling, or trouble breathing</li>
        <li>Voice change with <strong>severe throat pain</strong>, high fever, or inability to swallow liquids</li>
        <li>Rapidly enlarging <strong>neck swelling</strong> or after a neck/throat injury</li>
        <li>Blood in saliva/sputum with worsening throat symptoms</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These may indicate airway swelling, abscess, or bleeding needing urgent care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes by clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Hoarseness causes and clues">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Clue</th><th scope="col">More likely causes</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Recent cold/cough</strong></td>
          <td>Viral laryngitis</td>
          <td>Usually improves in 1–2 weeks with voice rest & fluids</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Heartburn, throat clearing, morning hoarseness</strong></td>
          <td>Laryngopharyngeal reflux (LPR) / GERD</td>
          <td>Try reflux precautions; consider acid-reducer trial</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sudden breathy voice after surgery/procedure</strong></td>
          <td>Vocal cord paralysis (post-thyroid, carotid, cardiac, or intubation)</td>
          <td>Needs ENT evaluation; risk of aspiration</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Long-term smoker / alcohol; neck lump; weight loss</strong></td>
          <td>Laryngeal or throat cancer</td>
          <td>Persistent hoarseness &gt;3–4 weeks → ENT laryngoscopy</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Voice overuse / shouting</strong></td>
          <td>Nodules, polyps, muscle tension dysphonia</td>
          <td>Speech-language therapy helps; avoid whispering</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Allergies / post-nasal drip</strong></td>
          <td>Rhinitis, sinusitis</td>
          <td>Saline rinses, gentle antihistamine options (see cautions)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>New meds: anticholinergics, diuretics, inhaled steroids</strong></td>
          <td>Dryness / thrush / irritation</td>
          <td>Rinse mouth after inhalers; review meds if dry mouth prominent</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <div class="note" style="margin-top:12px">
      <strong>See an ENT soon if:</strong> hoarseness lasts &gt;3–4 weeks, recurs often, or comes with swallowing trouble, a neck mass, ear pain on one side, weight loss, or history of smoking/alcohol.
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Voice care that’s usually safe</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">For 1–2 weeks (if no red-flags)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Relative voice rest:</strong> speak softly and briefly; <em>avoid whispering</em> (it strains cords)</li>
          <li><strong>Hydration & humidity:</strong> warm drinks, soups, steam inhalation or cool-mist humidifier</li>
          <li><strong>Lozenges/sips</strong> to reduce throat clearing; avoid menthol if it dries your throat</li>
          <li><strong>Rinse after inhaled steroids</strong> to prevent thrush</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <p class="kicker">Call your clinician soon if</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hoarseness persists &gt;3–4 weeks or worsens</li>
          <li>Painful swallowing, choking/coughing with liquids, or persistent cough</li>
          <li>You recently had thyroid/neck/chest surgery or intubation</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p class="small">This page is educational and not a diagnosis. Seek urgent care for the warning signs above.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds-caution" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine notes & cautions</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Medicines for hoarseness">
          <thead>
            <tr><th scope="col">Option</th><th scope="col">What it does</th><th scope="col">Notes for seniors</th></tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Acid reducers</strong> (H2 blockers/PPIs)</td>
              <td>Reduce reflux-related irritation</td>
              <td>Trial if reflux clues; use lowest effective dose; discuss long-term PPI risks</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Nasal saline</strong> (sprays/rinses)</td>
              <td>Moisturize, reduce drip</td>
              <td>Gentle, safe; good daily habit in dry climates</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Topical/oral antifungals</strong></td>
              <td>Treat oral/throat thrush</td>
              <td>Consider if on inhaled steroids, dentures, or antibiotics with white patches</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Voice therapy</strong></td>
              <td>Retrains healthy voice use</td>
              <td>Effective for nodules, muscle tension dysphonia, aging voice</td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Caution:</strong> Drying medicines (first-gen antihistamines, some antidepressants/anticholinergics, decongestants) can worsen hoarseness. Avoid smoking; limit alcohol. Do <em>not</em> use antibiotics for simple viral hoarseness.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet & habits -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Hydration, reflux & habits</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Helpful choices</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Warm water with honey/lemon, non-caffeinated teas, broths</li>
          <li>Reflux care: smaller meals, avoid late-night eating; elevate head of bed</li>
          <li>Gentle nasal saline; manage allergies with clinician-guided options</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Limit/avoid</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Smoking/vaping; secondhand smoke exposure</li>
          <li>Excess caffeine, alcohol, chocolate, peppermint, and spicy/fatty foods if refluxy</li>
          <li>Habitual throat clearing—sip water or swallow instead</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

          <td>Identify triggers & risks</td>
          <td>Onset, surgery/intubation, reflux/allergy, smoking/alcohol, swallowing or breathing issues</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Flexible laryngoscopy</strong></td>
          <td>Look directly at vocal cords</td>

          <td>Find causes/extent</td>

        <tr>
          <td><strong>Treatments</strong></td>
          <td>Relieve & prevent</td>
          <td>Voice therapy, reflux management, remove irritants; injections or surgery for paralysis/benign lesions; oncologic care if cancer</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Prevention & safer speaking</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Hydrate well; use a humidifier in dry seasons</li>
          <li>Warm up the voice before prolonged speaking; use a microphone when possible</li>
          <li>Avoid shouting/whispering; take voice breaks during the day</li>
          <li>Manage reflux and allergies; stop smoking</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Early evaluation of persistent hoarseness helps protect swallowing, prevent aspiration, and rule out serious causes.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How long can I wait?</p>
        <p>If hoarseness lasts longer than <strong>3–4 weeks</strong> (or sooner with risk factors), schedule an ENT evaluation.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is whispering better than talking?</p>
        <p>No—whispering strains the cords. Use a soft, normal voice and keep sentences short; rest the voice.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can reflux cause hoarseness without heartburn?</p>
        <p>Yes. “Silent reflux” can irritate the vocal cords. Lifestyle changes and an acid-reducer trial may help.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When is hoarseness an emergency?</p>
        <p>If you have trouble breathing, noisy breathing, drooling, severe throat pain, or rapidly worsening swelling—seek emergency care.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
