  <!-- What is -->
  <section id="what-is" class="section">
    <h2>What counts as diarrhea?</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">In short</span> Loose or watery stools occurring more often than usual (typically ≥3 times/day). Most brief episodes are viral or food-related and improve in a few days. Older adults are at higher risk of dehydration, so early fluid replacement matters.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Watch for:</strong> reduced urination, dry mouth, dizziness on standing, or fatigue — signs of dehydration that need attention.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>Urgent warning signs</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Black stools, visible blood, or severe abdominal pain</li>
        <li>High fever, repeated vomiting, confusion, or fainting</li>
        <li>Very little urine, fast heartbeat, or severe thirst</li>
        <li>Recent antibiotic use with worsening diarrhea (possible C. difficile)</li>
        <li>Diabetes with high sugars and dehydration symptoms</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">If these occur, seek urgent medical care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Short-term (usually days)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Viral gastroenteritis (“stomach flu”)</li>
          <li>Foodborne illness (toxins/infection)</li>
          <li>Traveler’s diarrhea</li>
          <li>Medication side effects (antibiotics, metformin, magnesium antacids, some sweeteners)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Longer or recurring</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Lactose intolerance or other food intolerances</li>
          <li>Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS)</li>
          <li>Bile acid diarrhea after gallbladder issues</li>
          <li>Inflammatory bowel disease, celiac disease (less common but important)</li>
          <li>Chronic infection (including <em>C. difficile</em> after antibiotics)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after Causes) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Hydration -->
  <section id="hydration" class="section">
    <h2>Hydration & ORS</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fluids first</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Frequent small sips: water, oral rehydration solution (ORS), clear soups</li>
          <li>ORS replaces both water and salts; plain water alone may not be enough</li>
          <li>Limit alcohol, very sweet drinks, and high-caffeine beverages</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        If you have <strong>heart or kidney disease</strong>, follow your clinician’s fluid plan. Rapid drinking may worsen swelling or breathlessness — use measured, steady intake.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Home care -->
  <section id="home-care" class="section">
    <h2>Home care & food choices</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What to eat (first 24–48 hours)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Simple, low-fat, low-fiber choices: rice, toast, crackers, bananas, applesauce, yogurt/curd, dal/clear soups</li>
          <li>Small amounts more often; advance diet as symptoms ease</li>
          <li>If lactose sensitive, choose lactose-free dairy or curd/yogurt instead of milk</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What to limit</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Greasy/fried foods, heavy spices early on</li>
          <li>Sorbitol/xylitol (sugar-free candies/gums), very sweet juices</li>
          <li>Raw salads or high-fiber bran during acute phase</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Helpful extras</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Probiotics or live-culture yogurt may help some cases</li>
          <li>Gentle rest; avoid strenuous activity until fully rehydrated</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Improvement is often seen within 1–3 days for simple viral/food causes. Continue ORS and light foods until comfortable.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after Home care) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Medicine cautions -->
  <section id="med-cautions" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine cautions</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Medicine</th><th scope="col">Notes for older adults</th><th scope="col">Discuss with your clinician</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Loperamide</strong> (anti-diarrheal)</td>
          <td>Can slow gut; avoid if high fever or blood in stool</td>
          <td>Safe short use for non-bloody diarrhea if advised</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bismuth subsalicylate</strong></td>
          <td>May darken stools/tongue; interacts with blood thinners</td>
          <td>Check interactions and kidney function</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Antibiotics</strong></td>
          <td>Not routinely needed; some cause diarrhea or <em>C. difficile</em></td>
          <td>Use only when indicated (travelers’ with fever, confirmed bacterial)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Metformin, magnesium antacids, orlistat</strong></td>
          <td>Can worsen diarrhea</td>
          <td>Ask about dose timing, alternatives, or temporary adjustments</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Diuretics (water pills), ACE inhibitors</strong></td>
          <td>Higher dehydration/low blood pressure risk</td>
          <td>Sick-day rules; monitor BP/weight and symptoms</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Never start/stop medicines without guidance — plans are individualized.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>When tests are needed</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Consider stool/blood tests if</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fever, blood/mucus in stool, severe pain, or illness > 3–7 days</li>
          <li>Recent antibiotic use, recent hospitalization, or travel</li>
          <li>Significant dehydration, frailty, or multiple conditions</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What may be ordered</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stool culture or PCR panel; test for <em>C. difficile</em> when appropriate</li>
          <li>Electrolytes, kidney function; sometimes thyroid, celiac panel for chronic cases</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Prevention -->
  <section id="prevention" class="section">
    <h2>Prevention & travel tips</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Handwashing before meals and after bathroom use</li>
          <li>Cook meats thoroughly; keep hot foods hot and cold foods cold</li>
          <li>Use safe water; peel fruits/vegetables or wash well</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Travel</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Bottled/boiled water; avoid ice if unsure</li>
          <li>Carry ORS packets and quick guidance from your clinician</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- When to call -->
  <section id="when-to-call" class="section">
    <h2>When to contact your clinician</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>No improvement after 48–72 hours, or symptoms for > 1 week</li>
        <li>Any red-flag sign (blood in stool, high fever, severe pain, confusion)</li>
        <li>Ongoing diarrhea with weight loss or at night</li>
        <li>You have heart/kidney disease, diabetes, or are on diuretics and feel dehydrated</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is the BRAT diet required?</p>
        <p>No. Simple, low-fat, low-fiber foods are fine. Include protein (dal, yogurt, eggs) and advance as tolerated.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I stop dairy?</p>
        <p>Some people are lactose sensitive during/after illness. Try yogurt/curd or lactose-free milk first.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When can I use loperamide?</p>
        <p>Short-term non-bloody diarrhea without high fever, if your clinician has said it’s okay. Avoid in bloody stools/fever.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How much should I drink?</p>
        <p>Frequent small sips aiming for pale yellow urine. If you have heart/kidney issues, follow your fluid plan and call if unsure.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
