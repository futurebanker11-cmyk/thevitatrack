  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What yellowing can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Yellow skin or eyes (scleral icterus) happens when <strong>bilirubin</strong> builds up in the blood. In older adults this may come from <strong>liver disease</strong>, <strong>bile duct blockage</strong> (gallstones, narrowing, or tumors), or faster <strong>breakdown of red blood cells</strong> (hemolysis). New jaundice needs timely medical evaluation.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p>Clues: right-upper belly pain or fever (gallbladder infection), dark urine and pale/gray stools (blocked bile), itching, easy bruising/bleeding, confusion or sleep-wake reversal (possible liver failure), weight loss (concern for blockage or tumor), recent new medicines or herbal supplements.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Severe right-upper abdominal pain with fever and chills (possible ascending cholangitis).</li>
        <li>Confusion, extreme sleepiness, or new personality change (possible liver failure/encephalopathy).</li>
        <li>Yellowing with <strong>very dark urine, pale stools, and fever</strong>.</li>
        <li>Severe vomiting, dehydration, or rapid worsening over hours to days.</li>
        <li>Bleeding (vomiting blood, black stools) or easy bruising with jaundice.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can be life-threatening. Seek urgent care.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes & clues</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Cause</th><th scope="col">Typical features</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bile duct blockage</strong> (gallstones, stricture, tumor)</td>
          <td>Dark urine, pale stools, itching; RUQ pain ± fever</td>
          <td>Often needs imaging and sometimes <strong>ERCP</strong> or surgery</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hepatitis</strong> (viral, autoimmune, alcohol-related, fatty liver)</td>
          <td>Fatigue, poor appetite, nausea; tender liver</td>
          <td>Lab pattern: high ALT/AST; identify cause for treatment</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Medication or supplement injury</strong></td>
          <td>Onset after new drug/herb; itching, abnormal labs</td>
          <td>Statins, amoxicillin-clavulanate, acetaminophen excess, herbal teas, etc.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Cirrhosis / chronic liver disease</strong></td>
          <td>Abdominal swelling, leg edema, spider veins, easy bruising</td>
          <td>Risk: alcohol, hepatitis B/C, metabolic fatty liver (diabetes, obesity)</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hemolysis</strong></td>
          <td>Dark urine, anemia, jaundice without pale stool</td>
          <td>High LDH/reticulocytes, low haptoglobin; evaluate causes</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Gilbert’s syndrome</strong></td>
          <td>Mild yellowing during illness/fasting, normal exam</td>
          <td>Benign; unconjugated bilirubin rises; clinician confirms</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Pancreatic or bile duct cancer</strong></td>
          <td>Painless jaundice, weight loss, itching</td>
          <td>Often obstructive pattern; needs imaging and specialist care</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- First steps -->
  <section id="first-steps" class="section">
    <h2>First steps at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Check medicines/supplements</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>List recent new pills, over-the-counter pain relievers, herbal products, or high doses of acetaminophen.</li>
          <li>Do not take more acetaminophen; avoid alcohol until evaluated.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Hydration & gentle diet</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Small sips of water or oral rehydration; light meals (soups, rice, toast).</li>
          <li>Skip heavy, very fatty meals if belly pain or nausea.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Arrange a <strong>prompt appointment</strong> for labs and imaging—new jaundice should be seen quickly, even if you feel well.
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Keep a short symptom log (pain, fever, stool/urine color, appetite, weight).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Test</th><th scope="col">Purpose</th><th scope="col">Details</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Liver panel</strong></td>
          <td>Pattern (hepatitis vs blockage)</td>
          <td>ALT/AST, ALP, GGT, total/direct bilirubin, albumin, INR</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Viral & autoimmune markers</strong></td>
          <td>Find hepatitis causes</td>
          <td>Hep A/B/C tests, ANA, SMA, IgG, others as indicated</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Abdominal ultrasound</strong></td>
          <td>Look for duct dilation, stones, masses</td>
          <td>Often first-line imaging; may follow with CT/MRCP</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Hemolysis workup</strong></td>
          <td>Rule in/out RBC breakdown</td>
          <td>CBC, reticulocytes, LDH, haptoglobin, smear</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>ERCP / EUS</strong></td>
          <td>Relieve obstruction or obtain tissue</td>
          <td>Specialist procedures when blockage is suspected</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If blocked bile duct</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stone removal with <strong>ERCP</strong>, stent placement, or surgery.</li>
          <li>Antibiotics for cholangitis; fluids and monitoring.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If hepatitis / medication injury</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stop the offending drug; treat viral or autoimmune causes specifically.</li>
          <li>Supportive care; avoid alcohol; monitor labs until improving.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If chronic liver disease</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Manage complications (ascites, varices, encephalopathy), vaccinations, nutrition, and cause-directed therapy.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">If hemolysis</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Treat underlying cause (immune, mechanical, medication); transfusion if needed.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Plans are individualized—other conditions (kidney, heart, diabetes) guide safe choices.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Diet & daily care -->
  <section id="diet" class="section">
    <h2>Diet & daily care</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Gentle nutrition</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Small frequent meals; lean proteins (fish, eggs, yogurt, legumes).</li>
          <li>Plenty of fruits/vegetables; limit deep-fried and very fatty foods if they trigger pain.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What to avoid (until cleared)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Alcohol and unnecessary acetaminophen; high-dose supplements/herbs.</li>
          <li>Very salty, ultra-processed foods if swelling or chronic liver disease.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        Stay up to date with vaccines (flu, COVID, hepatitis as advised).
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Gentle walking as energy allows; prioritize sleep; manage itching (cool room, moisturizer; medicines only if prescribed).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Onset date, stool/urine color changes, itching, fever, pain (location/score).</li>
          <li>Weight, appetite, energy, and any confusion or sleep-wake changes.</li>
          <li>All medicines and supplements with start dates and doses.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Bring your log to visits—helps pinpoint the cause and measure recovery.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can dehydration cause yellow skin?</p>
        <p>Dehydration deepens urine color but doesn’t cause true jaundice. Yellowing of the eyes/skin suggests raised bilirubin and needs evaluation.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Dark urine and pale stools—what does it mean?</p>
        <p>It points to <strong>blocked bile flow</strong>. Contact your clinician promptly, especially if fever or pain is present.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How fast should I be seen?</p>
        <p>New jaundice should be assessed <strong>soon</strong> (within days). Go to urgent care/ER for red-flag symptoms.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need to stop medicines?</p>
        <p>Do not abruptly stop prescribed medicines on your own. Call your clinician; bring the full list, including herbs and over-the-counter items.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
