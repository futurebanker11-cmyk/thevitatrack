  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What fatigue can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> <strong>Fatigue</strong> is more than feeling sleepy—it’s low energy or exhaustion not relieved by rest. In older adults, causes often include <strong>poor sleep</strong>, <strong>low mood</strong>, <strong>infections</strong>, <strong>anemia</strong>, <strong>thyroid</strong> or <strong>vitamin issues</strong>, <strong>heart/lung problems</strong>, <strong>pain</strong>, and <strong>medications</strong>.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Clues:</strong> weight change, shortness of breath, snoring/apneas, chest palpitations, new swelling, thirst/urination changes, fever, pain, or recent medication changes.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Fatigue with <strong>chest pain</strong>, pressure, or shortness of breath</li>
        <li>New <strong>confusion, fainting, weakness on one side, or severe headache</strong></li>
        <li>Very low blood pressure, fast/irregular heartbeat, or oxygen saturation <strong>&lt;90–92%</strong> at rest (if you monitor)</li>
        <li>Signs of severe infection: fever with shaking chills, persistent vomiting, inability to keep fluids, or rapidly worsening weakness</li>
        <li>Dark/tarry stools or visible blood with dizziness (possible bleeding)</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can signal heart/vascular problems, stroke, severe infection, low oxygen, or internal bleeding.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-checks -->
  <section id="self-check" class="section">
    <h2>Simple self-checks</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">At home (if no red flags)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Rate fatigue 0–10 <em>morning, afternoon, evening</em> for 1 week</li>
          <li>Check for <strong>snoring/apneas</strong> (ask a bed partner) and morning headaches</li>
          <li>Orthostatic check: note symptoms when rising; if able, compare sitting vs standing BP/HR</li>
          <li>Review appetite, weight changes, hydration (urine color), and recent illnesses</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Bring to visits:</strong> symptom diary, medication/supplement list, and any home BP/HR or oxygen readings.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Self-care -->
  <section id="self-care" class="section">
    <h2>Safe steps to boost energy</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Hydrate</strong>: steady sips; aim for pale-yellow urine unless on fluid restriction</li>
          <li><strong>Regular meals</strong> with protein at each (eggs, yogurt, lentils, fish)</li>
          <li><strong>Sunlight & movement</strong> within 1 hour after waking (5–15 minutes walk)</li>
          <li>Limit long daytime naps; if needed, keep to <strong>20–30 minutes</strong> early afternoon</li>
          <li>Plan one meaningful <strong>connection</strong> daily (call/visit)—mood lifts energy</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <strong>Pacing:</strong> Break tasks into chunks with short rests (activity–rest–activity). Use a timer and sit for prep tasks when possible.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Meds -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medication & supplement notes</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Ask for a <strong>medication review</strong> to reduce daytime sedatives (benzodiazepines, strong antihistamines, opioids)</li>
          <li>Avoid starting new “energy” supplements without guidance—interactions are common</li>
          <li>Iron/B12/folate should be taken only if deficient or advised by a clinician</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Caffeine:</strong> A small morning dose may help; avoid after early afternoon to protect sleep and reduce nighttime urination.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Sleep -->
  <section id="sleep" class="section">
    <h2>Better sleep routine</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Keep a <strong>consistent schedule</strong> (bed/wake within 30 minutes daily)</li>
          <li>Wind-down: dim lights, quiet reading/music; avoid news/screens 1 hour before bed</li>
          <li>Bedroom: cool, dark, and quiet; use night-lights for safe bathroom trips</li>
          <li>If you snore, gasp, or wake unrefreshed, ask about <strong>sleep apnea testing</strong></li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Gentle stretching, a warm shower, or brief relaxation breathing can ease sleep onset without meds.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Activity -->
  <section id="activity" class="section">
    <h2>Activity & pacing</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Build capacity safely</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Start with <strong>short walks</strong> or chair exercises on most days</li>
          <li>Add light strength twice weekly (bands, bodyweight, cans)</li>
          <li>Include balance practice (heel-to-toe, single-leg hold with support)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Stop and seek advice</strong> if you develop chest pain, fainting, extreme breathlessness, or irregular heartbeat during activity.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table" aria-label="Clinical evaluation for fatigue">
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Purpose</th><th>Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Identify contributors</td>
          <td>Sleep, mood, pain, falls, meds, vitals, heart/lung exam</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Labs</strong></td>
          <td>Check common causes</td>
          <td>CBC (anemia), CMP/electrolytes, TSH, B12/folate ± iron studies, A1C/fasting glucose, vitamin D as needed</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Cardio-respiratory</strong></td>
          <td>Assess exertional fatigue</td>
          <td>ECG, chest X-ray, BNP, echo, spirometry/pulmonary tests when indicated</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Sleep evaluation</strong></td>
          <td>Rule out apnea</td>
          <td>Questionnaires, home or lab sleep study</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Other</strong></td>
          <td>Targeted causes</td>
          <td>Depression/anxiety screening, inflammation/autoimmune tests if symptoms suggest</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p class="small">Treatment targets the cause—iron/B12 for deficiencies, thyroid/diabetes care, sleep apnea therapy, medication adjustments, mood support, rehab and activity plans.</p>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Daily fatigue scores and activities (what worsens/helps)</li>
          <li>Sleep schedule, naps, and awakenings</li>
          <li>Meals, fluid intake, weight changes</li>
          <li>Shortness of breath, palpitations, swelling, fevers</li>
          <li>All medicines/supplements and any recent changes</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        Bring this log to visits—helps pinpoint causes and avoid unnecessary tests.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Caregivers -->
  <section id="caregivers" class="section">
    <h2>For caregivers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Support with dignity</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Schedule <strong>morning tasks</strong> when energy is highest</li>
          <li>Encourage fluids, balanced meals, and short outdoor time daily</li>
          <li>Help with medication review and sleep-friendly routines</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Call the clinician for:</strong> new chest pain, fainting, severe shortness of breath, black/tarry stools, fevers/chills, or rapid functional decline.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How much fatigue is “not normal”?</p>
        <p>When tiredness <strong>limits daily activities</strong>, lasts &gt;2–3 weeks, or comes with red-flag symptoms—get evaluated.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will vitamins boost my energy?</p>
        <p>Only if you’re deficient. Random supplements can interact with medicines—check first and test for deficiencies.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Could sleep apnea be the cause?</p>
        <p>Yes—especially with loud snoring, witnessed apneas, morning headaches, or high blood pressure. Testing can help.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is exercise safe when I’m tired?</p>
        <p>Gentle, paced activity is usually helpful. Stop and seek care for chest pain, fainting, severe breathlessness, or irregular heartbeat.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
