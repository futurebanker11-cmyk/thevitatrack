  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What leakage can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Urinary incontinence is common and treatable. It can come from <strong>pelvic floor weakness</strong>, <strong>overactive bladder</strong>, <strong>prostate enlargement</strong>, medicines, constipation, mobility or memory issues, or bladder irritation (UTI, caffeine, acidic drinks).</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p>Identifying the <strong>pattern</strong> (leak with cough/laugh, sudden urge, dribbling, nighttime) is the key to the right plan.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD ===== -->
  

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Inability to pass urine</strong> with painful lower belly (urinary retention)</li>
        <li><strong>Fever</strong>, burning, back pain, confusion with urinary symptoms (possible infection)</li>
        <li>New <strong>weakness/numbness</strong> in legs or loss of bowel control</li>
        <li>Visible <strong>blood in urine</strong>, or severe lower abdominal pain</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These need urgent assessment to protect kidneys and nerves.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Types & causes -->
  <section id="types" class="section">
    <h2>Types & common causes</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Type</th><th scope="col">Clues</th><th scope="col">Common causes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Stress</strong> (movement)</td>
          <td>Leak with cough, sneeze, laugh, lifting</td>
          <td>Pelvic floor weakness (pregnancy/aging), pelvic surgery</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Urge / OAB</strong></td>
          <td>Sudden strong urge; barely make it in time; frequent trips</td>
          <td>Bladder overactivity, irritants (caffeine), UTIs, neuro conditions</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Overflow</strong></td>
          <td>Dribbling, weak stream, feels not empty, frequent small voids</td>
          <td>Prostate enlargement (BPH), severe constipation, some meds, diabetes neuropathy</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Functional</strong></td>
          <td>Leak because can’t reach toilet in time</td>
          <td>Mobility limits, balance issues, poor lighting, cognitive decline</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Mixed</strong></td>
          <td>Features of >1 type</td>
          <td>Very common in older adults</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- First steps -->
  <section id="first-steps" class="section">
    <h2>First steps at home</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fluids & bladder irritants</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Spread fluids during the day; reduce 2–3 hours before bed.</li>
          <li>Cut back on <strong>caffeine</strong> (tea/coffee/cola) and very acidic juices.</li>
          <li>Aim for pale-yellow urine; avoid “just-in-case” overdrinking at night.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Constipation matters</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Regular bowel routine; more fiber (as tolerated) and walking.</li>
          <li>Constipation can worsen leakage and retention.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">Bathroom setup</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Night lights to the toilet; clear path; raised seat or commode if needed.</li>
          <li>Clothing that’s <strong>easy to remove</strong> (elastic waist).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Bladder diary</strong>: for 3–7 days, write times of drinks/voids, amount (small/medium/large), leaks, and triggers. Bring to your visit.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Training -->
  <section id="training" class="section">
    <h2>Bladder training & pelvic floor</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Bladder training (urge/OAB)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Start with your usual interval (e.g., every 60–90 min), then <strong>delay by 5–10 min</strong> each week toward 2–3 hours.</li>
          <li>When urge hits: <strong>stop, sit or stand still, quick pelvic squeezes</strong>, breathe, then walk calmly to toilet.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Pelvic floor squeezes (stress/mixed)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Tighten muscles as if stopping gas/urine (don’t hold breath). Hold 3–5 sec, relax 5–10 sec. Repeat 10 times, three sets daily.</li>
          <li><strong>Squeeze before a cough/lift</strong> (“the knack”).</li>
          <li>Pelvic floor physiotherapy can check technique and progress.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Night -->
  <section id="night" class="section">
    <h2>Night strategies</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Last fluids 2–3 hours before bed (unless medically directed otherwise).</li>
          <li>Elevate legs for 30–60 min in the evening to shift daytime swelling (less night urine) if advised.</li>
          <li>Void right before bed; consider bedside commode.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card ok">
        <strong>Safety first:</strong> keep glasses, phone, and lights within reach; avoid rushing.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Pads & skin care -->
  <section id="pads" class="section">
    <h2>Pads, briefs & skin care</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Choosing products</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Use <strong>urine-specific pads/briefs</strong> (better absorption, odor control).</li>
          <li>For men with dribbling: male guards or drip collectors; for heavier leakage: pull-on briefs.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Protecting skin</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Change promptly; cleanse gently; apply <strong>barrier cream</strong> (zinc oxide or petrolatum) to prevent irritation.</li>
          <li>Air time for skin; watch for rashes—treat early to avoid breakdown.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after Pads & skin) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Medicine factors -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicine factors</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can worsen leakage</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Diuretics</strong> (water pills), especially late-day dosing → urgency/night trips</li>
          <li><strong>Alpha-blockers</strong> (some BP/prostate meds) → stress leakage in some</li>
          <li>Alcohol, caffeine, some sedatives → urgency or reduced awareness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Never change or stop medicines on your own. Ask about <strong>dose timing</strong> (morning vs evening) and alternatives.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="tests" class="section">

    <table class="table" role="table">

        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">Purpose</th><th scope="col">Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>

        <tr>
          <td><strong>Urinalysis & culture</strong></td>
          <td>Check for infection or blood</td>
          <td>Rule out UTI or irritation</td>
        </tr>

          <td><strong>Post-void residual (PVR)</strong></td>
          <td>Check emptying</td>
          <td>Bladder scan ultrasound after voiding</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Further tests (selective)</strong></td>
          <td>Clarify complex cases</td>
          <td>Urodynamics, cystoscopy, kidney/bladder ultrasound</td>

  <!-- Treatment -->
  <section id="treatment" class="section">
    <h2>Treatment options</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Behavioral first-line</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Bladder training</strong> and <strong>pelvic floor therapy</strong></li>
          <li>Fluid/caffeine timing; constipation plan; safe bathroom access</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Medicines (select types)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>OAB:</strong> antimuscarinics (may cause dry mouth, constipation, confusion in some older adults) or <strong>mirabegron</strong> (check BP/heart history).</li>
          <li><strong>BPH (men):</strong> alpha-blockers (relax prostate), 5-alpha-reductase inhibitors (shrink prostate) — discuss side effects and falls risk.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Devices & procedures</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Pessary</strong> for stress leakage in women (fitted device to support urethra)</li>
          <li><strong>Botox</strong> injections for refractory OAB; <strong>nerve stimulation</strong> (PTNS, sacral)</li>
          <li>For selected men with BPH: minimally invasive procedures or TURP</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        Plan is individualized by type, health history, and goals. Review medicines regularly to reduce side effects that worsen leakage or falls.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Times/amounts of drinking and voiding; leaks and triggers</li>
          <li>Urgency scale (0–3), night trips, pad changes</li>
          <li>Constipation, new medicines, caffeine intake</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

        A brief diary helps match the <strong>type</strong> to the <strong>right treatment</strong> and shows progress.

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is leakage a normal part of aging?</p>
        <p>No. It’s common but <em>treatable</em>. Matching the type to the plan often improves control.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will drinking less fix it?</p>
        <p>Extreme fluid restriction can cause dehydration and constipation. Smart timing and reducing irritants works better.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Are OAB pills safe for seniors?</p>
        <p>Some have memory and constipation side effects. There are alternatives. Discuss risks/benefits with your clinician.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do pelvic floor exercises really help?</p>
        <p>Yes—especially for stress or mixed leakage. Coaching improves technique and results.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
