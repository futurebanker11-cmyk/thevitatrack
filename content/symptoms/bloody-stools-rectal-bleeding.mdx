  <!-- About -->
  <section id="about" class="section">
    <h2>What bleeding can mean</h2>
    <p class="lead"><span class="badge">Overview</span> Blood noticed on toilet paper, in the bowl, mixed with stool, or stools that appear <strong>black/tarry</strong> can come from many conditions—from harmless fissures or hemorrhoids to ulcers, diverticular bleeding, colitis, or growths in the colon. Older adults and anyone on <strong>blood thinners</strong> should take new bleeding seriously.</p>
    <div class="card small">
      <p><strong>Key goals:</strong> identify red flags, avoid aggravating medicines, keep hydrated, prevent straining, and arrange timely evaluation.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD ===== -->
  

  <!-- Red flags -->
  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call emergency</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Passing <strong>large amounts</strong> of blood or clots, or feeling faint/weak</li>
        <li><strong>Black, tarry stools</strong> with dizziness, vomiting blood, or severe abdominal pain</li>
        <li>Bleeding while on <strong>warfarin, DOACs</strong> (apixaban, rivaroxaban, etc.), or <strong>dual antiplatelet</strong> therapy</li>
        <li>Bleeding after a recent procedure (polypectomy, surgery) or with fever</li>
        <li>Bleeding with fast heart rate, shortness of breath, or chest pain</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">These can signal significant gastrointestinal bleeding or anemia. Call your local emergency number.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Colors/appearance -->
  <section id="colors" class="section">
    <h2>What color/appearance suggests</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Appearance</th><th scope="col">Possible source</th><th scope="col">Notes</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Bright red on paper or bowl</strong></td>
          <td>Anal fissure, hemorrhoids, rectal irritation</td>
          <td>Often with constipation/straining or hard stools</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Maroon/dark red mixed with stool</strong></td>
          <td>Colon bleeding (diverticula, colitis, polyps)</td>
          <td>Needs timely evaluation, especially in older adults</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Black, tarry stools (melena)</strong></td>
          <td>Upper GI bleeding (stomach/duodenal ulcer)</td>
          <td>Emergency if associated with weakness, dizziness, or chest pain</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Stool looks red after foods</strong></td>
          <td>Beetroot, red dyes, some meds (e.g., rifampin)</td>
          <td>If unsure, still discuss with your clinician</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Causes -->
  <section id="causes" class="section">
    <h2>Common causes in later life</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Often minor</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Hemorrhoids</strong> (internal/external): painless bright red blood</li>
          <li><strong>Anal fissure</strong>: sharp pain with bowel movement + small bright red streaks</li>
          <li><strong>Diverticula</strong> (can bleed suddenly, sometimes large volume)</li>
          <li><strong>Inflammation</strong> (infectious colitis, ischemic colitis)</li>
          <li><strong>Polyps</strong> and <strong>angiodysplasia</strong> (fragile vessels)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Important to rule out</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Stomach/duodenal ulcers</strong> (melena, anemia)</li>
          <li><strong>Colon cancer</strong> or advanced polyps</li>
          <li><strong>Inflammatory bowel disease</strong> (ulcerative colitis, Crohn’s)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- First steps -->
  <section id="first-steps" class="section">
    <h2>Safe first steps</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Hydrate</strong> (small, frequent sips) if passing blood</li>
          <li><strong>Avoid straining:</strong> stool-softening habits (see below)</li>
          <li><strong>Don’t start or increase NSAIDs</strong> (ibuprofen/naproxen) or aspirin without advice</li>
          <li><strong>Note color/amount</strong>, presence of clots, and any dizziness</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card note">
        <strong>Call your clinician soon</strong> for any new bleeding, even if small—especially if age 60+, on blood thinners, or with anemia history.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- ===== In-article AD (after First steps) ===== -->
  

  <!-- Medicines -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines & blood thinners</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Can increase bleeding</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Anticoagulants:</strong> warfarin, apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, edoxaban</li>
          <li><strong>Antiplatelets:</strong> aspirin, clopidogrel, prasugrel, ticagrelor</li>
          <li><strong>NSAIDs:</strong> ibuprofen, naproxen, diclofenac</li>
          <li>High-dose steroids can contribute to ulcer risk</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card info">
        <strong>Do not stop prescription blood thinners on your own.</strong> Call the prescriber for individualized guidance if bleeding occurs.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Tests -->
  <section id="tests" class="section">
    <h2>What clinicians may do</h2>
    <table class="table" role="table">
      <thead>
        <tr><th scope="col">Step</th><th scope="col">Purpose</th><th scope="col">Examples</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>History & exam</strong></td>
          <td>Locate likely source</td>
          <td>Color, amount, pain, bowel habits, meds, vital signs</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Basic tests</strong></td>
          <td>Assess blood loss/inflammation</td>
          <td>CBC (hemoglobin), iron studies, stool tests, chemistries</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Anoscopy/rectal exam</strong></td>
          <td>Look for fissure/hemorrhoids</td>
          <td>Quick office procedures</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Endoscopy as needed</strong></td>
          <td>Find/treat source</td>
          <td>Colonoscopy; EGD for suspected upper GI bleed</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td><strong>Imaging</strong></td>
          <td>Active/obscure bleeding</td>
          <td>CT angiography, tagged RBC scan in select cases</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </section>

  <!-- Tracking & bowel care -->
  <section id="tracking" class="section">
    <h2>What to track & bowel care</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Track for your visit</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Stool appearance: <strong>bright red / maroon / black</strong></li>
          <li>How often, estimates of amount, clots present</li>
          <li>Abdominal or rectal pain, fevers, weight loss, dizziness</li>
          <li>All medicines and doses (especially blood thinners/NSAIDs)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card calm">
        <p class="kicker">Constipation prevention (if fissures/hemorrhoids)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Fluids (unless restricted), fruit/veg, and gradual <strong>fiber</strong> (oats, psyllium)</li>
          <li>Short sitting time on toilet, feet supported; avoid straining</li>
          <li>Ask about a gentle stool softener if needed</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Is a little bright red blood always hemorrhoids?</p>
        <p>No—common, but other causes exist. New bleeding in older adults still deserves a clinician review.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do black stools always mean bleeding?</p>
        <p>Iron pills or bismuth can darken stools, but <strong>tarry</strong> stools with weakness/dizziness may mean upper GI bleeding—seek urgent care.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Should I stop my blood thinner?</p>
        <p>Don’t stop on your own. Contact your prescriber quickly; plans depend on the medicine and risk of clots vs. bleeding.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What eases fissure pain?</p>
        <p>Soft stools, warm sitz baths, brief topical anesthetic if advised, and avoiding straining. Persistent pain needs assessment.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
