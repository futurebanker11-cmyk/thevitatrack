    <div class="section">
      <h2>Day-By-Day Overview (Easy To Follow)</h2>
      <div class="plan-grid">
        <div class="plan-card">
          <span class="day-label">Day 1</span>
          <strong>Know Your Starting Point</strong>
          <p style="margin-bottom:6px;">
            Today you simply watch what is already happening. Do not change anything yet – just
            notice and write it down in simple steps:
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Keep a small notebook or piece of paper by your bed.</li>
            <li>In the morning, write how many times you woke up to pee.</li>
            <li>Circle the hardest trip: the one where you felt the most urgency or strain.</li>
            <li>Write what you drank after 6 p.m. (water, tea, coffee, alcohol, soft drinks).</li>
            <li>Note whether your bladder felt empty or “still full” after each trip.</li>
          </ul>
          <p style="margin-top:8px;">
            By the end of Day 1, you have a clear picture of your real starting point.
            This makes every other day of the plan more meaningful.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="plan-card">
          <span class="day-label">Day 2</span>
          <strong>Gentle Drink Reset</strong>
          Today, shift your drinks earlier. Aim to get most of your water before late evening.
          Have a glass on waking, another mid-morning, and one in the afternoon. After dinner,
          cut down on large cups of tea, coffee, or alcohol. Keep a small sip of water by the
          bed only if your doctor advises it. Tonight, notice if the number or urgency of trips
          changes at all.
        </div>

        <div class="plan-card">
          <span class="day-label">Day 3</span>
          <strong>Bathroom Timing & “Double Voiding”</strong>
          Plan a calm bathroom visit just before bed. Take your time. Sit down if that helps you
          relax the muscles around the bladder and prostate. When you feel finished, wait a few
          moments, stand up or shift position, and try gently again. This “double voiding” can
          sometimes help more urine leave the bladder so you do not get that immediate “need to go
          again” feeling.
        </div>

        <div class="plan-card">
          <span class="day-label">Day 4</span>
          <strong>Prostate-Friendly Foods</strong>
          Add at least one prostate-friendly food to your day: a tomato-based dish, a handful of
          berries, or a salad with leafy greens. At the same time, reduce heavy late-night salty
          snacks that make you thirsty later. If you have diabetes, keep portions modest and fit
          them into your usual meal plan. Write down which foods feel easiest to keep long-term.
        </div>

        <div class="plan-card">
          <span class="day-label">Day 5</span>
          <strong>Light Movement For Blood Flow</strong>
          If your doctor says it is safe, include 15–20 minutes of light movement today: a
          comfortable walk, slow marching in place near a chair, or gentle stretching. The goal is
          not heavy exercise but simply better blood flow to the pelvis and legs. Set a simple
          reminder on your phone or place a note on the TV remote so you do not forget.
        </div>

        <div class="plan-card">
          <span class="day-label">Day 6</span>
          <strong>Organize Your Doctor Notes</strong>
          Now gather your notebook. Look at your week of nights, drinks, and bathroom trips.
          Circle anything that stands out: more trips after alcohol, less urgency when you drink
          earlier, or burning or pain that needs attention. Turn these into 3–5 clear questions
          for your doctor. This way, your visit becomes a working conversation, not just a quick
          “in and out.”
        </div>

        <div class="plan-card">
          <span class="day-label">Day 7</span>
          <strong>Review & Decide On Extra Support</strong>
          Use today to review the whole week. Did the number of trips change even a little? Did
          double-voiding help? Which food and drink changes felt realistic? Decide which habits to
          keep and which do not suit you. If you feel you would benefit from extra natural
          support, this is also the day to carefully review the prostate support supplement
          information below and, if needed, show it to your doctor.
        </div>

        <div class="plan-card">
          <span class="day-label">Week 2 And Beyond</span>
          <strong>Turn Small Steps Into Routine</strong>
          This plan is not “one week and done.” Most seniors see the best results when they keep
          the easier parts going – earlier hydration, calmer evenings, clear doctor notes – and
          repeat them week after week. Use this as a gentle base routine for your prostate, not a
          short-term challenge.
        </div>
      </div>
