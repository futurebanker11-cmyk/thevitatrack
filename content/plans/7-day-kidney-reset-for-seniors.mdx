  <section id="who" class="section">
    <h2>Who this plan fits (and who should not use it)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Best for</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Older adults with <strong>mild kidney concerns</strong> or early chronic kidney disease (CKD 1–3) looking to reduce salt and improve hydration.</li>
          <li>People who feel puffy, get leg swelling, or notice higher blood pressure when eating restaurant/packaged food.</li>
          <li>Caregivers who need a simple, repeatable 1-week structure.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Avoid or get medical guidance first</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>CKD stage 4–5</strong>, on dialysis, or rapidly changing kidney numbers.</li>
          <li>Recent illness, vomiting/diarrhea, or <strong>dehydration</strong>.</li>
          <li>Taking meds that affect potassium/fluid (e.g., spironolactone) without a clinician’s plan.</li>
          <li>Low-sodium restrictions <em>and</em> heart failure — discuss fluid limits with your clinician first.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">This is educational, not medical advice. Personalize with your healthcare team.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="goals" class="section">
    <h2>Weekly goals (simple & measurable)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Sodium:</strong> aim ~<strong>1,500–2,000 mg/day</strong> (unless your clinician sets different).</li>
          <li><strong>Fluids:</strong> sip through the day to meet your personalized target (often 1.5–2 L/day if no restriction).</li>
          <li><strong>Protein:</strong> steady, moderate: ~<strong>0.8 g per kg body weight</strong> unless told otherwise.</li>
          <li><strong>Fiber:</strong> add plants and whole grains to keep bowels regular and support blood sugar.</li>
          <li><strong>Movement:</strong> 10–20 minutes gentle walking or chair exercises daily (as safe).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="note">
        <strong>Label tip:</strong> “% Daily Value” for sodium — <em>5% is low</em>, <em>20% is high</em>. Choose products with ≤140 mg per serving when you can.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="shopping" class="section">
    <h2>Shopping list (1 week, low-salt)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Produce</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Apples, berries, grapes (kidney-friendly fruits)</li>
          <li>Cucumber, carrots, bell peppers, green beans, cabbage</li>
          <li>Leafy greens (small portions if potassium-restricted)</li>
          <li>Lemon/lime for flavor</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="kicker" style="margin-top:10px">Proteins</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Skinless chicken, white fish (tilapia/cod), eggs or egg whites</li>
          <li>Unsalted peanut butter, tofu (check sodium)</li>
          <li>Plain Greek yogurt (check phosphorus additives)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Grains & pantry</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Old-fashioned oats, rice, whole-wheat or low-sodium bread/wraps</li>
          <li>Dry lentils/split peas (portion mindful if potassium-restricted)</li>
          <li>Unsalted nuts/seeds (small handfuls)</li>
          <li>Low-sodium broth, no-salt spice blends (garlic powder, pepper, herbs)</li>
          <li>Olive oil, vinegar (apple cider/rice), mustard (check sodium)</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="kicker" style="margin-top:10px">Beverages</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Water, lemon water, unsweet tea, coffee (limit sugary drinks)</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="hydration" class="section">
    <h2>Hydration & sodium limits</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Fluids (general idea)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Common target is <strong>1.5–2 L/day</strong> unless you have a fluid restriction. Spread sips through the day.</li>
          <li>Use a marked bottle (500 mL) and aim for 3–4 fills.</li>
          <li>Limit sugary drinks; avoid “sports” drinks unless instructed.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Sodium</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Goal: <strong>1,500–2,000 mg/day</strong>. Main sources are packaged food, restaurant meals, deli meats, canned soups, pickles.</li>
          <li>Swap with herbs, garlic, onion powder, citrus, vinegar, pepper, smoked paprika.</li>
          <li>Choose “<strong>no salt added</strong>” or products with ≤140 mg per serving.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="minerals" class="section">
    <h2>Potassium & phosphorus — what to know</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Potassium</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Some with CKD must <strong>limit high-potassium foods</strong> (bananas, oranges, potatoes, tomatoes, spinach, beans). Your lab (“K+”) guides this.</li>
          <li>Lower-potassium options: apples, berries, grapes, cabbage, green beans, rice, oats, lettuce, peppers.</li>
          <li>Cooking trick: “<strong>leach</strong>” potatoes (peel, cube, boil in fresh water, drain) to reduce potassium.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Phosphorus</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Prefer fresh foods. <strong>Additives</strong> like “phosphate,” “phosphoric acid,” “pyrophosphate” in processed meats/cheeses/sodas raise phosphorus quickly.</li>
          <li>Choose dairy in modest portions; check if you need a binder (clinician-directed).</li>
          <li>Dark colas & processed cheese often contain phosphate additives — limit or pick alternatives.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines & supplements to review</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>NSAIDs</strong> (ibuprofen, naproxen) can strain kidneys — ask for safer pain options.</li>
        <li><strong>Salt substitutes</strong> often contain potassium chloride — not safe for everyone.</li>
        <li>Herbal supplements (e.g., licorice, aristolochia) may harm kidneys or raise blood pressure. Review all supplements with your clinician.</li>
        <li>Diuretics, ACE inhibitors/ARBs, and potassium-sparing meds may change potassium/fluid needs — follow your dosing plan; don’t stop on your own.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="daily-check" class="section">
    <h2>Daily check-in card (morning & evening)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Morning (2–3 minutes)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Weigh yourself after bathroom. <strong>Gain &gt;2 lb (1 kg) in 24–48 hr?</strong> Note it.</li>
          <li>Check blood pressure if you have a cuff. Record.</li>
          <li>Fill your water bottle for the day; schedule short walk.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Evening</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>How were <strong>swelling, breath, energy</strong>?</li>
          <li>Did you keep salt lower and take meds on time?</li>
          <li>Plan breakfast + one protein for tomorrow.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="red-flags" class="section">
    <h2>When to call for help</h2>
    <div class="danger">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>New or worsening <strong>shortness of breath</strong> or chest pain.</li>
        <li>Sudden <strong>leg swelling</strong>, or weight gain &gt;2 lb (1 kg) in 24–48 hours.</li>
        <li><strong>Very low urine output</strong> or dark, frothy urine; severe weakness or confusion.</li>
        <li>Muscle cramps or heart palpitations — could be electrolyte issues.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Seek urgent care for severe symptoms. For routine questions or lab-based changes, contact your clinician.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Kidney reset — quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Will I see changes in a week?</p>
        <p>Many people notice less ankle swelling, fewer nighttime bathroom trips, and steadier blood pressure within days. Lab changes (creatinine, potassium) are monitored by your clinician over time.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">How much protein is “moderate”?</p>
        <p>A common target is ~<strong>0.8 g/kg/day</strong> (for 70 kg ≈ 56 g/day), adjusted for diabetes, frailty, or dialysis. Spread protein through meals.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do I need special “renal” products?</p>
        <p>Not usually. Focus on <strong>fresh foods</strong>, low-sodium choices, and avoiding phosphate additives. Use specialty products only if advised.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What about potassium restrictions?</p>
        <p>Only if your labs or clinician say so. If restricted, swap to lower-potassium fruits/veggies and use leaching for potatoes. Recheck labs as scheduled.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="section">
    <h2>Keep going after Day 7</h2>
    <div class="card small">
      <ul class="list">
        <li>Repeat your favorite 2–3 days from the plan, rotate proteins, and try a new herb blend each week.</li>
        <li>Schedule lab follow-up and bring your <strong>weight/BP notes</strong>.</li>
        <li>Set a “low-salt pantry” rule: broths, beans, tomatoes = “no salt added.”</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>
