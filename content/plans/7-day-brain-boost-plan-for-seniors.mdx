  <section id="who" class="section">
    <h2>Who this plan fits (and when to get medical help first)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Good fit</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Older adults who want clearer focus, steadier energy, and better recall.</li>
          <li>Caregivers who need a simple, repeatable 1-week structure.</li>
          <li>People with controlled conditions (BP, diabetes) who can walk safely.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Check with your clinician first if</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>New or rapid <strong>memory decline</strong>, personality changes, or repeated falls.</li>
          <li>Untreated sleep apnea, severe depression, heavy alcohol use.</li>
          <li>Recent head injury, stroke/TIA, or major medication changes.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">This guide is educational and not medical advice. Personalize with your care team.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Pillars -->
  <section id="pillars" class="section">
    <h2>The 6 brain pillars for this week</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Food:</strong> Mediterranean-style, low added sugar, steady protein, colorful plants.</li>
          <li><strong>Movement:</strong> daily walk + light strength + balance drills.</li>
          <li><strong>Sleep:</strong> fixed wake/bed times, bright morning light, quiet evenings.</li>
          <li><strong>Mind:</strong> short skill mini-lessons (language, music, puzzles).</li>
          <li><strong>Social:</strong> 10–15 minutes of meaningful contact daily.</li>
          <li><strong>Medical:</strong> review meds that fog thinking; hydrate; protect hearing/vision.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="note">
        <strong>Small steps win:</strong> 10 minutes done daily beats 1 hour once. Stack new habits to existing ones (after breakfast → walk 10 minutes).
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Food -->
  <section id="food" class="section">
    <h2>Brain-forward foods (simple swaps)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Add more</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Omega-3s:</strong> salmon, sardines, trout; or ground flax/chia/walnuts.</li>
          <li><strong>Color plants:</strong> berries, leafy greens, peppers, tomatoes, carrots.</li>
          <li><strong>Whole grains & fiber:</strong> oats, brown rice, barley, beans, lentils.</li>
          <li><strong>Olive oil, nuts, seeds</strong> for healthy fats.</li>
          <li><strong>Hydration:</strong> water, unsweet tea, coffee (early day), soups.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Cut back</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>High sugar snacks/drinks, heavy alcohol, ultra-processed meals high in sodium.</li>
          <li>Late caffeine (after 2 pm) if it disrupts sleep.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="kicker" style="margin-top:10px">Plate pattern (MIND style)</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>½ plate vegetables/fruit, ¼ whole grain/bean, ¼ lean protein, olive oil drizzle.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- Movement -->
  <section id="movement" class="section">
    <h2>Movement & balance (safe at home)</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Daily</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li><strong>Walk 10–20 min</strong> (split into 2–3 short walks as needed).</li>
          <li><strong>Strength 10 min</strong>: sit-to-stand, wall pushups, band rows, calf raises.</li>
          <li><strong>Balance 5 min</strong>: stand near counter—feet together → semi-tandem → tandem.</li>
          <li>Finish with gentle stretches for calves, hamstrings, chest.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="note">
        <strong>Safety:</strong> Wear supportive shoes, clear pathways, keep a sturdy surface nearby. Stop with chest pain, severe shortness of breath, or dizziness.
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Sleep -->
  <section id="sleep" class="section">
    <h2>Sleep & circadian routine</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Anchor the day</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Wake within the same 30-minute window daily; get <strong>bright light</strong> within 1 hour.</li>
          <li>Limit long daytime naps (&lt;30 minutes, before 3 pm).</li>
          <li>Stop caffeine after 2 pm; finish dinner 2–3 hours before bed.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Wind-down</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>“Screens dim” 60 minutes before bed; low light, quiet activity, breathing 4-6.</li>
          <li>Bedroom cool, dark, quiet. Consider hearing/vision aids within reach.</li>
          <li>If awake &gt;20 minutes, get up to a chair and read until drowsy.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Medicines & supplements -->
  <section id="meds" class="section">
    <h2>Medicines & supplements to review</h2>
    <div class="card">
      <ul class="list">
        <li><strong>Anticholinergic</strong> burden (certain bladder, allergy, sleep aids) can cloud thinking — ask about alternatives.</li>
        <li>Check for sedatives, daytime alcohol, or opioid combinations increasing fall risk.</li>
        <li>Ensure <strong>B12</strong>, thyroid, hearing/vision are up to date if cognition seems off.</li>
        <li>Supplements: fish oil, vitamin D, or magnesium may be useful for some — only with clinician guidance and kidney awareness.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="small">Never stop or start prescription meds without medical advice.</p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Safety -->
  <section id="safety" class="section">
    <h2>Memory safety & home setup</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Home</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Good lighting at entry, stairs, and hallways; night-lights to bathroom.</li>
          <li>Remove loose rugs; wear non-slip footwear.</li>
          <li>Keep a <strong>grab-and-go health list</strong> (meds, allergies, contacts) on the fridge.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Memory supports</p>
        <ul class="list">
          <li>Large wall calendar + daily pillbox + phone reminders.</li>
          <li>Place “important basket” by the door for keys, glasses, hearing aids.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- FAQ -->
  <section id="faq" class="section">
    <h2>Brain boost — quick answers</h2>
    <div class="grid">
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">When will I notice a change?</p>
        <p>Many feel more steady energy and better sleep in 3–7 days; memory and stamina improve with weeks of repetition.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Do puzzles alone improve memory?</p>
        <p>They help, but the <strong>combination</strong> of sleep, movement, diet, social contact, and learning delivers the biggest gains.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">Coffee — good or bad?</p>
        <p>Morning coffee can aid alertness. Avoid late-day caffeine if sleep suffers. Keep added sugar minimal.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <p class="kicker">What if walking is hard?</p>
        <p>Try seated marching, arm cycling, or short hallway laps with support. Any safe movement counts.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
